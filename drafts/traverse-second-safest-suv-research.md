# Research: The Chevrolet Traverse — Second-Safest SUV in America

## Slug: traverse-second-safest-suv
## Article #512
## Journalist: Rex Driverton

## Core Data (FARS 2014-2023)

### Traverse Stats
- Deaths: 265 (10 years)
- Annual: 26.5
- Crashes: 675
- Avg fleet: 1,050,000
- VMT: 13,125 million
- Death rate: 0.20 per 100M VMT
- Impairment: 19.1% (perfectly average)
- Alcohol: 14.2%, Drugs: 8.1%

### Model Year Deaths
- MY 2009: 12, 2010: 15, 2011: 20, 2012: 21
- MY 2013: 12, 2014: 15, 2015: 20, 2016: 22
- MY 2017: 30 (peak), 2018: 20, 2019: 26, 2020: 25, 2021: 16
- Remarkably flat — no spike, no era-specific danger

### Ranking
- #2 safest SUV (200+ deaths): RAV4 (0.19) → **Traverse (0.20)** → Forester (0.26) → Sportage (0.28)

### Chevrolet Brand Spread
- Safest: Traverse (0.20)
- Deadliest: Tracker (7.83)
- Spread: **39x** within one brand
- Other Chevy SUVs: Equinox (0.36), Blazer (0.56), Suburban (1.36), Tahoe (2.49), Trailblazer (2.83)

### 3-Row SUV Competitors
- Traverse: 0.20 (baseline)
- Honda Pilot: 0.29 (1.45x worse)
- Toyota Highlander: 0.42 (2.1x worse)
- Ford Explorer: 1.54 (7.7x worse)

### The Paradox
- NHTSA: 5/5 stars overall
- 2026 recalls: zero model-specific
- BUT: **No IIHS Top Safety Pick for 2026** — failed under tightened rear-seat criteria
- Consumer Reports: pulled "Recommended" rating (owner complaints about transmission, electronics)
- The vehicle with the second-lowest death rate in America can't get an IIHS award

## Angle
The Traverse is the second-safest SUV on American roads by the hardest metric there is — actual deaths. Its death rate is 7.7x lower than the Ford Explorer. But IIHS won't give it an award, and Consumer Reports pulled its recommendation. The gap between lab testing and real-world outcomes has never been wider. Rex Driverton investigates.

## Original Contribution
First-ever analysis showing the Traverse as #2 safest SUV by FARS death rate, combined with the IIHS award paradox. Nobody has connected the real-world fatality data to the 2026 IIHS snub.
