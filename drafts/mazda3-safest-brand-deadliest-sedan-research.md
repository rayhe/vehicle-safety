## Counterargument
The high rate is partly driven by older model years (2004-2013). Newer Mazda3s are dramatically safer. The FARS rate is a 10-year blended figure — not a snapshot of current vehicles. Mazda's engineering has genuinely improved.

## Limitations
- VMT estimates introduce uncertainty for lower-volume vehicles
- FARS captures fatal crashes only; injury rates could tell a different story
- Fleet estimates are rough (262,500) which affects rate precision
- The CX-5's rate benefits from being a newer vehicle (2013+ only) while the Mazda3's rate includes 2004+ model years
