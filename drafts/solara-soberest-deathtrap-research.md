# Research Notes: Toyota Solara — The Soberest Death Trap in America

## Angle
The Toyota Solara has the lowest impairment rate of any vehicle in the FARS dataset (4.1% — 100+ drivers threshold) while simultaneously posting a death rate of 4.25 per 100M VMT — more than double the Camry's 2.03. 96% of fatal Solara crashes involved completely sober drivers. This is the anti-drunk-driving story: a car that kills responsible people.

## Kill Test
✅ **Genuinely surprising** — directly contradicts the assumption that the most dangerous vehicles attract reckless/impaired drivers
✅ **Novel cross-tabulation** — impairment × death rate across all 307 FARS models; Solara is a statistical outlier by any measure
✅ **Platform comparison** — the Solara shares the Camry platform, making the 2x death rate gap unexplainable by engineering alone
✅ **Not covered** — no existing Solara story in 224 articles

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulation of impairment rate × fatality rate across all FARS vehicle models reveals the Solara as the most extreme outlier: lowest impairment, highest-quartile death rate. This quantifies the "sober death trap" phenomenon — vehicles where engineering/demographics kill more than behavior.

## Probable Explanation
- Discontinued 2008 — all fleet units in FARS window (2014-2023) are 6-15+ years old
- Aging fleet = aging drivers (likely skewing 55+)
- Coupe body style: reduced rear visibility, smaller crumple zone vs sedan
- Older occupants more fragile in crashes (same impact force → higher fatality)
- The "sober but old" driver profile: doesn't drink, does drive aging hardware

## Strongest Counterargument
FARS toxicology data depends on testing rates. If jurisdictions systematically under-test elderly crash victims (presuming sobriety), the 4.1% figure could be artificially low. However, FARS toxicology testing rates for sedans are generally consistent (~80%+ testing rate), and even with correction, the Solara would still be the lowest-impairment vehicle in the dataset by a wide margin.

## Limitations
- FARS only captures fatal crashes — injury crashes unknown
- Fleet size estimates introduce ±15% uncertainty
- Solara's small sample (195 tested drivers) vs Camry's 13,811
- Cannot separate driver age directly from FARS model-level data
- Coupe classification (FARS tags it as "Sedan") masks structural differences
