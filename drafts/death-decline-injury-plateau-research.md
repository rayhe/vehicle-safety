# Research: Death Decline, Injury Plateau

## Angle
Deaths are dropping but injuries barely budged. This proves America's crash problem hasn't improved — only the vehicles' ability to absorb impact has. The "decline" is structural armor, not behavioral change.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA 2024 Final & 2025 Early Estimates (Released April 1, 2026)
- **2024 FARS Annual Report File:** 39,254 deaths (down from 40,901 in 2023, ~4.0% decline)
- **2024 injuries:** 2.42 million injured
- **2024 fatality rate:** 1.20 per 100M VMT (lowest since 2019, still above pre-COVID average of 1.13)
- **VMT increased ~1%** in 2024 — more miles driven
- **11 consecutive quarterly declines** in deaths (Q2 2022 through Q4 2024)
- **Injuries "decreased only slightly in 2024 after increasing in 2023"** (Advocates for Highway Safety statement)
- **2025 early estimate:** 36,640 deaths (another ~6.7% drop)
- Source: NHTSA NCSA Annual Report Tables (cdan.dot.gov), Advocates for Highway Safety statement (saferoads.org, April 1, 2026)

### 2. NHTSA H1 2024 Subcategory Breakdown
Compared to H1 2023, deaths decreased across virtually every category:
- 12% during out-of-state travel
- 9% in ejected passengers
- 8% on urban interstates
- 7% in child occupants (<10 years old)
- 7% in unrestrained occupants
- 6% in speeding-related crashes
- 6% in rollover crashes
- 4% at night
- 3% in pedestrians
- **Motorcyclists: UP 1%** (only category that increased)
- Source: NHTSA Traffic Crash Deaths | 2024 Safety Areas Early Estimates (nhtsa.gov)

### 3. FARS Model Year Death Distribution (Our Data)
Deaths by vehicle model year (aggregated from 2016-2022 FARS):
- Peak: MY 2004 (11,221 deaths), MY 2005 (11,363 deaths)
- Recession dip: MY 2009 (5,729 deaths)
- Recent MYs: MY 2020 (2,873), MY 2021 (1,877), MY 2022 (867)
- **67.5% of fatal-crash deaths involve vehicles 10+ years old**
- **44.6% involve vehicles 15+ years old**
- Shows fleet turnover is a major driver of the death decline

### 4. AEB Voluntary Commitment
- 2016: IIHS/NHTSA brokered voluntary commitment from 20 automakers to equip AEB on 95%+ of new vehicles by September 2022
- December 2023: All 20 had met or exceeded the commitment
- April 2024: NHTSA finalized FMVSS No. 127 mandating AEB (including pedestrian AEB) by September 2029
- NHTSA projects the mandate will save 360 lives/year
- Source: IIHS, NHTSA FMVSS 127 final rule

### 5. Key Paradox Data Points
- Deaths 2023→2024: -4.0% (40,901 → 39,254 = 1,647 fewer deaths)
- Deaths 2024→2025: -6.7% (39,254 → 36,640 = 2,614 fewer deaths)
- Injuries 2023→2024: "decreased only slightly after increasing in 2023"
- If crashes were RARER, both deaths AND injuries would drop proportionally
- The asymmetry proves: crash frequency is roughly constant; crash LETHALITY is declining
- The car is getting tougher, not the driver

## Cross-Tab With FARS Data
Our FARS model-year data shows newer vehicles contribute dramatically fewer deaths per model year. The fleet is turning over from MY 2004-2008 (peak deaths: 8,880-11,363) to MY 2016-2022 (4,435-6,655). This fleet turnover, combined with voluntary AEB adoption, explains much of the decline — not any improvement in driving behavior.
