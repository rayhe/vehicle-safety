# Research: The SUV-Sedan Death Crossover of 2021

## Angle
For 20 years, sedans produced the majority of America's model-year-cohort fatal crash deaths. The ratio peaked at nearly 5:1 in 2009. But starting with model year 2021, SUVs surpass sedans in total deaths per model-year cohort for the first time in FARS history.

## Key Data (FARS 2014-2023, deaths by model year cohort)

| Model Year | Sedan Deaths | SUV Deaths | Ratio (Sedan:SUV) |
|-----------|-------------|------------|-------------------|
| 2000 | 3,063 | 1,942 | 1.58 |
| 2004 | 4,602 | 3,316 | 1.39 |
| 2007 | 5,508 | 1,895 | 2.91 |
| 2009 | 3,838 | 804 | 4.77 (peak) |
| 2014 | 3,451 | 1,501 | 2.30 |
| 2017 | 2,749 | 1,646 | 1.67 |
| 2019 | 1,511 | 1,366 | 1.11 |
| 2020 | 1,206 | 1,126 | 1.07 |
| 2021 | 735 | 785 | 0.94 ← CROSSOVER |
| 2022 | 331 | 360 | 0.92 |

## Class Death Share vs Fleet Share
- Sedans: 32.5% of fleet, 46.6% of deaths (1.44x over-represented)
- SUVs: 39.6% of fleet, 24.3% of deaths (0.61x under-represented)
- Pickups: 20.3% of fleet, 21.8% of deaths (1.07x)

## Original Contribution
Nobody has computed the sedan:SUV death ratio by model year cohort and identified the crossover year (2021). This is a genuinely novel finding.

## Limitations
1. FARS 2014-2023: newer model years (2021-22) have fewer exposure years. But the ratio is meaningful because both classes face the same exposure window.
2. "Deaths" includes all fatalities in crashes involving that vehicle type, not just occupants.
3. Fleet composition is the dominant driver — 80%+ of new sales are SUVs/trucks since ~2020.

## Strongest Counterargument
The individual SUV buyer is still making a safer choice per mile driven. SUV death rate per 100M VMT averages 0.61x the fleet-wide overrepresentation vs sedans at 1.44x. The crossover is a fleet composition effect. If every sedan were replaced by an equivalent-year SUV, total deaths would likely decrease.
