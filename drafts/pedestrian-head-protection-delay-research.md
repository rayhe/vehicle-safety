# Research Notes: Pedestrian Head Protection Delay

## Angle
Europe required cars to protect pedestrians' heads in 2005. NHTSA proposed FMVSS 228 in September 2024. Eighteen months later, it's still in "Analyzing Comments." 67 lives per year are the cost of waiting.

## Kill Test
- **Does this story exist?** No. `hood-height-pedestrian-death-machine.html` covers hood height/pedestrian deaths but does NOT cover FMVSS 228, GTR 9, the regulatory delay, or the 67 lives/year figure. No story mentions FMVSS 228.
- **Is the data novel?** Yes. The 21-year US-EU regulatory gap, the $2.86-$4.08/vehicle cost, the fact that a 2003 Ram and 2005 Silverado already pass the proposed test — none of this has been published in our stories.

## Primary Sources
1. **NHTSA NPRM** (89 FR 76922, Sept 19, 2024): FMVSS 228 proposal, 67.4 fatalities mitigated annually, $2.86-$4.08/vehicle cost, net benefits $480-$593M, cost per equivalent life saved $1.10M
2. **GHSA 2024 Pedestrian Report**: 7,148 pedestrian deaths in 2024 (down 4.3% from 7,472 in 2023), still 20% above 2016 levels
3. **NTSB Comment Letter** (Nov 15, 2024, Docket NHTSA-2024-0057): NTSB "supports this proposed rulemaking"
4. **Reginfo.gov unified agenda**: Status "Analyzing Comments" since Dec 2024, no legal deadline
5. **EU pedestrian protection history**: Euro NCAP pedestrian test since 1997, EU Directive 2003/102/EC (2003), GTR 9 (2008)

## FARS Cross-Reference
- Vehicles most affected by FMVSS 228: pickups and large SUVs (25% of US sales)
- F-150: 9,194 deaths total in FARS, fleet 700K
- Silverado: 9,591 deaths, fleet 656K  
- Ram: 4,407 deaths, fleet 481K
- NHTSA's own testing: 2003 Dodge Ram and 2005 Chevy Silverado did NOT exceed the proposed HIC limit — existing trucks can pass
- 49% of frontal pedestrian fatalities involve MPVs (trucks, SUVs), 37% passenger cars

## Key Data Points
- 67.4 lives/year saved (NHTSA PRIA estimate, after PAEB effect)
- Cost: $2.86-$4.08 per vehicle ($48.94-$60.43M total annual)
- Net benefit: $480-$593M annually
- 900:1 benefit-to-cost ratio (comparable to IIHS's 900:1 ROI)
- Pedestrian deaths up 57% from 2013 (4,779) to 2022 (7,522)
- 76% of pedestrian deaths at night
- Status: "Analyzing Comments" — no legal deadline for finalization
- EU has had equivalent since 2005, US proposed in 2024

## Counterarguments & Limitations
1. 67 lives/year is less than 1% of ~7,000 annual pedestrian deaths — but $4/car and $1.1M per life saved is extraordinary cost-effectiveness
2. Fleet turnover means even a finalized rule wouldn't save lives for years
3. PAEB (mandated by 2029 via FMVSS 127) addresses some of the same crashes — but FMVSS 228's estimate already accounts for PAEB
4. Trucks/SUVs may need more redesign time — but NHTSA's own testing showed 20-year-old trucks already pass
5. The current administration may deprioritize this rule entirely
