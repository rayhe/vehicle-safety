# Research: The Armada/QX56 Overloaded & Over-Served

## Hook
Nissan just recalled 168,149 of its biggest SUVs — Armada, QX56, QX80 — because the weight label on the door was WRONG. The Gross Axle Weight Rating was too high, meaning these 5,700-lb trucks could be legally overloaded beyond what their axles can handle. The fix? A sticker.

But FARS tells a darker story: the Infiniti QX56 has the **7th-highest impairment rate of any vehicle in America** at 26.3%, while the mechanically identical Nissan Armada sits at 20.0% — exactly average. Same truck, same platform, same wrong weight label. But slap an Infiniti badge on it, and the driver impairment rate jumps 31.5%.

## FARS Data (2014-2023)

### Nissan Armada
- 182 deaths (18.2/year)
- 377 crashes
- Fleet: 262,500
- Rate: 0.55 per 100M VMT
- 764 drivers in fatal crashes
- Impairment: 20.0% any (14.8% alcohol, 9.2% drug)

### Infiniti QX56
- 58 deaths (5.8/year)
- 79 crashes
- Fleet: 131,250
- Rate: 0.35 per 100M VMT
- 274 drivers in fatal crashes
- Impairment: 26.3% any (18.6% alcohol, 10.9% drug) — **RANK 7 of 307 vehicles**

### Combined Platform
- 240 deaths total
- 1,038 drivers in fatal crashes
- 225 impaired drivers
- Combined impairment: 21.7%

## Dataset Context
- Average impairment across 307 vehicles: 20.3%
- QX56 is 30% ABOVE average
- Armada is exactly at average (20.0%)
- Badge premium = +6.3 percentage points = +31.5% relative increase

## Original Analysis: The Badge-Impairment Premium
Same platform (Nissan Y62/F-Alpha), same engine (VK56VD 5.6L V8), same frame, same axles — but the impairment rate diverges 31.5% based on which badge is on the hood. This isn't a vehicle engineering story. It's a buyer-profile story. The QX56 costs $15-20K more new, attracts a different demographic, and that demographic apparently drinks and drives at rates comparable to the Corvette (26.2%) and Cadillac CTS (25.9%).

## Recall Details (July 21, 2026)
- Recall numbers: R26B1 and R26B2
- 168,149 vehicles: 2020-2026 Armada, 2011-2013 QX56, 2014-2017 QX80
- GAWR listed incorrectly on certification labels
- Violation of FMVSS 110 (Tire Selection and Rims)
- Fix: dealers install new labels
- Owner notification: September 4, 2026
- VINs searchable on NHTSA.gov since July 16
