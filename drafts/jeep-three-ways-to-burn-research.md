# Research Notes: Jeep's Three Fire Mechanisms

## Slug: jeep-three-ways-to-burn
## Journalist: Rex Driverton (Senior Crash Correspondent, Investigation)
## Kicker: Investigation

## Angle / Thesis
Stellantis killed the Jeep 4xe hybrid line to stop the fire problem. Today, they recalled 1.3 million gas-powered Wranglers and Gladiators for a completely different fire mechanism. The fire problem was never about the batteries — it's systemic. Three distinct failure modes, three recall waves, one instruction: park outside.

## Why this is novel (kill test)
- Unique framing: No one has aggregated the THREE distinct fire mechanisms (battery cells, battery packs, steering pump wiring) into a systemic-failure narrative
- Original calculation: cumulative fire-affected Jeep fleet exceeds 1.4 million unique US vehicles
- Counterintuitive finding: killing the hybrid line didn't solve the fire problem — it just revealed a different one
- This is TODAY's news (June 9, 2026 recall announcement) with historical depth

## Three Distinct Fire Mechanisms
1. **Battery cell separator damage** (4xe PHEVs) — Samsung SDI cells, lithium exposed to air → fire. Recalled 2024 + 2025.
2. **Battery pack defect** (17,277 vehicles) — separate from cell damage, can fire when turned off. June 2026.
3. **Steering pump wiring overheating** (1.08M Wrangler/Gladiator) — electrical connection in non-hybrid vehicles → combustible materials ignite, even when parked with ignition off. June 2026.

## Strongest Counterargument
"Rare circumstances" — Stellantis's language. The fire incidents relative to fleet size are small percentages. Most of these vehicles will never catch fire. The recall is precautionary, and the company is working on a fix.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers crash fatalities, not vehicle fires — these are separate risk categories
- Exact number of steering pump fires not yet publicly disclosed in today's recall (Bloomberg Law cited 9 at time of lawsuit; actual count may be higher)
- Overlap between 4xe recalls makes unique vehicle counting imprecise
- "Park outside" compliance rate unknown — how many owners actually do it?
