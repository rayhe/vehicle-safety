# Research: Minivans Match SUVs on Death Rates

## Thesis
Americans abandoned minivans for SUVs over the past two decades, partly on safety grounds. FARS data shows vans and SUVs have nearly identical average fatality rates (0.626 vs 0.630 per 100M VMT). Many individual minivans outperform popular SUVs by wide margins. The "buy an SUV for safety" narrative isn't supported by the crash fatality data.

## Kill Test
- **Novel?** YES. No article on VS has made this direct class-level comparison. We've covered minivan rear seats (bad), minivan sobriety (good), specific van models, but never "vans = SUVs on death rate."
- **Surprising?** YES. This contradicts the dominant consumer narrative.
- **Data-backed?** YES. FARS 2014-2023, 28 van models, 121 SUV models.

## Primary Sources

### 1. FARS Data (2014-2023) — Our dataset
- Van class: 28 models, avg rate 0.626, total 7,897 deaths
- SUV class: 121 models, avg rate 0.630, total 46,442 deaths
- Key van standouts:
  - Chrysler Pacifica: 0.19 rate (700K fleet)
  - Ford Transit: 0.14 rate (1.05M fleet)
  - Toyota Sienna: 0.49 rate (743K fleet)
  - Honda Odyssey: 0.93 rate (787K fleet)
- Key SUV comparison points:
  - Chevy Tahoe: 2.49 rate
  - Ford Explorer: 1.54 rate
  - Chevy Trailblazer: 2.83 rate
  - RAV4: 0.19 rate (best SUV match for Pacifica)

### 2. IIHS Fatality Statistics
- IIHS has published that heavier vehicles protect occupants better
- Minivans weigh 4,000-4,500 lbs — comparable to midsize SUVs
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/topics/vehicle-size-and-weight

### 3. Market Data (Capital One, Fortune Business Insights)
- Minivan US market share declined from ~7% to ~2% since 2010
- SUV share grew from ~30% to ~55%+
- "Safety" frequently cited as reason for SUV preference
- Source: capitalone.com article on minivan comeback

### 4. IIHS Minivan Rear-Seat Critique (2023)
- IIHS found minivans underperform on rear-seat protection specifically
- This is a real weakness BUT separate from overall occupant fatality rates
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/minivans-dont-make-the-grade-when-it-comes-to-rear-seat-safety

## Novel Contribution
Cross-class fatality rate comparison: Van avg (0.626) vs SUV avg (0.630) = 0.6% difference = statistically indistinguishable. This exact comparison has not been published, as far as we can determine. The original analysis is the class-level rate equivalence calculated from FARS per-model VMT-adjusted death rates across 149 models.

## Strongest Counterargument
The van class includes commercial vehicles (E-350, Express, Savana) with high rates that pull the average up. If you isolate PASSENGER minivans only, the average drops even lower — making vans SAFER than SUVs. But the SUV class also includes old body-on-frame deathtraps (Tracker, Trailblazer) pulling its average up. The class averages are messy, and fleet composition differs. A more honest comparison is: the minivans families actually buy (Sienna, Odyssey, Pacifica) vs the SUVs families actually buy (Highlander, Explorer, Pilot). Both sets are safe. The van advantage shrinks but doesn't reverse.

## Limitations
- FARS captures fatal crashes only; injury rates may differ
- VMT estimates have ±15% uncertainty for low-volume models
- "Van" class includes commercial vehicles; isolating minivans gives a cleaner comparison
- SUV class is 121 models spanning compact to full-size — not homogeneous
- Newer SUVs (2020+) may have better safety tech not yet visible in 2014-2023 FARS data
