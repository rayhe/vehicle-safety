# Research: Commercial Vehicle Safety Gap

## Angle
IIHS just released first-ever safety evaluations for heavy-duty trucks and commercial vehicles (June 17, 2026). Only 4 of 9 vehicles passed all criteria. Chevy Express — still in production after 30 years on the same platform — lacks seatbelt force limiters, a feature standard on passenger cars since the mid-2000s.

**Novel cross-tabulation:** FARS toxicology data shows commercial vans have the LOWEST impairment rate of any vehicle class (18.1% any-impaired vs 22.5% for sports cars, 20.4% for sedans, 20.1% for pickups). These are America's soberest professional drivers — dying in vehicles that have never been crash-tested and lack safety equipment that's been standard in a Honda Civic since 2006.

## Primary Sources

### 1. IIHS Commercial Vehicle Safety Evaluation (June 17, 2026)
- **First-ever** safety evaluation of heavy-duty trucks and commercial vehicles
- Tested 9 vehicles: 3 Class 3 pickups, 6 cargo vans
- Only **4 of 9 passed** all criteria
- Five criteria tested: front airbags, side airbags, seatbelt pretensioners, force limiters, seatbelt reminders
- Federal rules only require seatbelt chime for 4-8 seconds; IIHS requires 90 seconds
- IIHS data: persistent reminders increase belt usage by 30%

**Passed all 5:**
- 2026 Chevrolet Silverado 3500HD
- 2025 Ford F-350 SuperCrew
- Chevrolet BrightDrop 400
- Ram ProMaster 2500

**Failed:**
- Chevrolet Express 2500 — WORST: missing force limiters AND seatbelt reminders
- Ford Transit T250 — failed seatbelt reminder
- Mercedes-Benz Sprinter 2500 — failed seatbelt reminder
- Rivian Delivery 500 — failed seatbelt reminder
- Ram 3500 — failed seatbelt reminder

### 2. FARS Fatality Data (2014-2023)
Commercial vans in FARS database:
- Ford E-350: 776 deaths, rate 2.51 per 100M VMT
- Chevy Express: 475 deaths, rate 0.92
- GMC Savana: 166 deaths, rate 0.54
- Ford Transit: 178 deaths, rate 0.14
- Ford E-150: 73 deaths, rate 0.35
- Ford E-250: 79 deaths, rate 0.31

### 3. FARS Toxicology by Vehicle Class
- Vans: 18.1% any-impaired (LOWEST of all classes)
- SUVs: 19.5%
- Pickups: 20.1%
- Sedans: 20.4%
- Sports Cars: 22.5% (HIGHEST)

### 4. IIHS/NHTSA Statistics
- 6,535 people died in 2023 in crashes involving heavy/medium-duty trucks or light vans
- That's 16% of all roadway fatalities in the U.S.
- Side curtain airbags NOT legally mandated for commercial vehicles
- IIHS 30x30 plan: reduce fatalities by 30% by 2030

## Novel Contribution
Nobody has cross-referenced the IIHS commercial vehicle safety gap with FARS impairment data to show the paradox: the vehicles with the soberest drivers have the least safety equipment. When a drunk driver hits a tree in a Camaro, that's behavior. When a sober plumber hits a tree in a Chevy Express that doesn't have force limiters, that's engineering negligence perpetuated by a regulatory exemption.

The Express has been in production on the same GMT610 platform since 1996. Thirty years. A human born the year GM started selling it can now get a mortgage. The vehicle still doesn't have seatbelt force limiters.
