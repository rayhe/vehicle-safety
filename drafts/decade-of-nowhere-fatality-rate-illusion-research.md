# Research Notes: The Decade of Nowhere

## Angle
The 2025 U.S. traffic fatality rate of 1.10 per 100M VMT is being celebrated as historic progress. It's the same number as 2019. It's barely below 2014 (1.08). A decade of ESC mandates, AEB proliferation, lane-keeping systems, billions in safety grants — and the death rate moved 0.02 points. The COVID behavioral spike (2020-2022) made "back to normal" look like a miracle.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. Every outlet is running "lowest since 2019!" without noting that 2014 was *lower*. The original contribution: calculating the cumulative excess deaths from the COVID anomaly, and showing that the entire "decline" is just regression to mean.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA 2025 Early Estimates (official)
- 36,640 deaths in 2025, down 6.7% from 2024
- Rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT
- VMT increased ~29.8 billion miles (0.9%) vs 2024
- 39 states + DC + PR saw YoY declines
- 15th consecutive quarterly decline
- Source: nhtsa.gov/press-releases/traffic-deaths-2025-early-estimates-2024-annual

### 2. NHTSA 2024 Annual Data (final)
- 39,254 deaths in 2024
- Rate: 1.19 per 100M VMT
- 2.42 million injuries
- Source: same NHTSA press release

### 3. NHTSA Historical Data (via FARS)
- 2014: 32,744 deaths, ~1.08 per 100M VMT
- 2015: 35,092 deaths, 1.12 per 100M VMT (FHWA resource guide)
- 2019: 36,096 deaths, ~1.10 per 100M VMT (NHTSA 2019 data release)
- 2020: 38,824 deaths, 1.34 per 100M VMT (21% rate spike — NHTSA FARS)
- 2021: ~42,915 deaths (est), ~1.37 per 100M VMT (16-year high)
- 2022: 42,795 deaths, ~1.33 per 100M VMT
- 2023: ~40,990 deaths (est), ~1.24 per 100M VMT
- Source: NHTSA annual FARS releases, BTS transportation statistics

### 4. AASHTO/FHWA VMT Data
- 2025 VMT: ~3.33 trillion miles (derived: 36,640 / 1.10 × 100M)
- 2024 VMT: ~3.30 trillion miles
- VMT has increased steadily, meaning more exposure
- Source: aashtojournal.transportation.org, FHWA

### 5. IIHS ESC Effectiveness
- ESC reduces fatal single-vehicle crash risk by 56% for SUVs, 49% for cars
- Federal ESC mandate: September 2011 (FMVSS 126)
- By 2025, essentially 100% of fleet under 14 years old has ESC
- Source: iihs.org/news/detail/life-saving-benefits-of-esc-continue-to-accrue

### 6. NHTSA Safety Grants
- $665 million in traffic safety grants distributed to states (2025)
- Source: NHTSA press release

## Counterargument (at full strength)
The rate holding steady at 1.10 despite massive fleet and behavioral changes could be an achievement, not a failure. Consider:
- Distracted driving (smartphones) increased dramatically since 2014
- Average vehicle weight increased ~300 lbs (more lethal to pedestrians/cyclists)
- SUV/truck share of sales went from ~50% to ~80% (heavier, taller vehicles)
- Urbanization continued, putting more pedestrians near traffic
- Rideshare VMT added billions of miles of for-hire driving
- The fleet SHOULD be killing more people given these headwinds; holding the line IS the achievement

This is genuinely strong. The counterargument deserves respectful treatment in the article.

## Limitations
1. VMT estimates for 2020-2023 are derived, not official final numbers for all years
2. "Excess deaths" assumes the 2019 rate as the counterfactual — but behavioral changes were partially driven by COVID itself (isolation, substance abuse, recklessness), not just statistical noise
3. The FARS data in our dataset (2014-2023) doesn't perfectly align with the rate discussion (which covers 2014-2025)
4. Rate per 100M VMT doesn't capture risk per trip or per person, which matters for individual decision-making
5. Some of the "excess" may reflect genuine behavioral deterioration that won't fully revert

## Actionable Takeaways
1. The national rate returning to 1.10 means YOUR individual risk hasn't changed in a decade if you drive a similar vehicle on similar roads
2. If you want to beat the average: drive a post-2018 vehicle with AEB (reduces rear-end crash involvement ~50% per IIHS)
3. Avoid driving 2-4 AM on weekends — this is the deadliest window per FARS data
4. Check your VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls — 53 million vehicles have unfixed recalls
5. The biggest safety upgrade you can make isn't tech — it's avoiding alcohol + driving (still 30%+ of all fatalities)
