# Research Notes: Ford's July 4 "Quality First" Ad Campaign Math

## Angle
Ford launches a massive July 4 ad campaign during MLB games claiming "We put quality first. Now Ford is first in quality" — the same week it filed its 51st recall of 2026. The math of advertising quality while operating at a recall velocity of one every 3.6 days.

## Key Data Points

### Ford's Recall Volume (Primary Source: iSeeCars study via Detroit Free Press, Apr 2026)
- 19.6 million vehicles recalled in past 12 months (Apr 2025-Mar 2026)
- More vehicles recalled than the rest of the auto industry combined
- Toyota second with 4.1 million (4.8x less than Ford)
- 152 recalls in 2025 (NHTSA record)
- 51 recalls since Jan 1, 2026 (nearest: Chrysler with 19)
- Ford accounts for 28% of all industry recall actions in 2026
- 12 million+ vehicles affected by 2026 recalls alone

### Novel Calculations
1. **Recall-to-Sales Ratio:** 19.6M recalled / 2.2M sold in 2025 = **8.9 recalls per car sold**
2. **Recall Velocity 2026:** 51 recalls in 182 days (Jan 1-Jul 2) = **1 recall every 3.57 days**
3. **JD Power Window vs Fleet Age:** JD Power IQS measures first 90 days. Average car on US roads is 12.6 years (NHTSA). Average Ford is ~13 years. That's **4,745 days** vs 90 days. JD Power measures 1.9% of a Ford's operational life.
4. **Lincoln Aviator lifetime recall prediction:** 23.4x more recalls than average new car (iSeeCars)
5. **Warranty cost:** $4.8B in 2023 alone (3x industry average, per Detroit Free Press)
6. **Ad reach probability:** Ford market share ~14% of US registered vehicles. With 19.6M recalled and typical recall completion rate of ~50%, roughly 9.8M unfixed Fords on the road. ~16M total registered Fords. **61% of Fords may have an active unfixed recall.**

- "Quality improvements on newer vehicles helped reduce warranty costs by $500 million in 2025 vs. 2024"

### FARS Data (Source: NHTSA FARS 2014-2023)
- Ford Explorer: 3,797 deaths, fatality rate 1.54 per 100M VMT
- Ford F-150: 9,194 deaths, rate 1.04
- Ford Expedition: 1,515 deaths, rate 2.31
- Ford Mustang: 2,739 deaths, rate 6.02
- Ford Escape: 2,284 deaths, rate 0.95
- Ford Ranger: 3,089 deaths, rate 2.91

### Strongest Counterargument
JD Power IQS genuinely measures something real: new vehicle quality in the first 90 days. The "trailing indicator" argument has merit — most recalls DO hit older vehicles. Ford's newer models (2023+) may genuinely be better built. The rehired engineers, new inspections, and reduced warranty costs suggest real improvement. It IS possible to simultaneously have the best new vehicles and the worst legacy fleet. These metrics measure different things.

BUT: Ford is advertising to ALL Ford owners, not just 2026 model year buyers. The average viewer of a July 4 MLB broadcast who owns a Ford is driving a ~2015 model. That person has a statistically significant chance of driving a vehicle with an active recall. Ford's ad doesn't say "our new cars are great." It says "Ford is first in quality." Period.

### Limitations
- 19.6M recall figure counts vehicle-recalls, not unique VINs — same car can appear in multiple recalls
- Recall completion rate estimates (~50%) are approximate and vary by recall severity
- FARS data covers 2014-2023 and includes legacy models no longer in production
- JD Power's methodology (consumer-reported problems, 90-day window) has known biases toward infotainment issues
- Ford's "90% older vehicles" claim is self-reported and not independently verified
