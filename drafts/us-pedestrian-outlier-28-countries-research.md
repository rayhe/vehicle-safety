# Research Notes: U.S. Pedestrian Death Rate Outlier Among 28 High-Income Countries

## Primary Source
CDC MMWR, March 13, 2025: "Pedestrian and Overall Road Traffic Crash Deaths — United States and 27 Other High-Income Countries, 2013–2022"
- Authors: Naumann RB, West BA, Barry V, Matthews S, Lee R
- DOI: 10.15585/mmwr.mm7408a2
- URL: https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/74/wr/mm7408a2.htm
- Data source: International Transport Forum's IRTAD + FARS

## Key Stats

### U.S. Pedestrian Deaths
- 2013: 4,911 deaths, rate 1.55/100K
- 2022: 7,768 deaths, rate 2.33/100K
- Change: +50.0% relative increase
- 2,857 MORE pedestrian deaths in 2022 vs 2013

### 27 Other Countries (Median)
- Median change: -24.7% decrease
- By 2022, U.S. rate (2.33) was ~3x the median of other countries (0.73)
- 3,071 FEWER pedestrian deaths in 2022 vs 2013 across all 27 countries combined

### Overall Traffic Deaths
- U.S.: +22.5% (10.41 → 12.76/100K)
- 27 others: -19.4% median decrease

### Notable Country Comparisons
- Norway: -58.1% pedestrian rate decline (0.40 → 0.17)
- South Korea: -54.0% (3.93 → 1.81) — started HIGHER than U.S., ended LOWER
- Poland: -59.2% (3.00 → 1.22)
- Lithuania: -66.5% (3.30 → 1.10)
- Japan: -37.0% (1.47 → 0.93)
- Germany: -36.1% (0.69 → 0.44)
- UK: -6.1% (0.63 → 0.59)
- France: +1.8% (only other country with slight increase in pedestrian rate)

### Age Groups (2018-2022 averages)
- U.S. has HIGHEST pedestrian death rate among ages 15-24 and 25-64
- Second highest for children 0-14
- South Korea highest for 65+

## The Vehicle Mix Explanation (from CDC paper)
- SUVs, vans, pickups: 79% of new U.S. sales in 2022, up from 50% in 2012
- Pedestrians 50-100% more likely to die when hit by SUV/pickup vs. car
- Higher bumpers, heavier weight, worse visibility
- SUVs more likely to strike pedestrians during turns

## FARS Cross-Reference (our data)
- U.S. fleet composition: 65.9% light trucks (SUV+Pickup+Van)
- Sedan+Sports Car: 34.1% of fleet, 49.8% of deaths
- This fleet mix is unique globally — no other IRTAD country has comparable light truck dominance
