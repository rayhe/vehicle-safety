# Research: Robotaxis Can't See Fire Trucks

## Angle
NHTSA Administrator Jonathan Morrison issued a formal letter to AV companies on July 8, 2026, calling out a "clear pattern" of robotaxis interfering with emergency responders. The industry sold itself on eliminating human error, but these machines can't do what every teenager learns on day one of driver's ed: yield to emergency vehicles. The regulatory response? A polite letter asking companies to "schedule meetings."

Rex Driverton — Investigations

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Administrator Morrison Letter (July 8, 2026)
- "Over the past several months, NHTSA has identified a clear pattern of driverless AVs interfering with law enforcement and other first responders."
- "The agency has documented multiple instances in which AVs drove directly into active emergency scenes, blocked the paths of ambulances and firefighters, or failed to recognize and respond to basic safety conditions like flashing lights, flares, smoke, fire, and traffic cones."
- "This is unacceptable. To state it bluntly: an AV that cannot safely interact with first responders is a danger to the general public."
- "Emergency scenes are not rare or extreme 'edge cases.'"
- "Every second matters when law enforcement officers, firefighters, or paramedics are answering a call because lives are on the line. That is why human drivers who impede these operations are subject to fines and even jail time."
- NHTSA will schedule meetings with AV developers by month's end to demand solutions.
- Source: TechCrunch, Reuters, InsideEVs, Gizmodo, TheStreet (all reporting on the same letter)

### 2. Zoox Recall (July 18, 2026 — NHTSA)
- Amazon's Zoox recalled all 105 autonomous vehicles
- Reason: vehicles may not detect heavy smoke, could impede emergency personnel
- Incident: June 20, 2026 — unoccupied Zoox AV drove into active fire scene obscured by smoke
- Vehicle "braked hard while attempting to steer away before coming to a stop"
- Remote operator had to reverse the vehicle out
- First responders placed cones to block lanes after the AV incident
- Fix: software update to detect heavy smoke
- NHTSA recall filing
- Source: Reuters, Gizmodo

### 3. Waymo Incidents
- Late May 2026: Waymo vehicle in Dallas partially blocked route fire trucks were using to reach apartment building fire (Texas local media)
- Videos showing Waymo vehicles blocking ambulances, driving through active police scenes
- Waymo vehicles passing stopped school buses with lights activated — violation of Texas state law
- Both NHTSA and NTSB investigating separate Waymo incidents
- Source: Reuters, TechCrunch

### 4. TechCrunch Investigation
- Found at least 6 incidents through March 2026 where first responders had to take control of Waymo vehicles
- Move them out of traffic during emergency situations
- One incident: officer in middle of responding to a MASS SHOOTING
- June: officer recorded moving a Waymo to unblock roadway for responders headed to a natural gas explosion at apartment building
- Source: TechCrunch (July 9, 2026)

### 5. Scale of the Problem
- Zoox fleet: 105 vehicles (entire fleet recalled)
- Waymo: largest US robotaxi fleet, operating in LA, Phoenix, SF
- NHTSA letter doesn't name specific companies but details clearly match Waymo and Zoox
- No consequences outlined for non-compliance
- NHTSA only asks companies to present "solutions" by month's end

## Novel Analysis
The regulatory asymmetry: human drivers who block fire trucks face fines ($250-$1,000+ depending on state) and potential jail time. AV companies that do the same get a politely-worded letter asking them to "schedule meetings." Morrison explicitly drew this comparison in the letter but stopped short of applying the same standard.

The "edge case" defense demolished: Morrison's letter explicitly rejected the industry's standard defense — calling emergency scenes "not rare or extreme edge cases." Fire departments respond to ~1.3M fires per year (NFPA). EMS responds to ~40M+ calls per year. If your self-driving car can't handle something that happens 40 million times a year, it can't drive.
