# Research Notes: The "Record-Low" Traffic Death Rate Illusion

## Angle
NHTSA announced 2025 U.S. traffic deaths at 36,640 — the "lowest since 2019" — and a fatality rate of 1.10 per 100M VMT, heralded as "record-low" or "second-lowest ever." But:

1. In 2014, the U.S. had 32,675 deaths and a rate of 1.08 per 100M VMT — LOWER than 2025.
2. The absolute death count is 12% higher than 2014 (36,640 vs 32,675 = ~3,965 more deaths per year).
3. The "record" is only impressive compared to the pandemic spike (42,939 in 2021).
4. The U.S. per-capita rate (~108 per million) is 2.5x the EU average (~44 per million) and ~5x Sweden's (~22 per million).

## Key Data Points

### U.S. Historical Timeline
- 2014: 32,675 deaths, 1.08/100M VMT (actual record low for rate)
- 2015: 35,092 deaths, 1.12/100M VMT (+7.2% deaths)
- 2019: 36,096 deaths, 1.10/100M VMT
- 2020: ~38,824 deaths, 1.34/100M VMT (pandemic: less driving, more reckless)
- 2021: 42,939 deaths, 1.37/100M VMT (pandemic peak)
- 2022: ~42,795 deaths, 1.26/100M VMT
- 2024: 39,254 deaths (4.3% decline from 2023)
- 2025: 36,640 deaths, 1.10/100M VMT (6.7% decline from 2024)

### International Comparison (per million population)
- U.S. 2025: ~108 per million (36,640 / ~340M)
- EU 2024: ~44 per million (19,800 / ~450M)
- Sweden 2024: ~22 per million (lowest in EU)
- Bulgaria/Romania: ~81 per million (worst in EU)

### Key Facts
- Americans drove 29.8 billion MORE miles in 2025 than 2024 (+0.9%)
- 39 states saw year-over-year declines in 2025
- Pedestrian deaths fell 11% year-over-year in 2025
- 15 consecutive quarters of decline
- EU deaths fell from ~25,300 (2014) to 19,800 (2024) = 22% decline in same period
- US deaths ROSE from 32,675 (2014) to 36,640 (2025) = 12% increase

### The VMT Trick
The per-VMT rate looks similar (1.08 vs 1.10) because Americans are driving more. VMT grew from ~3.02 trillion (2014) to ~3.33 trillion (2025). More driving = bigger denominator = lower rate even with more deaths. This is denominator inflation — the safety metric improves because the exposure metric grew, not because fewer people died.

### CDC MMWR Comparison (2013)
A 2013 CDC study of 20 peer countries found:
- U.S. had the HIGHEST crash death rate per 100,000 population (10.3)
- U.S. had the HIGHEST rate per 10,000 registered vehicles (1.24)
- U.S. was 5th-highest per 100M VMT (1.10) — same rate as 2025
- If U.S. matched even Belgium (second-worst), 12,000 fewer deaths annually
- Alcohol-impaired driving: 31% of US deaths

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing per-VMT rates with per-capita rates across a decade shows the U.S. "record" is a denominator illusion. Americans drive ~40% more per capita than Europeans, inflating the VMT denominator and making the per-VMT rate look better than the per-capita reality. The U.S. has 4,000 MORE deaths per year than in 2014 while celebrating "record safety." The EU achieved actual reductions. Same decade, same technology access, opposite outcome.
