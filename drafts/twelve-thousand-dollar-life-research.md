# Research: $12,409 Per Life — The Best Deal in Highway Safety

## Angle
IIHS spent $600M over 30 years and saved 48,352 lives. That's $12,409 per life saved. The DOT values a statistical life at $12.5 million. Insurance companies bought lives at 0.1% of what the government says they're worth — because fewer deaths means fewer claims. Market incentives beat regulatory mandates by three orders of magnitude.

**Differentiator from `iihs-blazer-30-years-saved.html`:** That article (Vin Wreckage, Jun 25) focused on the Blazer crash test spectacle and the fleet-age problem (75% of deaths in pre-2012 vehicles). THIS article focuses on the cost-effectiveness math: what does $12,409/life look like against other safety spending, and what does it say that the insurance industry's selfish investment outperforms the government's well-intentioned one?

## Primary Sources

### 1. IIHS 30th Anniversary Study (June 25, 2026)
- 48,352 lives saved, 1999–2024
- $538 billion societal savings
- ~$600M total IIHS funding from insurance industry
- 28,697 lives from front-crash evaluations alone
- 18,224 from side-impact testing
- Source: https://go.iihs.org/news-50k-lives-saved

### 2. NHTSA FARS Data (2014–2023)
- 191,193 total deaths across 337 tracked models
- 12 models with rate >3.0: 22,160 deaths (high-rate vehicles)
- 243 models with rate ≤1.0: 56,304 deaths (low-rate vehicles)
- Deaths concentrated in older, cheaper vehicles

### 3. DOT Value of a Statistical Life (VSL)
- FY2024: $12.5 million (USDOT guidance)
- Used for cost-benefit analysis of all federal safety rules
- IIHS cost per life = $12,409 = 0.099% of VSL

### 4. DADSS (Driver Alcohol Detection System for Safety)
- $92 million spent since 2008 (16+ years)
- Zero production vehicles equipped
- Zero lives saved
- Congressional authorization, automaker + NHTSA collaboration
- Already covered in `dadss-92-million-tech-doesnt-exist.html` — reference only, don't rehash

### 5. NHTSA Budget & FMVSS Effectiveness
- FY2024 budget: $1.38 billion
- FMVSS standards prevented 860,000+ deaths, 1968–2019
- $17.3 trillion societal benefit (NHTSA estimate)
- ~51 years of regulatory operation

## Key Calculations
- **IIHS cost per life:** $600M / 48,352 = $12,409
- **IIHS ROI:** $538B / $600M = 897:1
- **IIHS vs VSL:** $12,409 / $12,500,000 = 0.099%
- **DADSS cost per life:** $92M / 0 = undefined (∞)

## Thesis
The insurance industry's investment in IIHS crash testing is the single highest-ROI safety intervention in American transportation history. Not because insurance companies care about human life, but because dead policyholders and totaled vehicles cost money. The math worked because market incentives aligned with mortality reduction. Meanwhile, the government's regulatory apparatus — which has also saved hundreds of thousands of lives — operates at orders-of-magnitude higher cost per outcome.
