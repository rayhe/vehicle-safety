# Research: Motorcycle Summer Surge — State-by-State 2026 Data

## Angle
Multiple US states are reporting worst-in-decades motorcycle fatality starts in 2026, even as overall traffic deaths continue declining. No federal dashboard tracks this in real-time — you only learn about the surge from scattered state DOT reports. The divergence between motorcycle deaths and overall traffic safety is accelerating at the state level.

## DIFFERENT from existing article `below-one-not-if-you-ride` (Jul 9, Vin Wreckage)
- That article: National Q1 2026 rate milestone (0.99/100M VMT) + historical divergence since 1997
- This article: State-level 2026 surge data from multiple state DOTs. Real-time, hyper-local, consumer-actionable.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Minnesota Department of Public Safety (Jun 2026)
- 37 motorcycle deaths YTD vs 24 same period 2025 — **54% increase**
- Highest motorcycle fatality count in decades
- On pace for ~90 by year-end (would be near all-time record)
- Source: Minnesota DPS motorcycle safety reports

### 2. Colorado State Patrol / CDOT (Mar 2026)
- 15 motorcycle deaths January–March 2026 — tied highest Q1 since 2017
- Warm weather blamed for early riding season
- Speed is #1 contributing factor
- ~Half of fatally injured riders were not wearing helmets
- Source: CDOT/CSP crash data releases

### 3. Utah Department of Public Safety (Apr 2026)
- 3 motorcycle deaths in one week (week of April 12)
- 72 motorcycle deaths in the prior 12-month period — described as "unheard of"
- Most are single-vehicle crashes (rider error, loss of control)
- Source: Utah DPS press releases

### 4. UK Department for Transport (Feb 2026, for international context)
- Motorcyclist deaths rose 13% to 384 in 2024
- Second consecutive year of increase
- Global pattern, not just US

## FARS Data (National Context)

### Motorcycle deaths nationally
- 2024: 6,228 motorcyclist deaths (IIHS, posted Jun 2026) — near-record (2023 was 6,335)
- Motorcycle deaths = 16% of all traffic deaths (up from 5% in 1997)
- Tripled since 1997 (from ~2,116 to 6,228)

### Per-mile risk
- 2023: 31.39 motorcyclist fatalities per 100M VMT
- Passenger cars: 1.13 per 100M VMT
- **28× more dangerous per mile**
- Motorcycle fatality rate per VMT INCREASED 29.5% from 2012–2021 (23.32 → 30.20)

### Key risk factors
- 47.5% of fatally injured riders NOT wearing helmets
- Only 17 states + DC mandate universal helmet use
- 34% had no valid motorcycle license
- 26% alcohol-impaired (highest of any vehicle type)
- Ages 15-20: fatalities jumped 44% (350→505) from 2022→2023

### Overall traffic context (contrast)
- Q1 2026 overall fatality rate: 0.99 per 100M VMT (lowest since 2014)
- H1 2025: overall fatalities down 8.2% to 17,140
- Car occupant deaths declining, motorcycle deaths climbing

## Actionable Insights (Consumer)
1. Helmet use: 37% effective at preventing death. Only 17 states require it for all riders.
2. Licensing: 34% of dead riders had no valid motorcycle license. Get licensed.
3. Warm weather = higher risk: States with early warm seasons are reporting the worst numbers.
4. Speed and single-vehicle crashes: Most motorcycle deaths are self-inflicted, not caused by cars.
5. Age 15-20 surge: 44% increase in young rider deaths. New riders need training.
