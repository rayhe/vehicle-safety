# Research: Automatic Crash Notification Subscription Paywall Body Count

## Angle
Automakers install automatic crash notification (ACN) hardware in most new cars, then charge $100+/year to keep it active — and disable it when the subscription lapses. NHTSA estimates ACN could save 700+ lives/year. The EU mandated it free in 2018. The US has effectively paywalled survival.

## Journalist
**Clara Rollover** — Consumer Safety Advocate. Beat: car-buying guidance, class comparisons, "what this means for you." Direct, practical, angry on behalf of consumers.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Advanced Automatic Collision Notification Study (2019, published 2021)
- DOT HS report, FARS + NASS-CDS data, 2009-2015
- Average motor vehicle occupant fatalities: 21,934/year over that period
- AACN could reduce fatalities 1.6-3.3% per year
- Survival rate increases 1.6% when treatment time drops from 6→3 hours
- Benefits exceed costs by $2.18 billion (at CDC 20% injury risk threshold)
- Value per prevented fatality: $6,714,814
- Source: https://www.itskrs.its.dot.gov/2021-b01605 / rosap.ntl.bts.gov

### 2. NHTSA 700+ Lives Estimate
- 2019 NHTSA study estimated ACN could save more than 700 lives/year
- Cited repeatedly by Consumer Reports, CR Advocacy, NHTSA
- Source: Referenced in CR investigation, NHTSA study DOT HS 812 873

### 3. Intrado/SBD Automotive Whitepaper
- AACN could prevent 2,129 U.S. deaths annually (13.2% of roadway fatalities)
- Industry-backed study (Ford, Toyota supporting)
- Source: https://intrado.com (whitepaper)

### 4. Consumer Reports Investigation (August 2024)
- 16 car brands offer free ACN on at least some vehicles
- 3 brands (Nissan, Infiniti, Subaru) don't even offer free trial of 5+ years
- Most automakers charge $8-10/month ($100+/year) for ACN
- ACN often bundled with convenience features (remote start, WiFi hotspot)
- Hardware already installed — "automakers can simply activate ACN independently"
- GM was worst offender: $39.99/month ($480/year!) before reversing to 8yr free (2025)
- 51% of Americans say ACN is "extremely or very important" for next vehicle (CR survey, March 2023)
- Source: consumerreports.org

### 5. EU eCall Mandate (March 31, 2018)
- Mandatory in ALL new cars sold in EU — free, no subscription
- Expected to save 2,500 lives/year
- Reduces emergency response time 50% rural, 40% urban
- Cost: less than €100 per vehicle (total, one-time)
- 3+ million eCall vehicles sold in Europe within first 2 years
- 27 car brands, 65+ models
- Source: European Parliament, European Commission

### 6. New York S5708 (December 2025)
- Bans subscription fees for pre-installed vehicle features
- $250 civil penalty per violation per point of sale
- BUT: exempts "software services," ongoing data/connectivity
- ACN requires cellular connection — potential loophole
- Source: nysenate.gov, Autoblog, Jalopnik

### 7. GM Digital Revenue
- 13 million subscribers projected by 2026
- $3.1 billion digital revenue target
- Previously forced OnStar Premium ($1,500/3yr) on Buick/GMC/Cadillac
- Settled $12.75M lawsuit for selling OnStar driver data
- Source: CarBuzz, InsideEVs, TheStreet

### 8. Trauma Surgeon Testimony
- Dr. Eileen Bulger, Chief of Surgery, Harborview Medical Center (Level 1 trauma center), Seattle
- "All cars should be required to have this feature, and nobody should pay for it"
- Case: woman found 8 DAYS after crash in a ravine — survived but critically dehydrated
- ACN would have located her immediately
- Source: Consumer Reports

## Original Contribution (Novel Analysis)

**The body count math:**
- NHTSA says ACN could save 700+ lives/year if universally active
- ~17 million new vehicles sold/year in US
- Most have ACN hardware installed
- But ACN is disabled when subscription lapses (typical within 1-3 years)
- Average vehicle age on US roads: 12.6 years (S&P Global, 2024)
- So the vast majority of ACN-equipped vehicles on the road have INACTIVE subscriptions
- Conservative estimate: if 70% of ACN-equipped vehicles have lapsed subscriptions, that's the gap between "could save 700 lives" and actual lives saved
- Each year of subscription lapse across the fleet = deaths that hardware could have prevented

**Cost-per-life calculation:**
- GM charged $480/year before reversal. At 700 preventable deaths/year, industry ACN subscription revenue effectively "costs" a calculable amount per life NOT saved
- EU eCall: <€100 per vehicle (one-time). For 17M new US vehicles/year, universal free ACN would cost ~$1.7B once — GM alone projects $3.1B in recurring digital revenue

**The absurdity metric:**
- The EU decided this was a human right in 2018
- In the US 8 years later, we're still debating whether you should pay $10/month to have your car call 911 when you're unconscious in a ditch

## Limitations
- NHTSA 700+ estimate assumes universal deployment + optimal 911 response infrastructure
- Not all 911 systems equipped to receive AACN data yet
- Phone-based ACN (Ford, Mazda, Tesla) is free but less reliable — phone can be destroyed in crash
- EU eCall cost estimates from pre-deployment projections — actual costs may differ
- NY S5708 loopholes may be intentional (ongoing service costs are real)
- 1.6-3.3% fatality reduction from AACN specifically, not basic ACN

## Strongest Counterargument
Cellular connectivity costs money. Automakers pay carriers for data connections. The EU subsidized eCall through regulatory mandate and carrier obligations. In the US, no such mandate exists, and someone has to pay for ongoing cellular service. The ~$100/vehicle EU cost is a one-time hardware cost, not ongoing service. Automakers argue they're not paywalling safety — they're recovering ongoing operational costs.

**Rebuttal:** The hardware is already in the car. The cellular module is already installed. The marginal cost of an emergency-only ACN ping is negligible compared to the data-heavy convenience services bundled with it. Ford, Mazda, and Tesla prove it can work free by using the driver's own phone connection. And €100 one-time vs $100/year recurring tells you exactly where the margin goes.
