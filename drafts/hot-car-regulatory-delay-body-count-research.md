# Research: Hot Car Regulatory Delay Body Count

## News Peg (May 23, 2026)
- 3 children found dead in hot cars in ONE DAY across the US
  - Los Angeles, CA: 4-year-old girl, temp 87°F
  - Fredericksburg, VA: 2-month-old, temp 97°F — left by mother while at work
  - Brookwood, AL: 1-year-old, temp 90°F
- 5 total child hot car deaths in 2026 as of May 23 (source: Kids and Car Safety)
- Summer hasn't officially started

## Core Data (noheatstroke.org / Kids and Car Safety)
- 1,045+ children dead in hot cars since 1998
- Average: 37 deaths per year
- 86% of victims are age 3 or younger
- Majority are rear-facing car seats (over 50% per studies)
- 2019: 52 deaths, 2018: 53 deaths (recent peaks)
- 2025: 31 deaths (preliminary)

## Regulatory Timeline (IIJA Hot Car Mandate)
1. **2021 Nov** — Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act signed. Mandates NHTSA to require rear-seat occupant alert systems.
2. **2023 Nov** — Congressional deadline for NHTSA final rule. **MISSED.**
3. **2025 Apr** — NHTSA's own revised estimate for proposed rulemaking. **MISSED.**
4. **2026 May** — Still no proposed rule, let alone final rule. 2.5+ years past deadline.

## Technology Exists
- **FCC radar waivers (2021):** Granted to Infineon, Tesla, Valeo, Vayyar for 60 GHz in-cabin radar
- **Vayyar:** Claims 100% detection rate for in-cabin occupants
- **Tesla:** Has cabin overheat protection + Dog Mode (detects pets but marketed differently)
- **Euro NCAP:** Already requires child presence detection for 5-star rating (since 2023)
- **Cost:** Estimated ~$50/vehicle for radar sensor
- **20+ automakers** voluntarily committed to rear-seat reminders by 2025 model year

## NHTSA's Separate Rule (Not the Hot Car Rule)
- Jan 2025: Finalized rear seat BELT warning rule (FMVSS 208 amendment)
- This is about seatbelt reminders, NOT occupant detection/hot car prevention
- Compliance: Sept 2026-2027
- Often confused with the hot car mandate — they're different rules

## Novel Contribution: The Compound Cost of Delay
NHTSA deadline was Nov 2023. If met:
- MY2025 vehicles would have occupant detection
- ~17M new vehicles/year → by 2026, ~34M equipped (11.7% of ~290M fleet)
- At 37 deaths/year × 11.7% penetration × 80% effectiveness = ~3.5 lives saved already
- Each additional year: fleet penetration grows by ~5.9 percentage points
- By 2030 (if implemented on time): ~41% fleet → ~12 children saved/year
- Actual delay of 2.5+ years shifts the ENTIRE curve right
- **Cumulative excess deaths from delay through 2035: estimated 50-80 children**

## Strongest Counterargument
Rear-seat reminders (the voluntary automaker pledge) are already in many 2025+ vehicles. The alerts may be reducing deaths even without the federal mandate — the 31 deaths in 2025 is below the 37/year average. The federal rule might be redundant because industry moved faster.

## Limitations
- Fleet penetration math is estimated (exact registration data not public)
- "Effectiveness" of in-cabin detection is projected, not validated at scale
- Confounders: hotter summers (climate change) could increase baseline deaths
- Door-sequence alerts (the automaker pledge) are weaker than radar detection
