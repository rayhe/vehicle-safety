# Research: Below One, Not If You Ride

## Angle
The Q1 2026 fatality rate just broke below 1.0 per 100M VMT for the first time since 2014. Every subcategory of road death is declining — pedestrians, bicyclists, passenger vehicle occupants, speeding-related, rollover, you name it. Except motorcyclists. They're the only road user group going the wrong direction, and the long-term FARS data shows this isn't a blip — it's a 27-year structural divergence.

## News Hook
- NHTSA released Q1 2026 Early Estimate on July 8, 2026 (today)
- 7,770 deaths in Q1 2026, down 4.3% from Q1 2025
- Fatality rate: 0.99 per 100M VMT — lowest quarterly figure since 2014
- Source: Reuters, "US traffic deaths fell sharply in early 2026" (July 8, 2026)

## Full Year Context
- 2025: 36,640 deaths, down 6.7% from 2024's 39,254. Rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT.
- 2024 FARS: 39,254 total. Fourth consecutive annual decline since 2022 peak (42,721).
- Source: NHTSA "2025 Traffic Death Estimates & 2024 FARS" (April 1, 2026)

## Motorcycle Divergence Data
### NHTSA H1 2024 Subcategory Breakdown (most recent subcategory data):
- Motorcyclists: UP 1%
- Pedestrians: down 3%
- Bicyclists: down 1%
- Passenger vehicle drivers: down 3%
- Passenger vehicle passengers: down 7%
- Speeding-related: down 6%
- Rollover: down 6%
- Night crashes: down 4%
- Weekend: down 4%
- Only other increase: seniors 65+, also up 1%
- Source: RevZilla/NHTSA Early Estimate H1 2024

### IIHS/FARS Long-Term Trend (2024, posted June 2026):
- 6,228 motorcyclist deaths in 2024 (16% of all motor vehicle deaths)
- Near-record (6,366 in 2023 was highest ever)
- Triple the 1997 number (2,116)
- 23% increase since 2019 alone
- Motorcycle share of deaths: 5% (1997) → 16% (2024)
- Passenger vehicle share: 77% (1997) → 58% (2024)
- Per mile traveled: 28x more deadly on a motorcycle than in a car (NHTSA 2025 estimate, based on 2023 data)
- 36% of fatally injured motorcycle drivers had no valid license (vs 17% for car drivers)
- ~25% of fatal motorcycle crashes involve alcohol
- ~35% are single-vehicle crashes (can't blame cagers)
- Source: IIHS Motorcycles and ATVs fact page (updated June 2026)
