# Research: Toyota's EV Headlight Blind Spot

## Angle
Toyota built the safest SUV in the FARS dataset (RAV4, 0.19 deaths per 100M VMT). Its ICE lineup dominates safety awards. But every single Toyota EV currently on sale has "Poor" headlight ratings from IIHS, disqualifying all three from any safety award. The brand that built its reputation on keeping families alive is shipping electric SUVs that can't illuminate a left curve.

## Self-Critique Gate
**Proposed:** Toyota's ICE safety excellence vs EV headlight failure — the safety halo doesn't transfer.
**Challenge:** Is this genuinely surprising? Toyota's EV struggles are well-documented. But the SPECIFIC finding — that it's headlights, not crash structure, that fails — is novel. And the FARS data crossover (best SUV safety record on ICE side, zero awards on EV side) hasn't been done.
**Verdict:** Proceed. Novel cross-tabulation of IIHS ratings + FARS data, timely (finding published June 18, 2026), consumer-actionable.

## Primary Sources

### 1. IIHS 2026 Headlight Ratings — Toyota EVs
- **bZ:** Poor headlight rating. Inadequate illumination on straightaways (both sides) and left curves. LED projectors with high-beam assist. Acceptable in small overlap front and moderate overlap front crash tests.
- **bZ Woodland:** Same Poor headlight rating. Same deficiency pattern.
- **C-HR:** Same Poor headlight rating. Not yet tested for moderate overlap front.
- **Pattern:** All three share inadequate illumination on left curves specifically. No glare issues.
- **Subaru EVs:** Trailseeker, Solterra, Uncharted — all Poor headlights too. Same e-TNGA platform.
- **Among 32 small SUVs tested:** Only Kia Seltos, Lincoln Corsair, and Chevy Trailblazer also rated Poor — but those have alternative headlight packages that score better. Toyota's EVs do not.
Source: Autoblog, Jun 18, 2026; IIHS ratings pages

### 2. IIHS TSP/TSP+ Award Criteria (2026)
- Good or Acceptable headlights required for EITHER award tier
- Poor = automatic disqualification, no exceptions
- Also need: Good in moderate overlap front, small overlap front, side crash tests
- TSP+ additionally requires Good pedestrian AEB and Good V2V crash prevention
Source: IIHS website, Consumer Reports analysis

### 5. NHTSA Nighttime Fatality Data
- ~50% of all fatal crashes occur in darkness (NHTSA general statistic)
- Headlight quality directly affects nighttime visibility
- IIHS estimates that good headlights could prevent 90,000+ nighttime crashes annually
