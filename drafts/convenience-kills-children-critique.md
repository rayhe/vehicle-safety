- Excellent structure: incident → historical pattern → counterargument → cost analysis → limitations → conclusion

**Weaknesses:**

### ⚖️ Legal Accuracy — 8.5/10
**Strengths:**
- 8 references, all hyperlinked to verifiable sources
- Cameron Gulbransen Act citation correct: Pub. L. 110-189, signed Feb 28, 2008
- FMVSS 118 reference appropriate, Federal Register link provided
- UL 325 garage door standard correctly cited (January 1, 1993)
- DOT VSL ($13.2M in 2024 dollars) verifiable at transportation.gov
- Backup camera mandate timeline correct: FMVSS 111, full compliance May 2018
- Hyundai recall number (69,060) confirmed by The Drive and other sources

**Potential concerns:**
- "nearly four decades" from FMVSS 118 (1971) to auto-reverse (~2010) = 39 years — acceptable

- Ref [3] cites "Ibid" for European auto-reverse adoption rate — while the claim traces to the same advocacy source, a primary OEM survey would be stronger
