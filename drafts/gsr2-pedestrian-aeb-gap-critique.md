### 5. ⚖️ Legal Accuracy — 8.5 / 10

**Confirmed accurate:**
- FMVSS 127 finalized and published in the Federal Register **May 9, 2024** — article's "May 2024" is correct (the April 29 date in reference [1] is the press announcement, not the FR publication)
- September 2029 compliance deadline ✓
- 360 lives/year and 24,000 injuries/year projections ✓ (per NHTSA regulatory impact analysis)
- 62 mph vehicle-detection and 45 mph pedestrian-detection thresholds ✓
- GSR2 Phase 3 timeline for existing type-approvals: July 7, 2026 ✓
- D.C. Circuit abeyance ✓
- DOT March 2026 status report proposing 2-year extension ✓ (per Nelson Mullins citing docket)
- FARS data claims cited to FARS database ✓
- IIHS research cited to specific IIHS publications ✓

**Concern — timeline compression (non-blocking):**
The article says: "Five months later, the Alliance for Automotive Innovation…sued to block it…Three days after that lawsuit was filed, the incoming administration imposed a regulatory freeze."

Per Nelson Mullins, the Alliance **petitioned for reconsideration in June 2024** (one month after finalization, not five). NHTSA issued a revised final rule in November 2024 largely denying the petitions. The regulatory freeze was a general executive action in January 2025, not causally linked to the Alliance's filing.

The article compresses: petition → revised rule → freeze → D.C. Circuit abeyance into a simpler narrative of "sued → freeze." The underlying facts are correct (challenge exists, freeze exists, delay is real), but the temporal and causal framing slightly misleads. This is narrative shorthand, not fabrication.

**Recommended fix:** Adjust paragraph 2 to something like: "Within weeks, the Alliance for Automotive Innovation petitioned NHTSA to reconsider the rule. NHTSA largely denied those petitions in November 2024. In January 2025, the incoming administration imposed a regulatory freeze on pending rules, and the D.C. Circuit challenge has sat in abeyance since February 2025."

This preserves the narrative momentum while being temporally accurate.
