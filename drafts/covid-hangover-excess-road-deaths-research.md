# Research: The COVID Hangover — Excess Road Deaths That Never Reverted

## Hook / News Peg
NHTSA released 2024 early estimates (April 8, 2026): 39,345 deaths, down 3.8% from 2023's 40,901. First time below 40,000 since 2020. Fatality rate: 1.20/100M VMT. NHTSA celebrated this.

BUT: The pre-COVID 7-year average rate (2013-2019) was 1.13/100M VMT.

## Novel Calculation: The COVID Hangover Death Toll

At 2024's ~3.279T VMT, applying the pre-COVID rate of 1.13:
- Expected deaths: 37,050
- Actual deaths: 39,345
- **EXCESS: 2,295 deaths attributable to behavioral changes that never reverted**

## Behavioral Evidence
- AAA Foundation: speeding, distracted driving, aggressive driving all increased during COVID and persisted
- GHSA: decline in traffic law enforcement allowed unsafe behaviors to continue
- NHTSA's own statement: "Total road fatalities remain significantly higher than a decade ago"
- PMC studies confirm post-COVID driving behavior shifts persisted beyond pandemic period

## Self-Critique Gate
1. **Angle:** NHTSA celebrates 39,345 deaths as progress. At pre-COVID rates, 2,295 fewer would have died. Cumulative excess since 2020: ~27,600. The pandemic reshaped driving behavior — faster, more distracted, less seatbelts — and nobody is treating the behavioral shift as a public health emergency.
2. **Challenge:** Similar to #200 ("36,640 Dead Is Not Progress") and #206 (NHTSA decline celebration critique). But THIS is pegged to fresh data (just released), includes a novel quantitative calculation nobody else has published, and focuses specifically on the behavioral persistence angle rather than vehicle swaps.
3. **Verdict:** PROCEED. Fresh data, novel math, different angle from prior NHTSA critiques.
