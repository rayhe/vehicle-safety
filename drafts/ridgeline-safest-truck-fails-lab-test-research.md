# Research: The Safest Truck on the Road Just Failed Its Lab Test

## Angle
The Honda Ridgeline has the lowest fatality rate of any midsize pickup in FARS data (0.24 per 100M VMT — 12x lower than the Ford Ranger at 2.91). We published an article praising it. Then in May 2026, IIHS released new crash test results: the Ridgeline got the worst rating in the moderate overlap front test, Poor in crash prevention, and marginal headlights. It failed across the board.

Both findings are true simultaneously. The paradox reveals what "safe" actually means — and why crash tests and real-world deaths measure completely different things.

Ridgeline toxicology: 19.4% any impairment (14.3% alcohol, 8.9% drugs) — relatively low for pickups.

## IIHS 2026 Results (May 19, 2026)
- **Moderate Overlap Front**: Overall score pulled down by REAR PASSENGER safety — head/neck injury risk elevated, lap belt migrated to abdomen, headrest ejected during rebound
- **Front Crash Prevention (vehicle-to-vehicle)**: **Poor** — collision mitigation braking failed
- **Front Crash Prevention (pedestrian)**: **Poor** — vehicle failed to slow in multiple test conditions
- **Headlights**: **Marginal** — inadequate visibility on curves (LED projector low beams), halogen high beams inadequate on straightaways
- **Side Impact**: Good (the bright spot)
- **Small Overlap Front**: Some positives, but driver airbag contact was marginal — head slid off, approached dashboard
- **Child Seat Anchors**: All three positions rated poorly — tether anchors hard to find, lower anchors too deep

## Why the paradox resolves
1. **Driver selection bias**: Ridgeline buyers are generally older, more affluent, suburban. Lower impairment, fewer risky driving behaviors. The low death rate reflects WHO DRIVES IT as much as HOW IT PROTECTS THEM.
2. **Old tests measured old things**: Pre-2026 IIHS tests primarily measured DRIVER protection. The 2026 update added rear passenger evaluation and stricter AEB criteria. The Ridgeline was "safe for the driver" — not "safe for the family."
3. **Design age**: This is a 2016 design (2017 MY) with one refresh (2020/MY2021). The crash structure is 10 years old competing against 2026 standards.
4. **NHTSA vs IIHS disconnect**: NHTSA still gives the Ridgeline its maximum rating. Two federal/private safety organizations, same truck, opposite conclusions.

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing real-world fatality data (FARS) with lab crash test performance (IIHS) exposes a fundamental gap in how consumers assess vehicle safety. A vehicle can have the lowest death rate AND the worst crash test score because the two measure different things: crash tests measure structural engineering in controlled conditions; death rates measure the full system (vehicle + driver behavior + use patterns).
