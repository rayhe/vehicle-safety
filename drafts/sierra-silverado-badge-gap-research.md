# Research: Same Truck, Different Death Rate — Sierra vs Silverado Badge Gap

## Angle
The GMC Sierra and Chevrolet Silverado are mechanically identical full-size pickups built on the same GMT/T1XX platform, often on the same assembly line. Yet FARS data shows a 24% death rate gap: the Silverado kills at 1.25 per 100M VMT vs the Sierra's 1.01. Same engineering, different outcomes. The badge doesn't change the truck — it changes who buys it.

## Kill Test: Is This Genuinely Novel?
YES. Nobody has run this specific FARS cross-tabulation. The site has covered Silverado vs F-150, Tahoe vs Yukon twins, and brand safety spreads — but never isolated the Sierra vs Silverado comparison to test whether demographics alone explain a death rate gap between mechanically identical vehicles.

## FARS Data (2014-2023)

### Fatality Rates
| Metric | Silverado | Sierra | Gap |
|--------|-----------|--------|-----|
| Deaths | 9,591 | 3,337 | 2.87x |
| Fleet | 5,687,500 | 2,450,000 | 2.32x |
| Rate (per 100M VMT) | 1.25 | 1.01 | +24% |
| Annual deaths | 959.1 | 333.7 | |
| Crashes | 19,732 | 7,084 | |
| Deaths/crash | 0.486 | 0.471 | Similar |

### Key Finding
Fleet ratio (Silverado/Sierra) = 2.32x
Death ratio = 2.87x
If identical behavior, these should match. The Silverado overperforms in deaths by 24%.

### Toxicology (Nearly Identical)
| Metric | Silverado | Sierra |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Drivers | 23,675 | 9,319 |
| Any impaired | 20.6% | 21.0% |
| Alcohol | 15.7% | 15.7% |
| Drug | 8.7% | 9.2% |

Impairment rates are statistically indistinguishable. The death gap is NOT driven by substance use.

### Model Year Death Distribution
- Both show classic aging-fleet curves with peaks in early-2000s model years
- Silverado MY 2004: 663 deaths (peak)
- Sierra MY 2005: 239 deaths (peak)
- Both decline steeply after 2015 model years

## Sources Required
1. NHTSA FARS 2014-2023 (primary data)
2. NHTSA crash test ratings — both earn identical 5-star ratings (MotorTrend, 2014 confirmation)
3. IIHS large pickup ratings — both tested under same standards
4. GM platform documentation — GMT K2XX (2014-2018), T1XX (2019+)
5. Industry pricing data — GMC Sierra commands $5-10K premium at comparable trims

## Demographic Hypothesis
GMC is positioned as "Professional Grade" — premium trims, higher MSRP, older/wealthier buyer demographic. The Silverado has more fleet sales, work truck trims, and presumably younger/more price-sensitive buyers. This demographic stratification on an identical vehicle creates a natural experiment: same machine, different operator pool.

## Counterargument (Must Include)
Fleet mix could explain the gap. The Silverado sells many more base Work Truck (WT) trim levels to commercial fleets. Fleet vehicles may accumulate different VMT patterns (highway vs urban), different maintenance schedules, and different driver training levels. The rate calculation uses estimated VMT, which could systematically undercount commercial Silverado miles, making its rate appear higher than reality.

## Limitations
- FARS only captures fatal crashes, not all crashes
- VMT estimates are model-level averages, not individual odometer readings (±15% uncertainty)
- We can't disaggregate by trim level within FARS (WT vs LT vs High Country)
- The "same truck" claim applies to the base platform but trim-level equipment (side curtain airbags, active safety) may differ at lower price points
- Fleet age distribution matters — if Silverados skew older on average, the aging-fleet death effect could explain part of the gap
