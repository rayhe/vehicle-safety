# Research: Tesla's 207-Crash ADAS Month

## Angle
Tesla logged 207 ADAS crashes in May 2026 — the most any automaker has ever reported in a single month. 85% of all ADAS reports filed with NHTSA come from Tesla. But 99.9% of those crash narratives are blacked out. Meanwhile, FARS fatality data shows Teslas are among the safest cars on the road by death rate. The paradox: the brand with the lowest body count per mile is also the brand hiding the most crash data.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Standing General Order (SGO) ADAS Data
- Source: NHTSA SGO database, reported by Electrek (Jul 22, 2026)
- URL: https://electrek.co/2026/07/22/tesla-adas-crashes-record-207-one-month/
- **207 crashes in May 2026** — single-month record
- **3,763 cumulative Tesla ADAS crashes** — 85% of entire industry
- H1 2026: 826 crashes (vs 476 in H1 2025 — **73% jump YoY**)
- 2025 full year: 1,043 (first year above 1,000)
- Jan-Jun trend by year: 180 → 261 → 269 → 476 → 826
- **99.9% of Tesla crash narratives redacted** ("confidential business information")
- Software version field also redacted — can't separate Autopilot from FSD
- GM, Ford, Honda, Toyota redact essentially none of their narratives
- 207 is a floor — reporting lag revises figures upward
- Tesla is under separate NHTSA probe for HOW it reports crashes

### 2. NHTSA "Radar Saves Us" Probe
- Source: NHTSA Engineering Analysis letter to Tesla, Jul 21, 2026
- URL: https://electrek.co/2026/07/21/nhtsa-tesla-radar-saves-us-document-fsd-probe/
- Internal Tesla document titled "Radar Saves Us" surfaced in PE24031-01
- NHTSA demanding all communications, test records, and underlying data
- 9 "countable crashes" involved reduced visibility conditions
- Failure to respond: civil penalties up to $27,874/day, capped ~$139M
- Directly relevant: Tesla's own engineers may have documented vision-only limitations

### 3. FARS Fatality Data (2014-2023)
- Tesla Model Y: 0.03 deaths/100M VMT (57 deaths, 1.75M fleet) — **near-lowest in dataset**
- Tesla Model 3: 0.05 deaths/100M VMT (92 deaths, 1.575M fleet)
- Tesla Model X: 0.15 deaths/100M VMT (29 deaths)
- Tesla Model S: 0.50 deaths/100M VMT (100 deaths)
- For comparison: Honda Civic at 2.25, Ford F-150 at presumably higher
- Tesla total FARS deaths: 278 over 10 years

### 4. Tesla Impairment Data (FARS Toxicology)
- Model S: 24.0% any impairment, 19.1% alcohol — highest among Teslas
- Model 3: 20.1% any, 14.8% alcohol
- Model Y: 17.6% any, 13.2% alcohol
- Brand average: ~20.0% — near industry median

## Novel Contribution
Cross-tabulation of FARS fatality rates against SGO crash opacity. No one has calculated the "transparency gap" — the ratio of known crash outcomes (FARS fatals, fully documented) to unknown ones (SGO ADAS reports, 99.9% redacted). For every 1 Tesla fatality in FARS, there are roughly 13.5 ADAS crashes where NHTSA gets a blacked-out report.

## Counterargument (at full strength)
More ADAS-engaged miles → more crashes even if per-mile rate is falling. Tesla has by far the largest ADAS-active fleet. If GM had 2 million vehicles running Super Cruise as many hours as Tesla owners run Autopilot/FSD, GM's numbers might be similar. The comparison is fleet-size-naive. Tesla also has the most sophisticated data pipeline — other OEMs may underreport because their systems are less connected.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. A single-month record, a 73% YoY acceleration, and 99.9% redaction rate — all from the company pushing hardest for autonomous driving — is a legitimate data story. The FARS cross-reference (lowest fatality rates, most opaque crash data) is original analysis nobody else has published.
