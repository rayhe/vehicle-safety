# Research: Seat Belt Non-Use Concentration of Fatalities

## Angle
**9% of Americans don't wear seat belts. They generate roughly half of all vehicle occupant deaths.**

This is a classic Rex Driverton paradox investigation: the simplest, cheapest safety device — already installed in every car — is unused by a small minority who then dominate the fatality statistics. All the advanced safety engineering (AEB, crumple zones, airbags, ESC) can't compensate for refusing to use the most basic restraint.

## Kill Test
✅ Genuinely newsworthy — the concentration ratio (9% of drivers → 50% of deaths) is more extreme than any vehicle design factor. This hasn't been framed through our FARS lens before. No existing seat belt/restraint article on the site.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA NOPUS 2024 — National seat belt use survey
- 91.2% observed seat belt use rate (2024), statistically unchanged from 2023's 91.9%
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/risky-driving/seat-belts

### 2. NSC Injury Facts 2024 — Seat belt fatality data
- 49.2% of passenger vehicle occupant deaths in 2023 were unrestrained
- Pre-COVID: 46.6% unrestrained deaths (2019)
- COVID spike: 50.9% unrestrained deaths (2020)
- Slow recovery: 49.2% by 2023
- Age breakdown: 25-34 year olds — 61% of their deaths are unrestrained; 10-15 year olds also 61%
- Lowest unrestrained: 0-4 (28%), 75+ (27%)
- Males higher unrestrained death proportion than females
- Nighttime deaths higher unrestrained proportion than daytime
- Source: https://injuryfacts.nsc.org/motor-vehicle/occupant-protection/seat-belts/

### 3. NHTSA Press Release — Click It or Ticket
- "Half of All Passenger Vehicle Occupants Who Died in a Crash Were Not Buckled Up"
- Seat belts reduce front seat car occupant deaths by 45%
- Seat belts reduce front seat light truck occupant deaths by 60%
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/click-it-ticket-seat-belts

### 4. NHTSA 2025/2024 FARS Annual Data
- 39,345 total traffic fatalities in 2024 (3.8% decline from 2023)
- 2025 estimated at 36,640 (6.7% decline)
- Fatality rate: 1.20 per 100M VMT (2024), lowest since 2019
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/traffic-deaths-2025-early-estimates-2024-annual

### 5. State-level enforcement data (NSC Injury Facts)
- 36 states + DC: primary seat belt laws
- 13 states: secondary enforcement only (officer can't pull you over just for belt)
- New Hampshire: NO seat belt law for adults
- Primary states: 96.2% belt use, 41% of fatalities unrestrained
- Secondary states: lower use, higher unrestrained death share
- Pickup trucks: consistently lowest belt use by vehicle type (~89%)
- Source: https://injuryfacts.nsc.org/motor-vehicle/occupant-protection/seat-belts/data-details/

### 6. CDC Rural/Urban Study (2014 MMWR)
- Rural residents: higher crash death rates AND lower seat belt use
- Belt use declines from 88.8% → 74.7% as rurality increases
- Source: https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/66/ss/ss6617a1.htm

## Data Calculations
- ~9% of drivers unbuckled × ~50% of deaths = concentration ratio of ~5.5x
- If 9% of drivers generate 50% of the ~23,000 passenger vehicle occupant deaths/year ≈ 11,500 unbelted deaths
- If all had buckled up and belt effectiveness is 45%: ~5,175 lives saved annually
- That's roughly the population of a small town, every year

## Counterargument
The selection bias argument: people who don't wear seat belts also tend to engage in other risky behaviors (speeding, impaired driving, rural roads at night). The belt itself is a proxy for risk tolerance. But that doesn't diminish the intervention value — the belt still works regardless of why you weren't wearing it.
