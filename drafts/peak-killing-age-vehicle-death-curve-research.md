# Research: Peak Killing Age — Vehicle Age and Fatal Crash Deaths

## Slug: peak-killing-age-vehicle-death-curve
## Journalist: Mia Crumplezone (Safety Engineering Editor)
## Angle: Vehicle design analysis + model year trends

## Core Finding (Original Contribution)
Cross-tabulating FARS 2014-2023 model-year death data to construct a vehicle age-at-crash distribution reveals a bimodal curve with a clear **peak killing age of 14 years**. Vehicles aged 11-17 account for 72,334 deaths — **38.7% of all 187,058 deaths** in the dataset.

### The Age Distribution (deaths by vehicle age, approximate)
- Age 0-5 (newest): 39,095 deaths (20.9%)
- Age 6-10: 31,430 deaths (16.8%)
- Age 11-17 (PEAK): 72,334 deaths (38.7%)
- Age 18+ (oldest): 44,199 deaths (23.6%)

### Key Data Points
- **Peak age: 14 years** — 11,363 deaths (6.1% of total)
- **Trough age: 10 years** — 5,729 deaths (3.1%)
- The step-change from age 10 → age 14 is nearly a doubling (5,729 → 11,363)
- Pattern holds across ALL vehicle classes:
  - Sedans: 39.5% of deaths from 11-17yr cohort
  - SUVs: 40.5%
  - Pickups: 39.8%
  - Vans: 48.4% (highest)
  - Sports Cars: 32.2%

### Why This Happens — ESC Mandate Timeline
- ESC mandated for all new vehicles starting **model year 2012** (NHTSA rule, announced June 2007, effective September 2011)
- In the FARS study period (2014-2023, midpoint ~2018.5):
  - A 14-year-old vehicle = model year ~2004-2005 → pre-ESC
  - A 10-year-old vehicle = model year ~2008-2009 → ESC becoming common
  - A 6-year-old vehicle = model year ~2012-2013 → ESC mandatory
- IIHS found ESC reduces fatal single-vehicle crashes by **56%** and fatal single-vehicle rollovers by **77%**
- NHTSA estimates ESC saved **2,202 lives** in 2008-2010 alone (Consumer Reports, Dec 2012)

### External Validation
- Average US vehicle age: **12.6 years** in 2024 (S&P Global Mobility) → meaning the median vehicle on the road is IN the peak killing cohort
- Average passenger car age: **14.5 years** → exactly at the peak
- Average age risen to **12.8 years** in 2025 (MOTOR, May 2025)

### The Novel Argument
The national conversation focuses on distracted driving, impaired driving, and speed enforcement. This data suggests the single largest structural risk factor is simply the AGE of the vehicle fleet. The peak killing age corresponds precisely to the pre-ESC, pre-advanced-airbag generation of vehicles. As the fleet continues aging (now 12.8 years average), more vehicles are entering and staying in the peak killing zone rather than cycling out.

### Policy Implication
Cash-for-clunkers style programs targeting pre-2012 vehicles (pre-ESC mandate) could eliminate more fatal crashes than any enforcement campaign. The math: if 38.7% of deaths involve 11-17yr-old vehicles, and ESC alone reduces fatal crashes 56%, accelerating fleet turnover could prevent roughly 7,000-8,000 deaths per year.

## Methodology Note
The midpoint approach (using 2018.5 as average crash year for data spanning 2014-2023) introduces ±4.5 years of uncertainty for any individual vehicle. However, the aggregate pattern is robust because it averages across 10 years of crash data. A model year 2004 vehicle appears in the dataset at ages ranging from 10 (2014 crash) to 19 (2023 crash); the midpoint of 14 is the expected value. The bimodal shape would persist under any reasonable weighting.
