# Research: The Sedan Replacement Dividend

## Thesis
The ongoing market shift from sedans to crossover SUVs is producing the single largest occupant safety improvement in American automotive history. FARS data shows that for 14 major sedan-crossover pairs from the same manufacturer, switching from the sedan to the crossover equivalent reduces death rates by 23-94%. Over the 2014-2023 study period, if those sedan miles had been driven in crossovers instead, an estimated 45,438 fewer people would have died — roughly 4,544 per year.

## Kill Test
- **Novel?** YES. Nobody has calculated the aggregate "replacement dividend" across all major sedan-crossover pairs. Articles exist about sedans being dangerous (sedan-death-penalty), about crossovers being safer (compact-suv-safety-lottery, mazda-cx5-safest-compact-suv), and about the SUV shift generally, but nobody has quantified the lives saved by the ongoing sedan extinction.
- **Surprising?** YES. Safety advocates have spent years warning about SUVs being dangerous to pedestrians and smaller vehicles. The data shows that for occupants, the sedan-to-crossover shift is saving thousands of lives per year.
- **Data-backed?** YES. Pure FARS data calculation with clear methodology.

## Original Contribution
Novel cross-tabulation: using FARS death rates for matched sedan-crossover pairs from the same manufacturer, calculating "replacement dividend" — the deaths prevented if sedan VMT had been driven in the crossover equivalent. This produces a per-pair and aggregate estimate of lives saved by the market shift.

## Limitations (MUST INCLUDE)
1. **Fleet age confounder**: Crossovers are systematically newer vehicles than sedans. The average CR-V on the road is newer than the average Accord because crossover adoption accelerated recently. Newer vehicles have more safety tech regardless of body style.
2. **Survivor bias**: We're comparing currently available crossovers against sedan lineups that include many discontinued models (Focus, Cruze, etc.) whose last model years were 2018-2019.
3. **Demographics**: Sedan buyers and crossover buyers may have different risk profiles. Budget-constrained buyers who buy cheap used sedans may not have the option of buying a newer crossover.
4. **This does NOT mean body style inherently protects**: The higher seating position and marginally better crash structure of crossovers helps, but fleet age and safety tech are likely the larger factors.
5. **VMT estimates introduce ±15% uncertainty** for individual models.

## Strongest Counterargument
The safety improvement attributed to "sedan replacement" is largely an artifact of fleet age. If we compared 2020 Camry to 2020 RAV4, the gap would be much smaller. The real story isn't that crossovers are inherently safer — it's that the sedan fleet is aging into obsolescence while the crossover fleet is young and packed with modern safety technology. Attributing 4,500 annual lives saved to "body style choice" overstates the case.
