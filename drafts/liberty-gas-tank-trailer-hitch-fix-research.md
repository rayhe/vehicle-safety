# Research: Jeep Liberty Gas Tank — Rex Driverton

## Angle
The Jeep Liberty (2002-2007 KJ) had a rear-mounted plastic fuel tank behind the rear axle, known to rupture in rear-end collisions causing fires. NHTSA investigated, Chrysler fought the recall, eventually 1.56 million vehicles recalled in 2013. The "fix" was installing a trailer hitch on the rear bumper. Only 12% of recalled vehicles were actually repaired as of Feb 2015. 801 total deaths in FARS data at a 1.22 death rate — 6.4x a RAV4, 3.4x an Equinox, 2.3x a CR-V.

## Key Facts
1. **Rear gas tank design:** Plastic fuel tank mounted behind rear axle. No structural protection in rear impact. Industry had moved away from this design by the 2000s. Ford had the same issue with the Pinto in the 1970s.
2. **NHTSA investigation:** Opened formal investigation in 2010. Expanded to 5.1M Jeep SUVs (Liberty + Grand Cherokee) in 2012.
3. **Chrysler resistance:** Initially refused to recall, calling the vehicles "safe." First major automaker to refuse a NHTSA recall request in decades.
4. **Recall R13-003 (June 2013):** 1.56 million 1993-1998 Grand Cherokees and 2002-2007 Libertys.
5. **The trailer hitch "fix":** Instead of moving the gas tank, Chrysler's recall remedy was to install a Class II trailer hitch on the rear as a "protective shield." Government testing showed it worked up to 40mph stationary-rear-impact only.
6. **12% completion rate (Feb 2015):** Only 12% of recalled vehicles had been repaired 18 months after recall. Chrysler's normal completion rate was 78%. Parts reportedly unavailable at dealers.
7. **Kayla White death (Nov 2014):** 23-year-old, 8 months pregnant, killed in 2003 Liberty fire. Had tried to get recall repair — dealer said parts unavailable.
8. **70+ fire deaths linked:** AP investigation documented 70+ deaths in fires involving rear-tank Jeeps.
9. **2026 Jeep PHEV fire recall:** 320,065 Wrangler/Grand Cherokee PHEVs recalled for battery fire risk while parked or driving. Pattern continues.
