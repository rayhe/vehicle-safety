# Research: America's Accidental Safety Program

## Thesis
The 2025 traffic fatality decline (36,640 deaths, down 6.7% from 2024) is being credited to enforcement campaigns and regulatory action. But FARS data reveals the primary mechanism is simpler: Americans stopped buying sedans and started buying SUVs. The fleet composition shift from high-lethality sedans (64.5% crash lethality, 1.44x death ratio) to lower-lethality SUVs (52.4% lethality, 0.61x death ratio) is an "accidental safety program" that outperforms the entire recall infrastructure.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: NHTSA 2025 Early Estimate (Publication 813800, April 2026)
- 36,640 fatalities estimated in 2025
- Down 6.7% from 39,254 in 2024 (which was down 4.3% from 41,025 in 2023)
- Fatality rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT (second lowest in FARS history, behind 2014's 1.08)
- VMT increased 0.9% (29.8 billion additional miles)
- Fifth-largest percentage decrease in FARS recorded history
- All 10 NHTSA regions projected to decrease
- URL: https://crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov/Api/Public/ViewPublication/813800

### Source 2: FARS 2014-2023 Data (Our Dataset)
- Class-level lethality (deaths/crashes):
  - Sports Car: 68.2%
  - Sedan: 64.5%
  - SUV: 52.4%
  - Van: 51.4%
  - Pickup: 48.9%
- Death share vs fleet share:
  - Sedan: 46.6% deaths, 32.5% fleet → 1.44x overrepresented
  - SUV: 24.3% deaths, 39.6% fleet → 0.61x underrepresented
  - Pickup: 21.8% deaths, 20.3% fleet → 1.07x (roughly neutral)
  - Sports Car: 3.2% deaths, 1.7% fleet → 1.93x (worst ratio)
  - Van: 4.1% deaths, 6.0% fleet → 0.69x

### Source 3: Q1 2026 Recall Report (BizzyCar, April 2026)
- 12.1 million vehicles recalled in Q1 2026 — highest single quarter in recent years
- Ford's 26C10 electrical recall: 4.3 million vehicles alone (35% of all Q1 recalls)
- Ford recalled 8 million total in Q1 (two-thirds of all recalls)
- 45% of recalled vehicles were for electrical system issues
- 20% for back-over prevention (camera/visibility)
- None of these recall categories address crashworthiness or structural protection
- Source: https://www.bizzycar.com/blog/q1-2026-automotive-recall-report

### Source 4: Market Composition Data (public industry data)
- SUV/crossover share of new-vehicle sales: ~55% in 2024 (up from ~30% in 2012)
- Sedan share: ~20% in 2024 (down from ~50% in 2012)
- The fleet takes ~12-15 years to fully turn over

## Novel Calculation: The Fleet Composition Dividend

If sedans produce 1.44x their fleet-share in deaths while SUVs produce 0.61x, then every percentage point of fleet that shifts from sedan to SUV reduces the death ratio by:
- 1.44 - 0.61 = 0.83 fewer deaths per "expected share unit"

The sedan-to-SUV shift from ~50% to ~20% sedan share (30 percentage points) over a decade represents a massive structural safety improvement that requires zero regulatory action, zero recalls, and zero enforcement campaigns.

## Strongest Counterargument
SUVs are safer for their OCCUPANTS but deadlier for everyone ELSE. Higher hood heights kill pedestrians. Greater mass crushes smaller vehicles in collisions. The SUV safety dividend may be partially offset by increased pedestrian fatalities (which have been rising). IIHS research on vehicle size/weight confirms the aggressor vehicle problem.

## Limitations
- FARS data is 2014-2023; the 2025 fatality data is preliminary
- Fleet composition shift is one of MANY factors (ESC adoption, AEB mandates, improved road design, COVID-era driving pattern changes)
- Cannot isolate the exact contribution of fleet composition vs. other factors
- Our estimated_rate uses VMT estimates, not actual odometer data (±15% uncertainty)
- The "lethality" metric captures FARS-reported fatal crashes only, not the full crash population
