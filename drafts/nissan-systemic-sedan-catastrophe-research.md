# Research: Nissan's Systemic Sedan Catastrophe

## Angle
Nissan is the #2 deadliest brand in FARS by aggregate death rate. Individual Nissan model articles exist (Altima, Maxima, Sentra, Frontier) but nobody has connected the dots across the entire brand. The novel contribution: computing a "brand-level above-median" metric showing Nissan's problem is systemic — 13 of 19 models rank above their class median death rate. Their sedans are catastrophically worse: Maxima is literally the #1 deadliest sedan in America by death rate (5.11 per 100M VMT vs class median 0.81).

## Kill Test
- Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. Individual Nissan models have been covered, but the brand-level synthesis is novel.
- Novel angle? YES. The "every single Nissan sedan beats class median" finding is a genuinely new cross-tabulation nobody has run.
- Shareable? YES. Nissan is the 3rd largest Japanese brand, a household name. People buy these cars.

## Key Data Points

### Brand-Level
- **14,456 total deaths** across 19 Nissan models in FARS 2014-2023
- That's more than Hyundai (6,854), BMW (2,196), and Volkswagen (2,234) **combined**
- **#2 brand death rate** behind Chevrolet (which has nearly 3x the fleet)
- 13 of 19 models (68%) rank above their class median death rate — in a fair world, you'd expect ~50%

### Sedan Catastrophe
- **Maxima**: Rate 5.11 — **#1 deadliest sedan in FARS** (100th percentile), 6.3x class median
- **Altima**: Rate 2.88 — 95th percentile, 3.6x class median, 4,787 deaths (more than the Corvette, Camaro, and Mustang combined)
- **Sentra**: Rate 2.13 — 86th percentile, 2.6x class median, 2,571 deaths
- **Versa**: Rate 0.90 — 55th percentile (only one at roughly median)
- Combined sedan deaths: 9,624 (66.6% of all Nissan deaths)

### Beyond Sedans
- **Xterra**: 87th percentile SUV death rate
- **Pathfinder**: 78th percentile
- **Frontier**: 76th percentile pickup
- **Kicks**: 73rd percentile SUV (with 73.9% lethality — horrific for an SUV)
- Only the **Rogue** (35th percentile), **Titan** (32nd), and commercial vans are decent

### Lethality Context
- Kicks: 73.9% lethality (comparable to Pontiac Grand Am, a 2000s sedan)
- Versa: 72.3% lethality
- Sentra: 67.1%
- Maxima: 66.2%
- Compare to class-best SUVs: Land Cruiser 34.7%, Ram 1500 34.1%

### Financial Context
- Nissan projecting $4.2B loss in FY26
- Deep restructuring underway
- IIHS 2025 Kicks got "Poor" in moderate overlap front test
- Brand struggling to compete on quality, safety, and price simultaneously

## Novel Contribution
1. First brand-level synthesis of all Nissan models' class-relative death rates
2. The "13 of 19 above median" metric — a quantifiable brand-level safety deficit
3. Cross-tabulation showing sedan deaths (9,624) account for 66.6% of brand deaths while representing a smaller fleet share
4. The "Maxima is #1 deadliest sedan" finding in context of the broader brand pattern

## Limitations
- FARS data is fatal crashes only — Nissan may perform differently in non-fatal crashes
- Death rate estimates use VMT and fleet proxies, not actual odometer data (±15% uncertainty for low-volume models)
- Brand-level aggregation mixes discontinued and current models
- Can't isolate causation: Nissan's demographics (younger, lower-income buyers) contribute to risk independent of vehicle design
- Financial correlation doesn't imply causation — cost-cutting on safety is hypothetical

## Strongest Counterargument
Nissan's buyer demographic skews younger, lower-income, and urban. These are independently high-risk groups for fatal crashes regardless of vehicle. The Altima is a fleet/rental staple, meaning its exposure to high-mileage, unfamiliar drivers inflates its rate. The "brand is deadly" framing may really be "brand attracts higher-risk drivers." The Rogue — Nissan's best-selling model by far — actually ranks below median, suggesting the brand CAN build safe vehicles when the product matters commercially.
