# Research Notes: IIHS 30th Anniversary / Blazer Crash Test

## News Hook
- **Date:** June 24, 2026
- **Event:** IIHS 30th anniversary crash test — 1996 Chevrolet Blazer vs 2026 Chevrolet Blazer, moderate overlap front crash test
- **Study:** IIHS crashworthiness tests saved an estimated 48,352 lives from 1999–2024, valued at $538 billion (U.S. DOT VSL methodology)
- **ROI:** $600M in total IIHS funding → $538B in saved lives = ~900:1 return
- **Previous demo:** 1959 Bel Air vs 2009 Malibu (50th anniversary, 2009)

## Crash Test Results
- 2026 Blazer: Occupant compartment intact, all but one dummy metric showed minimal injury risk. Driver would have walked away with bumps and bruises.
- 1996 Blazer: Cabin crushed, dashboard and steering column pushed into dummy's lap. Airbag hit chin, snapped head back toward window. **Dummy's upper neck nodding joint broke, head detached from body.** Serious/fatal injuries to head, neck, both legs.
- 1996 Blazer: Rated "Poor" — applies to model years 1995–2004
- 2026 Blazer (2019 reintroduction): Rated "Good"

## Lives Saved Breakdown (IIHS study)
- 3 front crash tests combined: 28,697 lives (majority from moderate overlap test, launched 1995)
- Side crash test (2003): 18,224 lives
- Roof-strength evaluation (2009–2022): 1,432 lives
- Total: 48,352 lives (1999–2024)
