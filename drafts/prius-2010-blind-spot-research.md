# Research: The 2010 Prius Model Year Anomaly

## Angle
The Toyota Prius is the safest sedan in America by FARS death rate (0.55/100M VMT). But the 2010 model year accumulated 67 fatalities during the 2014-2023 FARS study period — nearly triple the 22.5 average of adjacent model years. The 2010 MY was the subject of two separate brake recalls. No other compact sedan shows a comparable spike for its 2010 model year.

## Novel Contribution
- Cross-tabbing FARS model year deaths with recall campaign timelines (10V-039 and 13V-235)
- Demonstrating the 2010 MY anomaly is Prius-specific (Civic, Corolla, Sentra all show only 1.3-1.5x bumps for their 2010 MYs)
- Connecting the "safest sedan" overall framing to a specific model-year vulnerability

## Primary Data Sources

### FARS (2014-2023)
- Prius overall: 495 deaths, 49.5 annual, 0.55 per 100M VMT, 787,500 fleet, 16% impairment rate
- Prius model year 2010: 67 deaths
- Adjacent MYs: 2009=19, 2011=26 (avg=22.5)
- Gen3 range (2010-2015): 67, 26, 23, 20, 16, 23
- 2010 spike ratio: 2.98x vs adjacent average

### Comparison (2010 MY spike ratios):
- Prius: 2.98x ← OUTLIER
- Civic: 1.35x
- Corolla: 1.44x
- Sentra: 1.52x

### NHTSA Recalls (2010 Prius)
1. **Recall 10V-039** (Feb 9, 2010): 148,549 units. ABS ECU software. "Inconsistent brake feel after ABS actuation during slow, steady braking on rough/slick surfaces." Remedy: ABS ECU reprogram. Began March 3, 2010.
2. **Recall 13V-235** (June 5, 2013): 87,060 units. Brake booster pump assembly. "Nitrogen gas from brake booster pump assembly could leak into brake fluid," reducing hydraulic pressure. Remedy: Replace brake booster pump assembly. Began Aug 7, 2013.

### Context
- 2010 was the debut year of the Gen3 Prius (complete redesign)
- Gen3 was the peak popularity period for the Prius in the US
- The 2010 unintended acceleration crisis was primarily about other Toyota models (Camry, Corolla) — the Prius issue was specifically about regenerative braking software
- The 2009 MY was the last Gen2 Prius (lower sales, smaller car)
- However, 2011 MY was also Gen3 with similar sales → only 26 deaths vs 67

### Limitations
- FARS does not include per-model-year registration counts, so cannot normalize deaths by fleet size
- 2010 Prius may have sold more units than 2009 (gen change) — but 2011 was same gen with similar sales and only 26 deaths
- Cannot isolate whether fatalities were brake-related or coincidental
- Cumulative deaths over 10-year study period, not single-year count
- Recall 10V-039 remedy was a software update — likely high completion rate, but unknown
- Recall 13V-235 came 3+ years later — some 2010 MYs may have been driving with degraded brake boosters for years before repair
