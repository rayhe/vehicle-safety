# Research: NHTSA Gutted — 550 Employees for 39,000 Annual Dead

## Angle
NHTSA has been cut from ~800 employees to ~550 — a 30% reduction. The autonomous-vehicle safety team specifically was disproportionately gutted (7 investigators → 4), with DOGE cuts directed by Elon Musk — whose Tesla is the subject of more open NHTSA investigations than any other automaker. Beyond the AV team, the impaired-driving division went from 7 to 2 people, and the behavioral research division from 16 to 3-4. Meanwhile, $475M+ of $750M in congressionally-appropriated crash-data modernization funds sit unused and are expiring.

This is a story about an agency being asked to do dramatically more (AEB mandate by 2029, EV battery safety, OTA update oversight, autonomous vehicle regulation, expanding recall volumes) with dramatically less.

## Kill Test
✅ Genuinely newsworthy: the man whose company is under more NHTSA investigations than any other directed the staffing cuts. The AV safety team was formed in 2023 and disproportionately hit because members were still probationary.
✅ Novel data angle: We can calculate the ratio of employees to annual fatalities, recalls, and investigations. 550 employees for 39,000+ annual deaths = ~71 fatalities per employee. 1,073 recalls in 2024 = ~2 recalls per employee. The per-employee workload math exposes the absurdity.
✅ Not previously covered on The Crash Report (checked all 278 stories, no DOGE/staffing coverage).

## Key Data Points

- **NHTSA workforce:** ~800 (2024) → ~550 (2026) = 30% cut
- **AV safety team:** 7 → 4 (43% cut)
- **Impaired-driving division:** 7 → 2 (71% cut)
- **Behavioral research:** 16 → 3-4 (75-81% cut)
- **No leadership:** Administrator, deputy administrator, director of governmental affairs all vacant
- **Annual traffic fatalities:** ~39,000+
- **Recalls (2024):** 1,073 affecting 29M+ vehicles
- **Per-employee math:** 550 employees / 39,000 deaths = 1 employee per 71 annual fatalities
- **Unused funding:** $475M+ of $750M crash-data modernization funds expiring
- **Key conflict:** DOGE directed by Musk; Tesla has most open NHTSA investigations
