# Research: Credit for the Sunrise

## Angle
NHTSA just announced 36,640 traffic deaths in 2025 — a 6.7% decline from 2024's 39,254. The Trump administration claims credit via enforcement campaigns ("Drive Sober or Get Pulled Over," cracking down on unqualified truckers, "Freedom Means Affordable Cars"). But the FARS per-model data tells a different story: the fleet is turning over from high-rate death traps to low-rate modern crossovers. The decline is engineering, not enforcement.

## Kill Test
✅ Genuinely newsworthy — fresh NHTSA data released June 2026
✅ Novel angle — nobody has cross-referenced per-model fleet turnover math with the national trend
✅ Data-backed — FARS model-level rates + model year death trends + NHTSA's own 2025 estimate
✅ Not a repeat of thirty-six-thousand-floor.html (that was about the floor, not the cause of decline)

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Press Release (June 2026)
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/traffic-deaths-2025-early-estimates-2024-annual
- 2025 estimate: 36,640 fatalities, -6.7% from 2024
- 2024 final FARS: 39,254 fatalities, rate 1.19 per 100M VMT
- 2025 rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT ("second-lowest in recorded history")
- VMT increased 29.8B miles (+0.9%)
- Quotes: Sec. Duffy credits "law enforcement partnerships," "cracking down on unqualified truckers," "Freedom Means Affordable Cars"
- NHTSA Admin Morrison credits "doubling down on safety strategies that reduce risky driving behaviors"

- Source: NHTSA quarterly estimates
- "12th consecutive quarterly decline in fatalities"
- Q1 2025 rate: 1.05 per 100M VMT (lowest quarterly rate since Q1 2019)
- Decline started well before current administration took office

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing FARS per-model fatality rates with model year death trends to show:
1. The 12 highest-rate vehicles (rate >3.0) contributed 22,160 deaths on ~52,588 fleet-adj VMT
2. 90+ replacement vehicles (rate <0.5) contributed 20,345 deaths on ~758,097 fleet-adj VMT — 15x more miles, fewer deaths
3. Rate differential: ~15.7x safer per mile driven
4. The national rate decline tracks almost perfectly with the pace of high-rate vehicle retirement

## Counterargument (at full strength)
DUI enforcement campaigns do measurably reduce impaired driving deaths — roughly 30% of all traffic fatalities involve impairment. The "Drive Sober or Get Pulled Over" campaign has peer-reviewed evidence of effectiveness. If "Freedom Means Affordable Cars" actually reduces new-car prices, that WOULD accelerate fleet turnover, which IS the mechanism that works. So the administration might be directionally correct about one of its three claimed mechanisms — just not the one it's emphasizing.

## Limitations
- FARS per-model data covers 2014-2023; the 2025 estimate uses different methodology
- Fleet composition change is a contributing factor, not the sole one
- We cannot isolate fleet turnover from other variables (road design, speed limits, ride-sharing effects, pandemic behavior shifts) without controlled studies
- The "rate" calculations use estimated VMT from fleet size × average annual mileage, introducing ±15% uncertainty for low-volume models
