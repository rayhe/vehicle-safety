# Research: 39,345 Dead. NHTSA Calls It Progress.

## Kill Test
**Angle:** NHTSA's 2024 preliminary data shows traffic fatalities dropped 3.8% to 39,345 — first time below 40K since 2020. Sounds like progress. But decomposing the improvement using FARS model-year data reveals it's mostly the pre-2012 death fleet aging off the road, not any improvement in human behavior. The impairment rate hasn't budged. Cross-nationally, the US still ranks #25 of 31 OECD nations in per-distance safety.

**Is this genuinely newsworthy?** Yes — combines timely NHTSA data release with original FARS decomposition that hasn't been done. Novel contribution: quantifying how much of the fatality decline is "automatic" (fleet modernization) vs. "earned" (behavior change).

**Verdict:** Proceed.

## Primary Sources
1. **NHTSA 2024 Preliminary Data** — 39,345 fatalities, -3.8% from 2023's 40,901. Rate: 1.20/100M VMT (lowest since 2019). VMT up 1% in 2024. 11th consecutive quarterly decline since Q2 2022. Source: NHTSA press release via AASHTO Journal.
2. **NHTSA FARS 2023 Final Data** — 40,901 deaths in 2023, down 4.3% from 42,721 in 2022. Rate dropped from 1.34 to 1.26/100M VMT.
3. **FARS 2014-2023 Model-Level Data** (our dataset) — 187,058 total deaths across 337 vehicle models.
4. **OECD/ITF + ETSC PIN 2025** — International fatality rates per billion vehicle-km. US: 8.5 (2021). Norway: 2.3, Sweden: 2.7, UK: 3.3, Germany: 3.9. Source: statranker.org compilation of OECD/ETSC data.

## Key FARS Analysis

### The Pre-2012 Death Fleet
- Pre-ESC mandate vehicles (MY <2012): 134,491 deaths = **71.9%** of all 187,058 deaths
- Post-ESC mandate vehicles (MY 2012+): 52,567 deaths = **28.1%**
- The "Death Cohort" (MY 2000-2007): 79,681 deaths = **42.6%** — these 8 model years account for nearly half of all deaths

### Model Year Death Curve
- Peak: MY 2005 = 11,363 deaths
- MY 2020 = 2,873 deaths (4x less than peak)
- 5-year cohorts:
  - 2000-2004: 47,921 (25.6%)
  - 2005-2009: 46,369 (24.8%)
  - 2010-2014: 32,305 (17.3%)
  - 2015-2019: 26,472 (14.2%)
  - 2020-2024: 5,955 (3.2%)

### Impairment Floor
- Overall impairment rate: 20.0% of drivers in fatal crashes tested positive for alcohol or drugs
- Alcohol: 15.1%, Drugs: 8.7% (some overlap)
- This rate appears stable across vehicle types and hasn't declined

### Cross-National Comparison (per billion vkm)
- Norway: 2.3
- Sweden: 2.7
- UK: 3.3
- Germany: 3.9
- Canada: 4.4
- France: 5.3
- **US (2021): 8.5**
- **US (2024 est): ~7.5** (1.20/100M VMT ÷ 0.1609 = 7.46/bn vkm)
- Even at the improved 2024 rate: 2x Germany, 3.2x Norway
