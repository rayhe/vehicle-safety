# Research: Ford Expedition/Navigator Seat Belt Pretensioner Triple Recall

## Story Angle
Ford has recalled the Expedition and Navigator's front seat belt pretensioners THREE TIMES in 2.5 years — each time expanding the scope because the previous fix didn't address the full problem. The affected population grew from 77,574 to 419,967 vehicles (5.4x expansion). The actual root cause — a specific propellant and stabilizer combination that degrades in heat — wasn't identified until the third recall. And the fix won't be available for 3 months.

The paradox: a pyrotechnic device designed to cinch your seat belt tight in the first 10 milliseconds of a crash is instead deploying on its own, locking the belt in place, and filling the cabin with smoke. Owners describe an "explosion-like sound" followed by being "punched in the chest."

## Three Recalls Timeline

### Recall 1: 24V099 (Ford #24S06) — February 9, 2024
- **Scope:** 77,574 vehicles (2018-2020 Expedition/Navigator)
- **Production dates:** October 1, 2018 – June 30, 2019 (~9 months)
- **Root cause theory:** Corroded squib pins where bridge wire is welded. Partial weld separation causes high resistance or open circuit → inadvertent deployment. HVAC drain tube elbow installed to reduce moisture.
- **Reports:** 140 reports across 119 vehicles, 11 minor injuries (torso bruising from rapid belt tightening)
- **Preceded by:** NHTSA PE23-021 investigation opened November 22, 2023

### Recall 2: 25V197 (Ford #25S31) — March/April 2025
- **Scope:** 105,250 vehicles (2018-2020 Expedition/Navigator)
- **Production dates:** October 1, 2018 – July 31, 2020 (~22 months)
- **Expansion ratio:** 1.36x from Recall 1
- **Root cause update:** Oxidation within pretensioner assembly. Initially blamed water leak from AC system, but "the problem may be with the pretensioners themselves" (Consumer Reports). Ford's CCRG identified vehicles outside 24S06 population having same issue (Jan 9, 2025).
- **Reports:** 17 dealer reports/warranty claims in new batch; no crashes or injuries in expanded population
- **Note:** Vehicles previously serviced under 24S06 might still need work under 25S31

### Recall 3: 26V344 (Ford #26S34) — June 3, 2026
- **Scope:** 419,967 vehicles (342,283 Expeditions + 77,684 Navigators, 2018-2022)
- **Production dates:** May 15, 2017 – October 25/26, 2022 (~65 months)
- **Expansion ratio:** 5.4x from Recall 1, 4.0x from Recall 2
- **Root cause (final):** Specific combination of propellant and stabilizer degrades in high-heat environments → oxidation of internal components → corrosive byproduct → open circuit triggers pretensioner
- **Fix:** New micro gas generator propellant and stabilizer combination. Minimal internal corrosion of bridgewire and isolated pin, improved chemical stability.
- **Timeline:** Interim notification June 5-8, 2026. Remedy parts not available until August 31 – September 4, 2026 (~3 months)
- **Reports:** 1 injury globally
- **Replaces and expands** both 24V099 and 25V197

### Exposure Calculation (3-month window)
- 419,967 vehicles × ~3,125 miles (3 months at 12,500 mi/yr) = ~1.31 billion VMT
- At Expedition's fatality rate of 2.31/100M VMT → ~30 expected fatal crashes in this window
- Not all would involve pretensioner failure, but any occupant in a crash during this window has compromised protection

## What Owners Experience
- Loud "explosion-like sound" — the pyrotechnic charge firing
- Sudden, violent belt tightening around torso — "like being punched or kicked in the chest"
- Burning smell and smoke filling cabin
- Belt locks in position, won't retract or extend
- Airbag malfunction light illuminates
- Happens during normal driving, no crash involved

## Root Cause Evolution
1. **Recall 1 theory:** Water intrusion from AC system corrodes squib pins → partial weld separation → open circuit. Fix: replace pretensioner + install HVAC drain tube elbow.
2. **Recall 2 theory:** Oxidation within pretensioner assembly, not just water. Problem "may be with the pretensioners themselves."
3. **Recall 3 (final):** Specific propellant + stabilizer combination degrades in high heat → corrosive byproduct → open circuit. New propellant/stabilizer formulation eliminates issue.
