## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? Yes. The disparity — 3x the next automaker — combined with Ford's #2 FARS death toll creates a natural question: are the recalls catching the right problems? Original contribution: cross-referencing recall volume with FARS death rate to show whether Ford's recall hyperactivity correlates with its fatality performance.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers 2014-2023; recall data is 2026. Different time windows.
- Recall volume partly reflects fleet size — Ford sells a lot of vehicles.
- Not all recalls address life-threatening defects; many are sticker/label issues.
- High recall volume could indicate better self-reporting, not worse quality.

## Strongest Counterargument
Ford's recall volume may reflect corporate responsibility, not failure. If Ford is more diligent about self-reporting than competitors, its recall numbers look worse despite potentially being safer per-vehicle. Toyota recalled fewer but had the 2009-2011 unintended acceleration crisis that killed 89 people and resulted in a $1.2B criminal penalty — arguably worse than many recalls combined.
