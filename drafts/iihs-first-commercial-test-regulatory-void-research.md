# Research: IIHS's First Commercial Vehicle Evaluation Reveals a Regulatory Black Hole

## Angle
IIHS released its first-ever commercial vehicle evaluations on June 9, 2026. After decades of testing passenger cars, the institute finally checked whether the vans and heavy-duty pickups killing 6,535 people annually have basic safety equipment. Five of nine vehicles are missing at least one feature considered essential for driver protection. The Chevy Express 2500 — a van that's been in production since 1996 — still doesn't have standard force limiters in 2025, a technology that's been routine in passenger cars for over two decades.

## Primary Sources (3+)

### 1. IIHS (June 9, 2026) — Commercial Vehicle Evaluation
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/iihs-launches-evaluations-of-commercial-vehicles-starting-with-driver-protection
- First-ever IIHS commercial vehicle evaluation
- 9 vehicles tested: 6 cargo vans, 3 Class 3 pickups (GVWR 10,001-14,000 lbs)
- Only 4 of 9 check all boxes for driver protection
- Five features evaluated: effective seat belt reminder, standard pretensioners, force limiters, front airbag, side airbag
- Results:
  - ✅ ALL BOXES: Chevy Silverado 3500HD, Ford F350 SuperCrew, Chevy BrightDrop 400, Ram ProMaster 2500
  - ❌ Seat belt reminder only: Ram 3500, Ford Transit T250, Mercedes Sprinter 2500, Rivian Delivery 500
  - ❌ Force limiters + seat belt reminder: Chevrolet Express 2500

### 2. NHTSA FARS Data (2014-2023) — Fatality Records
- Ford E-350: 776 deaths, rate 2.51/100M VMT, impairment 15.7%
- Chevy Express: 475 deaths, rate 0.92/100M VMT, impairment 19%
- Ford Transit: 178 deaths, rate 0.14/100M VMT, impairment 16%
- Key insight: E-350 and Express impairment rates are LOWER than passenger vehicle averages. Professional drivers dying sober.

### 3. IIHS/NHTSA Statistics
- 6,535 people killed in 2023 in crashes involving heavy/medium trucks or light vans = 16% of all US roadway fatalities
- Front airbags reduce driver fatalities in frontal crashes by 29% (IIHS research)
- Side airbags reduce driver death risk in side crashes by 25-52%
- Persistent seat belt reminders increase belt use by 30% in part-time users
- Airbags NOT REQUIRED on commercial vehicles — many government standards for passenger vehicles don't apply

### 4. NHTSA Regulatory Context
- Federal standard requires audible seat belt signal to last only 4-8 seconds (vs IIHS's 90-second standard)
- Stricter rules slated for 2028 for passenger vehicles and "some commercial counterparts"
- No AEB requirement for commercial vehicles

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing IIHS's first commercial vehicle evaluation with FARS fatality data reveals: the Chevy Express has killed 475 people over the past decade while lacking force limiters — a technology that costs a few dollars per belt retractor and has been standard in passenger cars since the early 2000s. The Express impairment rate is 19% — well below passenger car averages. These are sober professional drivers dying in a vehicle that nobody was checking.

## Counterargument (Required — Strongest Case)
Commercial vehicles have different use cases. They spend less time in high-speed travel. Their drivers are professionally trained. The regulatory gap may reflect a reasonable prioritization of different vehicle types. Also: the raw FARS numbers may overcount commercial vehicle involvement — a van involved in a crash doesn't mean the van's safety equipment failed.

## Actionable Takeaway
Fleet managers: check whether your commercial vehicles have side airbags, force limiters, and pretensioners. If you're buying Express vans, know they're missing force limiters in 2025. IIHS results are at iihs.org. If you're buying a Rivian Delivery van for Amazon routes, the seat belt reminder is weak — add a third-party persistent reminder.
