# Research: Power-Folding Seat Contagion — Three Recalls, One Death, Dozens Unchecked

## Angle
When a 2-year-old was crushed by a power-folding seat in a Hyundai Palisade in March 2026, the recall system did something unusual: it triggered cross-manufacturer checks. A GM engineer proactively tested the Cadillac Vistiq's third-row seats — and found the same defect. But this "contagion recall" was voluntary. There's no NHTSA mandate requiring OEMs to check their own vehicles when a competitor recalls for a similar defect. Dozens of three-row SUVs with power-folding seats remain untested.

## Journalist
Vin Wreckage — Existential Dread. Paradox: the system worked (GM caught it), but only because one engineer chose to check. What about the engineers at Toyota, Honda, Ford, Nissan who might not have?

## Primary Sources (3+)

### Source 1: NHTSA Recall — Hyundai Palisade (26V160000)
- 2-year-old Lucia Ayala, Akron, Ohio, March 7, 2026
- Pinned by third-row power-folding seatback in Restaurant Depot parking lot
- Suffocated before bystanders freed her
- 60,000+ Palisade Calligraphy and Limited trims recalled
- Seats lacked "sufficient anti-pinch protection" during power-folding stow function
- Fix: software update via Bluelink or dealer

### Source 2: NHTSA Recall — Kia Telluride (26V173000)
- 568 vehicles: 2027 Telluride Hybrid SX Prestige + X-Line SX Prestige with Executive Package
- Same defect (shared HMG platform)
- Lower trims have manual folding seats — not affected

### Source 3: NHTSA Recall — Cadillac Vistiq (N262555780)
- 14,540 vehicles: 13,629 2026 MY + 911 2027 MY
- 100% estimated defect rate
- GM engineer submitted report March 31, 2026 via Speak Up For Safety (SUFS) program
- Trigger: "the March 2026 announcement of a recall by a different vehicle manufacturer"
- Test: 33-40 lb box placed on seat, seatback continued folding and trapped box
- 6 prior complaints/incidents (May 2025 – June 2026), zero injuries
- Fix: replace folding seat module with auto-reverse capability. Parts not ready — interim: disable power-folding feature
- GM halted 2027 shipments June 8, 2026
- Owner notifications: August 3, 2026

### Source 4: Jalopnik investigation (July 7, 2026)
- First to connect all three recalls as linked "domino effect"
- Noted "many other vehicles with power-folding seats" remain untested
- Byron Hurd / The Drive: detailed the timeline

## The Systemic Gap
- No NHTSA rule mandating cross-manufacturer defect checks
- GM acted voluntarily — not every OEM has a SUFS program
- FARS doesn't capture non-crash vehicle deaths (this happened in a parking lot)
- Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard 207 governs seat strength in crashes, but doesn't address powered-seat entrapment scenarios

## Data Gap Note
FARS captures fatal crashes only. Non-crash vehicle deaths (entrapment, power equipment, carbon monoxide in enclosed spaces) fall outside FARS reporting. This is itself a finding: the deadliest vehicle features may be ones that never show up in the crash database.
