# Research: TPMS Warns Too Late — 600 Dead

## Angle
The TREAD Act (2000), born from the Firestone/Ford Explorer tire scandal that killed 271 people, mandated tire pressure monitoring systems (TPMS) in every new car. GAO estimated TPMS would prevent 124 fatalities per year. Twenty-four years later, 600+ people still die annually in tire-related crashes. The federal standard (FMVSS 138) only requires a warning at 25% underinflation — a threshold so generous that the tire industry's own trade association (USTMA) publicly admits it's "often too late to prevent damage." Congress paid $771 million per year for a dashboard light that blinks after the damage is done.

## Primary Sources

### 1. FMVSS 138 — Federal Standard (49 CFR § 571.138)
- URL: https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/section-571.138
- Key: TPMS must illuminate warning within 20 minutes when pressure is 25% below manufacturer's recommended cold inflation pressure
- This is the FLOOR — no earlier warning required by law

### 2. NHTSA — National Tire Safety Week / Tire Crash Statistics
- URL: https://www.nhtsa.gov/equipment/tires/safety-and-savings-ride-your-tires
- Key data: Nearly 11,000 tire-related crashes per year, 600+ fatalities
- 15-passenger vans especially at risk

### 3. USTMA / AAA — National Tire Safety Week 2026
- Source: Democrat & Chronicle / USA TODAY Network (June 30, 2026)
- Key: USTMA says TPMS warns at 25% underinflation, "often too late to prevent damage for some vehicles"
- AAA: 72.2M travelers for July 4th (record), 61.4M driving
- National Tire Safety Week: June 28 - July 4, 2026

### 4. GAO Assessment (GAO-02-823R)
- URL: https://www.gao.gov/assets/gao-02-823r.pdf
- TPMS estimated to prevent 124 fatalities/year and 8,722 injuries
- Cost: $771 million/year (four-tire, 25% option)

### 5. Firestone/Ford Explorer Tire Controversy — Wikipedia + Congressional Record
- 271 fatalities, 700+ injuries from tread separation
- Firestone replaced tires in Venezuela (1998), Malaysia, Thailand, Gulf states — didn't tell NHTSA
- Congress passed TREAD Act October 2000
- NHTSA survey: 1 in 4 passenger cars, 1 in 3 light trucks driven with significantly underinflated tires

### 6. NHTSA/OHS — Driver Behavior
- 85% of drivers fail to properly check tire pressure (tire industry survey)
- 660 lives/year, 33,000 injuries/year (older NHTSA estimate)
