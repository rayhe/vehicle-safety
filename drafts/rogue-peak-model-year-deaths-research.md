# Research: Nissan Rogue Peak Model-Year Deaths

## Angle
The Nissan Rogue's deadliest model year (MY2017: 127 deaths) produced 44% more fatalities than the Toyota RAV4's deadliest model year (MY2019: 88). These are America's two best-selling compact SUVs, competing in the same showroom aisle, at the same price, for the same buyer. The Rogue's death trajectory diverged sharply upward in MY2016 and sustained an elevated plateau (107-127 deaths/year) through MY2019 — a pattern no competitor matched.

## Kill Test
- Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. Peak model-year deaths are an underexplored metric. Most safety analyses use cumulative totals or per-VMT rates. Identifying the single worst vintage of each vehicle isolates the question: "which specific generation was the deadliest?"
- Novel contribution: Cross-segmental peak-model-year death comparison. Nobody publishes this.
- After 172 articles: No Rogue-specific article exists. The Nissan subprime pipeline article covers lending demographics, not the Rogue's engineering trajectory specifically.

## Primary Data (FARS 2014-2023)

- Total deaths: 968 | Crashes: 1,777 | Lethality: 54.5% | Fleet: 2.19M
- Impairment: 19.0% (normal range)
- Model year deaths: MY2008:27, MY2009:19, MY2010:24, MY2011:39, MY2012:47, MY2013:59, MY2014:43, MY2015:68, MY2016:109, MY2017:127, MY2018:109, MY2019:107, MY2020:76, MY2021:49, MY2022:9

- Generation 1 (T31): MY2008-2013, peak 59

## Key Findings
1. The Rogue's MY2017 death count (127) was 44% higher than the RAV4's worst-ever year (88)
2. The Rogue sustained 100+ deaths/year across four consecutive model years (2016-2019) — no competitor matched this plateau
3. The CR-V redesign (MY2017) REDUCED deaths 26%; the Rogue's redesign (MY2021) also reduced deaths, but only after a 4-year elevated run
4. CX-5 peak was 6x lower than Rogue peak despite overlapping price/segment
5. The Escape spiked in MY2017 too (165 deaths) but immediately collapsed to 87 the next year — the Rogue stayed elevated

## Strongest Counterargument
Fleet growth. Nissan sold dramatically more Rogues in 2016-2019 than earlier years (the Rogue became Nissan's #1 seller around 2016). More vehicles on the road means more exposure to fatal crashes, even at the same per-vehicle risk rate. Without per-model-year registration data to normalize, the raw death count increase could be explained entirely by sales volume. The RAV4 ALSO grew its fleet in this period — but Toyota's TNGA platform may have genuinely improved crashworthiness while Nissan's T32 platform stayed static.

## Limitations
- FARS captures fatal crashes only — the ~36K annual deaths are a fraction of ~6.7M total crashes
- Fleet size is aggregate, not per-model-year — can't compute death rate per MY cohort
- Exposure time varies: MY2017 had 7 years in the FARS window, MY2022 had 2 years
- VMT estimates use national averages, not model-specific driving patterns
