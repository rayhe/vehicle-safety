# Research: Ford Explorer — Recall as a Subscription Model

## Hook
Ford Explorer second-row seat re-recall (26V451) filed July 19, 2026 — 387,911 vehicles. The fix from the previous recall (25V393) didn't work. Same month, 288,314 Explorers recalled for roof rail covers detaching — also a repeat fix. Total: ~676,000 active Explorer/Aviator recalls in July 2026 alone.

## Key Data Points

### FARS Data (2014-2023)
- **3,797 deaths** — 9th deadliest vehicle in America by raw body count
- **1.54 deaths per 100M VMT** — 2nd highest rate among midsize/large SUVs
- **379.7 deaths per year average**
- **6,626 fatal crash involvements** — Explorer occupants died in 57.3% of those
- **19.5% impairment rate** — below class average, meaning 80.5% of fatal crashes are sober drivers
- Worst model year: 2002 (503 deaths — Firestone tire / rollover scandal era)

Explorer rate is 5.3x the Honda Pilot and 3.7x the Highlander.

### July 2026 Active Recalls
1. **26V451 (Jul 19):** 387,911 Explorer/Aviator — second-row seat bezel misalignment can cause seat to tip/slide unexpectedly. RE-RECALL: previous recall 25V393 failed to resolve. 14 field reports of unintended seat movement while driving. 6 of those had already gotten the "fix."
2. **Roof rail recall (Jul 17):** 288,314 Explorers — trim covers can detach at highway speed. RE-RECALL: 2021 epoxy fix failed.
3. **Lincoln Aviator included:** 160,391 Aviators in the seat recall (same platform).

### Original Cross-Tab Finding
The Explorer is simultaneously America's most-recalled current-production SUV AND has the second-highest death rate in its segment. The question is whether the recall treadmill is masking a deeper design problem. The 6th-generation Explorer (2020+) shares a platform with the Lincoln Aviator and has been recalled repeatedly for:
- Roof rails detaching (twice)
- Second-row seats failing (twice)  
- Stop-start system failures
- Various other issues
