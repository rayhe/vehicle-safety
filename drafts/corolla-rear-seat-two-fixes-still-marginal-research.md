# Research: Two Fixes Later, the Corolla's Back Seat Still Fails

## Angle
The 2026 Toyota Corolla — America's most iconic economy sedan — cannot earn even a basic IIHS Top Safety Pick award. The reason: the back seat. IIHS rated rear passenger chest protection and rear passenger restraints/dummy kinematics both "Marginal" in the updated moderate overlap front test. Toyota knew the back seat was a problem. They added rear seat belt load limiters and pretensioners for 2023 models. They redesigned the rear seat cushions for 2025 models. IIHS tested the 2025-2026 model with ALL those fixes — and it's still Marginal.

Meanwhile, the Camry, Accord, Civic Hatchback, Prius, Elantra, and Sonata all earned TSP+. The Corolla is the only high-volume Toyota sedan that earned nothing.

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing IIHS 2026 results with FARS data to show that the Corolla is paradoxically the SAFEST of the Big Four sedans by fatality rate (1.85 per 100M VMT) — lower than Camry (2.03), Civic (2.25), and Accord (3.07) — yet the one that can't earn a safety award. Rate measures how often people die in your car. IIHS measures how well it protects them when the crash comes. The Corolla fails the second test.

## Core Data Points

### IIHS 2026 Results — Corolla Sedan
- **Test vehicle:** 2026 Toyota Corolla LE 4-door (Test ID CEF2605)
- **Overall moderate overlap front:** MARGINAL
- **Structure and safety cage:** Good
- **Driver injury measures:** All Good (head/neck, chest, thigh/hip, leg/foot)
- **Driver restraints and dummy kinematics:** Good
- **Rear passenger head/neck:** Good
- **Rear passenger chest:** MARGINAL ← the failure
- **Rear passenger thigh:** Good
- **Rear passenger restraints/kinematics:** MARGINAL ← the failure
- **Rating applies to:** 2025-2026 models
- **Prior fixes attempted:**
  - 2023 MY: added rear seat belt load limiters and pretensioners
  - 2025 MY: further changes to rear seat cushions
- **Result:** Still Marginal after BOTH fixes

### FARS Data (2014-2023) — Corolla
- **Total deaths:** 4,945
- **Annual average:** 494.5
- **Fatal crashes:** 7,713
- **Estimated fleet:** 2,318,750
- **Estimated VMT:** 26.7 billion miles
- **Fatality rate:** 1.85 per 100M VMT
- **Toxicology:** 19.2% any impairment (14.9% alcohol, 7.9% drugs)
