# Research: The Mandate Gap — Pre-ESC Body Count

## Angle
72% of all occupant fatalities in FARS 2014–2023 data involve vehicles built before electronic stability control (ESC) was federally mandated (MY2012). Pickups are the worst at 82%. This cross-tabulation maps real deaths to specific federal safety technology mandates, revealing the "mandate gap" — the body count attributable to vehicles that pre-date proven life-saving technology.

## Kill Test
- **Is this genuinely newsworthy?** Yes — nobody has published a cross-tabulation of FARS deaths against specific federal safety mandates by model year. The 72% figure is striking and actionable (it quantifies the fleet modernization dividend).
- **Novel angle on data?** Yes — original FARS data analysis mapping deaths to technology generations (ESC 2012, backup cameras 2018, AEB voluntary 2022+).
- **After 228 articles, is this still surprising?** Yes. We've covered fleet age (`kill-curve-vehicle-age`, `fleet-turnover-lag`, `pickup-zombie-fleet`) but never specifically mapped deaths to federal mandate model year thresholds. The pickup truck breakout (82% pre-ESC vs 64% for sports cars) is a new finding.

## FARS Data Analysis (Original)

### Deaths by Safety Tech Generation (FARS 2014–2023)
- **Total deaths in dataset:** 187,058
- **Pre-ESC (MY<2012):** 134,491 (71.9%)
- **Post-ESC (MY2012+):** 52,567 (28.1%)
- **Pre-backup-cam (MY<2018):** 172,849 (92.4%)
- **Post-backup-cam (MY2018+):** 14,209 (7.6%)
- **Pre-AEB-era (MY<2022):** 185,853 (99.4%)
- **Post-AEB-era (MY2022+):** 1,205 (0.6%)

### Pre-ESC Deaths by Vehicle Class
- Sedan: 60,746/87,406 (69%)
- SUV: 30,758/45,242 (68%)
- Pickup: 33,630/41,167 (82%)
- Van: 5,647/7,464 (76%)
- Sports Car: 3,710/5,779 (64%)

### Key Finding
Pickups have the highest pre-ESC death share (82%) — 18 percentage points above sports cars. This aligns with ESC being especially effective at preventing rollover crashes, which disproportionately kill pickup occupants.

## External Sources

### ESC Effectiveness (IIHS, 2016)
- ESC reduces fatal crash risk by 38% for cars, 56% for SUVs
- 75% fewer single-vehicle rollovers for SUVs, 72% for cars
- ESC saved 4,100 lives between 2010–2014; 1,580 in 2014 alone
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/life-saving-benefits-of-esc-continue-to-accrue

### ESC Mandate Timeline
- FMVSS 126: Phased in 2009–2012, all light vehicles by MY2012
- Source: https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2007-06-22/html/E7-11965.htm

### NHTSA ESC Effectiveness Study
- 2008–2010: ESC saved 2,202 lives (634 + 705 + 863)
- NHTSA estimated ESC reduces fatal single-vehicle crashes by 30–50% for cars, 50–70% for SUVs
- Source: NHTSA DOT HS 812 042

### Backup Camera Mandate
- FMVSS 111: All new vehicles MY2018+ must have rearview camera
- Prevents ~58 deaths and 267 injuries annually (NHTSA estimate)
- Source: NHTSA Final Rule, 2014

- Source: IIHS, NHTSA

## Limitations
- FARS 2014–2023 observation window naturally includes more older vehicles (a MY2000 vehicle has 10 crash-years of exposure vs a MY2020 vehicle having 3)
- Pre-ESC vehicles are older and may be less safe for reasons beyond ESC (weaker structures, no side curtain airbags, etc.)
- Backup camera prevents mostly low-speed backing deaths (~58/year) — the 92.4% figure doesn't mean cameras would have prevented those deaths
- Post-2022 AEB vehicles have very little FARS exposure time (0.6% of deaths doesn't mean AEB prevents 99.4%)
- Fleet composition vs technology: older vehicles correlate with lower income, different driving patterns

## Strongest Counterargument
The 72% figure conflates "pre-ESC model year" with "old vehicle." A MY2008 car lacks not just ESC but also modern airbags, better structure, side curtain airbags, and dozens of other improvements. ESC alone doesn't explain the entire gap — it's the marker for a generation of safety technology that arrived together. The intellectually honest framing is: "72% of deaths involve vehicles from the pre-ESC generation" not "ESC would have prevented 72% of deaths."

## Actionable Insights
- If buying used: MY2012+ is the minimum safety floor (ESC standard)
- If buying used for a teen: MY2018+ adds backup camera + likely better AEB
- If you drive a pre-2012 pickup: you're in the highest-risk mandate gap (82%)
- Check your car's model year against the ESC mandate: https://www.nhtsa.gov/equipment/electronic-stability-control
