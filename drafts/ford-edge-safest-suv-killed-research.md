# Research: Ford Killed Its Safest Mainstream SUV

## Article Number: 516
## Journalist: Clara Rollover
## Scheduled: 2026-07-16

## Angle
Ford discontinued the Edge after 2024. FARS data shows the Edge was the safest mainstream SUV Ford made — safer per mile than the Escape, Explorer, Expedition, and Excursion. Ford kept the Explorer, which is 3.3x deadlier per mile with nearly identical driver impairment rates. An Edge buyer's most logical upgrade path is now the Explorer, a vehicle with a death rate that's more than triple the Edge's.

## FARS Data

- Deaths: 508 (10-year FARS)

- Crashes: 1,091
- Fleet: 875,000
- Death rate: 0.46 per 100M VMT
- D/C ratio: 0.466

- Model years in data: 2005–2022

### Ford Explorer (comparison)

- Fleet: 1,968,750

## Key Insight
Edge and Explorer have nearly identical impairment rates (19.6% vs 19.5%). The driver profiles are essentially the same. The difference is the vehicle: the Explorer is bigger, heavier, higher center of gravity, more rollover-prone. The Edge's lower D/C ratio (0.466 vs 0.573) means Edge occupants survive crashes more often.
