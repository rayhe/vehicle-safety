# Research: The Fleet Minority That Produces the Death Majority

## Angle
29.4% of America's registered vehicle fleet produces 63.1% of all traffic fatalities. Cross-referencing FARS per-model death rates against the 2025 national average (1.10 per 100M VMT — second-lowest in recorded history per NHTSA's April 2, 2026 release), a minority of models with above-average rates account for a supermajority of deaths.

## Kill Test
- **Novel?** YES — original fleet-weighted rate threshold cross-tabulation
- **Newsworthy?** YES — pegged to NHTSA's April 2, 2026 release of 2025 preliminary fatality data (36,640 killed, 1.10/100M VMT)
- **Surprising?** YES — the split is 29/63 (not 50/50), and the class breakdown reveals sedans are the structural drag

## Key Data Points (original calculations)

### The 29/63 Split
- Models with death rate ABOVE 1.10/100M VMT: 85 models, 50.4M fleet, 120,641 deaths
- Models with death rate BELOW 1.10/100M VMT: 252 models, 121.1M fleet, 70,552 deaths
- 29.4% of fleet → 63.1% of deaths

### Class Breakdown (% of class fleet above national rate → % of class deaths)
- Sedans: 54.5% of fleet above rate → 83.3% of sedan deaths
- Sports Cars: 59.2% → 87.5%
- Pickups: 23.6% → 42.9%
- SUVs: 11.2% → 40.6%
- Vans: 24.8% → 54.8%

### The Sedan Problem
Sedans with rate > 2.0 (double national avg): Accord (3.07), Civic (2.25), Camry (2.03), Altima (2.88), Impala (5.0), Malibu (2.03), Focus (2.52), Sentra (2.13), Taurus (2.74), Maxima (5.11), Cobalt (5.1)
Combined: ~40,000+ deaths from just 21 sedan models

### 2025 Context
- 2024: 39,254 fatalities (↓4.3% from 2023)
- 2025 estimate: 36,640 (↓6.7%, fifth-largest decline in FARS history)
- 2025 rate: 1.10/100M VMT (second-lowest ever, behind only 2014's 1.08)
- VMT INCREASED 0.9% — people drove more but died less

### Counterfactual
If above-rate vehicles matched the average rate: estimated ~50,000 fewer deaths over the FARS period (rough calculation: excess deaths from above-rate vehicles)
