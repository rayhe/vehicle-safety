# Research: The Death of the Sub-$20K Car and Its Safety Toll

## Angle
Zero new cars are available under $20,000 in 2026. Down from 17 options in 2020. Budget buyers are being pushed into older used vehicles with significantly higher fatality rates. The death of affordable new cars is an emerging safety crisis that nobody is quantifying.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES.
- **Novel cross-tabulation:** No one has mapped the FARS death rates of discontinued cheap cars against the used-vehicle alternatives budget buyers are now forced into.
- **Timely:** Nissan Versa (last sub-$20K holdout) discontinued December 2025. Kia K4 at $22,290 is now the cheapest new car.
- **Counterintuitive:** Cheap new cars were dangerous. But the used cars replacing them are MORE dangerous. Killing the cheap car segment didn't fix the safety problem — it made it worse.

## Primary Sources
1. **NHTSA FARS 2014-2023** — death rates, lethality ratios, impairment data for all 9 discontinued sub-$20K cars
2. **Kelley Blue Book / Cox Automotive** — Jan 2026 average new car price: $49,191
3. **CarCupid / KBB / Edmunds** — sub-$20K market: 17 options (2020) → 0 (2026). Versa discontinued Dec 2025.
4. **J.D. Power** — used vehicles under $20K fell from 53% (2019) to 30% (2025)
5. **IIHS 2026 awards** — cheapest TSP+ is Kia K4 at $22,290; back seat protection now required
6. **NHTSA 2024 final data** — 39,254 deaths; 2025 estimate 36,640

## The Comparison: What Budget Buyers Now Face

Cheapest new car: Kia K4 ($22,290) — based on Forte platform, rate 0.40
If buyer can't afford K4, they're buying a used car. Used under $20K = 6-12 year old vehicles.

Model year death curve (all vehicles):
- MY 2018: 4,435 deaths (vehicles 0-5 years old during data window)
- MY 2013: 6,903 deaths (vehicles 1-10 years old during window)
- MY 2005: 11,363 deaths (vehicles 9-18 years old during window)

Older vehicles accumulate dramatically more deaths — partly exposure time, but partly because older cars lack ESC, AEB, modern airbags, and structural improvements.

## Original Contribution
1. **Collective death toll of the dead cheap car category:** 3,213 deaths across 9 models
2. **Lethality gap:** Average lethality of dead cheap cars (~70%) vs. cheapest current new car (Kia Forte/K4: 66.8%) — marginal. But rate gap is massive: avg dead-car rate ~0.91 vs Forte 0.40 = 2.3x
3. **The real danger:** Budget buyers pushed to 8-12 year old used cars will face model-year death rates 2-3x higher than current-year vehicles, based on MY death curve
4. **The Mirage exception:** Mitsubishi Mirage (rate 0.40, lethality 65.3%) was actually reasonably safe for its price — and it was killed too

## Strongest Counterargument
Cheap new cars were genuinely dangerous — the Chevy Sonic had a 75.4% lethality rate and 1.40 rate per 100M VMT. Killing them removes dangerous vehicles from production. A 5-year-old used Civic (rate 2.25 but modern structural design) might actually be safer than a new Spark.

Response: True for some pairings. But the median used car under $20K is now 8+ years old. MY 2016 and older vehicles predate many AEB mandates. The safety floor dropped.

## Limitations
- FARS data window (2014-2023) captures vehicles at different ages — MY 2018 had less exposure time than MY 2005
- Rate data (deaths per 100M VMT) uses estimated VMT, not actual odometer readings (±15% uncertainty for low-volume models)
- We can't directly measure the safety impact of discontinued models pushing buyers to used vehicles — this is a prospective analysis, not retrospective
