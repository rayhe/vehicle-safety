# Research: Chevrolet S-10 Pickup — 1,427 Dead, Discontinued 2004

## Core FARS Data
- **Make/Model:** Chevrolet S-10 PICKUP
- **Class:** Pickup
- **Total deaths (2014-2023):** 1,427
- **Annual average:** 142.7 deaths/year
- **Total FARS crashes:** 1,891
- **Estimated fleet:** 218,750
- **VMT (estimated 100M):** 2,954
- **Rate per 100M VMT:** 4.83 — 5th highest of ALL vehicles, 3.2x sedan average (1.51)
- **Crash lethality:** 75.5% (1,427/1,891) — when an S-10 is in a FARS crash, 3 out of 4 die
- **Crash involvement per 1M fleet:** 8,640 — 4th highest of all vehicles

## Toxicology
- **Total drivers in fatal crashes:** from tox dataset
- **Impairment rate:** 20.5% — right at national average
- **Alcohol-positive:** typical
- **Drug-positive:** typical
- **Key finding:** Impairment does NOT explain the S-10's death rate. It's average. The vehicle is the problem.

## Context
- **Discontinued:** 2004 (replaced by Colorado)
- **Production years:** 1982-2004 (22 years)
- **Safety features (typical):** No ESC (pre-2012 mandate), often no ABS, no side airbags, basic frontal airbags only on later models (1998+)
- **Architecture:** Body-on-frame, rear-wheel drive, compact pickup
- **Design problem:** Light rear end (no payload) = rear oversteer in emergency maneuvers, especially wet/icy
- **FMVSS 126 (ESC mandate):** Required for all vehicles from MY 2012. S-10 stopped production 8 years before it took effect.

## Novel Analysis — "Zombie Fleet" 
All S-10s on the road are 20-42 years old. They pre-date:
- Electronic Stability Control (2012 mandate)
- Side curtain airbags (widespread ~2007)
- Advanced AEB (2020s+)
- IIHS side crash test (2003)
- Updated frontal offset test (2012)

These are trucks built to 1990s-era crash standards (or earlier) being driven on 2020s roads alongside 5,000-lb SUVs with crumple zones designed for a different weight class.

## The GMC Sonoma Connection
The GMC Sonoma is the badge-engineered twin of the S-10. Same truck, different badge.
- Sonoma: 378 deaths, 2.13 rate
- S-10: 1,427 deaths, 4.83 rate
- Combined: 1,805 deaths from the same platform

The rate difference (4.83 vs 2.13) likely reflects fleet composition — more S-10s remain on the road, skewed toward cheaper/higher-risk owners.

## Angle
The Chevrolet S-10 was discontinued in 2004. In the 10 years of FARS data since 2014, it has killed 1,427 people — more than the Porsche 911, BMW Z3, Mercedes S-Class, Tesla Model 3, and Tesla Model Y COMBINED. It dies at a rate of 4.83 per 100M VMT, while America's overall rate just hit 0.99. The S-10's occupants experience a road 5x more dangerous than the national average, and 37x more dangerous than a modern Ram 1500 driver's road. These trucks were built before NHTSA required electronic stability control. You cannot add ESC to a 1997 S-10. It is a fixed, irreversible deficit that will kill people for as long as these trucks remain registered.
