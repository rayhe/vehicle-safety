The Gap

Your Minivan’s Airbags Can Fire From a Pothole. Honda Knew for Four Years.

Clara Rollover • Clautomotive Desk
June 27, 2026

Honda recalled 440,830 Odyssey minivans because the side curtain airbags in the second and third rows can mistake a pothole for a collision, deploying inches from where children sit in car seats without any crash occurring at all.[1] Twenty-five injuries since January 2017 from airbags that fired on intact vehicles. The SRS electronic control unit contains deployment parameters so poorly calibrated that speed bumps and road debris register as side impacts, which means the safety system designed to protect rear-seat passengers is itself a hazard to rear-seat passengers.

4 years
Between Honda’s production fix and the recall for vehicles already sold

Honda first learned about inadvertent deployments in late 2017. By July 2021, engineers had pinpointed the conditions causing false triggers: potholes, rough surfaces, underbody impacts.[2] Honda’s official determination was “no safety concerns.” In June 2022 the company quietly corrected the SRS ECU in production, and every 2023 Odyssey rolled off the line with the fix while 440,830 older models continued driving with hair-trigger airbags and no notification whatsoever.

NHTSA opened a preliminary evaluation in October 2025, eight years after the first injury report, and Honda finally declared a safety defect on April 2, 2026.[1] A class-action lawsuit followed in June, with plaintiffs citing repair bills between $3,000 and $11,000.[3] NHTSA closed its probe June 27 after confirming the recall addresses the defect.[4]

FARS data reveals who these vehicles serve. Among 337 tracked models, the Odyssey has the 12th-highest sober driver rate in fatal crashes: 84.6% tested clean for alcohol and drugs, placing it in the top 4% of all tracked vehicles.[5] Odyssey drivers are families doing the school run, hauling groceries, sitting in carpool lines. Eight hundred sixty-four occupants died between 2014 and 2023 at a rate of 0.93 per 100 million miles, low for its class, reflecting a careful population driving a careful vehicle with defective airbags positioned exactly where their children sit.

What to do right now

Check NHTSA recall 26V-248 at nhtsa.gov/recalls or enter your VIN at honda.com/recalls. The fix is a free SRS ECU reprogramming at any Honda dealer, and if your dealer declines coverage, document the refusal because that is exactly what class-action attorneys collect.

Limitations

Honda’s 0.1% affected-vehicle estimate is self-reported and unverified. FARS fatality data covers 2014–2023 and cannot isolate crashes where airbag malfunction contributed. Impairment rates proxy for driver demographics rather than directly measuring family use.

Strongest counterargument

Zero deaths have been linked to inadvertent deployment, Honda’s voluntary recall covers every affected model year, and the repair is free. Automakers routinely prioritize severity over speed when no fatalities exist, and Honda’s behavior fits that pattern. This holds on the no-deaths metric but collapses on timeline: if the software fix was ready by June 2022, every month after that was a choice to leave families exposed to a known hazard the company had already solved.
