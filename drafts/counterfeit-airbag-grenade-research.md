# Research Notes: Counterfeit Airbag Inflators

## Angle
NHTSA has identified DTN (Jilin Province Detiannuo Safety Technology Co.) counterfeit airbag inflators that have killed 10 people and injured 2 more in 12 "survivable" crashes. The inflators explode on deployment, sending metal shrapnel into drivers' chests, necks, eyes, and faces. All known incidents involve Chevy Malibu and Hyundai Sonata with salvage or rebuilt titles.

This is NOT just a Takata repeat. Takata was an OEM defect — every car shipped with a time bomb. DTN is a supply chain infiltration: counterfeit parts entering through collision repair shops, targeting the used car market's most vulnerable buyers.

## Novel Cross-Tabulation (Original Contribution)
Both the Malibu and Sonata are high-volume, high-crash vehicles in FARS:
- **Malibu:** 3,465 deaths, 5,469 fatal crashes, 1.49M fleet. Rate: 2.03/100M VMT. Lethality: 0.634 deaths/crash.
- **Sonata:** 1,887 deaths, 3,491 fatal crashes, 1.05M fleet. Rate: 1.56/100M VMT. Lethality: 0.541 deaths/crash.
- Combined: 5,352 deaths across 8,960 fatal crashes in a decade.

These are NOT exotic cars. They're among America's most common sedans — Malibu #5 in FARS sedan body count, Sonata in the top 15. Their "entry-level" status makes them disproportionately likely to end up as salvage-rebuilt vehicles. Budget buyers → salvage titles → counterfeit parts.

**Key calculation:** FARS captures 40,000+ fatalities annually. Frontal airbags save an estimated 50,000+ lives over 30 years (~1,700/year). DTN inflators have killed 10 in ~3 years. That sounds small — until you consider these are deaths in "survivable" crashes where the safety system itself became the weapon. It's a 100% lethality-on-deployment failure mode. Every DTN inflator that activates kills.
