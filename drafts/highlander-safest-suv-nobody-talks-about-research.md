# Research: The Toyota Highlander — Safest Popular SUV Nobody Talks About

## Journalist: Clara Rollover (Consumer Safety Advocate)
## Kicker: The Gap
## Date: 2026-07-04

## Core Finding
The Toyota Highlander has 2.1 million vehicles on American roads — a fleet larger than the Ford Explorer's. It has 1,106 FARS deaths (2014–2023). If it had the Explorer's fatality rate, it would have ~4,043 deaths. That's roughly 2,937 people who are alive because they (or the person who hit them) was driving a Highlander instead of an Explorer.

## FARS Data

### Toyota Highlander
- Deaths: 1,106 (annual: 110.6)
- Crashes: 2,434 (fatal crashes where a Highlander was involved)
- Fleet: 2,100,000
- VMT: 26,250 (estimated, 100M VMT)
- Rate: 0.42 per 100M VMT
- Crash lethality: 0.454 (45.4% of fatal crashes kill the Highlander occupant)

### Impairment (FARS Toxicology)
- Drivers in fatal crashes: 3,457
- Any impairment: 16.4% (LOWEST of any major SUV)
- Alcohol: 11.9%
- Drugs: 7.7%

### Model Year Deaths (remarkably stable)
2004: 49, 2005: 47, 2006: 52, 2007: 54, 2008: 46, 2009: 36, 2010: 66, 2011: 66, 2012: 73, 2013: 75, 2014: 36, 2015: 75, 2016: 46, 2017: 58, 2018: 51, 2019: 43

No spike. No "bad year." Just steady, low numbers.

## Original Analysis: Lives Saved Calculation
- At Explorer rate (1.54): expected deaths = 1.54 * 26250 / 10 = 4,043 → 2,937 lives saved
- At Grand Cherokee rate (0.51): expected 1,339 → 233 lives saved
- At segment-weighted average (~0.55): ~1,444 → ~338 lives saved
- Even vs the Pilot (0.29): Highlander rate is higher — the Pilot is actually safer per mile

## Strongest Counterargument
The Highlander's safety advantage may be partly demographic self-selection. Highlander buyers skew toward suburban families with higher household incomes and lower DUI risk. Its 16.4% impairment rate (vs Explorer's 19.5%) suggests driver behavior contributes to the safety gap. But the impairment gap (3.1 percentage points) can't explain the full rate gap (3.7x). Even impairment-adjusted, the Highlander is dramatically safer per mile.

## Limitations
- FARS only captures fatal crashes; injury rates may differ
- Fleet and VMT estimates carry ±15% uncertainty
- Rate differences between Highlander and Traverse/Pilot could narrow with more data
- Model year death counts aren't normalized by per-MY sales volume

## Actionable Takeaway
If you're shopping for a three-row SUV: the Highlander, Pilot, and Traverse are the safest bets in the FARS data. The Explorer has 3.7x the fatality rate of the Highlander. Check your VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls regardless of what you drive.
