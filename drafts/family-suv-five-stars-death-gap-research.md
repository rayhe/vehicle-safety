# Research: Family SUV Five Stars / Death Rate Gap

## Core Finding
The Ford Explorer has every safety credential — NHTSA 5-star overall, IIHS Top Safety Pick+ — and a FARS death rate of 1.54 per 100M VMT (3,797 deaths 2014-2023). The Kia Telluride, in the same segment, has a rate of 0.04 (31 deaths). That's a 38.5x gap.

## Toxicology (impairment rates in fatal crashes)
All within a tight 19-21% band except Highlander (16.4%). The death rate gap is NOT explained by impairment differences.

## This Week's Recalls
1. **Ford Explorer/Lincoln Aviator seat recall** — 387,911 vehicles (2020-2026 Explorer, 2020-2027 Aviator). Second-row easy-entry seats can unlatch, tip, or slide while driving. Expansion of prior Recall 25V393 (which was itself an expansion of a Field Service Action). 14 reports of seat movement while in drive. Ford identified issue ~1 year ago, tried supplier fix, it failed.
2. **Ford Explorer roof rail recall** — 288,314 vehicles (2016-2019). Roof rail covers can detach. Expansion of previous recall 21V316.
3. **Toyota Grand Highlander/Lexus TX axle recall** — 5,408 vehicles (2026). Rear axle carriers can break due to incomplete manufacturing process. No incidents reported.

## Safety Ratings vs. Reality
- Ford Explorer: NHTSA 5-star, IIHS TSP+, Good in all crash tests
- But: 3,797 deaths, rate 1.54, 676K vehicles recalled this week alone
- Honda Pilot: NHTSA 5-star, IIHS TSP+, Good in all crash tests
- Deaths: 514, rate 0.29 — 5.3x safer than Explorer on the road

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing crash test ratings with FARS real-world death rates for the same segment. The ratings are identical; the body count isn't. Crash tests measure barrier performance. FARS measures reality.

## Strongest Counterargument
Fleet age bias. The Explorer has been on sale since 1990; older models with less safety tech inflate its FARS numbers. The Telluride launched in 2020 — its fleet is young, small, and driven by a different demographic. The 38x gap likely overstates the true design difference. A fairer comparison would be same-model-year Explorer vs. Highlander (both have long production runs), where the gap narrows but doesn't close.

## Limitations
- FARS rate uses VMT estimates, not odometer readings (±15% uncertainty for low-volume models)
- Telluride/Palisade/Atlas fleets too new for statistically stable rates
- FARS counts all occupant deaths, not just those attributable to vehicle design
- Crash test ratings measure survivability in controlled impacts; FARS captures full real-world spectrum
