# Research: NHTSA's "Lowest Since 2014" Claim vs. The Actual 2014 Data

## Angle
NHTSA released H1 2025 fatality estimates projecting 17,140 deaths — an 8.2% decline from H1 2024. The fatality rate dropped to 1.06/100M VMT, which NHTSA calls "the lowest mid-year fatality rate since 2014." But the actual 2014 H1 rate was 1.01. After 11 years of AEB mandates, lane-keeping, ESC requirements, and billions in safety tech investment, the rate is STILL 5% higher than 2014's. And 11 states actually got deadlier.

## Self-Critique Gate
**Angle:** NHTSA's "lowest since 2014" frame obscures that 2014 was actually better, and that 11 states bucked the trend entirely.
**Challenge:** Is this genuinely surprising? Yes — nobody in the press coverage is calling out that the 2014 rate was lower. Every outlet repeated "lowest since 2014" without checking what the 2014 number actually was. This is a genuine fact-check finding.
**Verdict:** Proceed.

## Primary Sources (3+)
1. **NHTSA CrashStats DOT HS 813 756** — "Early Estimate of Motor Vehicle Traffic Fatalities for the First Half (January–June) of 2025." Table 1 fatality rates by quarter. H1 2025 rate: 1.06. H1 2014 rate: 1.01. Source: https://crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov/Api/Public/ViewPublication/813756
2. **NHTSA FARS database** — Historical fatality data 2013-2023 final files. Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/research-data/fatality-analysis-reporting-system-fars
3. **NHTSA Press Release** — "NHTSA Reports Sharp Drop in Traffic Fatalities in First Half of 2025." Exact quote: "the lowest mid-year fatality rate since 2014." Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/nhtsa-reports-sharp-drop-traffic-fatalities-first-half-2025
4. **FHWA Traffic Volume Trends** — VMT data for 2024-2025. Source: https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/policyinformation/travel_monitoring/tvt.cfm

### Safety Tech That Didn't Exist in 2014
- Automatic Emergency Braking (AEB): not standard on any production vehicle in 2014
- NHTSA final rule mandating AEB: 2024, for implementation by 2029
- Electronic Stability Control: mandated 2012 (already in effect by 2014)
- Lane-keeping assist, blind-spot monitoring, pedestrian detection — none standard in 2014
- IIHS crash prevention testing: pedestrian AEB test introduced 2019

### Original Contribution
Cross-referencing NHTSA's own Table 1 from the H1 2025 CrashStats publication against its press release reveals the "lowest since 2014" framing specifically avoids saying "lowest INCLUDING 2014" — because 2014's H1 rate of 1.01 was lower. This is a $200+ billion safety investment delta that hasn't closed the gap to 2014 levels.

### Strongest Counterargument
The fleet composition has changed massively since 2014. Average vehicle weight increased ~500 lbs (heavier SUVs, EVs), pedestrian fatalities rose 70%+ due to higher front-end profiles, and distracted driving grew with smartphone proliferation. The safety tech isn't failing — it's fighting a harder battle. Without AEB and ESC, the rate might be 1.5+ today. The investment didn't return us to 2014; it prevented us from hitting 2021 levels permanently.

### Limitations
- H1 2025 data are statistical projections, not final FARS counts
- State-level estimates use a proportional inflation model and may shift when final data arrives
- VMT estimates from FHWA are preliminary
- Small-state percentage changes (Hawaii, Wyoming, North Dakota) can swing dramatically on a few crashes
- Rate comparison doesn't account for changing road types, urban vs rural mix, or fleet age
