# Research: The "Kill Switch" Body Count

## Angle
Congress mandated alcohol detection technology in new cars (HALT Drunk Driving Act, Nov 2021). NHTSA missed its November 2024 deadline to publish a draft rule. Now GOP lawmakers are trying to defund the mandate before it exists, framing passive impairment detection as an "Orwellian kill switch." Meanwhile, the deaths the technology would prevent keep accumulating.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. The collision of hard FARS body-count data with the live political fight over the HALT Act is novel. Nobody has quantified the deaths during the rulemaking delay. The "kill switch" framing has gone viral on social media but the actual death toll is absent from the discourse. Original contribution: using FARS toxicology data to calculate the body count during Congressional inaction.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Drunk Driving Statistics (Official)
- **2024:** 11,904 alcohol-impaired driving deaths (BAC ≥ 0.08)
- **2023:** 12,429 deaths
- **2022:** 13,524 deaths  
- **2021:** 13,384 deaths
- ~30% of all traffic fatalities involve alcohol-impaired drivers
- 34 people die per day in drunk-driving crashes
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/risky-driving/drunk-driving

### 2. HALT Drunk Driving Act (Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, Nov 2021)
- Bipartisan law signed by Biden, November 2021
- Required NHTSA to develop safety standard for passive impaired driving detection in new vehicles
- Deadline: Draft rule by November 2024
- 5-year implementation window
- NO DRAFT PUBLISHED as of May 2026 — 18 months past deadline
- Technology: passive monitoring of driver performance, detect impairment, prevent/limit operation
- NOT a remote "kill switch"
- Source: Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, Sec. 24220 ("HALT Drunk Driving Act")

### 3. DADSS Research Program (Driver Alcohol Detection System for Safety)
- NHTSA/Automotive Coalition for Traffic Safety joint program
- Two technology approaches: touch-based tissue spectrometry + exhaled breath analysis
- Non-invasive, passive, designed for all drivers
- NHTSA 2021 study: **9,000 lives could be saved annually** with this technology
- Open-source licensing approved — Alliance for Automotive Innovation supports it
- October 2024 progress report submitted to Congress
- Source: https://dadss.org, NHTSA Countermeasures That Work

### 4. Congressional Opposition (April-May 2026)
- **Rep. Chip Roy (R-TX):** "direct threat to Fourth Amendment rights," introduced FISA amendment to defund
- **Rep. Thomas Massie (R-KY):** "judge, jury, executioner," calls mandate "Orwellian"
- Massie acknowledged "the technology doesn't exist" — fighting an undefined system

- Source: military.com reporting (April 29, 2026)

### 5. FARS Toxicology Data (Our Original Analysis)
- 490,736 drivers in fatal crashes (2014-2023)

## Strongest Counterargument
The technology genuinely doesn't exist in production form yet, and mandating undefined technology creates real engineering risk. False positives could strand sober drivers. The privacy concerns aren't entirely manufactured — any system that monitors driver behavior COULD theoretically be repurposed. The question is whether these valid engineering concerns justify killing the mandate entirely, or whether they argue for getting the rulemaking right.

## Limitations
- FARS toxicology data has known gaps: only ~38% of fatal crash drivers had known BAC results in 2021 (varies by state, 9%-81%). NHTSA uses statistical imputation to estimate totals.
- Our "body count during delay" assumes linear death rates; actual rates have been declining
- The 9,000 lives/year estimate assumes 100% fleet penetration, which would take 15-20 years
- DADSS technology performance in production vehicles is unproven
- Some alcohol-impaired crashes involve victims in other vehicles; detection in the impaired driver's car wouldn't prevent all of these
