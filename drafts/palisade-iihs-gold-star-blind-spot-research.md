# Research: IIHS Gave the Palisade Its Highest Award 68 Days After It Killed a Toddler

## Angle
IIHS's safety rating system has zero testing protocols for powered interior mechanisms that can injure or kill occupants outside of a crash. The 2026 Hyundai Palisade received IIHS Top Safety Pick+ on May 14, 2026 — exactly 68 days after its power-folding third-row seat killed 2-year-old Lucia Ayala in Akron, Ohio. The IIHS press release didn't mention the recall. The defect has now spread across 3 automakers and ~83,600 vehicles.

## Timeline (verified)
1. **August 2025**: Ashley Groussman warns Hyundai about power-folding seats trapping her child. Hyundai inspects, finds "system working as intended," closes case. (Source: Carscoops, News 5 Cleveland)
2. **March 7, 2026**: 2-year-old Lucia Ayala killed by power-folding third-row seat in 2026 Hyundai Palisade, Akron, OH. (Source: Akron PD, multiple outlets)
3. **~March 13, 2026**: Hyundai stops sales of Palisade, recalls ~68,500 vehicles (Limited and Calligraphy trims). (Source: Reuters, NHTSA)
4. **Late March 2026**: Kia recalls 568 Telluride Hybrid SX Prestige/X-Line with same power seat system. (Source: Carscoops, NHTSA)
5. **Late March 2026**: GM engineer reports Vistiq has same issue through GM's Speak Up For Safety (SUFS) program, triggered by Hyundai recall. (Source: Jalopnik, NHTSA recall filing)
6. **May 14, 2026**: IIHS awards 2026 Hyundai Palisade **Top Safety Pick+** — the industry's highest safety award. Only Palisades built after Nov 2025 qualify. IIHS press release does NOT mention the power seat recall. (Source: iihs.org)
7. **June 8, 2026**: GM halts shipments of 2027 Cadillac Vistiq.
8. **July 1, 2026**: GM recalls all 14,540 Cadillac Vistiqs. 100% defect rate. 6 prior incidents dating to May 2025. (Source: Jalopnik, Electrek, NHTSA)

## Key Numbers
- 68 days between child's death and IIHS's highest award
- 3 automakers affected (Hyundai, Kia, GM)
- ~83,608 vehicles recalled for same class of defect (68,500 + 568 + 14,540)
- 100% defect rate on all Cadillac Vistiqs
- 1 death, 1 injury (reported)
- 6 Vistiq incidents/complaints dating to May 2025 (before the Palisade death)
- GM's 6 prior incidents mean they HAD data about this before Hyundai's child died — but it took the death to prompt action

## IIHS Testing Categories (what they DO test)
- Small overlap front crash
- Moderate overlap front crash (updated for rear-seat dummy)
- Side crash
- Headlights
- Front crash prevention: vehicle-to-vehicle 2.0
- Front crash prevention: pedestrian
- Seat belt reminders
- LATCH ease of use

## What IIHS Does NOT Test
- Power-folding seat entrapment/anti-pinch
- Power window/sunroof auto-reverse
- Power liftgate entrapment
- Any powered mechanism that can trap/injure occupants
- Non-crash interior hazards of any kind

## The "Contagion Recall" Pattern
GM explicitly said it launched its investigation after "a recall by a different vehicle manufacturer." They didn't proactively test their own power seats despite having 6 complaints dating back to May 2025. The death of a child in a competitor's vehicle is what finally triggered action. This reveals that automakers don't systematically cross-check their own products against known failure modes in similar systems across the industry.

## Original Insight
GM had 6 complaints about Vistiq power seat issues between May 2025 and June 2026. The first complaint predates the Palisade death by 10 months. Yet GM only investigated after Hyundai's recall — not after their own customer reports. The "contagion" pathway (competitor death → internal investigation → recall) exposed a systemic failure in how automakers process their own complaint data.
