# Research: Ford Ranger/Bronco Seat Bolt Recall + FARS Exposure

## Hook (Breaking, May 1, 2026)
Ford recalled 179,698 2024-2026 Rangers and Broncos (NHTSA recall 26V-330) for a loose bolt in the front seat frame. A seat that can't hold you in place defeats the purpose of every other safety system in the truck.

## Primary Sources
1. **NHTSA recall 26V-330** — Ford Ranger and Bronco (2024-2026), front seat frame bolt may be loose. Dealers replace bolt and pivot link at no cost. Source: NHTSA.gov, multiple news reports (Ford Authority, AP via local radio, May 1 2026).
2. **FARS 2014-2023** — Ford Ranger: 3,089 deaths, 4,476 fatal crashes, death rate 2.91/100M VMT. Fleet: 787,500. Overrepresentation index: 3.52x (0.46% of fleet, 1.62% of deaths).
3. **NHTSA recall completion rates** — 76% for vehicles 1-5 years old, 56% for 6-10 year old. GAO report GAO-24-106356 identified five factors influencing repair rates. AAA estimates 25% of recalled vehicles remain unrepaired nationally.
4. **FARS model year data for Ranger** — Peak: MY2003 (262 deaths), MY1999/2000 (258 each). New gen: MY2019 (25), MY2020 (11), MY2021 (10), MY2022 (5). Total for new gen: 51 deaths. Massive improvement.

## Novel Contribution
**Recall exposure gap calculation:**
- 179,698 vehicles recalled
- ~76% completion rate for 1-5 year old vehicles (NHTSA data)
- = ~136,571 fixed, ~43,127 NOT fixed
- Average annual VMT per truck: ~13,500 miles (NHTS survey, pickup trucks)
- Unrepaired exposure: 43,127 vehicles × 13,500 miles = ~582 million unrepaired vehicle-miles per YEAR
- At the Ranger's historical crash involvement rate (crashes/VMT), some fraction of these miles will involve fatal crashes where the seat might not restrain the occupant
- This is NOT saying the seat bolt WILL cause a death. It's saying the engineering margin was already thin for the Ranger, and now Ford is saying one of the bolts holding the driver in place might not be tight.

## The Deeper Issue
The seat is pre-regulatory. Before FMVSS 208 (airbags), before ESC mandates, before AEB requirements, the seat existed. It's the zero-th safety system. Getting the seat wrong in 2024-2026 model years — the same vehicles that might have AEB, lane keep assist, and adaptive cruise — is like building a hospital and forgetting to install a floor.

## Counterargument (Required)
- No injuries or crashes reported related to this defect (Ford says)
- The bolt may be loose but not yet failed in any vehicle
- New-gen Ranger (2019+) has dramatically better safety (51 deaths vs 3,089 total historical)
- The recall is proactive — Ford identified the issue before incidents occurred
- Recall completion rates for new vehicles (76%) are far better than the fleet average

## Limitations
- FARS data is historical (2014-2023) dominated by old-gen Rangers (pre-2012)
- New-gen Ranger barely has FARS data (only 51 deaths across 4 model years)
- We cannot attribute any FARS deaths to seat bolt failures specifically
- The 76% completion rate is an average; Ford's actual rate may differ
- VMT estimates for the Ranger fleet carry ±15% uncertainty

## Actionable Takeaway
If you drive a 2024-2026 Ford Ranger or Bronco: check your VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls. Schedule the repair. It's free and takes under an hour. Don't be in the 24% that never shows up.
