# Research Notes: Wrangler Seven-Year Crash Test Tipping Saga

## Angle
The Jeep Wrangler tipped onto its side during IIHS small overlap crash tests THREE TIMES over seven years (2019, 2022, 2026 models). Stellantis called it "anomalous" each time and needed three rounds of frame rail modifications to fix it. Even now, the 2026 model only earns "acceptable" (not "good"). Every Wrangler built before October 2025 — millions of vehicles — still has the pre-fix frame rail.

## Novel Contribution
1. **Exposure calculation:** Wrangler sold ~190,000-250,000 units/year in US (2019-2025). That's roughly 1.3-1.75 million Wranglers sold during the tipping era (2019-Oct 2025) that will NEVER get the frame rail fix because it's a design change, not a recall.
2. **FARS cross-reference:** 1,842 people died in Wranglers over 2014-2023. The tipping behavior compounds rollover risk in the exact crash scenario (small overlap frontal) that real drivers encounter. No way to isolate what fraction of those deaths involved tipping dynamics, but the vehicle's removable roof/doors and lack of side curtain airbags mean any tipping event is disproportionately lethal.
3. **The "anomalous" defense:** FCA/Stellantis called it anomalous after THREE separate tests showed the same behavior. This is a case study in manufacturer denial.

## Counterargument (stated at full strength)
Stellantis is correct that IIHS lab conditions (rigid barrier, 25% overlap at precisely 40 mph) don't perfectly replicate real-world crashes. Real-world small overlap impacts involve deformable objects (trees, poles, other vehicles) at varying speeds and angles, which could produce different dynamics. The Wrangler's body-on-frame construction is inherently more prone to center-of-gravity effects in offset impacts, and its high ground clearance — the same feature that makes it capable off-road — raises the CG above what unibody crossovers present. It's possible that most real-world Wrangler small overlap impacts don't produce tipping. The FARS data doesn't isolate rollover-from-frontal-impact events from other rollover causes.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers 2014-2023, spanning model years that include pre-JL (JK generation) Wranglers. The tipping issue was identified on the JL (2018+), so the full FARS dataset includes older-generation vehicles.
- Cannot isolate small overlap frontal impacts from FARS aggregate data
- Cannot determine what fraction of FARS Wrangler deaths involved tipping or rollover dynamics specifically from frontal impacts
- Wrangler sales figures are approximate (based on industry reporting, not official registration data)

## Actionable Advice
- If you own a 2018-2025 Wrangler built BEFORE October 2025: your frame rail has not been modified. There is no recall. In the specific scenario tested by IIHS (25% driver-side overlap at 40 mph), your vehicle may tip.
- Always use doors, roof, and seat belts — the tipping risk is most dangerous with removable components off.
- If shopping: the 2026 Wrangler (built after Oct 2025) has the fix, earning "acceptable." The Ford Bronco passed the same test without tipping.
- Check VIN build date with your dealer if buying a "2026" Wrangler — only those built after October 2025 have the modification.
