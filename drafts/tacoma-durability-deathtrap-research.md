# Research: The Toyota Tacoma Durability Deathtrap

## Angle
Toyota Tacoma is America's most beloved midsize truck — cult following, legendary resale value, "the truck that runs forever." But FARS data reveals a dark irony: the Tacoma's famous durability is killing people. Because old Tacomas never die mechanically, they stay on the road decades past their safety expiration date. **71% of Tacoma deaths involve pre-2010 model years.** The average Tacoma in a fatal crash is a 2006 model — 17+ years old during the data window.

## Kill Test
- **Genuinely newsworthy?** YES. The Tacoma is the #1 midsize truck in America (300K+ annual sales). It has a death rate of 0.80 — nearly 3x the Chevy Colorado (0.28), 3.3x the Honda Ridgeline (0.24). Nobody talks about this because everyone assumes Toyota = safe.
- **Novel contribution?** YES. Cross-tabulating the Tacoma's model year death distribution against its competitors reveals that durability is a safety LIABILITY — the thing people love most about the Tacoma is what's killing them. This is a paradox nobody has quantified.
- **After 137 articles, is this surprising?** YES. We've never done a Tacoma article, and the "durability as danger" thesis is genuinely counterintuitive.

### Tacoma Model Year Deaths
- Peak: MY 2006 (156 deaths), MY 2004 (152), MY 2003 (150)
- Steep decline: MY 2014 (42), MY 2019 (50), MY 2020 (35)
- Newer Tacomas (2015+) average ~50 deaths/year — much safer

### Toxicology (Impairment)
- Tacoma: 14.6% alcohol, 8.4% drug, 19.4% any — BELOW segment average
- Segment average: ~15.1% alcohol, ~8.7% drug, ~20.0% any
- The Tacoma's problem is NOT its drivers — impairment is lowest in segment

### Key Insight: Lethality Paradox
- Tacoma lethality (52.3%) is HIGHER than the larger Tundra (41.5%)
- In a fatal crash, you're more likely to die in a Tacoma than a Tundra
- The Tundra's extra mass protects its occupants; the Tacoma has midsize mass with old-vehicle structural weakness

## External Sources

1. **IIHS:** 2024 Tacoma crew cab earned Top Safety Pick — but only the NEW generation. Pre-2016 Tacomas predate ESC requirements (2012) and lack any ADAS.
2. **NHTSA:** Frame rust recall affected 2005-2011 Tacomas. Toyota offered frame replacements/buybacks. But many pre-2005 Tacomas with rust frames are still being driven.
3. **Resale value:** Tacoma retains ~80% of value after 5 years (highest in segment). This means 15-year-old Tacomas sell for $15K+, keeping them economically viable to drive indefinitely.
4. **ESC:** Federal ESC mandate took effect September 2011 (MY 2012). 48% of Tacoma deaths are pre-2005 models — none had ESC. 71% are pre-2010 — most lacked ESC.

## Novel Contribution
**The "Durability Tax" calculation:** If the Tacoma had the Colorado's fleet age profile (avgMY 2011.3 vs 2006.1), its death rate would be dramatically lower. I can estimate how many of the 2,274 deaths are attributable to the old fleet — roughly 1,089 deaths (48%) come from pre-2005 models that wouldn't exist in a Colorado-like fleet age distribution. The Tacoma's legendary durability costs approximately 109 extra lives per year.

## Strongest Counterargument
The Colorado only launched in 2004 (current gen 2015); the Tacoma has been sold continuously since 1995. Of course the Tacoma has more old vehicles on the road — it had more model years in production. The fair comparison is only post-2015 Tacomas vs post-2015 Colorados. 

## Limitations
- FARS captures only fatal crashes — Tacoma might perform better in injury-only crashes
- VMT estimates use average annual mileage assumptions; off-road-heavy Tacomas may drive fewer highway miles, inflating their rate
- The rate includes all people killed in crashes involving the vehicle, not just occupants
- Fleet size estimates may undercount surviving old Tacomas due to their unusual longevity
