# Research: SUV Deaths — Only Category to Increase in 2024

## Thesis
The 2024 FARS annual data (released April 2026) reveals that SUV occupant deaths were the ONLY vehicle category to increase (+112, +1.6%) while every other category — cars, pickups, vans, motorcycles, pedestrians, cyclists — decreased. The national 4.3% decline masks a divergent SUV trend. America's favorite vehicle type is the only one getting worse.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: NHTSA 2024 FARS Annual Report (DOT HS 813 791, April 2026)
- **Total 2024 fatalities: 39,254** (down 4.3% from 41,025 in 2023)
- **Fatality rate: 1.19 per 100M VMT** (down 5.6% from 1.26 in 2023)
- **VMT: 3,294 billion** (up 1.5% from 3,247B in 2023)
- URL: https://crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov/Api/Public/ViewPublication/813791

#### Alcohol-Impaired SUV Drivers in Fatal Crashes:
- 2023: 2,641 (19% of all SUV drivers in fatal crashes)
- 2024: 2,624 (19%)
- Change: -17 (-0.6%)
- **Key finding:** SUV death increase is NOT driven by impairment. Alcohol-impaired SUV drivers actually decreased slightly.

### Source 2: NHTSA 2025 Traffic Death Estimates
- **2025 estimated fatalities: 36,640** (down 6.7% from 2024)
- **2025 fatality rate: 1.10** (second-lowest in recorded history)
- Fatalities decreased in 39 states, DC, and Puerto Rico
- URL: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/traffic-deaths-2025-early-estimates-2024-annual

### Source 3: NSC 2025 Preliminary Estimates
- NSC estimates 37,810 deaths in 2025 (-12% from 2024)
- 8 states saw increases: Hawaii (+25%), Wyoming (+12%), Kansas (+10%), NM (+8%), Idaho (+7%), LA (+5%), VT (+5%), CO (+3%)
- California alone: -40%, 2,017 fewer deaths

### Source 4: FARS per-model data (2014-2023, fars_output.js)
- SUV class: 46,442 deaths across 121 models
- Average rate: 5.47 per 100M VMT
- Crash:death ratio: 1.91 (vs 1.55 for sedans, 2.05 for pickups)
- Within-SUV rate range: 0.19 (RAV4) to 7.83 (Tracker) — 41x gap

### Source 5: 2024 FARS demographic data
- Older driver (65+) fatalities: INCREASED by 115 (+1.4%)
- This is the only driver age group to increase
- Correlation: older drivers increasingly drive SUVs

## Novel Cross-Tabulation
The 2024 FARS category data + FARS per-model data reveals:
1. SUVs are the only vehicle type where both deaths (+1.6%) AND injuries (+4.2%) increased in 2024
2. The SUV injury increase (+4.2%) exceeds VMT growth (+1.5%) — this is not just "more driving"
3. Alcohol-impaired SUV drivers decreased, meaning the increase is sober crashes
4. The correlation with older driver increases suggests demographics are shifting danger within the SUV fleet

## Counterargument (at full strength)
SUV registrations are growing faster than any other segment. If SUV fleet size grew by ~3-4% in 2024, then a 1.6% increase in deaths represents a per-vehicle improvement. The argument: SUVs aren't getting more dangerous — there are just more of them, and the per-unit death rate is actually declining.

Rebuttal: Even if true, this means SUV fleet growth is eating the safety gains that every other category is achieving. Cars dropped 9.7% in deaths. If SUVs traded places at scale with cars, the national death count would be falling faster. The "SUVs are safer than they used to be" argument doesn't change the fact that they're the one category adding bodies.

Also: injuries grew 4.2%, which likely exceeds even fleet growth. And the 2024 data showed pickup deaths dropped 4.9% despite pickups also being a growing segment. The SUV anomaly isn't just fleet math.

## Limitations
- 2024 FARS is the Annual Reporting File (ARF), not the Final File — some data may be revised
- SUV category includes everything from a Chevy Trax to a Chevy Tahoe — a massive range
- No per-model 2024 breakdown available yet (only category-level)
- VMT growth by vehicle type not available — can't calculate per-vehicle-mile rate change for SUVs specifically
- Older driver correlation is plausible but not proven causal
