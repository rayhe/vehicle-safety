# Research: Toyota V35A Engine — Three Recall Waves, 270K Engines

## Angle
Toyota's V35A twin-turbo V6 engine has been recalled in three expanding waves totaling ~270,000 vehicles. Some engines in the second wave were built AFTER Toyota claimed to have fixed the manufacturing process. Cross-reference with FARS data showing the Tundra as one of the safer full-size pickups — a safety record the V35A now threatens.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Recall Campaigns
- **24V381000** (May 30, 2024): 102,092 vehicles — 2022-2023 Tundra (98,568) + Lexus LX600 (3,524)
  - Toyota campaign: 24TB07/24TA07/24LB04/24LA04
  - Issue: machining debris contaminating main bearings → engine seizure/stall
  - Remedy: FULL ENGINE REPLACEMENT (not repair)
  - Production dates: Tundra Jul 30 2021–Nov 25 2022; LX Nov 2 2021–Feb 13 2023
  - Toyota stated: engines after this period had "new or improved processes that better clear machining debris"
  
- **25V767000** (Nov 2025): 126,691 vehicles — 2022-2024 Tundra + Lexus LX + 2024 Lexus GX
  - Toyota campaign: 25TB14/25TA14/25LB07/25LA07
  - EXPANDS 24V381 — same defect but in vehicles built AFTER the supposed fix
  - Production dates: Tundra Nov 22 2021–Feb 14 2024 (15 months past original cutoff)
  - GX added (different V35A tune)
  - Remedy: STILL "under development" as of Dec 2025

- **May 2026**: ~44,000 additional Tundras (per CarBuzz, The Drive reporting)
  - Third expansion of the same fundamental defect
  - Total across all waves: ~270,000+ vehicles

### 2. FARS Data — Toyota Tundra
- **Overall:** 1,223 deaths (2014-2023), rate 0.94/100M VMT, fleet ~962,500
- **Pickup comparison (rate/100M VMT):**
  - Ranger: 2.91 | Frontier: 1.45 | Silverado: 1.25 | F-150: 1.04 | Sierra: 1.01 | **Tundra: 0.94** | Tacoma: 0.80 | Ram: 0.78 | Colorado: 0.28
- **Model year deaths (V8 era trend):** 2007:58, 2008:49, 2009:9, 2010:39, 2011:28, 2012:39, 2013:43, 2014:43, 2015:28, 2016:26, 2017:12, 2018:25, 2019:21, 2020:17, 2021:8
- **Tox:** 19.6% impairment (14.7% alc, 8.6% drug) — lower than segment average
- Tundra is the 4th-safest full-size pickup by rate in FARS

### 3. The Drive Podcast (June 3, 2026)

## Novel Contribution
1. **The timeline betrayal:** Wave 2 proves the manufacturing fix failed — engines built Nov 2022–Feb 2024 (post-claimed-fix) have the same defect
2. **FARS safety ranking at risk:** Tundra was 4th-safest full-size pickup (rate 0.94). Engine seizure at highway speed introduces a risk vector that no crash structure can protect against.
3. **Cost calculation:** 102,092 engine replacements × ~$10K = $1B+ for wave 1 alone. Across all waves at full replacement: ~$2.7B
4. **V8 → V6 transition created the problem:** The 5.7L i-FORCE V8 (2007-2021) built the safety record. The V35A replacement destroyed it.
