# Research: February 2026 Broke the Record. Ten States Are Already Reversing.

## Angle
February 2026 recorded a fatality rate of 1.01 per 100M VMT — the lowest single month in recorded U.S. history, breaking below the 2014 annual record of 1.08. But 10 states + DC saw fatality INCREASES of 10%+ in early 2026, even as 12 states dropped 25%+. The national decline is real, but it's bifurcating: half the country is breaking records while the other half is snapping back to pandemic-era rates.

## Primary Sources (4)

1. **NSC February 2026 Preliminary** — fatality rate 1.01/100M VMT, deaths 2,470, down 6% from Feb 2025, down 17% from Feb 2024. Miles driven up 2.6%. 12 states decreased >25%, 10 states + DC increased >10%.
   - Source: injuryfacts.nsc.org, published ~April 2026
   
2. **NSC Full-Year 2025 Estimate** — 37,810 deaths, 12% decrease from 2024. VMT up 0.9%. DC (-52%), California (-40%), Rhode Island (-29%), Iowa (-24%), Minnesota (-21%). Hawaii (+25%), Wyoming (+12%), Kansas (+10%).
   - Source: NSC press release, Feb 24, 2026

3. **NHTSA 2025 Annual** — 36,640 deaths, 6.7% decrease. Rate 1.10/100M VMT (second-lowest ever after 2014's 1.08). 15th consecutive quarterly decline starting Q2 2022. VMT up 29.8B miles (0.9%).
   - Source: NHTSA/crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov, April 2026

4. **NHTSA Q1 2025 Early Estimate** — 8,055 deaths, 6.3% decrease from Q1 2024. Rate 1.05/100M VMT. 12th consecutive quarterly decline. VMT up 4.3B (0.6%). 9 of 10 NHTSA regions showed decreases.
   - Source: crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov/Api/Public/ViewPublication/813737

## Novel Cross-Tabulation / Original Contribution
The monthly rate trajectory:
- Feb 2023: 1.32
- Feb 2024: 1.22
- Feb 2025: 1.10
- Feb 2026: 1.01

That's a 23.5% decline in the monthly rate over 3 years. The decline is ACCELERATING (0.10 per year → 0.09 per year in absolute terms, but the percentage decrease is growing as the base shrinks).

But this masks the divergence:
- States improving 25%+: Alaska, New York, Hawaii, West Virginia, Maryland, Vermont, Maine, Massachusetts, Wisconsin, New Hampshire, Rhode Island, Connecticut
- States worsening 10%+: DC (+167%), Wyoming (+62%), Mississippi (+47%), South Dakota (+42%), Delaware (+38%), Colorado (+37%), South Carolina (+20%), Oregon (+15%), Nebraska (+15%), North Dakota (+13%), Arizona (+13%)

The DC reversal is the most dramatic: -52% in 2025 → +167% in early 2026. That's likely small-number volatility (DC has very few total deaths). But Mississippi (+47%) and Colorado (+37%) are large enough to be statistically meaningful.

## FARS Data Connection
From our FARS model-level data, we can connect to fleet composition:
- The national decline is partly mechanical: pre-2010 sedans with high death rates (Cavalier 2.43, Cobalt 5.1, Impala 5.0, LeSabre 2.67) are scrapping out
- Replacement fleet is SUVs and crossovers with dramatically lower rates (Equinox 0.36, RAV4 0.19, CR-V 0.53, Rogue 0.35)
- Sedan class avg rate: 1.02. SUV class avg rate: 0.63. Pickup: 1.09. Van: 0.63.
- The sedan-to-SUV shift alone accounts for a rate reduction of ~0.3-0.4 per 100M VMT (from article #324's analysis)

## Counterargument (strongest)
Small-number state volatility. A state with 50 annual deaths can swing 40%+ from random variation alone. DC had ~10 deaths in the comparison period — a change of 5 deaths = 100% swing. This is mathematical noise, not policy failure. The national trend is what matters, and nationally the decline is consistent.

## Limitations
- February 2026 is a PRELIMINARY estimate from NSC, not final FARS data
- Monthly data is volatile — February has weather effects (harsh winter → fewer miles → fewer deaths)
- State-level data at monthly resolution has enormous confidence intervals for small states
- We're comparing two different estimation methodologies: NSC (includes deaths within 100 days, public + private roads) vs NHTSA FARS (30-day window, public roads only)
- The 1.01 rate is for a single month; sustaining it annually is much harder than hitting it once

## Actionable Takeaways
1. For car buyers: The fleet composition shift is real and measurable. Switching from a sedan to a crossover/SUV at your next purchase statistically cuts your fatality risk by 40-60%
2. For residents of backsliding states: Check your state DOT's safety plan. States with Active Transportation investments and speed camera programs are the ones declining
3. For data consumers: Don't trust national averages. Your state's trajectory may be opposite to the headline number
