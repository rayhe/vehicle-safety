# Research: Senior Zero Improvement 2025

## Angle
NHTSA's 2025 full-year sub-category estimates (DOT HS 813 829, July 2026) show every age group's traffic fatalities declined — except people 65 and older, who saw a 0% change. This is the most striking divergence in the entire report: while the nation celebrated a 6.7% decline and a near-record-low fatality rate, seniors were completely left behind.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: NHTSA Early Estimates of Motor Vehicle Traffic Fatalities and Fatality Rate by Sub-Categories in 2025 (DOT HS 813 829, July 2026)
- URL: https://crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov/Api/Public/ViewPublication/813829
- Total 2025 deaths: 36,640 (down 6.7% from 39,254 in 2024)
- Rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT (second-lowest ever)

**Age group changes 2024→2025:**
- <15: 1,037 → 918 (-12%)
- 15-24: 6,658 → 6,113 (-8%)
- 25-34: 6,956 → 6,065 (-13%)
- 35-44: 6,283 → 5,715 (-9%)
- 45-54: 4,988 → 4,672 (-6%)
- 55-64: 5,272 → 5,117 (-3%)
- **65+: 8,060 → 8,039 (-0%)**

**Key proportion shift:** 65+ went from 20.5% of all traffic deaths in 2024 to 21.9% in 2025. In some months (Nov, Dec 2025), seniors accounted for 24% of all traffic deaths.

### Source 2: NHTSA Early Estimates Q1 2026 + 2025 Full Year (DOT HS 813 800, July 2026)
- URL: https://crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov via Reuters coverage
- 2025 full year: 36,640 deaths, 1.10 fatality rate
- Q1 2026: 7,770 deaths, 0.99 rate (lowest since 2014)
- Reuters: https://www.reuters.com (David Shepardson, July 8, 2026)

### Source 3: IIHS fatality statistics on older drivers
- URL: https://www.iihs.org/topics/fatality-statistics/detail/older-people
- IIHS has documented that per-mile crash fatality rates increase starting at age 70, sharply after 75
- Fragility is the primary factor — similar crashes produce worse outcomes in older bodies

## Original Analysis

### Excess deaths calculation
If the 65+ group had matched the national -6.7% decline:
- Expected 2025 deaths: 8,060 × 0.933 = 7,520
- Actual: 8,039
- **Excess senior deaths: ~519**

If they'd matched the 25-34 group's -13% decline:
- Expected: 8,060 × 0.87 = 7,012
- **Excess: ~1,027**

### Share growth
- 2024: 8,060 / 39,254 = 20.5%
- 2025: 8,039 / 36,640 = 21.9%
- Growth: +1.4 percentage points in one year
- At this rate, seniors will account for 25% of all traffic deaths within 2-3 years

### Why didn't improvements help seniors?
1. **Newer vehicles (-12% for <10yr)** — should help everyone. But seniors may be disproportionately driving older vehicles. OR newer vehicle designs are tested with 50th-percentile male dummies, not elderly body types.
2. **Seatbelt compliance (-13% for unrestrained)** — seniors already have high belt-use rates. Limited room for improvement.
3. **Speeding (-11%)** — seniors speed less. Not their problem. So the speeding decline doesn't directly benefit them.
4. **Fragility** — this is the core issue. A crash at 35 mph that gives a 30-year-old a concussion kills a 75-year-old. Same vehicle, same crash test rating, different outcome. Crash test dummies don't age.
5. **Growing population** — more seniors driving = more exposure. But the flat NUMBER (not rate) means the per-capita improvement is slightly positive. Still, it's the worst of any age group by far.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES.
- Novel angle: cross-cutting the brand-new NHTSA 2025 sub-category data to isolate the one age group that got zero benefit from a historic safety improvement year
- Original calculation: 519 excess senior deaths quantified
- Counterintuitive: you'd expect safety tech to help everyone equally. It doesn't.
- Actionable: check your elderly parents' cars, understand that crash ratings don't account for fragility
