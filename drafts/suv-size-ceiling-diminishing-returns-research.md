# Research: The Weight Ceiling — Why Full-Size SUVs Aren't Protecting You

## Angle
FARS data cross-tabulated by SUV size tier independently confirms the IIHS "supersizing" study (2025): going bigger doesn't make you safer past a point. In fact, the biggest SUVs kill their OWN occupants at dramatically higher rates than compact SUVs.

## Novel Contribution
Original FARS computation: death rate per 100K fleet by SUV size tier.
- **Compact SUVs** (RAV4, Rogue, CR-V, etc.): 43.3 deaths per 100K fleet (23 models, 25.9M fleet, 11,227 deaths)
- **Midsize SUVs** (Explorer, Highlander, Wrangler, etc.): 79.0 per 100K (19 models, 18.2M fleet, 14,413 deaths)
- **Full-size SUVs** (Tahoe, Expedition, Yukon, etc.): 253.0 per 100K (10 models, 4.1M fleet, 10,292 deaths)

**5.8x death rate gap from compact to full-size.** The conventional wisdom that bigger = safer doesn't hold.

## Key Data Points
- Compact SUV avg rate: 0.33 (fleet-weighted)
- Midsize SUV avg rate: 0.79
- Full-size SUV avg rate: 2.53
- Fleet-wide average: 111.5 per 100K

- Safest SUVs: Kia Seltos (0.04), Subaru Crosstrek (0.08), Mazda CX-5 (0.12)
- Deadliest "safe" SUVs: Toyota Land Cruiser (6.27!), Chevy Trailblazer (2.83), GMC Yukon (2.55), Chevy Tahoe (2.49)

## Important Counterargument: Vehicle Age Confound
Full-size SUV category includes older body-on-frame vehicles (Trailblazer 2002-2009, Envoy 2002-2009) that inflate the rates. Newer full-size SUVs (Durango 0.54, Armada 0.55) perform much better. This actually SUPPORTS the thesis: modern safety tech (ESC, better airbags, crash structures) matters more than raw mass.

## IIHS Supersizing Study (Jan 2025)
- Above ~4,000 lbs (fleet average), additional weight provides "hardly any decrease in risk for occupants"
- BUT adding 500 lbs to above-average vehicles increases crash-partner death rates
- For pickups >4,000 lbs: +500 lbs only reduces own driver death rate by 1 per million RVY, but increases partner death rate by 7
- David Harkey: "Choosing an extra-heavy vehicle doesn't make you any safer, but it makes you a bigger danger to other people"
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/supersizing-vehicles-offers-minimal-safety-benefits--but-substantial-dangers

## Limitations
1. FARS only captures fatal crashes (not injury crashes)
2. Vehicle age confound: full-size SUVs have older fleet mix
3. Fleet estimates use VMT approximations (±15% for low-volume)
4. Death rate is per 100M VMT estimated, not actual odometer data
5. Does not separate occupant vs non-occupant deaths
6. Driver demographics (age, income, driving patterns) differ by SUV tier
