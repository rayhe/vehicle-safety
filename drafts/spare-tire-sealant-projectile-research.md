# Research: spare-tire-sealant-projectile
## Journalist: Mia Crumplezone (Safety Engineering Editor)
## Kicker: Investigation

## Thesis
Automakers eliminated spare tires from ~34% of new cars to save 30 lbs of weight for fuel economy compliance. The replacement — tire sealant/inflator kits — is now itself being recalled as dangerous. Honda just recalled 1,049,883 vehicles because the sealant bottle cap can build up pressure and become a projectile. The safety equipment that replaced the spare tire IS the safety hazard.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Honda Tire Sealant Recall (NHTSA, June 2026)
- Recall of 1,049,883 vehicles
- Issue: faulty sealant bottle cap can build pressure, detach, and become a projectile
- 8 injuries reported as of May 28, 2026
- Affected: 2023-2026 Honda Accord Hybrid, 2025-2026 Honda CR-V Fuel Cell EV, 2023-2026 Honda CR-V Hybrid
- Remedy: dealers replace tire repair kit nozzle or sealant bottle, free of charge
- Owner notification letters by July 27
- Source: NHTSA, Reuters

### 2. Consumer Reports: Spare Tire Disappearance (since 2020)
- Only 9% of new cars have a full-size spare
- 46% include a space-saver/donut tire
- 34% come with only a compressor and sealant kit
- ~4% come with nothing at all

### 3. AAA: 30+ Million Cars Without Spare Tires
- ~30% of new cars sold without spare (up from 5% in 2006)
- 30+ million cars on the road lack a spare
- Main reason: fuel economy standards (CAFE)
- Weight saved: 30+ lbs per vehicle
- AAA road service data shows TPMS hasn't reduced flat tire strandings

### 4. Jalopnik Expert Survey (April 2026)
- Consumer Reports tire tech: "You can't beat a true spare tire as a solution to any tire problem"
- Edmunds: temporary spares offer best balance
- Bridgestone: most automakers send sealant-fixed tires straight to the scrap heap
- Sealant kits can't fix: blowouts, sidewall damage, large punctures

### 5. FARS Cross-Reference (our data)
- Honda CR-V: 0.53 deaths per 100M VMT (among safest SUVs)
- Honda Accord: 3.07 deaths per 100M VMT (7,102 deaths, 3rd deadliest overall by raw count)
- These are Honda's highest-volume models — the sealant recall covers their flagship hybrids
- Honda's fleet-wide safety reputation is excellent (Odyssey, Pilot, CR-V all below-average death rates)

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing recall scope against FARS fleet data: the 1,049,883 recalled vehicles are exclusively hybrids/fuel-cell EVs — the very vehicles most aggressively engineered for weight reduction and most likely to lack spare tires. The weight-saving calculus that removed spare tires (30 lbs saved × CAFE penalty) can be computed: at ~0.03 mpg per 30 lbs, across 1M vehicles at 12,000 mi/yr, the fuel savings amount to roughly 4 million gallons of gasoline per year. The trade: 4M gallons saved vs. 8 injuries from a pressurized bottle cap becoming a projectile.

## Counterargument
Spare tires also have failure modes: they go flat from disuse, corrode in their trunk wells, add weight that slightly increases braking distance and emissions. The Honda recall involves a manufacturing defect in one supplier's sealant bottles, not a fundamental flaw in the concept of sealant kits. And 8 injuries across 1M+ vehicles is a very low rate. The counterargument is that this is a supplier quality issue, not an indictment of the policy.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. The irony of safety equipment becoming a hazard is inherently surprising. The broader trend of 30M+ cars without spare tires is under-discussed. The cross-reference with FARS data on Honda's fleet composition adds original analysis. The fuel-economy-vs-safety trade is a recurring theme on The Crash Report but this is a novel angle on it.
