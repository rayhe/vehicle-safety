# Research: Ford's Recall-for-the-Recall Problem

## Angle
Ford has recalled 11.2 million vehicles YTD in 2026 — a 166% increase over the same period last year, and more than every other automaker combined. In a single week (June 15-17), four of Ford's five new NHTSA campaigns were re-recalls: vehicles that had been previously recalled and given incorrect repairs. The automaker that once claimed "Quality is Job 1" now needs recalls for its recalls.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. The re-recall angle is novel — not just "Ford has lots of recalls" (surface-level) but "Ford can't even fix the cars it recalls correctly." The iSeeCars data quantifies it: Ford's 11.2M recalled vehicles YTD is more than Honda (2.6M), Hyundai (1.7M), and Toyota (1.3M) combined. And 4 of 5 new campaigns in one week were because previous fixes were botched. That's a systemic quality failure, not a one-off.

Distinct from existing articles:
- `unfixed-recall-epidemic.html` covers general recall completion rates (58.1M vehicles with open recalls)  
- This is about Ford specifically AND the re-recall problem (fixes that need fixing)

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Recall Database (June 2026)
Ford recall campaigns filed June 15-17, 2026:
- **26V373**: 5,252 units (2015-18 Focus, 2013-16 Fusion) — transmission clutch fracture/fire risk. "Repaired incorrectly" under previous campaigns. 100% of vehicles affected by bad repair.
- **26V374**: 10,742 units (2018 F-150) — shifter can cause unintended gear selection (Reverse/Neutral). Previously repaired incorrectly, needs powertrain control module software update.
- **26V375**: 18,124 units (2017 Escape) — power windows exert excessive force, fail to auto-reverse. Previously botched recall, needs window software update.
- **26V376**: 91,198 units (2018-20 F-150) — daytime running lights stay fully bright. Previously repaired incorrectly, needs body control module software update.
- **26V371**: 2024 Explorer — rollaway risk from powertrain control module resets (new, not re-recall)

Total re-recalled: ~125,316 vehicles in one week
Source: NHTSA.gov, Carscoops (June 17, 2026)

### 2. iSeeCars Recall Dashboard (as of June 18, 2026)
YTD recall populations by manufacturer:
- **Ford: 11,234,087** (166% increase YoY)
- Honda: 2,615,008 (259% YoY)
- Hyundai: 1,679,983 (3,487% YoY)
- Toyota: 1,320,333 (51% YoY)
- Nissan: 768,723 (549% YoY)
- Tesla: 233,616 (DECREASE -65%)
- Total YTD all manufacturers: 22,395,094
- Ford's share: ~50.2% of all recalled vehicles

Last year same period: 10,580,072 total
Source: iSeeCars.com (NHTSA data as of June 18, 2026)

### 3. NHTSA Recall Completion Rate Report to Congress
- Average recall completion rate: ~59%
- Vehicles 10+ years old: 15% completion rate
- Takata airbag recalls: 46% completion
- Light trucks: 81% in one year (2014, skewed by GM mass recalls of new vehicles)
Source: NHTSA Report to Congress on Vehicle Safety Recall Completion Rates

### 4. Broader Ford June 2026 recall context (HowToGeek, June 16, 2026)
- 7 separate Ford recall campaigns in first 16 days of June
- Two carry "Do Not Drive" warnings (26V340: Bronco Sport/Maverick ball joints; 26V343: Bronco/Explorer/Ranger engine component)
- 419,967 Expedition/Navigator seat belt pretensioner recall (26V344)
- Instrument cluster failures on F-150, Navigator, Mustang

### 5. FARS Data (2014-2023)
- Ford total deaths: 34,954 (#2 after Chevrolet at 35,905)
- F-150 deaths: 9,194 (919.4/year) — death rate 1.04 per 100M VMT
- Ford has 15 models in FARS dataset
- Vehicles being re-recalled include: Focus (3,046 deaths), Escape (2,284 deaths), F-150 (9,194 deaths), Fusion (2,168 deaths), Expedition (1,515 deaths)

Cross-referencing the iSeeCars YTD recall data with the re-recall campaigns to show Ford accounts for 50%+ of all 2026 recalls AND is now re-recalling vehicles it already botched — a double failure mode no other manufacturer is exhibiting at this scale.
