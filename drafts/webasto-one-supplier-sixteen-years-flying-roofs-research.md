# Research: One Supplier, Sixteen Years, Flying Roofs

## Angle
Webasto, the world's largest sunroof/moonroof supplier, has had the identical defect — glass-to-frame adhesive debonding causing roof glass to separate from vehicles at highway speed — across multiple OEMs and multiple continents for 16 years. The latest: 69,663 Subaru Foresters recalled in June 2026. Before that, nearly a million Mercedes-Benz vehicles across four rounds of recalls (2020-2024). Before that, aftermarket sunroofs in Australia (2010), and Hollandia-series recalls with multiple US installers (2010-2012).

The defect description is nearly word-for-word identical across a 16-year span. The root cause never changes: insufficient primer or improper adhesive application between glass panel and metal frame. The glass separates. It becomes a highway projectile.

## Kill Test: Is this genuinely newsworthy?
YES. This is a supplier-chain investigation that nobody has written:
- Same supplier, same defect, 16 years apart
- Nearly 1 million cumulative vehicles affected
- The Forester is one of the safest compact SUVs in FARS (0.26 rate, 5th in segment)
- No crash test evaluates whether your roof stays attached
- Webasto is the world's largest roof systems manufacturer (market share >50%)

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing NHTSA recall filings across 16 years reveals Webasto's adhesive bonding defect is systemic, not isolated. OEMs change; the supplier defect persists.

FARS data shows the Forester at 0.26 per 100M VMT — among the safest compact SUVs. But FARS only measures fatal crashes. It doesn't measure whether your roof leaves the vehicle at 70 mph. Supplier quality is the blind spot in vehicle safety ratings.

## Primary Sources (3+)
1. **NHTSA Subaru recall (2026)**: 69,663 MY2026 Foresters. Recall filed May 28, 2026. Supplier: Webasto Roof Systems Inc., Kentucky. Defect: improper primer application. 3 US reports, 0 crashes/injuries.
2. **NHTSA Mercedes recall 24V-874 (2024)**: 33,456 MY2001-2011 C-Class, E-Class, CLK, CLS. Supplier: Webasto Roof & Components SE (Germany). Same defect. Fourth round of recalls for same issue:
   - 2020: 744,852 vehicles
   - 2021: 11,356 vehicles
   - 2022: 123,696 vehicles (+ 2,976 improperly repaired)
   - 2024: 33,456 vehicles
   - Total Mercedes: ~916,000 vehicles
3. **NHTSA Webasto Hollandia recalls (2010-2012)**: Recall 10V-432, 10V-526, 11V-124, 12V-131. Hollandia 600/700/900 series. Aftermarket + factory-installed. Exact same defect description: "THE ADHESIVE BOND BETWEEN THE GLASS AND THE METAL FRAME CAN DEBOND"
4. **FARS data (2014-2023)**: Subaru Forester rate 0.26/100M VMT, segment avg 0.44. 396 deaths, 1,225,000 fleet.
5. **Australia recall (2010)**: ~50,000 vehicles (Holden, Ford Australia, aftermarket). goauto.com.au and drive.com.au coverage.

## FARS Data
- Forester: 0.26 rate, 396 deaths, 1.225M fleet — 5th safest compact SUV
- Segment average: 0.44 rate
- Jeep Cherokee (worst): 1.73 rate — 6.6x more dangerous
- Crosstrek (best): 0.08 rate

## Key Details
- Webasto is headquartered in Stockdorf, Germany. Operations in Kentucky (US), Italy, Australia.
- Hollandia was Webasto's aftermarket sunroof brand
- Mercedes knew about the issue since December 2017 but the first US recall wasn't until 2020
- Subaru: Webasto reported improper bonding to Subaru March 1, 2026. Recall decision May 21, 2026.
- Only ~2.9% of recalled Foresters estimated to have the defect (2,020 of 69,663)
- Fix: inspect moonroof glass panel for proper adhesion, replace if necessary
