### The 2021-to-2022 Cliff
For the Kia Forte, the 2021-to-2022 model year transition is the starkest signal. The ONLY difference between those model years that could explain an 82% death drop: the 2022 got an immobilizer. Same platform, same crash structure, same airbags. One got a $20 chip.

1. **NHTSA confirmed**: 14 crashes, 8+ deaths from TikTok-related thefts (Feb 2023 bulletin)

8. NYC: 977 thefts in first 4 months of 2023 vs 148 same period prior year

### Strongest Counterargument
FARS doesn't tag whether a vehicle was stolen at time of crash. The 2021 MY spike could theoretically reflect COVID bounce-back driving or model popularity surges. However, the control vehicles (same size sedans, same demographic) show NO equivalent spike, and the 2022 MY cliff aligns precisely with the immobilizer addition.

### Limitations
- FARS aggregates deaths across 2014-2023 by model year of vehicle, not crash year. Can't isolate 2022-2023 crash year specifically.
- 2022-2023 MYs have limited exposure time (1-2 years on road), so raw counts may undercount.
- The 8 NHTSA-confirmed deaths are almost certainly an undercount — not all jurisdictions report theft status.
- Can't calculate a per-VMT rate for specific model years (no VMT-by-MY data).
- Software fix reduced theft claims 46% but was "easily bypassed" — unclear how many deaths occurred AFTER the fix.
