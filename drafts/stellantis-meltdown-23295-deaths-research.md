# Research Notes: Stellantis Meltdown — 23,295 Deaths

## Article #423 — Rex Driverton — Body Count

## Angle
Stellantis is simultaneously on fire financially and literally. The corporate-wide meltdown piece: 23,295 FARS deaths across 36 models (12.2% of the entire dataset, 3rd behind GM and Ford), combined with a current recall avalanche (1.08M Wrangler fire recall, 419K Jeep airbag recall, Takata do-not-drive orders), a €22B writedown, securities fraud lawsuit, and a stock price at $5.81 (near 52-week low of $4.91). No existing story covers the full Stellantis corporate portfolio through FARS + current crisis.

## Novel Contribution
- First corporate-level Stellantis piece crossing FARS body count with financial/recall crisis
- Existing individual vehicle stories (Wrangler fire, Challenger horsepower, Pacifica fire, Grand Cherokee fault) don't connect the corporate dots
- The question: what happens to safety when the company responsible for 12.2% of American traffic deaths is also fighting for its financial survival?

### Key insight: Stellantis barely edges Toyota (23,295 vs 23,275 — a 20-death margin) despite having more models. But Toyota sells far more vehicles in the US. Per-sale lethality is dramatically worse for Stellantis.

## Current Crisis (June-July 2026)

### 1. Wrangler/Gladiator Fire Recall (June 9, 2026)
- 1,076,999 vehicles recalled (MY 2021-2025)
- Electric hydraulic power steering pump wiring overheats
- **Fires occur even when vehicle is OFF**
- 72 fires, 1 injury
- Owners told to PARK OUTSIDE
- Stellantis investigated 2023-2024, CLOSED investigation citing "low rate," REOPENED Aug 2024
- Timeline: 3+ years from first fire to recall

### 2. Jeep Airbag Recall (2026)
- 419,000 Jeep vehicles for airbag issues
- Separate from fire recall

### 3. Global Fire Recall
- 700,000+ vehicles globally for additional fire risks

### 4. Takata Do-Not-Drive Orders
- Older Stellantis vehicles still under Takata airbag recall
- Some under active "do not drive" advisory

### Financial Crisis
- Stock: $5.81 (52-week low $4.91)
- €22 billion writedown
- Securities fraud class-action lawsuit
- CEO Carlos Tavares resigned December 2024
- Interim leadership / CEO search
- US market share declining
- Discontinued entire PHEV line (Wrangler 4xe, Grand Cherokee 4xe)
- Multiple plant closures/idling
