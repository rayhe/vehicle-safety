# Research Notes: The Spare Tire Extinction

## Angle
45% of new cars ship without spare tires. Automakers replaced them with sealant kits to save weight and boost fuel economy. Honda just recalled 1,049,883 vehicles because those sealant kits can shoot their bottle caps at users like projectiles. 8 injuries reported. The safety equipment meant to help stranded drivers became the hazard. This is a story about a systemic cost-cutting design decision across the industry — told through the most absurd recall of the year.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES.
- A million-vehicle recall for a SAFETY KIT that injures people is inherently ironic and compelling
- The broader trend (45% of new cars lacking spares) affects nearly every car buyer
- The hybrid-specific angle (battery takes spare tire space) adds a novel dimension
- Nobody has connected the recall to the broader spare tire elimination trend in a data-driven way
- Original contribution: calculating the cost-benefit of the weight savings vs. the recall/injury cost

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Honda Recall (Campaign 26V306) — June 11, 2026
- 1,049,883 vehicles recalled
- Affected: 2023-2026 CR-V Hybrid (744,530), 2023-2026 Accord Hybrid (305,013), 2025-2026 CR-V Fuel Cell EV (340)
- Defect: Tire repair kit sealant bottle cap can detach and become projectile
- Root cause: Relief valve improperly adjusted by supplier (TEK Corporation); one-way valve traps pressure
- 8 injuries, 54 warranty claims, 0 deaths
- Honda knew since April 2023 — supplier blamed "customer misuse" for 3 years
- Fix: Replace nozzle and sealant bottle at no charge
- Source: NHTSA, Reuters, USA Today, Autoblog, MotorTrend, CarBuzz, AutoEvolution

### 2. Consumer Reports — Spare Tire Statistics (since 2020)
- 9% of new cars have full-size spare
- 46% have space-saver/donut spare
- 34% have sealant kit only
- 7% have run-flat tires
- 4% have NOTHING
- ~45% of new cars don't come with any spare tire
- Reason: weight savings (50-60 lbs), fuel economy, cost savings, space for batteries
- Source: consumerreports.org

### 3. AAA — Spare Tire Disappearance
- ~30% of new cars lack spare (up from 5% in 2006)
- 30+ million cars on US roads without spare tires
- Sealant kits won't fix sidewall damage, blowouts, holes >0.24"
- TPMS doesn't prevent flats despite automaker claims
- Source: aaa.com

### 4. AAA Foundation — Roadside Fatalities
- 123 roadside assistance providers struck and killed while working, 2015-2021
- 89% at locations with 55+ mph speed limits
- 63% during darkness
- Source: aaafoundation.org

### 5. NHTSA 2023 Final Data
- 40,901 traffic deaths in 2023 (down 4.3% from 2022)
- 2025 early estimate: fatality rate dropped to 1.06/100M VMT (lowest since 2014)
- Source: nhtsa.gov

### 6. NHTSA Legal Position
- NHTSA does NOT mandate spare tires
- Maryland attempted to require them; NHTSA said it was a state law question
- No federal requirement = automakers free to eliminate them
- Source: NHTSA letter response, 2008

## Novel Cross-Tabulation: The Hybrid Spare Tire Tax
All 1,049,883 recalled vehicles are hybrids or fuel-cell EVs. These vehicles specifically lack spare tires because their battery packs occupy the space where a spare would go. The electrification transition is creating a new class of roadside vulnerability:
- Hybrids/EVs = heavier (battery weight) + no spare
- Weight savings from removing spare (~50 lbs) is ironic when the battery adds 200-400+ lbs
- The sealant kit isn't replacing the spare to save weight overall — it's making room for something heavier

## Strongest Counterargument
Sealant kits DO work for many common flats (small tread punctures from nails). Most drivers with sealant kits never experience a problem. AAA roadside service and cell phones mean stranded drivers aren't truly helpless. The recall injuries are non-severe and the fix is free. Automakers may be making a rational trade-off: spare tires go unused 99%+ of the time, and eliminating them saves billions in materials, improves fuel economy marginally, and frees valuable cargo space.

## Limitations
- FARS data doesn't track tire-related incidents specifically in a way that separates "had spare" from "didn't have spare"
- The 8 injuries from Honda may be underreported
- 30+ million vehicles without spares is an AAA estimate, not census
- Consumer Reports data is based on their test fleet, not a comprehensive survey
