# Research: Consumer Reports Named Four Safest Brands. FARS Data Disagrees About One.

## Angle
Consumer Reports released its first-ever Safety Verdict in February 2026, ranking 29 brands. The top four: Mazda, Genesis, Acura, Lincoln. But cross-referencing with FARS 2014-2023 fatality data reveals Lincoln's real-world fleet death rate is nearly double the other three. Lab-tested safety and road-proven safety are different measures — and Lincoln's gap is the widest.

## Original Contribution
First cross-reference of Consumer Reports' new Safety Verdict against FARS real-world fatality data at the brand level. The finding: three of CR's top four brands (Mazda, Genesis, Acura) align with FARS. One doesn't. Lincoln's fleet death rate of 7.30 is nearly double Mazda's (4.23) and double Acura's (3.66). Even comparing only modern models, Lincoln's death rates are 2-6× higher than Acura's comparable models.

The explanation isn't that Lincoln cars are poorly designed — CR's lab tests confirm they're well-engineered. The explanation is that lab tests measure new cars in controlled conditions. FARS measures what actually happens. And Lincoln/Ford's industry-leading recall rate, middling reliability, and fleet degradation erode the safety that was designed in.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers 2014-2023, including older discontinued Lincoln models (Town Car, LS, Continental) that skew the brand average. However, even modern-only comparisons show Lincoln lagging Acura by 2-6×.
- Fleet composition differs: Acura's fleet skews younger (more recent purchases), while Lincoln's includes more older used vehicles.
- The CR Safety Verdict is a new metric (first year); it may evolve.
- Genesis lacks sufficient FARS data (too new/small fleet) for comparison.

## Counterargument
Ford/Lincoln might argue that their Safety Verdict ranking reflects their CURRENT engineering philosophy, not their historical fleet. Lincoln's newest models (2024+ Nautilus, updated Aviator) may perform differently as they age. The FARS data includes many older vehicles that predate modern safety tech.

Response: True, but the point is precisely that brand-level safety isn't just about new-car design — it's about the entire ownership lifecycle. A brand with 23.4× the average recall rate and #20 reliability ranking degrades faster than one with #7 reliability and fewer recalls. The safety you buy on day one isn't the safety you have on day 2,000.
