# Research: The VMT Tax — Rate Dropped, Body Count Didn't

## Angle
2025 estimated fatality rate (1.10 per 100M VMT) nearly matches 2014's all-time low (1.08). But 36,640 people died in 2025 vs 32,744 in 2014 — 3,896 MORE deaths at essentially the same per-mile risk, because Americans drove ~12% more miles. The rate improvement is real. The body count improvement is an illusion created by VMT growth.

## Key Calculations (Novel Contribution)

### The VMT Tax
- At 2025's rate of 1.10 deaths/100M VMT:
  - If VMT were at 2014 levels (3,026B): deaths ≈ 33,286
  - Actual 2025 deaths: 36,640
  - VMT growth "cost": ~3,354 extra deaths
  
### Rate needed to hold deaths at 2014's count
- To get 32,744 deaths at 2025's ~3,331B VMT:
  - Required rate: 32,744 / 33.31 = 0.983 per 100M VMT
  - The US has NEVER achieved a rate below 1.00

### Rate improvement savings
- If 2025 had 2021's rate (1.37): deaths ≈ 45,635
- Actual 2025 deaths: 36,640
- Rate improvement "saved": ~8,995 lives/year
- But VMT growth "returned" ~3,354 of them

### The structural problem
- Every 1% VMT increase at current rates = ~367 additional deaths
- VMT has grown ~10% since 2014, adding ~3,000+ annual deaths
- To simply HOLD deaths constant as VMT grows 1%/year, rate must decline 1%/year forever
- Compound growth means this gets harder each year

## Counterargument (stated at full strength)
The rate IS what matters to individual safety. A driver in 2025 is individually safer per mile than at any time since 2014. The aggregate body count rising because more total miles are driven is a population-level outcome, not a per-person risk increase. Framing VMT growth as "killing people" conflates individual and systemic risk.

## Limitations
- 2025 data is still an early estimate; final FARS could shift ±2-3%
- VMT estimates are themselves approximations
- The rate/count divergence is well-known to safety researchers; the novelty is in the specific 2025 vs 2014 comparison and the "VMT tax" framing
- Does not account for demographics, rural/urban mix, or fleet composition changes
