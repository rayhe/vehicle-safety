# Research: Honda J35 V6 Engine — "Bulletproof" Myth Meets 1.4 Million Defective Crankshafts

## Angle
Honda's J-series V6, in production since 1999, is one of the most respected engines in automotive history. Now NHTSA is investigating 1.4 million vehicles after 3,000+ complaints of connecting rod bearing failures from improperly ground crankshaft pins. Honda denied 100% of post-recall complaints. The affected vehicles — Pilot, Odyssey, Ridgeline, MDX, TLX — are among the safest, soberest family haulers in FARS data.

## Key Data Points

### NHTSA Investigation & Recall
- **Recall 23V-751** (November 2023): 248,999 vehicles
  - 2015-2020 Acura TLX
  - 2016-2020 Acura MDX
  - 2016, 2018-2019 Honda Pilot
  - 2018-2019 Honda Odyssey
  - 2017, 2019 Honda Ridgeline
- **Root cause:** "Improper settings of equipment used to manufacture the engine crankshaft" → improperly ground crank pins → convex/crown shape out of spec → connecting rod bearing premature wear → seizure
- **First US complaint:** February 2020
- **First recall:** November 2023 (3 years, 9 months later)
- **NHTSA Preliminary Evaluation PE25008** (August 2025): Expanded to 1,410,806 vehicles
  - 414 ODI reports + 2,598 manufacturer reports = 3,012 total complaints
  - 7 crashes, 0 injuries, 0 fatalities
- Honda estimated only 1% of flagged vehicles had the defect
- **Honda denied every one of 173 post-recall complaints** — NHTSA opened new investigation specifically because of this denial pattern
- **June 2026:** I Do Cars teardown of 85,000-mile 2020 Odyssey confirms catastrophic engine failure

### FARS Data — The Safety Paradox
Affected Honda/Acura models are among the safest vehicles in FARS:

| Vehicle | Deaths | Crashes | Rate/100M VMT | Impairment % |
|---------|--------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Honda Odyssey | 864 | 2,028 | 0.93 | 15.4% (lowest among all vans) |
| Honda Pilot | 514 | 1,135 | 0.29 | 19.4% |
| Honda Ridgeline | 84 | 174 | 0.24 | 19.4% |
| Acura MDX | 163 | 391 | 0.30 | 21.5% |
| Acura TLX | 33 | 109 | 0.11 | 18.6% |

- **Odyssey lethality ratio:** 0.426 (bottom 25 — very survivable)
- **Odyssey impairment rate:** 15.4% — lowest impairment rate among ALL vans in FARS
- These vehicles are driven by sober, cautious families — exactly the demographic least equipped to handle sudden engine seizure at highway speed

### The "Exposure Window"
- First complaint: February 2020
- First recall: November 2023
- Estimated affected fleet: 1.41 million vehicles
- Average annual VMT per vehicle: ~12,000 miles
- Approximate VMT during exposure window (3.7 years): 1.41M × 12,000 × 3.7 = ~62.6 billion miles driven with potentially defective engines

### Novel Contribution
1. The J-series has been in production for 27 years (1999-2026). This isn't a first-generation design failure — it's a manufacturing quality control failure in a mature, proven architecture
2. Cross-referencing 1.4M affected vehicles with FARS shows these are the safest, soberest vehicles on the road — a sudden mechanical failure is especially perverse for this demographic
3. Honda's 100% denial rate on post-recall complaints is a pattern seen in the recall-remedy quality gap
4. The 3.7-year exposure window between first complaint and first recall represents ~63 billion miles of unaddressed defect
