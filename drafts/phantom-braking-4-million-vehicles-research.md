# Research: Phantom Braking Cross-Manufacturer Epidemic

## News Hook
Hyundai recalled 421,078 vehicles (May 19, 2026) for Forward Collision-Avoidance system causing unexpected braking. 376 reports, 4 crashes, 4 injuries. Affects 2025-2026 Santa Cruz, Tucson, Tucson Hybrid, Tucson PHEV. Front camera software bug activates brakes prematurely. Fix: free software update at dealers by July 17.

## NHTSA Complaint Data (from Cardog/NHTSA analysis of 2.15M complaints)
- Phantom braking complaints surged **1,280% in 2022** (132 → 1,820)
- Tesla: 17.12% of all complaints are phantom braking (2,292 total). 11x higher than #2 Mercedes-Benz (1.54%)
- Model Y: 1,144 phantom braking complaints. Model 3: 1,086.
- AEB complaints since 2019: 8,957 total, 953 crashes, 643 injuries, 9 deaths
- Forward Collision Warning: 5,097 complaints, 671 crashes, 388 injuries, 10 deaths
- Combined AEB+FCW since 2019: ~1,742 crashes, ~1,169 injuries, 19 deaths

## The Paradox
- NHTSA mandated AEB for ALL new passenger vehicles by September 2029 (FMVSS No. 127, finalized 2024)
- Projected benefit: 360 lives saved, 24,000 injuries prevented annually
- But the current generation of the technology has ALREADY caused 1,742+ crashes and 1,169+ injuries
- A safety system designed to prevent front-end crashes is **creating rear-end crashes** (car brakes for nothing, following car rear-ends it)
- 1,286 NHTSA complaints specifically mention both phantom braking AND rear-end collision concern

## The 2022 Spike — Tesla Vision
- Tesla removed radar sensors 2021-2022, switched to camera-only "Tesla Vision"
- Phantom braking complaints: 132 (2021) → 1,820 (2022) = 1,279% spike
- Camera-only systems struggle with: shadows, overpasses, adjacent-lane vehicles, low light, reflective surfaces
- Post-2022 decline (855 in 2023, 307 in 2024) suggests OTA updates helped but didn't eliminate problem

## Honda's Response Is Damning
- Honda told NHTSA that some owners "didn't understand" how CMBS worked
- Dealerships "unable to reproduce" the problem, told owners it was "normal operation"
- NHTSA escalated to Engineering Analysis anyway — 47 crashes and 93 injuries across 3M vehicles
- No recall issued yet despite investigation since 2022

## Actionable Takeaways
1. Check your VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls for phantom braking recalls
2. Know how to disable AEB in your vehicle (every car allows this)
3. If shopping: Toyota (0.11%) and Ford (0.07%) have lowest phantom braking complaint rates
4. If you drive a 2025-2026 Hyundai Tucson/Santa Cruz: schedule dealer visit for software update
5. If you drive a 2017-2022 Honda CR-V or Accord: no recall yet, but NHTSA investigation active — report incidents
