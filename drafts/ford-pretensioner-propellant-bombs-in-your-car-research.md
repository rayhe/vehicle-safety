# Research Notes: Your Car Has 14 Explosive Charges Inside It

## Angle

Ford's new 420K-vehicle pretensioner recall proves the same failure pattern that killed 28 Americans via Takata is not an anomaly — it's chemistry. The industry "fixed" Takata but never addressed the systemic issue of propellant aging across ALL pyrotechnic safety devices.

Three separate manufacturers have now confirmed the same failure mode in pyrotechnic automotive safety devices:

## Primary Sources (3+ required — ✓)

### Source 1: NHTSA Recall 26S34 (Ford Expedition/Navigator Pretensioner)
- **Vehicles:** 419,967 (342,283 Ford Expedition + 77,684 Lincoln Navigator), MY 2018-2022
- **Defect:** Seat belt retractor pretensioner propellant degrades in high-heat environments → oxidation of internal components → inadvertent deployment (locks belt, prevents retraction/extension)
- **Consequence:** Belt may fail to restrain in crash; rapid retraction itself can cause injury
- **History:** Third recall for same pretensioner issue (replaces/expands two prior recalls)
- **Injuries:** 1 globally; 2 warranty claims, 2 field reports
- **Fix:** Dealers inspect + replace retractors. Remedy not available until August/September 2026.
- **Warning sign:** Airbag malfunction light on dash precedes inadvertent deployment

### Source 2: NHTSA Recall 14V-353 (Takata Airbag Inflators)
- **Vehicles:** ~67 million inflators in US, 19 manufacturers
- **Defect:** Ammonium nitrate propellant degrades with heat + humidity → over-pressurizes on deployment → ruptures metal housing into shrapnel
- **Deaths:** 28 in US, 36+ worldwide
- **Injuries:** 400+ in US
- **Key fact:** "The older a defective Takata air bag inflator gets, the more dangerous it becomes" (NHTSA)
- **Recall phases prioritized by age of inflators and exposure to high humidity/high temperatures

### Source 3: ARC Automotive Class-Action (Airbag Inflators)
- **Vehicles:** 51 million vehicles with ARC inflators on US roads (10-20% of all passenger vehicles)
- **Defect:** Same ammonium nitrate secondary propellant → moisture → microscopic cracks → burns too fast → ruptures canister → shrapnel
- **Deaths:** 2 confirmed, 4 injuries
- **NHTSA investigation open for ~7 years without resolution
- **Quote:** "You could have a ticking time bomb in your lap and you've got no way of knowing" — attorney Frank Melton

### Source 4: Wikipedia / ATF Regulations
- Modern vehicles contain up to 10 airbag modules (driver, passenger, side-curtain, seat-mounted, door-mounted, knee, inflatable belt, pedestrian)
- Plus 2-4 seat belt pretensioners (driver, front passenger, possibly rear)
- Each is a pyrotechnic device regulated under ATF 27 CFR Part 555 in bulk
- Propellant historically ammonium nitrate, now usually guanidine nitrate
- Only legal in cars because of a federal motor vehicle safety rules carve-out

### Source 5: FARS Data — Ford Expedition
- Death rate: 2.31 per 100M VMT (1,515 deaths, 2014-2023)
- 50% deadlier than the Ford Explorer (1.54/100M VMT)
- Impairment rate: 20% (moderate — not primarily a drunk-driving car)
- Annual deaths: 151.5
- MY peak: 2003-2004 (198 deaths in MY2004 alone)
