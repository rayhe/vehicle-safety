# Research: NHTSA's 459% Budget Increase, 16% Fatality Reduction

## Source
**Primary:** Viano DC. "Why the U.S. lags other countries in reducing traffic fatalities the past 25 years." *Traffic Injury Prevention*. 2026 Apr 10:1-22. doi: 10.1080/15389588.2026.2636773. PMID: 41962029.

## Key Findings

### The International Gap
- Since 1979, US traffic fatalities dropped **16.0%**
- 14 other high-income countries dropped **77.4% ± 5.9%**
- The gap has been statistically significant since **1996** and has grown every year since
- US is the worst-performing high-income country on traffic safety

### The Budget Paradox
- NHTSA's budget increased **459%** over 25 years (14.4% annual increase)
- Traffic deaths barely moved
- Study conclusion: "NHTSA has little to show for the extremely large budget, except 'we could do better with more money.'"

### The Automaker Thesis
- **Fatalities would have INCREASED** without voluntary automaker technologies
- Technologies that actually saved lives were introduced voluntarily by manufacturers, NOT mandated by NHTSA:
  - ESC (Electronic Stability Control) — IIHS pushed this, NHTSA mandated it years after it was standard
  - AEB (Automatic Emergency Braking) — voluntary industry agreement
  - High retention seats
- NHTSA's contribution: crash tests with "no relevance to fatal accidents"

### 12 Specific NHTSA Failures
**Primary (3):**
1. Not set targets focusing on fatality reductions
2. Not pursued research with measurable reductions in fatalities
3. No meaningful engagement with industry, IIHS, others

**Secondary (9):**
4. Not conducted critical analysis of programs
5. Inherent problems managing research + regulations + investigations under one leadership
6. Not verified assumptions for field accident data collection
7. Wrong sampling frequencies/case weights in NASS-CDS and CISS
8. Not terminated testing that doesn't reduce fatalities
9. Not followed up on useful research
10. Crash tests irrelevant to fatal accidents; wrongheaded MAIS 2 injury focus
11. Not required timely engineering reports
12. Inaccessible archives

### The Real Problem
- Most fatalities are in **10+ year old vehicles**
- Main causes: no seatbelt use, alcohol/drug impairment, aggressive speeding
- NHTSA focuses on new car technologies in crash tests, missing the actual death fleet

## Cross-Reference with FARS Data

- Our own FARS data shows impairment in ~20-28% of pickup truck fatal crashes

## CDC Confirmation
CDC MMWR (2025): Between 2013-2022, US pedestrian death rates increased 50% while other high-income countries DECREASED by median of 24.7%. US had highest pedestrian death rates of all 28 countries studied.

## Angle
The regulator's budget went up 459%. Deaths barely moved. Every life actually saved in America was saved by automakers who installed ESC and AEB voluntarily. The agency charged with protecting you spent 25 years testing new cars in ways that don't prevent fatal crashes, while the real death toll piled up in aging vehicles driven by impaired, unbelted, speeding drivers. NHTSA was along for the ride.
