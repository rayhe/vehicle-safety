# RESEARCH: Hit-and-Run Epidemic — One in Four Pedestrian Deaths

## Thesis
One in four pedestrians killed in traffic crashes in 2023 was struck by a hit-and-run driver — up from one in five just six years earlier. A new AAA Foundation study (March 2026) reveals the epidemic has structural roots: 40% of fleeing drivers don't have valid licenses, most crash near home, and the poorest ZIP codes suffer six times the fatalities of the wealthiest. This isn't a crime wave — it's a system failure.

## Key Data Points

### AAA Foundation for Traffic Safety (March 2026)
**"Understanding the Increase in Fatal Hit-and-Run Crashes: Prevalences of Crashes, Injuries, and Deaths in the United States, 2017-2023"**
- Source: https://aaafoundation.org/understanding-the-increase-in-fatal-hit-and-run-crashes-prevalences-of-crashes-injuries-and-deaths-in-the-united-states-2017-2023/
- 919,000+ police-reported hit-and-run crashes in 2023
- 242,000+ injuries
- 2,872 fatalities (7% of all traffic fatalities)
- All-time high: 2,972 fatalities in 2022
- 15% of ALL police-reported crashes in 2023 were hit-and-runs (highest ever recorded)
- Trend: increasing since 2010, spiked in 2020 (COVID)
- 70%+ of hit-and-run fatalities were pedestrians and cyclists
- ~80% occurred in darkness
- Highest in largest cities, lowest in rural areas
- Most vulnerable 20% of ZIP codes: 3x fatalities vs. middle 20%, 6x vs. least vulnerable 20%
- Among caught drivers (less than half!): majority young males, crashed near home ZIP
- 40% lacked valid driver's license
- 50%+ driving vehicles not registered in their name
- Only 3 states have "Yellow Alert" systems (CA, CO, MD) — like Amber Alerts for hit-and-runs

### IIHS Fatality Facts 2023: Pedestrians
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/research-areas/fatality-statistics/detail/pedestrians
- 7,314 pedestrian deaths in 2023 (18% of all crash fatalities)
- 78% increase since 2009 low point
- Hit-and-run as % of pedestrian deaths:
  - 2014: 953 (19%)
  - 2015: 1,123 (20%)
  - 2016: 1,250 (21%)
  - 2017: 1,226 (20%)
  - 2018: 1,292 (20%)
  - 2019: 1,310 (21%)
  - 2020: 1,603 (24%) ← COVID spike
  - 2021: 1,831 (25%)
  - 2022: 1,939 (26%)
  - 2023: 1,818 (25%)

### NHTSA / Traffic Safety Marketing
- Source: https://www.trafficsafetymarketing.gov/safety-topics/pedestrian-safety
- 77% of pedestrian fatalities occurred in dark
- 89% in single-vehicle crashes
- 1 in 4 pedestrians killed was hit-and-run (2023)

## Strongest Counterargument
The increase could partly reflect better reporting/coding of hit-and-run crashes in FARS rather than an actual behavioral change. More dashcams and surveillance cameras mean more crashes are *identified* as hit-and-runs that previously would have been coded as "driver unknown." However, the AAA study accounts for this by using consistent FARS coding methodology, and the 2020 spike (coinciding with COVID behavioral changes, not reporting changes) suggests a real behavioral shift.
