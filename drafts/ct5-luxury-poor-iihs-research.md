# Research: Cadillac CT5 — $48,600 for POOR IIHS Safety

## Angle
The 2026 Cadillac CT5 just got "poor" marks on FOUR IIHS tests — the worst performance of any vehicle in the latest test batch. GM's defense? "5-Star NHTSA rating." Same car, two tests, opposite conclusions. Meanwhile, a Mazda CX-5 at half the price got Top Safety Pick+.

Cross-reference: The CTS (CT5's direct predecessor) has a 1.32 fatality rate per 100M VMT in FARS — 4x the Audi A4 (0.32). The FARS data says GM's luxury sedans were already killing at elevated rates. The IIHS just confirmed why.

## Self-Critique Gate
**Proposed angle:** The CT5 is the only vehicle to score POOR on four IIHS tests in this batch, while costing twice as much as vehicles that got TSP+. The predecessor CTS already had elevated FARS death rates. GM's pricing doesn't buy safety.

**Challenge:** Have we done a "luxury car fails safety test" angle before? We've done seville-luxury-deathtrap (Seville at 3.89 rate), but nothing about current IIHS test failures vs FARS. This is genuinely fresh — it's breaking news from TODAY (July 9, 2026) combined with historical FARS data showing the pattern predates the redesign.

**Verdict:** PROCEED. Novel data combination (breaking IIHS + historical FARS), timely hook, consumer-actionable.

## Primary Sources (3+)
1. **IIHS July 2026 test results** — CT5 scored POOR in moderate overlap front crash, side impact, headlights, and front crash prevention. Source: freep.com coverage of IIHS results, July 9, 2026. IIHS VP Jessica Jermakian quoted.
2. **NHTSA 5-Star Rating** — CT5 scored 5/5 across ALL NHTSA tests: frontal barrier, side crash, side barrier, combined side, rollover (9.5% risk, No Tip). Source: Edmunds 2026 CT5 page.
3. **FARS data (2014-2023)** — CTS: 1.32 deaths/100M VMT, 265 deaths, 175K fleet, 25.9% impairment. Compare: Audi A4 at 0.32, Mercedes C-Class at 0.63, Lexus IS at 0.87. Cadillac's luxury sedans already at elevated risk.
4. **Edmunds pricing** — CT5 Premium Luxury starts at $48,600 MSRP vs Mazda CX-5 starting ~$28K (TSP+), Subaru Crosstrek ~$31K (TSP+).

## Key Data Points
- CT5: POOR on 4 IIHS tests (moderate overlap, side impact, headlights, front crash prevention)
- CT5: 5-Star on ALL NHTSA tests
- Same test batch: Audi A6, BMW X1, Mazda CX-5, Subaru Crosstrek Hybrid got TSP+
- CT5 MSRP: $48,600+
- Mazda CX-5 MSRP: ~$28,000 (TSP+)
- CTS (predecessor) FARS: 1.32 rate, 25.9% impairment
- Audi A4 FARS: 0.32 rate — CTS is 4.1x the A4
- IIHS quote: "It seems the crashworthiness and crash avoidance results weren't important for GM when they were redesigning the Cadillac CT5"
- B pillar crushed into occupant compartment in side test
- Airbag didn't protect dummy from windowsill contact
- Rear passengers showed severe head and chest injury risk
- CT5 introduced 2019, 2025 styling refresh had NO structural changes
