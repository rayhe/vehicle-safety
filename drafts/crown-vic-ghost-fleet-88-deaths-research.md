# Research: Crown Victoria Ghost Fleet

## FARS Data (2014–2023)
- **Deaths:** 881 (88.1/year)
- **Fatal crashes:** 1,349
- **Crash-to-death ratio:** 1.53 (mostly kills its own occupant, not others)
- **Fleet estimate:** 525,000
- **Rate:** 1.46 deaths per 100M VMT
- **Impairment:** 22.3% (alc 15.8%, drug 10.5%) — 2nd highest among full-size sedans

## Fire History (sourced)
- At least 30 police officers killed in fiery rear-impact Crown Vic crashes since 1983 (Center for Auto Safety)
- At least 69 total fire deaths in Panther platform vehicles since 1979 (Detroit Free Press, 2003)
- Gas tank behind rear axle in crush zone — bolts punctured tank in rear impacts
- Ford added fuel tank shields in 2003 models; maintained earlier models safe
- NHTSA investigated 2001-2002, found Crown Vic met 30 mph federal fuel leak standard, closed probe
- "The Crown Vic is a big Pinto" — Clarence Ditlow, Center for Auto Safety executive director
- Senator Schumer called for federal investigation after officer deaths
- Multiple Florida agencies suspended Crown Vic purchases temporarily

## The Pipeline: Police Fleet → Low-Income Buyers
- Crown Vic Police Interceptor (CVPI) was THE American police car from ~1992-2011
- At peak: ~85% of US police fleet was Crown Vic (estimated)
- Police drove them hard: 8-12 hour shifts, idling hours, high-speed pursuits
- After retirement from duty (typically 3-5 years), departments auctioned them cheaply
- Ex-police Crown Vics sold for $2,000-$5,000 at auction
- Buyers: low-income individuals attracted by low price + body-on-frame durability
- Result: a car designed for institutional fleet use ended up as primary transportation for people who couldn't afford anything better

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing: The Crown Vic has been out of production for 15 years (2011). Yet FARS 2014-2023 records 881 deaths. The fleet estimate of 525,000 means roughly 1 in 6,000 Crown Vics on the road was involved in a fatal crash during the FARS period. Every single one of these deaths happened in a car that was already old when the FARS recording period started.
