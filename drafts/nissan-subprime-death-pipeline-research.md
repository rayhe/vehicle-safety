# Research: Nissan's Subprime Death Pipeline

## Thesis
Nissan's strategy of becoming America's de facto subprime auto lender — via NMAC's SignatureAccess Sub-Prime Dual Pass-Through Program — funnels buyers into sedans (Altima, Sentra, Maxima) that kill at 4x the rate of their own SUVs (Rogue, Pathfinder). The death gap is NOT explained by driver behavior: impairment rates are virtually identical across the lineup (~20%). The excess deaths are attributable to the vehicles themselves.

## Kill Test
- Genuinely surprising? YES. Individual Nissan vehicles have been covered (altima-energy, maxima-four-door-sports-car, sentra-entry-level-killer) but never a brand-level analysis connecting BUSINESS STRATEGY to FARS outcomes.
- Novel contribution? YES. Three original computations: (1) identical impairment rates across Nissan sedans/SUVs proving vehicle-caused gap, (2) ~725 excess deaths/year attributable to sedan choice, (3) Nissan sedans 1.74x deadlier than average sedan.
- 3+ primary sources? FARS data, NMAC subprime program documentation (CarsDirect), CVT reliability data (Endurance, Lemon Law Help), IIHS ratings.

### Class Breakdown
- **Nissan Sedans: 9,624 deaths** / 3,456,250 fleet = 278.4 deaths per 100k fleet
- **Nissan SUVs: 3,031 deaths** / 4,418,750 fleet = 68.6 deaths per 100k fleet
- **Sedan-to-SUV death ratio: 4.06x**

### The Impairment Control
The devastating finding: impairment rates are FLAT across the lineup.
- Altima: 20.0% any impairment
- Sentra: 20.0% any impairment
- Maxima: 20.9% any impairment
- Rogue: 19.0% any impairment
- Pathfinder: 19.1% any impairment

**Sedan avg ~20.3% vs SUV avg ~19.5% — statistically identical.** The 4x death gap is not driver behavior. It's the car.

### Extreme Spread
- Maxima: 5.11 rate (per 100M VMT)
- Rogue: 0.35 rate
- **Spread: 14.6x** — same dealership, same badge, 15x the death risk

### Excess Death Calculation
If all Nissan sedan owners had been in a vehicle with Nissan-SUV death rates:
- Excess rate: 278.4 - 68.6 = 209.8 per 100k fleet
- Applied to sedan fleet: 3,456,250 × 209.8/100,000 = 7,246 excess deaths over 10 years
- **~725 excess deaths per year**

### vs Other Sedans
Overall sedan death rate: 160.1 per 100k fleet
Nissan sedan death rate: 278.4 per 100k fleet
**Nissan sedans are 1.74x more dangerous than the average sedan.**

## Angle
Investigation kicker. Rex Driverton. The piece connects Nissan's volume-at-all-costs lending strategy to a quantifiable body count. The impairment-rate identity across classes is the smoking gun — it proves this isn't a "bad driver" story, it's a "bad car" story enabled by a predatory lending strategy.

## Strongest Counterargument
Nissan sedans might attract different USE PATTERNS — more highway commuting, longer daily miles, different geographic distribution — even if impairment rates are similar. The impairment control is strong but doesn't capture all confounders. Also, ANY brand's sedans would show higher death rates than their SUVs — this is partly the sedan death penalty at work, not Nissan-specific. The Nissan premium over average-sedan rate (1.74x) is the true Nissan-specific signal.
