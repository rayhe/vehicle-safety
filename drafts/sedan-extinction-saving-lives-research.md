# Research: The Sedan Is Dying. Good.

## Angle
The American sedan is disappearing from showrooms. Every major automaker is killing sedans and shifting buyers to crossovers. FARS data reveals this unplanned migration might be the most effective safety intervention nobody designed.

## Original Contribution
Matched-pair methodology: 15 direct brand-matched sedan→crossover comparisons. Same brand, similar price tier, similar buyer demographics. Every single pair shows the sedan is deadlier. No exceptions.

### Aggregate
- Total sedan deaths: 48,023 (fleet: 20.7M)
- Total crossover deaths: 9,776 (fleet: 20.2M)
- Sedans = 83.1% of combined deaths with roughly equal fleet sizes
- Average ratio: ~10x deadlier per sedan

### Pull stat
**83%** — the share of combined deaths attributable to sedans, despite near-identical fleet sizes

## Timeliness
- NHTSA Q1 2026 data shows overall fatality rate fell to 0.99 per 100M VMT
- The sedan-to-crossover shift is accelerating: GM killed the Malibu, Chrysler killed the 200, Ford killed the Fusion and Focus, Buick killed the Regal
- Every IIHS TSP+ winner in the sedan category has a death rate 2-17x higher than its crossover sibling from the same brand

## Limitations
- Fleet age bias: sedans in FARS span more model years (older, pre-ESC vehicles inflate sedan deaths)
- Self-selection: sedan buyers may differ demographically from crossover buyers
- VMT differences: crossovers may be driven fewer miles per year (suburban vs commuter use)
- Rate calculation uncertainty: ±15% for low-volume models

## Strongest Counterargument
The sedan-crossover gap may be mostly a proxy for vehicle age. The average Civic in FARS is older than the average HR-V because HR-V is a newer nameplate. Newer vehicles = more safety tech = fewer deaths. This is a confound, not proof that the body style itself is dangerous.
