# Research: Tariff → Fleet Age → Body Count

## Angle
Trump's 25% auto tariff makes new cars more expensive. When new cars cost more, people keep older cars longer. Older cars lack modern safety tech and kill at dramatically higher rates. Every pricing policy that slows fleet turnover has a body count — and nobody's counting it.

## Journalist
Axle McScatter — Data Visualization Editor. "By The Numbers" kicker.

## Key Data Points

### Tariff Facts
- 25% tariff on all imported vehicles and parts not covered under USMCA
- Goldman Sachs: $5,000–$15,000 price increase on imported vehicles; $3,000–$8,000 on US-assembled (imported parts)
- 0 current vehicles meet the 85% domestic content threshold for exemption (per government data)
- 47% of US aftermarket parts imported from Mexico (S&P Global Mobility)
- $22.4B in added aftermarket parts duties (S&P Global Mobility 2026 review)

### Fleet Age Facts
- Average US vehicle age: 12.8 years — all-time record (S&P Global Mobility, May 2025)
- Average passenger car: 14.5 years
- 289 million light vehicles in operation
- Passenger cars dropped below 100 million for first time since 1970s
- ESC mandated for all new vehicles starting MY 2012 (NHTSA FMVSS 126)

### FARS Cross-Reference (our data)
- Average model year in fatal crash: 2006.7 (across FARS 2014-2023)
- Pre-2000 vehicles: 28,036 deaths (15.0%)
- 2000-2004: 47,921 deaths (25.6%)
- 2005-2009: 46,369 deaths (24.8%)
- 2010-2014: 32,305 deaths (17.3%)
- 2015-2019: 26,472 deaths (14.2%)
- 2020+: 5,955 deaths (3.2%)
- Total: 187,058 deaths
- **82.6% of all fatalities in vehicles built 2014 or earlier**

### Recall Completion Connection
- Recall completion rate: 50.3% (NHTSA 2025 Annual Report, published March 2026)
- 14 million recalled vehicles still unrepaired
- Average 3-year-old used car: $31,548 (Edmunds Q1 2026)
- 2026 YTD recalls: 24.6M affected vehicles, nearly double same period 2025 (iSeeCars)

### Counterfeit Parts Connection
- 10+ deaths from counterfeit DTN airbag inflaters smuggled from China
- Authentic replacement airbag: $1,000+; counterfeit: as low as $100
- NHTSA banned DTN-marked inflaters April 2026
- Traditional recall "impossible" — no distribution records (NHTSA Administrator Morrison)
- Higher parts prices from tariffs → more incentive for cheaper counterfeits

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing FARS death distribution by model year with fleet age economics and tariff policy. Nobody has drawn the line from "tariff increases new car price" → "slower fleet turnover" → "more deaths in pre-ESC vehicles." The 82.6% stat is our own FARS cross-tabulation.

## Strongest Counterargument
Tariffs might incentivize domestic manufacturing, which could eventually lower prices. And fleet age is rising for many reasons (vehicle durability, high interest rates, consumer preference) — tariffs are just one factor. The causal chain has multiple links, each with uncertainty.
