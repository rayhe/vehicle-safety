# Research: NHTSA's SCI Investigation Rate Collapse

## Angle
NHTSA's Special Crash Investigations program normally opens 100+ investigations per year. In 2026 through late June, it has opened exactly 2. That's a 96% decline coinciding with DOGE-driven staffing cuts that disproportionately targeted the autonomous vehicle safety division.

## Kill Test
**Is this genuinely newsworthy?** Yes — the SCI program has operated since 1972 and is the agency's most detailed crash investigation tool. A 96% decline in its output is an institutional collapse nobody has quantified. The breaking news (Texas Tesla crash opening only the 2nd SCI of 2026) provides the hook.

**Novel angle on data?** Yes — nobody has computed the SCI opening rate for 2026 vs historical baseline, or connected DOGE staffing numbers to investigation output.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: NHTSA Official — SCI Program Description
- NHTSA website: "over 100 crashes designated for study annually" since 1972
- Federal Register (April 2025): "SCI conducts investigations on approximately 100 crashes per year"
- URL: https://www.nhtsa.gov (SCI page)

### Source 2: DOT Office of Inspector General — SCI Program Audit
- Report ST2025035: SCI Program published 43-70 reports/year from 2018-2022
- Found the program "lacked adequate procedures in key areas"
- Noted 45 contracted field offices, 5 CIREN centers in the investigation network
- URL: www.oig.dot.gov

### Source 3: NHTSA 2024 Fatality Estimates (April 2026)
- 39,345 estimated traffic deaths in 2024
- Fatality rate: 1.20 per 100M VMT (lowest since 2019, still above pre-COVID avg of 1.13)
- Deaths decreased in 35 states, increased in 14 states + DC
- URL: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/nhtsa-releases-2023-traffic-deaths-2024-estimates

### Source 4: Reuters — Texas Tesla SCI (June 23, 2026)
- NHTSA opened SCI into fatal Tesla Model 3 crash in Katy, TX (June 19, 2026)
- "NHTSA has opened nearly 50 special crash investigations since 2016 into incidents involving Teslas"
- ~24 deaths reported in those investigations
- "NHTSA typically opens more than 100 special crash investigations annually"
- "This is the first Tesla SCI investigation opened since July 2023 and only the second this year following a probe into a fatal Toyota crash in April in Louisiana"
- KEY: Only 2 SCIs opened in all of 2026 through late June

### Source 5: DOGE Staffing Cuts — Multiple Sources
- Feb 2025: NHTSA lost 4% of staff (~30 people) in DOGE cuts (Detroit Free Press)
- Autonomous vehicle safety division had 7 people; lost 3 (43% of the team) (Washington Post)
- Projected total reduction: 772 → 555 employees (28% cut) (Reuters/Carscoops)
- Financial Times: cuts "disproportionately affected" staff assessing self-driving tech
- Tesla manager quoted: "Letting DOGE fire those in the autonomous division is sheer madness"
- Consumer Reports coalition warned against "harmful cuts" in 2026 THUD Appropriations bill

## Key Calculations
- Normal SCI rate: ~100/year ≈ ~50 by mid-year
- 2026 actual: 2 opened through late June = ~4% of expected pace
- At current 2026 pace: ~4 SCIs for the full year (vs 100+ normal)
- SCIs per death: normally ~1 per 393 deaths; at 2026 pace, ~1 per 9,836 deaths
- NHTSA employees per registered vehicle: 555 staff ÷ ~283M registered vehicles ≈ 1 per 510,000 vehicles
- Investigation network contraction: staffing down 28%, SCI output down 96%
