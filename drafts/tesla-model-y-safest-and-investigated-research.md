# Research: Tesla Model Y — Safest Car in America and Largest Safety Investigation at the Same Time

## Angle
The 2026 Tesla Model Y just became the FIRST vehicle to pass NHTSA's brand-new ADAS safety tests. It also has the lowest fatality rate of any vehicle in FARS (0.03 per 100M VMT). And it's simultaneously part of NHTSA's largest-ever FSD investigation covering 3.2 million vehicles after crashes in reduced visibility. NHTSA is handing Tesla a safety trophy with one hand and serving a subpoena with the other.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely surprising after 272 articles? **YES.** We've done `tesla-paradox.html` (impairment rates), but the NHTSA ADAS test is brand new (May 2026) and nobody has connected three data points: lowest FARS rate + first ADAS pass + largest FSD probe.

## Primary Sources

### 1. FARS Data (NHTSA, 2014-2023)
- **Model Y**: 57 deaths, 112 fatal crashes, 1.75M fleet, rate 0.03/100M VMT — LOWEST of 337 models
- **Model 3**: 92 deaths, 212 crashes, 1.575M fleet, rate 0.05
- **Model S**: 100 deaths, 134 crashes, 175K fleet, rate 0.50
- **Model X**: 29 deaths, 45 crashes, 157.5K fleet, rate 0.15
- For comparison: RAV4 rate 0.19, Civic rate 2.25, Mustang rate 6.02

### 2. NHTSA ADAS Test (May 2026)
- 2026 Model Y first vehicle to pass NHTSA's new NCAP ADAS criteria
- Tests cover: pedestrian AEB, lane keeping, blind spot warning, blind spot intervention
- 8 total test criteria; Model Y passed all
- Implementation delayed to 2027 for other manufacturers after Alliance for Automotive Innovation lobbying
- Source: Reuters, TechCrunch, Autoblog (all May 2026)

### 3. NHTSA FSD Engineering Analysis (2025-2026)
- Covers 3.2 million Tesla vehicles (Models S, 3, X, Y)
- FSD camera-based system fails to detect glare, dust, fog
- 9+ incidents including 1 fatal crash, 2 injuries
- Upgraded from preliminary evaluation (2024) to engineering analysis
- Fatal crash: November 2023
- Third concurrent federal probe into FSD
- Source: Reuters, Electrek, NHTSA.gov, Carscoops

### 4. IIHS Context
- 2026 IIHS Top Safety Pick+ awards require Good scores in front/side crashes + pedestrian safety
- Tesla Model Y has earned TSP+ in recent years
- IIHS rates the structural crashworthiness, NHTSA now rates the ADAS — two complementary tests
- Source: IIHS.org, Consumer Reports

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulation of FARS rate data with NHTSA's new dual-track evaluation (ADAS safety award + FSD investigation). Nobody has connected these three simultaneous NHTSA actions on the same vehicle. The finding: NHTSA's own data infrastructure treats the same vehicle as both exemplar and threat depending on which test you run.

## Limitations
- FARS covers 2014-2023; Model Y only entered market 2020, so data window is short
- Rate of 0.03 is based on 57 deaths and estimated VMT; small-number effects may inflate the advantage
- FSD investigation hasn't concluded; defect may not be confirmed
- ADAS tests measure basic ADAS (pedestrian AEB at low speed), not FSD-level autonomy — they're different systems
- Fleet estimation methodology for Tesla may not be as reliable as for legacy OEMs

## Strongest Counterargument
The ADAS test and FSD investigation are measuring COMPLETELY DIFFERENT THINGS. The ADAS test checks basic emergency braking (pedestrian pops out, does the car stop?). The FSD probe examines high-autonomy driving in degraded visibility. Celebrating the ADAS pass while investigating FSD isn't a paradox — it's two different evaluations of two different systems, and conflating them is journalistic malpractice. The real story is that regulators are correctly evaluating each capability independently.

## Actionable Takeaway
- If shopping for an SUV: the Model Y has the lowest fatality rate in FARS, period. The physics (heavy, low center of gravity, modern crumple zones) matter more than the headlines.
- If using FSD: understand that the system has documented failure modes in fog, glare, and dust. Keep hands on wheel especially in low-visibility conditions.
- Check your vehicle's NHTSA safety rating at nhtsa.gov. The new ADAS ratings are being rolled out.
