# Research: "Park Outside" Summer 2026

## Angle
In 30 days (June 9 – July 10, 2026), NHTSA issued "park outside" fire-risk warnings for 1.54 million vehicles across two separate recalls. "Park outside" means: your car is too dangerous to keep in your garage. It could spontaneously catch fire while you sleep. Two separate automakers. Two completely unrelated defects. One shared instruction: get it away from your house.

The Kia recall is a RE-RECALL — the 2024 fix failed, fires continued.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Jeep Wrangler/Gladiator Recall (June 4/9, 2026)
- NHTSA Campaign: 26V363000, Stellantis recall #21D
- Vehicles: 1,076,999 (787,887 Wranglers + 289,112 Gladiators), MY 2021–2025
- Defect: Electric hydraulic power steering pump wiring overheats → fire, even with ignition OFF
- Confirmed: 51 fires (Autoblog says 72 field reports, 35 confirmed at EHPSP interface), 1 injury
- Status as of July 18: Remedy expected "no later than July" — still no confirmed available fix
- NHTSA: "park outside and away from structures until their vehicle is repaired"
- Source: NHTSA, Reuters (June 7), Autoblog (June 9), AARP, MotorTrend, Carfax

### 2. Kia Telluride Recall (July 9-10, 2026)
- NHTSA recall, Kia recall #SC316
- Vehicles: 462,869, MY 2020–2024
- Defect: Front power seat motor overheats when slide knob becomes dislodged → fire
- Confirmed: 18 incidents (7 fires, 11 melted motors) between Oct 2024 – April 2026
- KEY FACT: This is a RE-RECALL. Previous recall 24V407 (2024) for the same defect. Kia's new investigation found "sporadic dealer workmanship issues" — meaning the first fix was done wrong, and fires continued
- New fix: Electronic fuse assembly (available early August)
- Owner notification letters: Aug 13
- Source: NHTSA, Autoblog (Jul 9), Washington Examiner (Jul 10), TMJ4/AP (Jul 10), TopSpeed (Jul 10)

### 3. Subaru Park Outside (earlier in 2026)
- ~70,000 Forester Hybrid and Crosstrek Hybrid for fuel filler cap leaks → fire risk when temp rises
- Advised: "park outdoors and keep fuel tank below 50% capacity"
- Source: NY Post (Jul 14), citing NHTSA

## FARS Data

- Rank: 22nd of 121 SUVs by death rate
- Toxicology: 19.3% of drivers in fatal crashes tested positive for any substance (14.5% alcohol, 7.8% drugs)

### Kia Telluride

## Original Contribution
Nobody has aggregated the "park outside" recalls as a trend/category. The concept itself — NHTSA telling you your car is too dangerous for your own garage — is the story. The 1.54M figure in 30 days is novel. The Kia re-recall + dealer workmanship angle hasn't been explored in the vehicle safety press.

Cross-referencing with FARS: Wrangler already had elevated crash death rate (0.84, 53% above SUV avg) before the fire recall. Telluride was among the safest SUVs by FARS rate (0.04).

## Strongest Counterargument
The actual fire incidence is low relative to the recall population. 51 fires out of 1.08M Jeeps = 0.005%. 18 incidents out of 463K Tellurides = 0.004%. NHTSA issues "park outside" as a precaution, not because every vehicle will catch fire. The recalls are working as intended — defects discovered, owners notified, repairs forthcoming.
