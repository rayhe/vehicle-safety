# Research: The Recall Remedy Quality Gap

## Angle
NHTSA's recall system is built to measure process, not outcomes. They track whether dealers perform the repair. They don't track whether the repair actually fixes the defect. The Ram BTSI case (RQ25003) is the poster child: 1.66 million trucks recalled twice for the same defect, dealers performed the repairs, and NHTSA is now investigating because the defect keeps happening.

**Kill test:** Is this genuinely newsworthy after 280 articles?
YES. We've covered unfixed recalls (completion rates), recall volume (tsunami), and recall authority (paper tiger). We have NOT covered the specific gap where the remedy itself is inadequate — where the fix doesn't fix. This is the quality assurance blind spot: NHTSA measures process compliance, not outcome verification.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Investigation RQ25003 (opened July 3, 2025)
- **Subject:** Post-Recall Remedy Brake Transmission Shift Interlock (BTSI) Failure
- **Manufacturer:** Chrysler (FCA US, LLC), now Stellantis
- **Affected vehicles:** 2013-2018 Ram 1500, 2500, 3500, 4500, 5500 (column-mounted shift lever)
- **Estimated affected population:** 1,187,232 vehicles
- **Original recalls:** 17V-821 (Dec 2017, 1,482,874 vehicles) and 18V-100 (180,462 vehicles), total 1,663,336
- **Post-remedy complaints:** 14 VOQ reports + 6 EWR Death & Injury reports
- **Post-remedy outcomes:** 12 crashes/fires, 7 injuries, 0 fatalities
- **Defect:** BTSI locking pin sticks in open position, allowing transmission to shift out of Park without brake pedal pressed or key in ignition → vehicle rollaway
- **Dealer remedy:** Check BTSI operation, replace assembly if necessary, reprogram body control module, add owner's manual addendum
- **Investigator:** Robert Caple; Reviewer: Bruce York; Approver: Tanya Topka
- Source: https://static.nhtsa.gov/odi/inv/2025/INOA-RQ25003-13801.pdf

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing NHTSA's recall system with the Ram BTSI timeline reveals a structural gap:
1. Dec 2017: First recall (17V-821) — 1.48M trucks
2. 2018: Second recall (18V-100) — 180K additional trucks
3. Post-repair: 20 total incident reports, 12 crashes, 7 injuries
4. July 2025: NHTSA opens Recall Query — 8+ YEARS after the first recall, investigating whether the fix works

The system has no mechanism to proactively verify remedy effectiveness. NHTSA only learns the fix failed when injured owners file complaints.

## Strongest Counterargument
The recall system was designed for scale, not perfection. With 300+ million vehicles and thousands of recalls, verifying each remedy's long-term effectiveness would require resources NHTSA doesn't have. The current system relies on post-market surveillance (complaints) as a feedback loop, which, while slower than proactive verification, does eventually catch failures — as the Ram case demonstrates.

## Limitations
- FARS data doesn't isolate rollaway-specific fatalities by vehicle model
- We don't know how many of the 1.66M recalled Ram trucks actually received the repair
- Post-remedy complaint volume (20 reports) is small relative to the affected population — it's possible the remedy works for the vast majority
- NHTSA's Recall Query may conclude the remedy IS adequate and the post-remedy failures have a different root cause
