# Research: FARS Drug Testing Blind Spot

## Angle
FARS toxicology data dramatically undercounts drug involvement in fatal crashes. Our data shows ~9% drug-positive. New peer-reviewed research says 55%. The gap is a measurement failure.

## Kill Test
- ✅ Genuinely surprising: most people assume FARS tox data is reliable
- ✅ Novel computation: cross-referencing our per-vehicle FARS tox with new study showing 6x undercount
- ✅ Not a data dump: this is a methodological exposé with concrete implications
- ✅ 3+ primary sources: peer-reviewed study, NIJ, NHTSA/CODOT

## Primary Sources

### 1. Accident Analysis & Prevention, March 2026
**"Impact of the enhanced Fatality Analysis Reporting System on drug detection in fatally injured drivers"**
DOI: 10.1016/j.aap.2025.108354
- Repeated cross-sectional study, n=138,505 fatally injured drivers (2013-2022)
- Alcohol prevalence STABLE: 37.1% (2013-2017) → 37.9% (2018-2022)
- Any nonalcohol drug: 39.9% → 55.0% (p < 0.001)
- Marijuana: 14.4% → 26.4% (nearly doubled)
- Stimulants: 9.3% → 25.1% (nearly tripled — meth/fentanyl)
- Both alcohol + marijuana: 8.3% → 11.8%
- 2+ nonalcohol drugs: 13.6% → 19.2%
- 3+ nonalcohol drugs: 2.0% → 5.8% (tripled)
- Enhanced FARS data collection in 2018 increased detection odds 42% (aOR 1.42)
- Pre-2018 FARS limited to 3 nonalcohol drugs per driver

### 2. NIJ Study - Drug-Impaired Driving: Emerging and Undertested Drugs
URL: https://nij.ojp.gov/topics/articles/drug-impaired-driving-contribution-emerging-and-undertested-drugs
- 2,514 suspected DUID cases, tested for 850 substances
- 79% tested positive for drugs (Tier I or II)
- Only 40% positive for alcohol
- 23% had BOTH drugs and alcohol
- **Stop-limit testing**: if BAC ≥ 0.08, labs STOP testing for drugs entirely
- Rationale: budget constraints, "impairment already explained," no enhanced penalties

### 3. NHTSA/CODOT - FARS Drug Testing Completeness
URL: https://www.codot.gov/safety/shift-into-safe-news/2025/january/...
- FARS drug testing completeness varies from >90% to <10% by state
- Average: only 60% of fatally injured drivers are tested for drugs
- GAO and NTSB have both flagged data unreliability
- NHTSA added new data fields in 2022 to improve
- Oral fluid testing being deployed as a solution

## Our FARS Data (2014-2023)
- 490,736 total drivers in fatal crashes
- 15.1% alcohol positive (74,188)
- 8.7% drug positive (42,496)
- 3.7% both (18,336 via inclusion-exclusion)
- 20.0% any impairment (98,348)
- By class: Sports Cars highest any% (22.5%), Vans lowest (18.1%)

## The Gap
- Our data: 8.7% drug positive
- Published research: 55% of fatally injured drivers drug positive
- That's a 6.3x undercount

## Three Causes of the Undercount
1. **Incomplete testing**: Only 60% of drivers tested on average, varies 10-90% by state
2. **Stop-limit testing**: If BAC ≥ 0.08, many labs don't test for drugs at all
3. **Pre-2018 FARS cap**: Before 2018, FARS only recorded 3 nonalcohol drugs per driver
4. **Testing scope**: Most labs test only Tier I (35 drugs). Full panel finds way more.

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing per-vehicle FARS toxicology with published research to show the magnitude of the drug testing blind spot. Nobody has done this comparison at the vehicle level. When we say a vehicle has "low drug involvement" in FARS, we're saying "nobody bothered to test."
