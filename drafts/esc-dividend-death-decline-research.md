# Research: The ESC Dividend — Why Traffic Deaths Are Really Falling

## Journalist: Axle McScatter (Data Visualization Editor)
## Kicker: By The Numbers

## Thesis
The 12% decline in US traffic fatalities in 2025 (37,810 deaths, per NSC) is not random fluctuation or the result of speed cameras or public awareness campaigns. It's a structural dividend: the "kill zone" — vehicle ages 11-17, where deaths peak — is transitioning from pre-ESC vehicles to post-ESC vehicles. FARS data shows this age cohort swap produces a 34.6% death reduction. This is a one-time windfall, not a trend.

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulating FARS model year death data with the vehicle age death distribution to show:
1. Deaths peak at vehicle age 14 (not new, not ancient — the sweet spot of old-enough-to-lack-features + young-enough-to-still-drive)
2. In 2025, the kill zone (age 11-17) hits MY 2008-2014 — the ESC transition era
3. The pre-ESC kill zone (MY 2001-2007) had 71,778 deaths; the post-ESC zone (MY 2008-2014) had 46,914 — a 34.6% reduction (24,864 fewer deaths)
4. 57.6% of all FARS deaths came from pre-2008 vehicles
5. The improvement will plateau once pre-ESC vehicles fully retire, until the AEB cohort (2023+ standard) enters the kill zone around ~2037

## Key Data Points
- 2025 US traffic deaths: 37,810 (NSC preliminary), down 12% from 2024
- VMT increased 0.9% in 2025 — so the decline is real, not just fewer miles
- FARS death peak: vehicle age 14 (~11,363 deaths)
- Ages 11-17 account for a disproportionate share of deaths (72,273 deaths in FARS data)
- ESC mandate: FMVSS 126, effective 2012 model year (all new LTVs)
- IIHS: ESC reduces fatal single-vehicle crashes ~50%, all fatal crashes ~33%
- Pre-ESC kill zone (MY 2001-2007): 71,778 deaths
- Post-ESC kill zone (MY 2008-2014): 46,914 deaths
- Delta: 24,864 fewer deaths (34.6% reduction)
- AEB voluntary agreement: most OEMs by 2023, FMVSS mandate coming 2029

- Surprising? YES — most coverage attributes the decline to "safety efforts" vaguely; this identifies the structural mechanism

## Limitations
- FARS data is 2014-2023; 2025 deaths are NSC preliminary (different methodology)
- ESC adoption was gradual (many pre-2012 vehicles had it voluntarily)
- The "kill zone" age is approximate (FARS doesn't track exact crash dates per record in our dataset)
- State-level variance (California -40%) likely has additional factors beyond fleet age
- VMT estimates have ±15% uncertainty for individual models

## Strongest Counterargument
The decline could be driven by factors unrelated to ESC: improved road design, DUI enforcement, ride-sharing reducing drunk driving, COVID-era behavior shifts, or simply reversion to mean after the 2020-2022 spike. ESC is one variable in a complex system. However, the magnitude of the kill-zone death reduction (34.6%) closely matches IIHS's predicted ESC effectiveness (33% for all fatal crashes), suggesting it's a dominant — not sole — factor.
