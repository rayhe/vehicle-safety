# Research: The Kill-Switch Ceiling

## Angle
Congress mandated impaired-driving detection technology in all new cars (HALT Drunk Driving Act, Section 24220, Bipartisan Infrastructure Law 2021). The tech must "passively monitor the performance of a driver" and "prevent or limit motor vehicle operation if an impairment is detected." NHTSA missed the November 2024 rule deadline. Congress voted 268-164 in January 2026 to keep the mandate alive.

The FARS data lets us compute the exact ceiling of what this technology could possibly achieve — and it's lower than the headline numbers suggest.

## Novel Computation: The Ceiling

### FARS Toxicology (2014-2023, 490,736 drivers in fatal crashes)
- Any impairment: 98,348 drivers (20.0%)
- Alcohol-positive: 74,188 (15.1%)
- Drug-positive: 42,496 (8.7%)
- SOBER: 392,388 (80.0%)

### NHTSA Official Numbers (different methodology)
- 2024: 11,904 deaths in alcohol-impaired crashes
- 2023: 12,429 deaths
- NHTSA counts ALL deaths in crashes where any driver had BAC ≥ 0.08 (including sober passengers and other-vehicle occupants killed by drunk drivers)
- That's why NHTSA says "30% of fatalities" while FARS tox shows 15.1% of drivers

### The Kill-Switch Ceiling Calculation
- Technology targets alcohol impairment (BAC sensors, eye tracking, driving pattern analysis)
- NHTSA's own number: ~12,000 alcohol-impaired crash deaths/year
- Total traffic deaths: ~40,000/year
- **Theoretical max: ~30% of deaths prevented** (if tech is 100% effective, 100% deployed, 0% false positives)
- Reality: no tech achieves 100%. Ignition interlocks have ~70% effectiveness per IIHS.

### The False Positive Math (ORIGINAL)
- ~280 million registered vehicles in the US
- Kill-switch tech monitors ALL of them
- False positive rate of 0.1% = 280,000 incorrect lockouts
- False positive rate of 1.0% = 2,800,000 incorrect lockouts
- Even a "good" 99.9% accuracy rate strands 280K sober drivers

### What the tech CAN'T detect
- Drug impairment (8.7% of fatal crash drivers) — no BAC sensor catches THC, opioids, meth
- Distracted driving (~3,300 deaths/year per NHTSA)
- Drowsy driving (~800+ deaths/year per NHTSA)
- Speeding (~12,000 deaths/year per NHTSA)
- The 80% of fatal crashes involving completely sober drivers

### The IIHS Ignition Interlock Study
- Existing interlock devices reduce repeat DUI crashes by ~70%
- But interlocks only target convicted DUI offenders, not all drivers
- IIHS study: if all-offender interlocks were universally mandated, ~7,000 lives/year
