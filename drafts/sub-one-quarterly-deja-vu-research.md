# Research: Sub-One Quarterly Déjà Vu

## Angle
Q1 2026 fatality rate of 0.99 per 100M VMT — "lowest since 2014." But Q1 2014 was ALSO 0.99. Twelve years, 500 billion more miles driven annually, 50 million more registered vehicles, and the best America can do is tie its own winter quarter record. The annual rate has NEVER been below 1.0.

## Self-Critique
Is this genuinely surprising after 452 articles? YES — the data was released TODAY (Jul 8, 2026, Reuters/NHTSA). We've covered fatality trends before, but this is about the ILLUSION of progress: a Q1 number always looks good because winter suppresses driving. Q1 has been below 1.0 before (Q1 2014). The story isn't "we broke a record." The story is "we matched a 12-year-old seasonal low and called it progress."

## FARS Cross-Analysis (Original Contribution)

From our FARS 2014-2023 data:
- Sedans: 89,127 deaths from 138,154 crashes (64.5% lethality per crash)
- SUVs: 46,442 deaths from 88,568 crashes (52.4% lethality per crash)
- Pickups: 41,593 deaths from 85,128 crashes (48.9% lethality per crash)

Fleet shift calculation: In 2014, sedans were ~45% of new sales. By 2026, sedans are ~20%. SUVs went from ~33% to ~55%. This fleet composition shift ALONE accounts for a meaningful portion of the rate decline — not because vehicles got safer per mile, but because deadlier vehicle classes are being replaced by less-lethal-per-crash vehicle classes.

The irony: the rate decline is partly because Americans switched from sedans (64.5% crash-to-death ratio) to SUVs (52.4% crash-to-death ratio). The occupants of bigger vehicles survive more. The pedestrians those bigger vehicles hit... don't. Pedestrian deaths have been RISING as the fleet shifts to SUVs and trucks.

## Counterargument (Strongest)
The 0.99 rate represents real progress: fewer people are dying even as VMT increases. AEB mandates, lane-departure warnings, and advanced airbags are genuinely saving lives. Fleet composition shift doesn't explain all of the decline — the per-vehicle death rate within each class is also falling. The 4.3% Q1 decline is meaningful even if Q1 always looks better than Q3.

## Limitations
- Q1 is always the lowest quarter seasonally
- 0.99 rounds to the same as Q1 2014's 0.99 — may not be a true new low
- VMT estimates are projections, not actual odometer readings
- State-level data has reporting lag issues (New York's -72% may be a reporting artifact)
- FARS data (2014-2023) doesn't perfectly overlap with the 2026 discussion
