# Research: Teen Drivers Get the Oldest Car — And the Highest Fatality Risk

**Slug:** teen-oldest-car-fatality-premium
**Journalist:** Clara Rollover
**Kicker:** The Gap

## Core Thesis
Parents default to handing their teen the oldest car in the family. FARS data shows this "hand-me-down" instinct creates a measurable fatality premium: teens in vehicles >15 years old face 31% higher risk of dying in a fatal crash, and the vehicles they drive are the least likely to have crash avoidance tech that IIHS estimates could prevent 78% of teen driver deaths.

Meanwhile, the Mott Poll shows 96% of parents rate their teen as an "average" or "better than average" driver — even as 51% observe unsafe driving behavior. Only 24% take action.

## Primary Sources (3+)

### 1. JAMA Network Open — Vehicle Age and Driver Assistance Technologies in Fatal Crashes (2025)
- **Source:** PMC (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12059974/)
- 81,145 drivers in fatal crashes (2016-2021), FARS data
- **27.6% of teen drivers** in fatal crashes driving vehicles >15 years old (vs 22.8% mid-age)
- **Only 18.1% of teens** in vehicles ≤5 years old (vs 29.2% mid-age)
- Vehicles >15 years: aRR 1.31 (95% CI: 1.28-1.34) — 31% higher death risk
- Vehicles 6-15 years: aRR 1.19 (95% CI: 1.17-1.22) — 19% higher death risk
- Each installed ADAS tech: 6% reduction in fatality risk (aRR 0.94, CI: 0.90-0.98)

### 2. IIHS — Driving Technology Promises Large Safety Benefits for Teens (2021)
- **Source:** https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/driving-technology-promises-large-safety-benefits-for-teens
- Crash avoidance + teen-specific tech could prevent **78% of teen driver deaths**
- AEB alone reduces relevant crashes by 50%
- Speeding = ~40% of teen driver deaths; ~40% of dead teen drivers unbelted
- Only ~25% of vehicles on road have AEB + LDW + BSM
- Teens drive older, less-equipped vehicles than other drivers

### 3. Mott Children's Hospital National Poll — Driving Habits of Teens and Young Adults (March 2026)
- **Source:** https://mottpoll.org/reports/driving-habits-teens-and-young-adults
- **51% of parents** observed at least 1 unsafe driving behavior
- **Only 4%** rated their teen's driving as "worse than average"
- **43%** rated their teen as "better than average" — Lake Wobegon effect
- 41% speeding, 17% texting, 12% tailgating
- 17% observed impaired driving (sleepy, upset, alcohol, marijuana)
- Only **24%** took steps to address behavior
- Parents who saw aggressive driving were LESS likely to rate kid as worse

### 4. IIHS Teenagers Fatality Data
- **Source:** https://www.iihs.org/topics/fatality-statistics/detail/teenagers
- 3,048 teen deaths in 2023
- Teens have 4x crash rate per mile vs adults 20+
- Teen fatal crash rates: 13.3 per 100K for ages 20-24
- 26% of fatally injured teen drivers had BAC ≥0.08%
- Motor vehicle crashes = #1 cause of death for US teens

### 5. IIHS — Parents and MyKey Awareness
- Only ~half of parents who owned Ford MyKey-equipped vehicles knew the tech existed
- Of those who knew, ~1/3 didn't use it
- Some parents said they didn't think it had safety benefits

## Kill Test
**Is this genuinely newsworthy?** YES. The JAMA study was published in 2025 and the Mott Poll in March 2026. The convergence of three independent data sources (FARS crash outcomes, IIHS tech effectiveness, parental behavior survey) creates a novel synthesis. The counterintuitive finding — parents give their most vulnerable driver the least safe vehicle — is actionable and emotionally resonant.

**Novel angle on data?** YES. Most teen driver coverage focuses on behavior (don't text, don't speed). This flips it: the VEHICLE itself is a risk factor parents control, and they're making the wrong choice for observable, fixable reasons.

## Actionable Takeaways
- Give your teen the NEWEST car in the household, not the oldest
- Check which car has AEB, LDW, BSM — that's the teen car
- Activate teen-specific tech (Ford MyKey, GM Teen Driver, Hyundai BlueLink)
- Vehicle age >15 years = 31% fatality premium. That's measurable.
- If buying a teen's first car: prioritize crash avoidance over mileage/price
