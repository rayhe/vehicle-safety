# Research Notes: blazer-head-to-head
## Article #363 — Rex Driverton

### Angle
IIHS just crashed a 1996 Blazer into a 2026 Blazer head-to-head. The dummy's head popped off. Our FARS data has the actual body count behind the test.

### Primary Sources
1. **IIHS press release (June 24, 2026):** "IIHS crashworthiness tests save nearly 50,000 lives since program's launch" — https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/iihs-crashworthiness-tests-save-nearly-50-000-lives-since-programs-launch
2. **NHTSA FARS database (2014-2023):** Our fars_output.js cross-tabulations
3. **IIHS 1996 Blazer rating page:** https://www.iihs.org/ratings/vehicle/chevrolet/blazer-4-door-suv/1996

### Key Facts from IIHS Study
- 48,352 lives saved from 1999-2024
- $538 billion societal economic benefits
- 900-fold ROI ($600M IIHS funding → $538B saved)
- 1996 Blazer: dummy's head literally detached from body (neck nodding joint broke)
- Occupant compartment of 1996 model crushed — dashboard/steering column pushed into dummy's lap
- 33cm footwell intrusion, 20cm instrument panel rearward movement
- 2026 Blazer: driver would have walked away with bumps and bruises
- 1996 Blazer rated Poor (1995-2004 models); 2019+ Blazers earn Good

### FARS Data Cross-Reference
- **Chevrolet Blazer:** 369 deaths, rate 0.56 (mostly new platform 2019+)
- **Chevrolet Trailblazer:** 2,473 deaths, rate 2.83 (successor to old Blazer platform)
- **Chevrolet Tracker:** 856 deaths, rate 7.83 (old GM small SUV, "double dangerous" score 97.8)
- Old Blazer model years in FARS: 1996=23, 2000=45 (peak), 2002=44, then decline
- New Blazer: 2019=12, 2020=23 deaths
- Trailblazer peak: 2003=430, 2004=409 deaths per model year

### Original Contribution
IIHS did the crash test; we connect it to actual fatality data. The old Blazer platform and its successors (Trailblazer, Tracker) accumulated 3,698 deaths in FARS. The 1996 model year alone: 23 Blazer deaths + 58 Trailblazer deaths = 81 deaths from that single model year cohort. The "Poor" rating that applies to 1995-2004 models corresponds to the deadliest window in FARS.

### Counterargument
Survivorship bias: the old Blazers still on the road are driven differently (higher rural use, lower-income demographics, less maintenance) which inflates their fatality rate beyond what the structural differences alone would predict. The IIHS test isolates engineering; FARS captures everything.
