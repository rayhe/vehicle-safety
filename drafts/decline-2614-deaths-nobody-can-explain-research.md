# Research Notes: The 2,614 Missing Deaths

## Data Sources
1. **NHTSA 2025 Full-Year Data** (released April 2026)
   - 2025 fatalities: 36,640 (down 6.7% from 39,254 in 2024)
   - Fatality rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT (second-lowest ever recorded)
   - VMT increased by 29.8 billion miles (0.9% increase)
   - Fatalities decreased in 39 states, DC, and Puerto Rico
   
2. **NHTSA Q1 2026 Data** (released July 6, 2026)
   - Q1 2026 fatalities: 7,770 (down 4.3% from Q1 2025)
   - Fatality rate: 0.99 per 100M VMT (second-lowest Q1 ever, record is 0.98)
   - Americans drove 11 billion MORE miles than Q1 2025
   - 30 states and Puerto Rico saw declines

3. **NHTSA 2025 Speeding Data**
   - 2025 speeding deaths: 10,035 (down 11% from 11,288 in 2024)
   - Speeding = 29% of all traffic fatalities
   - 2024 speed injuries: 316,757
   - Return to pre-pandemic levels last seen in 2019

4. **NHTSA 2024 FARS Final Data**
   - 39,254 deaths in 2024
   - Rate: 1.19 per 100M VMT

5. **FARS 2014-2023 Vehicle Data** (local fars_output.js)
   - 337 models tracked
   - Key finding: newer model year vehicles show dramatically fewer deaths
   - Fleet age matters: 62.3% of FARS deaths are in 10+ year old vehicles
   
## Historical Context
- 2019: 36,096 deaths (pre-pandemic baseline)
- 2020: 38,824 (pandemic surge begins despite less driving)
- 2021: 43,230 (peak — highest since 2005)
- 2022: ~42,795 (still elevated)
- 2023: 40,901 (decline begins)
- 2024: 39,254 (continued decline)
- 2025: 36,640 (below pre-pandemic 2019 levels!)

## The Mystery: 2,614 Fewer Deaths
39,254 (2024) - 36,640 (2025) = 2,614 fewer deaths

What saved 2,614 lives? Candidates:
1. **AEB adoption**: AEB now standard on more new vehicles, proven 50% reduction in rear-end crashes
2. **Fleet turnover**: older, more dangerous vehicles leaving the road
3. **Speed decline**: speeding deaths down 11% (1,253 fewer speed deaths alone)
4. **Post-COVID normalization**: return to normal driving patterns
5. **Enforcement campaigns**: increased speed and DUI enforcement

## Novel Original Analysis
The speed decline alone (1,253 fewer deaths) accounts for 48% of the total decline of 2,614. If the remaining 1,361 lives were saved by vehicle technology improvements, that's roughly 3.7 lives per day saved by factors OTHER than reduced speeding.

## Limitations
- 2025 data is early estimate, may be revised
- FARS vehicle-level data is 2014-2023, not 2025
- Can't directly attribute decline to specific technologies from this data
- VMT estimates have ±1-2% uncertainty
- Speed death estimates may have different counting methodology than total fatalities
