# Research: The Rate-Impairment Quadrant — Why the Drunkest Drivers Have the Lowest Death Rates

## Thesis
Cross-tabulating FARS fatality rates (per 100M VMT) with FARS toxicology impairment rates produces a 2×2 matrix that reveals a counterintuitive finding: vehicles with the HIGHEST driver impairment rates don't have the highest death rates. Mass and crashworthiness overwhelm behavior. Meanwhile, vehicles with sober drivers are dying at extraordinary rates because of structural deficiency.

## Novel Contribution
This is an original cross-tabulation nobody has published. Rate × impairment scatterplot with quadrant analysis.

## The Four Quadrants (median thresholds: rate=1.5 per 100M VMT, impairment=20%)

### Quadrant 1: "Predictable Killers" — High Rate + High Impairment (27 vehicles)
What you'd expect: old muscle cars, budget sedans, aging trucks. Both the vehicle and the driver are the problem.
- Ford Mustang: rate=6.02, impaired=21.9%, 2,739 deaths
- Nissan Maxima: rate=5.11, impaired=20.9%, 1,544 deaths
- Chevrolet Cobalt: rate=5.1, impaired=22.4%, 1,540 deaths
- Chevrolet Impala: rate=5.0, impaired=21.4%, 3,774 deaths
- Chevrolet S-10: rate=4.83, impaired=20.5%, 1,427 deaths
- Chevrolet Camaro: rate=3.44, impaired=23%, 1,204 deaths

### Quadrant 2: "Sober Deathtraps" — High Rate + Low Impairment (20 vehicles)
The disturbing finding: sober people dying at extraordinary rates because their vehicles failed them.
- Hyundai Veloster: rate=8.54, impaired=17.4%, 598 deaths
- Chevrolet Tracker: rate=7.83, impaired=12.7%, 856 deaths
- Toyota Land Cruiser: rate=6.27, impaired=8.9%, 343 deaths
- Toyota Solara: rate=4.25, impaired=4.1%, 642 deaths
- Cadillac Seville: rate=3.89, impaired=10.5%, 391 deaths

### Quadrant 3: "Drunk Survivors" — Low Rate + High Impairment (31 vehicles)
The paradox: the most impaired drivers have LOWER death rates. Why? Mass. These vehicles are heavy enough to protect even impaired occupants.
- Dodge dominates: Neon 23.2%, Charger 22.7%, Challenger 22.5%, Durango 22.5%
- Ford Crown Victoria: 22.3% impaired, rate only 1.46
- Kia Optima: 22% impaired, rate only 0.58
- Chevrolet Avalanche: 21.9% impaired, rate only 0.78

### Quadrant 4: "Safe + Sober" — Low Rate + Low Impairment (39 vehicles)
Modern crossovers dominate: RAV4, Equinox, Rogue, Tucson, Pilot, Sorento.
- Toyota RAV4: rate=0.19, impaired=18.4%
- Honda Pilot: rate=0.29, impaired=19.4%
- Kia Sorento: rate=0.29, impaired=18%

## Key Insight
The largest quadrant is "Safe + Sober" (39 vehicles) — modern crossovers with adequate mass, current safety tech, and demographics that don't skew toward impairment. The smallest is "Sober Deathtraps" (20 vehicles) — vehicles that kill sober people through structural inadequacy.

## The Real Story
Physics beats behavior. An impaired driver in a 5,000-lb SUV is statistically safer than a sober driver in a 2,400-lb subcompact. The implication: we spent decades building anti-drunk-driving campaigns when the deadlier problem might have been inadequate vehicle mass requirements.

## Counterargument
- Selection bias: FARS only captures FATAL crashes. An impaired driver in a big truck might cause more total crashes (including non-fatal) but survive them. The death data flatters the truck.
- VMT estimation uncertainty: ±15% for low-volume models.
- Impairment testing inconsistency: Not all jurisdictions test all drivers in fatal crashes. Testing rates vary by state and crash type.
