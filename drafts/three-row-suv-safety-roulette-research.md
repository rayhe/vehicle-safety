# Research: Three-Row SUV Safety Roulette

## Angle
The 3-row family SUV segment shows the widest safety spread of any single-purpose vehicle category. Parents buying vehicles specifically to haul children face a 62x difference in death rate between the safest and deadliest options.

## Self-Critique Gate
- **Propose:** 3-row SUVs occupy a unique market position — they're bought explicitly for family use. The death rate spread (Telluride 0.04 vs Tahoe 2.49) is 62x within vehicles bought for the SAME purpose. That's not a sedan vs sports car comparison — it's minivan vs minivan.
- **Challenge:** Is this just another segment ranking? We have same-segment-lethality-rankings.html already. NO — this is specifically about vehicles marketed TO FAMILIES for child transport. The stakes are different. And the 62x spread is the largest we've found in any single-purpose category.
- **Verdict:** PROCEED. The parental angle + 62x spread = genuinely shareable.

## Novel Cross-Tabulation
- 62x spread: Telluride (0.04) to Tahoe (2.49)
- The Tahoe kills at 12.5x the Traverse rate — both are GM vehicles
- Impairment is NOT the explanation: Tahoe impairment (20.6%) vs Telluride (17.5%) is only a 3pp gap, not 62x
- The Explorer has nearly 4,000 deaths on a 2M fleet; Highlander has 1,106 on 2.1M fleet
- Explorer vs Highlander: 3.7x death rate gap for vehicles of similar price and purpose

## Methodology
- Rate = deaths per 100 million VMT (vehicle miles traveled)
- Fleet = estimated registered vehicles (NHTSA + industry sales data)
- Impairment = any positive BAC or drug screen in FARS toxicology
- FARS captures only fatal crashes (not all crashes)
- Newer vehicles (Telluride since 2020, Palisade since 2020) have less exposure time, potentially inflating low counts

## Limitations
- Fleet estimates carry ±15% uncertainty for lower-volume models
- FARS only records fatal crashes — the Tahoe might have excellent crashworthiness despite its rate (heavier vehicle more likely to kill others, not occupants)
- The Telluride/Palisade/Atlas have only 3-4 years of data; their rates may increase as vehicles age
- Body-on-frame (Tahoe, 4Runner) vs unibody (Telluride, Palisade) is a confounding variable — frame type affects crash dynamics
- Vehicle age in fleet matters: older Tahoes/Explorers have no ESC, while all Tellurides have modern safety tech

## Strongest Counterargument
The Tahoe and Explorer fleet includes millions of 15-20+ year old vehicles with no ESC, no AEB, and minimal airbag coverage. The Telluride fleet is 100% post-2020 with full ADAS suites. This is partly a fleet age comparison, not a pure engineering comparison. A 2024 Tahoe vs 2024 Telluride would show a much smaller gap.

## Actionable Takeaways
- If shopping for a 3-row SUV: Telluride, Palisade, Atlas, CX-9 are the safest per mile
- Avoid older Explorer and Tahoe model years (pre-2020 especially)
- Check VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls
- Weight and body-on-frame construction correlate with higher OTHER-vehicle deaths
- Modern safety tech (ESC, AEB, lane keeping) is the primary differentiator
