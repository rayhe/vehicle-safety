# Research Notes: Recall Theater — The Sharp Edge vs. 1,515 Deaths

## Angle
Ford recalled 548,463 Expeditions (2018-2024) for chrome plating that can bubble and create sharp edges on the center console. Zero deaths, zero crashes from the defect. The same vehicle has 1,515 deaths in FARS (2014-2023), a rate of 2.31 per 100M VMT. The recall system is structurally incapable of addressing the thing that actually kills people.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Ford Expedition Chrome Console Recall (June 11, 2026)
- Source: Reuters, NHTSA
- 548,463 vehicles, 2018-2024 model years
- Defect: Chrome plating bubbles/peels → sharp edges → laceration risk
- Cause: Supplier used parameters that didn't meet Ford specs
- Remedy: Dealers inspect and replace consoles, free of charge
- Deaths from this defect: 0
- Crashes from this defect: 0
- Injuries reported: Not specified (risk of injury, not actual injuries)

### 2. FARS Data — Ford Expedition
- Total deaths: 1,515 (2014-2023)
- Annual: 151.5 deaths/year
- Fatal crashes: 2,641
- Fleet size: 525,000
- VMT (est): 6,562 million
- Rate: 2.31 per 100M VMT
- Death-to-crash ratio: 0.57 (moderate — it kills its occupants in more than half of fatal crashes)

### 3. NHTSA Recall Authority (49 USC § 30102, § 30118)
- Motor vehicle safety = "performance of a motor vehicle in a way that protects the public against unreasonable risk of accidents occurring because of the design, construction, or performance"
- Defect = "any defect in performance, construction, a component, or material"
- Key: The statute says "design, construction, or performance" — all three
- In PRACTICE: Recalls target manufacturing deviations, not inherent design risk
- 49 USC § 30118(d): Secretary can exempt from recall if defect is "inconsequential to motor vehicle safety"
- The chrome edge IS arguably inconsequential compared to the vehicle's inherent crash risk

### 5. The Math

## Original Analysis
1. Calculate ratio of "recalled defect risk" vs "inherent crash risk" for each vehicle recalled this week
2. Show that for MOST recalls (excluding genuine fire/structural risks like the Jeep), the recalled defect is orders of magnitude less dangerous than the vehicle's normal crash profile
3. The recall system creates false comfort: "My vehicle was recalled and fixed, so it's safe now" — when the vehicle's inherent design risk dwarfs any manufacturing defect

## Strongest Counterargument
The recall system works as designed. It catches manufacturing deviations that shouldn't exist. Design risk is addressed through FMVSS standards, crash testing, and consumer information (NCAP ratings). These are complementary systems, not redundant ones. Asking NHTSA to recall vehicles for inherent design risk would effectively ban entire vehicle categories.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers all model years, not just the recalled 2018-2024 cohort
- Older Expeditions (pre-2018) likely have higher death rates than newer ones
- The chrome edge recall addresses a real, if minor, injury risk
- "Deaths from design" is not a clean category — it conflates vehicle design, road design, driver behavior, and physics
