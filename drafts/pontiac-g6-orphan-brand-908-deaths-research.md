# Research: The Pontiac G6 — 908 Deaths in a Brand That No Longer Exists

## Angle
GM killed Pontiac in 2010 during bankruptcy. The Pontiac G6 has killed 908 people in the FARS database (2014-2023) — every single death after the brand ceased to exist. The G6 has the highest lethality of any mid-size sedan in its competitive set, and the highest drug impairment rate. A ghost brand's body count.

## Journalist
**Dale Impactor III** — Toxicology Desk Chief. Drug impairment angle fits his beat. Sardonic tone suits the absurdity of a dead brand killing people.

## Key Data

### FARS Core Stats
- Deaths: 908
- Fatal crashes: 1,299
- Lethality: 69.9% (908/1299)
- Fleet estimate: 481,250
- Annual deaths: 90.8
- Death rate: 1.64 per 100M VMT
- VMT estimate: 5,534 (100M)

### Toxicology (1,292 drivers tested)
- Any impairment: 22.1% (286 drivers)
- Alcohol: 16.2% (209 drivers)
- Drug: 10.4% (134 drivers)
- Drug rate is HIGHEST in mid-size sedan comparison group

### Recalls
1. **EPS recall 14V153000** (March 2014): Sudden loss of electric power steering at any speed. 1.37M vehicles affected across GM platforms.
2. **Brake light recall 09V036000** (Jan 2009): Corrosion causes brake lights to illuminate randomly or not work at all.
3. **EPS expansion 15V064000** (Feb 2015): Additional G6 vehicles added to power steering recall.
4. **Body control module 14V-252** (May 2014): Brake light malfunction, ESC/traction control disabled. 855 complaints, 8 crashes, 1 injury.

### GM/Pontiac History
- GM filed Chapter 11 bankruptcy June 1, 2009
- Pontiac officially discontinued October 31, 2010
- G6 production ran 2004-2010 (6 model years)
- Total US sales: ~750,000 units
- The G6 was Pontiac's best-selling model in its final years
