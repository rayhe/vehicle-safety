# Research: ESC Mandate Didn't Stop the Body Count

## Angle
Safety mandates work on a per-mile basis. ESC reduces single-vehicle fatal crashes by ~33% (IIHS). But when you compare pre-ESC mandate model years (2005-2011) to post-mandate model years (2013-2019), 15+ vehicles show HIGHER average annual death counts. Safety technology was overwhelmed by sales volume growth.

## Original Contribution
Novel FARS cross-tabulation: comparing average deaths per model year in pre-ESC (2005-2011) vs post-ESC (2013-2019) windows, filtered for vehicles with ≥50 pre-ESC deaths and ≥5 model years in each window.

### Confounding Factors (MUST state at full strength)
1. **Sales volume** — Kia Optima US sales went from ~60K/yr (2008) to ~130K/yr (2017). Equinox from ~60K to ~330K. RAV4 from ~150K to ~420K. More cars = more deaths, even if per-mile rate dropped.
2. **Fleet age** — newer model years have less exposure time in the FARS window (2014-2023). A 2019 MY has only 4-5 years of exposure vs 8-9 for a 2005 MY.
3. **Survivor bias** — older model years lose vehicles to scrappage, reducing their fleet exposure over time.

### What This Actually Shows
The raw body count per model year is NOT a death rate. It's a volume-weighted metric. But that's precisely the point: safety mandates are sold as saving X lives per year. The per-mile rate drops. But the absolute body count for individual popular models can still go UP because manufacturers sell more of them. The mandate works for the individual driver but the population-level death count for that specific model can increase.

This is the tension: ESC saves YOUR life. But it didn't save the Kia Optima's body count.

## Self-Critique
**Is this genuinely surprising after 474 articles?** Yes. This is a novel cross-tabulation nobody runs — comparing pre/post mandate death counts per model year. The finding (some vehicles got worse) is counterintuitive and forces a nuanced conversation about what "mandates work" actually means.

**Kill test:** The article must clearly acknowledge the confounding factors (sales volume, fleet age) and state the strongest counterargument at full strength. The thesis is NOT "ESC doesn't work." The thesis is "ESC works per-mile, but the body count for popular models still went up because more people bought them."
