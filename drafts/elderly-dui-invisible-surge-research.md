# Research: Elderly DUI Invisible Surge

## Angle
The fastest-growing DUI demographic is adults 66-90 (up 44.8% since 2019), yet enforcement, policy, and public awareness remain fixated on 21-34 year-olds. Our FARS data reveals that vehicles associated with elderly demographics already have some of the HIGHEST impairment rates in fatal crashes — this isn't a new problem, it's an accelerating one that nobody is watching.

## Kill Test
- Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES — the 44.8% surge among 66-90 year-olds is from new data released June 5, 2026
- Novel angle on data? YES — cross-referencing Avian Law Group's age-group DUI violation data with FARS per-vehicle toxicology data, connecting demographic vehicle preferences to impairment patterns
- Has this been covered? Partially — we've done Buick Park Avenue (#1 impairment) and LeSabre as individual vehicles, but NOT the trend narrative connecting post-pandemic behavioral shifts to the broader elderly DUI surge

## Primary Sources (3+)

### 1. Avian Law Group — "The $1.77 Trillion Wreck" (June 5, 2026)
- DUI violations among ages 66-90: UP 44.8% since 2019
- Ages 36-45: up 27.4%
- Post-pandemic speeding violations: up 55.5% vs only 1.2% more miles
- Total crash cost: $1.77 trillion annually
- 39,254 killed in 2024, 2.42 million injured
- Source: PR Newswire via Morningstar

### 2. NSC/CDC Injury Facts — Older Drivers (2024 data)
- 9,727 motor-vehicle deaths involving adults 65+ in 2024 (CDC estimate)
- Up 1.5% from 2023 (9,587)
- Up 33% over the last decade (population grew only 28% — rate increase of 3.5%)
- NHTSA estimate: 8,019 older adult traffic deaths in 2024
- 66% of deaths in crashes involving older drivers were the driver/passenger 65+ themselves
- 8,484 older drivers involved in fatal crashes (4,784 ages 65-74, 3,700 ages 75+)
- URL: https://injuryfacts.nsc.org/motor-vehicle/road-users/older-drivers/

### 3. FARS Toxicology Data (NHTSA, 2014-2023)
Our own dataset — original cross-tabulation:
- Buick Park Avenue: 31.7% impaired (HIGHEST in dataset), n=259
- Cadillac CTS: 25.9%, n=931
- Buick Verano: 24.7%, n=397
- Buick LeSabre: 23.5%, n=1,209
- Buick Lucerne: 22.0%, n=569
- Cadillac Escalade: 22.0%, n=1,169
- Lincoln Continental: 22.2%, n=176
- Lincoln Town Car: 20.4%, n=754

Elderly-skewing brands average 20-32% impairment, vs overall dataset average of ~20%. The "grandparent brands" are AT or ABOVE the drunk-driving average.

### 4. University of Memphis/Traffic Injury Prevention study
- More than 1/3 of drivers 70+ in two-vehicle fatal crashes tested positive for substances
- Substance use increases at-fault probability 2-4x for older drivers
- Lead author: Dr. Satish Kedia, University of Memphis School of Public Health

### 5. Brown University Health
- For drivers 70-79: crash risk equivalent to teenage drivers
- For drivers 80+: crash risk is FOUR TIMES higher than expected
- Per-mile crash rates increase sharply starting in 60s

### 6. CDC NSDUH data (2008-2012, published PubMed)
- Of past-year substance users aged 65+: 6.19% self-reported DUI
- Risk factors: higher frequency alcohol use, binge drinking, marijuana use, major depressive episode
- Lifetime arrest history was a predictor ONLY in the 65+ group (not younger)

## Original Contribution
1. Novel cross-tabulation: Avian Law Group's age-group DUI violation trends × FARS per-vehicle impairment rates for elderly-associated vehicles
2. The "invisible drunk" thesis: elderly DUI drivers are invisible because they drive boring vehicles (Buicks, Town Cars, Encores) that don't get profiled, they don't go to bars where checkpoint stings operate, and the assumption is grandma doesn't drink
3. Calculation: elderly-brand vehicles (Buick/Cadillac/Lincoln combined) average ~21.7% impairment rate across 14,000+ fatal-crash drivers — ABOVE the overall sports car average (22.5% but barely)

## Strongest Counterargument
The FARS data spans 2014-2023 and cannot prove the post-pandemic TREND directly — we're correlating a new DUI-violation trend (Avian Law Group, 2019-present) with a historical toxicology pattern (FARS). The 44.8% increase in violations among 66-90 year-olds could reflect increased enforcement attention to elderly drivers, not increased impaired driving.

## Limitations
- FARS toxicology captures fatal crashes only — not total DUI incidents
- Vehicle-level impairment can't be directly mapped to driver age (a young person can drive a Buick)
- The Avian Law Group data comes from a personal injury law firm's report — not a peer-reviewed study
- FARS data is 2014-2023; the DUI surge is 2019-present; temporal mismatch in datasets
