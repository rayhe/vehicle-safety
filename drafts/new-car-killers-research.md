# Research: New Car Killers — Vehicles That Kill While Nearly New

## The Novel Finding
Cross-tabulating FARS deaths by model year reveals a striking split: most vehicles peak in fatal crash involvement at age 12-18 (old, depreciated, often lacking modern safety tech). But a handful of vehicles are disproportionately killing their owners while practically brand new — within 1-5 years of manufacture.

## Key Data Points

### Vehicles with Youngest Average Death Age (FARS 2014-2023):
1. **Ram 1500**: avg death age 1.2 years — 41.8% of ALL deaths from 2019-2023 MY
2. **Kia Forte**: avg death age 1.6 years — 35.9% from 2019-2023 MY  
3. **Dodge Challenger**: avg death age 1.6 years — 38.4% from 2019-2023 MY
4. **Kia Soul**: avg death age 2.5 years — 26.6% from 2019-2023 MY
5. **Nissan Rogue**: avg death age 3.0 years — 26.7% from 2019-2023 MY
6. **Dodge Charger**: avg death age 3.7 years — 26.9% from 2019-2023 MY

### Contrast with "Old Killers" (avg death age 15+ years):
- Chevy GMT-400: 23.7 years
- Chevy S-10: 21.0 years
- Ford Ranger: 18.2 years
- Buick LeSabre: 17.9 years
- Chevy Cavalier: 17.4 years

### The Paradox
The conventional story: old cars lack safety tech → deaths. These "young killers" destroy that narrative. Why are people dying in cars with airbags, ESC, blind-spot monitoring, lane-departure warning, forward collision warning?

## Three Hypotheses Emerging:

### 1. Power + Performance (Charger, Challenger)
- Challenger: 38.4% of deaths from 2019-2023 MY, avg death age 1.6yr
- Charger: 26.9% from 2019-2023 MY, avg death age 3.7yr
- These are muscle cars. 300-800hp. Marketed to drive fast. Buyers comply.
- The car's safety tech can't overcome 707hp in a Hellcat.

### 2. Theft Epidemic (Kia Forte, Kia Soul, Hyundai Elantra)  
- Kia Forte: 35.9% from 2019-2023 MY, peaking at MY 2021 (76 deaths!)
- Kia Soul: 26.6% from 2019-2023 MY
- Hyundai Elantra: 9.2% from 2019-2023 MY
- "Kia Boys" TikTok theft epidemic — vehicles stolen using USB cable exploit
- Stolen vehicles driven recklessly by teens → fatal crashes
- NHTSA/IIHS reported theft rates 8-11x higher for Hyundai/Kia vs other brands
- Software fix deployed Feb 2023 but many vehicles remain unpatched

### 3. Volume Surge (Ram 1500, RAV4, Rogue, Equinox)
- Ram 1500 brand split from Dodge in 2011 — new VIN coding
- MY 2019 Ram 1500 = 156 deaths, more than any other single model year
- RAV4: 233 deaths from 2019-2023 MY (26% of total) — but this is partly volume
- Equinox: 230 deaths from 2019-2023 MY (22.3%)
- These are just selling in enormous numbers, so raw deaths go up

## Original Contribution
Nobody has cross-tabulated "death age profile" by vehicle — the average age at which a vehicle's FARS deaths occur. This reveals qualitatively different failure modes:
- **Old killers** = structural/safety deficit (no ESC, weak crumple zones, aging fleet driven by lower-income owners)
- **Young killers** = behavior problem (too much power, theft vulnerability, or sheer volume)

The fact that a 2021 Kia Forte (avg death age 1.6 years) and a 2004 Chevy Cavalier (avg death age 17.4 years) both kill at roughly the same rate per 100M VMT tells us safety tech isn't the whole story.

## Strongest Counterargument
The "young killer" pattern could be an artifact of FARS observation window. FARS data spans 2014-2023. A MY 2019 vehicle only has 4-5 years of exposure in the dataset. Its death count will grow as the dataset extends. We're comparing a 5-year window for new vehicles against a 10-year window for older ones. The Ram 1500 brand-split (2011) also means early model years are partially captured under "Dodge RAM."

This is real and must be acknowledged. However, the per-model-year death counts are absolute within the observation period, and the Kia Forte MY 2021 having 76 deaths in just 2-3 years of FARS exposure is alarming regardless.
