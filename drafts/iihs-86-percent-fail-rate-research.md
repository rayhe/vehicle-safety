# Research: IIHS 86% Failure Rate — May 2026 Batch

## Angle
IIHS tested 16 vehicles in its May 2026 batch. Only 2 earned any safety designation. 14 failed to earn either Top Safety Pick or Top Safety Pick+. That's an 87.5% failure rate. One criteria change — adding rear-seat protection to the moderate overlap front test — and the industry collapsed.

Cross-referencing with FARS data: the 14 vehicles that failed have ~13,825 cumulative FARS deaths (2014-2023). The 2 that passed have 533. The models with the highest real-world death rates are also failing the lab test.

## Original Contribution
1. First aggregate analysis of IIHS May 2026 batch failure rate (87.5%) with FARS body count
2. Demonstrates that the IIHS test correlates with real-world outcomes: the 2 vehicles that passed have dramatically lower FARS death rates (avg ~0.30) vs failed vehicles (rates up to 6.27)
3. Quantifies the "rear-seat tax" — how many of those ~14,000 deaths might have been prevented if rear-seat protection had been prioritized earlier

## Limitations
- FARS data covers 2014-2023 model years, not the specific 2026 MY vehicles tested. Rates reflect older generations.
- 3 failed vehicles (BMW i4, Kia K4, Ford Maverick) are too new for FARS data
- FARS death count includes ALL crash types, not specifically moderate overlap front crashes
- Correlation between IIHS test failure and FARS rates doesn't prove the test failure caused the deaths — confounders include fleet age, driver demographics, VMT assumptions

## Strongest Counterargument
IIHS RAISED the bar. The 14 vehicles didn't get MORE dangerous — the standard got harder. Prior to 2022, most of these vehicles earned safety picks. This is how progress works: you move the goalposts. The 87.5% failure rate is a measurement of how much headroom the industry had been operating with, not a sudden safety crisis.

The counterargument has merit. But the FARS data shows these vehicles were ALREADY killing people in the real world at rates that varied by 100x. The test update didn't create the problem — it finally measured it.

## Actionable Insight
If shopping for one of these 16 vehicles: Palisade (post-Nov 2025) and Prius are the only ones with full IIHS backing. For everyone else, rear-seat passengers face unknown risks. Check iihs.org/ratings before signing.
