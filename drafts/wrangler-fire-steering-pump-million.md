# Research Notes: Stellantis Wrangler/Gladiator Steering Pump Fire Recall

## News Peg (June 2026)
- Stellantis recalled **1,077,000** Jeep Wranglers and Gladiators (MY 2021-2025) for spontaneous fire risk
- Cause: Electric hydraulic power steering pump wiring can overheat, causing fire even when vehicle is OFF
- NHTSA aware of **51 fires and 1 injury**
- Owners told to **park outside and away from structures**
- Stellantis "anticipates a solution no later than July"
- Publicly available recall paperwork published by NHTSA on June 9

## Key Twist
- Stellantis discontinued its entire plug-in hybrid line at end of 2025 — Wrangler 4xe, Grand Cherokee 4xe recalled at least 3 times for fires, explosions, sandy engines, loss of power
- Those fire recalls were blamed on the BATTERY SYSTEM
- This new recall is for REGULAR GAS-POWERED Wranglers — not hybrids
- Different cause (steering pump wiring), same symptom (spontaneous combustion)
- Killing the hybrids didn't solve the fire problem — the fire risk migrated

## Broader Pattern (source: WSJ Jun 12, 2026)
- Since 2020, **90+ recalls** for **11+ million vehicles** with "park outside" fire advisories
- Stellantis accounts for roughly **20%** of these vehicles
- Hyundai is #1 (Hyundai Motor fire risk recall campaign earlier this decade)
- GM also featured (Bolt EV/EUV battery fires)

## FARS Data on Jeep Wrangler
- Deaths: 1,842 (2014-2023)
- Annual average: 184.2
- Fatal crashes: 3,718
- Rate: 0.84/100M VMT (moderate for SUV class)
- Survivability: 0.495 deaths/crash
- Fleet: 1,750,000 est.
- Impairment rate: 19.3% (near median)
- Gladiator too new for FARS dataset

## Model Year Death Distribution
- Peak deaths in MY 2014 (94), MY 2015 (103)
- MY 2008 (85), MY 2018 (86) also high
- Death counts for the recalled MY range (2021-2025):
  - 2021: 38 deaths, 2022: 9 deaths (fleet still young)
  - Gladiator not in dataset

## Novel Contribution
- FARS only captures crash fatalities — non-crash vehicle fires are invisible to FARS
- The "park outside" warning implies a risk category that crashworthiness metrics don't measure
- A vehicle that can kill you while parked in your garage adds a risk vector not reflected in any standard safety metric
- Cross-tabulation: Wrangler already has 1,842 crash deaths + now adds garage fire risk
