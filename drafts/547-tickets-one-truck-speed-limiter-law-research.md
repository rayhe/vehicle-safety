# Research: 547 Tickets, One Truck, One Law

## Angle
New York just became the first US jurisdiction to mandate speed-limiting devices on private vehicles. The law was catalyzed by a single NYPD officer — James Giovansanti — whose Ram 1500 accumulated 547 speed camera and red-light violations since 2022 on Staten Island. The Stop Super Speeders Act, passed as part of the state budget in May 2026, requires drivers with 16+ speed camera tickets in a 12-month period to install Intelligent Speed Assistance (ISA) devices.

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing the "super speeder" population (~14,600 vehicles, per Transportation Alternatives) with national FARS speeding fatality data. The concentration of risk: 14,600 vehicles represent ~0.005% of NYC's registered fleet but consume a wildly disproportionate share of enforcement resources and risk exposure.

## Key Facts

### The Cop
- **Officer:** NYPD officer James Giovansanti, Staten Island
- **Vehicle:** Ram 1500
- **Violations:** 547 speed camera and red-light camera tickets since 2022 (through May 2026)
- **2025 alone:** 187 speeding tickets
- **Fines:** $36,650.02 total
- **Status:** Remained on active duty, no public disciplinary action
- **Locations:** Near schools and residential areas on Staten Island
- **Context:** Streetsblog's reporting on his record catalyzed the final push for the bill

### The Law
- **Name:** Stop Super Speeders Act
- **Passed:** Part of NY state budget deal, May 2026
- **Signed by:** Gov. Kathy Hochul (expected)
- **Threshold:** 16+ speed camera violations in a 12-month period
- **Technology:** Intelligent Speed Assistance (ISA) — GPS-based, prevents vehicle from exceeding posted speed limits
- **Cost to driver:** ~$150 installation, ~$4/day monitoring, ~$100 removal
- **Non-compliance:** Escalating civil fines ($1,500-$2,500), registration suspension after 45 days
- **Affected pool:** ~14,600 vehicles currently qualify (per Transportation Alternatives)
- **Original bill:** Would have hit drivers with 6+ tickets (affecting ~150,000 statewide); final version raised threshold to 16

### Context
- NYC fleet vehicle ISA pilot: 700 vehicles, 64% speeding reduction (US DOT evaluation)
- Late-model BMW/Mercedes/luxury SUV drivers poured $10M+ into speed camera fines in 12 months
- One 2024 Mercedes GLS owner: ~$94,000 in unpaid tickets
- Speeding kills ~12,000 Americans per year (NHTSA)
- Civil remedy, not criminal — mirrors ignition interlock approach for DUI
- Assembly Speaker Heastie was primary opponent; amended from criminal to civil framework

### The DUI Interlock Parallel
- Ignition interlock devices for DUI have been mandated in all 50 states since at least 2005
- They prevent starting the car if BAC exceeds a threshold
- ISA is the speeding equivalent: prevents exceeding posted speed limit
- DUI interlocks reduced repeat DUI offenses by approximately 70% while installed

### National Speeding Data (FARS)
- ~12,000 annual speeding-related fatalities (NHTSA)
- Speeding is a factor in ~29% of all traffic fatalities
- Small fraction of drivers responsible for majority of speeding violations
- NYC's 14,600 super speeders = ~0.005% of city's registered vehicles
