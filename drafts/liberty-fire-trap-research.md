# Research: The Jeep Liberty — Dead Brand, Active Body Count

## Angle
The Jeep Liberty was discontinued after 2012. Every single one of its 801 deaths in FARS (2014-2023) occurred after the vehicle stopped being made. A dead vehicle killing 80 people a year. Combined with the notorious rear-mounted fuel tank recall (2.7M vehicles), this is a case study in how discontinued vehicles become rolling coffins as they age into lower-income markets.

## Kill Test
**Is this genuinely newsworthy?** Yes.
- **Novel cross-tab:** Quantifying a discontinued vehicle's ongoing death rate — 801 deaths in a 10-year window entirely after production ended.
- **Paradox:** The Liberty's replacement (Jeep Renegade) has the same fleet size (525K) but kills at 0.21 vs 1.22 — a 5.8x safety improvement from Jeep's own engineering progress.
- **Known defect tie-in:** The 2.7M-vehicle fuel tank fire recall, $150M jury verdict, NHTSA investigation.
- **Not yet covered:** No Liberty article exists on The Crash Report.

## External Sources

### Rear Fuel Tank Fire Recall
- **2013:** Chrysler recalled 2.7 million 1993-2004 Grand Cherokees and 2002-2007 Libertys for rear-mounted fuel tanks that could rupture in rear-end collisions
- **Initially refused:** Chrysler first rejected NHTSA's recall request (extremely rare), then relented
- **NHTSA investigation:** Linked at least 51 deaths to the fuel tank issue (as of 2013)
- **$150M jury verdict (2015):** A Georgia jury awarded $150M for a 4-year-old's death in a 1999 Grand Cherokee fuel tank fire
- **2015:** NHTSA considered reopening the investigation, questioning whether Chrysler's recall fix (a trailer hitch) was adequate
- Source: Reuters, Consumer Reports

### Design Context
- **Platform:** KJ (2002-2007, 1st gen) and KK (2008-2012, 2nd gen)
- **Body-on-frame:** Yes (unlike modern unibody crossovers like the RAV4, CR-V, Renegade)
- **Weight:** ~3,800 lbs (similar to competitors)
- **Replacement:** Jeep Cherokee (KL, 2014+) on Fiat Compact Wide platform, then Renegade
- **Known issues:** Fuel tank fires, Takata airbag recalls, suspension ball joint failures, window regulators

## Original Contribution
1. **Dead vehicle's ongoing death rate:** 801 deaths entirely post-discontinuation, quantified at 80.1/year
2. **Replacement comparison:** Same brand, same fleet size, 5.8x safety improvement (Liberty 1.22 → Renegade 0.21)
3. **Impairment-demographics link:** 22.2% impairment rate = highest in segment, suggesting the Liberty has migrated to higher-risk used-car demographics
4. **Model year death concentration:** 47% of deaths from 2002-2004 MY, vehicles that were 10-21 years old during the data window

## Limitations
- FARS captures fatal crashes only — injury rates unknown
- Fleet estimates use VMT calculations that may under-count older, discontinued vehicles still on the road
- Impairment rates reflect driver toxicology at time of fatal crash, not causation
- The fuel tank fire deaths (NHTSA's 51+ figure) overlap with but don't fully explain the 801 total
- Cannot separate KJ (2002-2007) from KK (2008-2012) platform deaths beyond model year

## Strongest Counterargument
The Liberty's high death rate may largely reflect **survivor bias in the used car market** — the cheapest, most abused Libertys are the ones still being driven, by the youngest, least experienced, least wealthy drivers. The same pattern would affect ANY discontinued economy SUV. The Liberty's engineering may not be uniquely bad; it may simply be uniquely cheap and available. However, the 5.8x gap vs the Renegade (same brand, same fleet size, different era) and the known fuel tank defect argue that engineering IS a major factor, not just demographics.
