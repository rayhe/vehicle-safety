# Research: Phantom Braking Epidemic — AEB Creating the Crashes It Was Built to Prevent

## Angle
421,000 Hyundai vehicles recalled (May 22, 2026) because front camera software triggers unexpected braking. This isn't isolated — Honda is under NHTSA investigation for 3 million vehicles with the same problem, Freightliner trucks too. The AEB system mandated to save lives is now causing crashes. FARS data shows the affected vehicles are already among the safest on the road, making the irony sharper: the safety net is creating the hazard.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. This is a systemic problem across manufacturers, not a single recall story. The original calculation — estimating phantom braking crash contribution at scale vs. the crash prevention mandate promises — hasn't been done. The irony that the safest vehicles are getting the false positives is novel.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: Hyundai 421K Recall (May 22, 2026)
- Models: 2025-2026 Santa Cruz, Tucson, Tucson Hybrid, Tucson Plug-In Hybrid Electric
- Defect: Front camera software error triggers unexpected brake application
- NHTSA announcement via Reuters/SRN News
- Fix: Dealer software update (free)
- 421,078 vehicles affected

### Source 2: Hyundai Tucson Class-Action Lawsuit
- Case No. 8:26-cv-01042, Central District of California
- Filed by Ahdoot & Wolfson PC + Lemberg Law
- ECU misinterprets shadows, road signs, pavement changes as stationary objects
- NHTSA complaint ID 11608921: vehicle under highway overpass decelerated from 70 mph to 20 mph in seconds
- Dealers told owners system was "operating as designed"
- Separate from Palisade case (Maldonado v. Hyundai, May 2025 — ABS/traction control)

### Source 3: Honda 3 Million Vehicle Investigation
- NHTSA investigating phantom AEB activation
- 412 reports of crashes and fires
- 47 confirmed crashes, 93 injuries
- Expanded investigation from 1.7M to 2.997M vehicles
- Sources: Carscoops, TheLeronFirm

### Source 4: Freightliner Cascadia Trucks
- NHTSA investigating false positive AEB braking in Daimler Trucks' Cascadia
- 18 owner complaints of brakes applying without obstacles
- Source: Carscoops

### Source 5: NHTSA AEB Mandate
- Finalized rule: AEB required for all new vehicles by September 2029
- Must stop at speeds up to 62 mph
- Must brake automatically at 90 mph when collision imminent
- NHTSA estimates: prevent 19,118 crashes and 155 deaths annually
- Annual crash cost: $340 billion, 36,500 deaths
- Sources: NHTSA.gov, Exponent

## Original Calculation
If Honda's phantom braking rate is ~412 events per 3M vehicles = 0.0137% per vehicle:
- Apply to projected 90M+ AEB-equipped vehicles by 2029
- Result: ~12,300 phantom braking events per year
- If 10% result in a crash (conservative — highway speeds, following traffic): ~1,230 crashes/year
- That wipes out 6.4% of the 19,118 annual crashes AEB is supposed to prevent
- If 1% are serious injury/fatal: ~12 serious crashes from the safety system itself

The denominator problem: NHTSA counted how many crashes AEB prevents. Nobody counted how many it creates.
