# Research: The Technology Cohort — 72% of FARS Deaths in Pre-ESC Vehicles

## Angle
NHTSA just published DOT HS 813 829 (July 2026): full-year 2025 sub-category estimates. Headline: 36,640 deaths, down 6.7% from 39,254 in 2024. But the sub-category data reveals the decline is overwhelmingly a TECHNOLOGY story:
- Newer vehicles (<10 years old): DOWN 12%
- Older vehicles (≥10 years old): DOWN only 9%
- Rollover crashes: DOWN 11% (ESC effect)
- Ejected occupants: DOWN 13% (seatbelt tech)
- Speeding-related: DOWN 11%
- Rural interstates: UP 1% (no infrastructure change)
- 65+ age group: DOWN 0% (flat — this group drives the oldest vehicles)
- Pedalcyclists: UP 4% (no vehicle protection)

## Original FARS Analysis
Cross-referencing model year data from FARS 2014-2023 (337 models):
- **71.9% of all fatalities** are in pre-2012 model year vehicles (before the MY 2012 ESC mandate under FMVSS 126)
- Pre-2018 vehicles (before AEB became widely standard): 172,849 deaths
- Post-2018 vehicles: only 14,209 deaths
- Ratio: 12.2:1

### Pre-2012 death leaders (pre-ESC mandate)
1. Chevrolet Silverado — 7,731 pre-2012 MY deaths
2. Ford F-150 — 7,307
3. Honda Accord — 5,611
4. Honda Civic — 4,907
5. Toyota Camry — 4,561

### Post-2018 death leaders (AEB era — still dying)
1. Toyota Camry — 490
2. Ford F-150 — 452
3. Toyota Corolla — 448
4. Honda Civic — 417
5. Nissan Sentra — 394

## Key insight
The 2025 decline is engineered, not earned. Safety technology (ESC, AEB, curtain airbags, seatbelt reminders) is saving lives in newer vehicles. But the people dying are overwhelmingly driving old cars without these technologies. The 65+ group saw ZERO decline because they're the demographic most likely to drive decade-old vehicles without modern safety tech.

The economic dimension: if you bought a 2022+ vehicle with standard AEB, you're riding the 12% decline. If you're in a 2008 Altima from a buy-here-pay-here lot, the headline doesn't apply to you.
