## Core Data (FARS)
- 37 models with death rate >2.0 per 100M VMT: 73,048 deaths (38.2% of all)
- 159 models with rate ≤0.5: 22,065 deaths (11.5%)
- If every driver in a >2.0 rate vehicle had been in a 0.5 rate vehicle: ~61,223 fewer deaths over 10 years (6,122/year)
- Distracted driving kills ~3,308/year (NHTSA)
- Vehicle swap saves 1.9x more lives than eliminating ALL distracted driving
- Hands-free laws estimated to prevent 5-15% of distracted driving deaths = ~165-496 lives/year
- Vehicle swap saves 12.3x-37x more lives than hands-free laws

## Key Comparisons
- Sedan death rate spread: 102x (Nissan Maxima 5.11 vs Tesla Model 3 0.05)
- SUV death rate spread: 261x (Chevy Tracker 7.83 vs Tesla Model Y 0.03)
- Impairment rates uniform across classes: 18.1% (Van) to 22.5% (Sports Car)
- 20% of fatal crash drivers were impaired. 80% were sober.
- Vehicle choice predicts death at orders of magnitude beyond behavior

## Novel Analysis
This is the first calculation comparing the lives-saved ceiling of hands-free laws to the lives-saved ceiling of vehicle substitution using the same FARS dataset. Previous pieces compared behavior vs. design in general terms; this one puts specific dollar amounts and policy instruments side by side.

## Counterargument (at full strength)
Phone laws work across ALL vehicles simultaneously. You can't force 30M people to trade their Altima for a RAV4. Regulatory tools for distracted driving (enforcement, fines, tech mandates) are cheaper, faster, and politically feasible. Vehicle substitution requires either massive subsidies, forced scrappage, or waiting 15+ years for fleet turnover. The hands-free law prevents injury crashes too, not just fatalities, and injury prevention has its own economic value.

## Limitations
- FARS only captures FATAL crashes. Distracted driving may cause a higher share of non-fatal crashes.
- Our rate calculations use estimated VMT, not actual odometer data (±15% uncertainty for low-volume models).
- NHTSA's 3,308 distracted driving death figure is widely acknowledged as an undercount (self-reported, no phone forensics required).
- Hands-free law efficacy estimates (5-15%) are from early studies and may improve with stricter enforcement.
- Vehicle swap calculation assumes VMT stays constant (unrealistic; cheaper vehicles tend to be driven more miles).
