# Research Notes: Work Zone Deaths — The Motorist Majority

## Angle
Work zones killed 850 people in the U.S. in 2024. The safety campaigns say "slow down to protect workers." But ~80% of the dead aren't workers — they're motorists. The I-81 bus crash (May 30, 2026, 5 dead, 44 injured) happened in a work zone. FHWA is spending $500K on training to protect crews, but the data says the problem is overwhelmingly on the other side of the orange barrels.

## Kill Test
- Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES — FHWA published the 850 figure on April 22, 2026. The I-81 crash just happened. No major outlet has cross-referenced the "who actually dies" breakdown.
- Novel angle? YES — flips the "protect the workers" narrative using the government's own data.
- Not a data dump? Correct — this is a genuine paradox discovery.

## Primary Sources

### 1. FHWA / NHTSA (April 22, 2026)
- 850 people killed in work zone crashes in 2024
- Nearly 20% were pedestrians, construction, maintenance, or utility workers
- Therefore ~80% (680+) were motorists/passengers
- Source: https://highways.dot.gov/newsroom/trumps-transportation-department-highlights-importance-safe-driving-work-zone-awareness-week

### 2. CDC/NIOSH — Work Zone Fatality Data (2016-2020)
- 4,048 people died in work zone crashes (avg 810/year)
- 62% were drivers of motor vehicles in transport
- 19% were passengers
- 16% were pedestrians (includes workers on foot)
- 30% of fatal work zone crashes involved a large truck
- Speeding: 31%, Alcohol: 28%, Distracted: 13%, Drowsy: 3%
- Nearly half of all work zone deaths: TX (826), FL (379), CA (371), GA (243), IL (150)
- Source: CDC/NIOSH Behind the Wheel at Work

### 3. NTSB / I-81 Bus Crash (May 30, 2026)
- Charter bus operated by E&P Travel Inc (Kings Mountain, NC) hit stopped traffic in work zone
- 5 dead, 44 injured
- Driver Jing Dong, 48, charged with involuntary manslaughter
- Company: 4 vehicles, 11 drivers, incorporated Nov 2023
- Previous FMCSA violation for English language proficiency (Sept 2025)
- NTSB Tom Chapman: "If there was any braking, there wasn't much"
- FMCSA rating: "satisfactory" despite prior violations
- Sources: Reuters, CNN, AP, NY Post, Autoblog

### 4. AGC Survey (May 2026)
- 55% of contractors say work zone crashes pose greater risk than a year ago
- Only 4% say risk has declined
- 64% of contractors reported a crash in their work zone since 2020
- 22% of those who experienced crashes reported driver/passenger fatalities
- 27% reported injuries to construction workers
- More than half said crashes delayed projects
- 40% want more severe penalties; 39% say penalties exist but aren't enforced
- Over 1/3 say rising material costs affect ability to buy safety equipment
- Source: Associated General Contractors of America (AGC.org)

### 5. BLS / CFOI (February 2026 release)
- 5,070 fatal work injuries in U.S. in 2024 (down 4% from 5,283 in 2023)
- Transportation/material moving: 1,391 fatalities (most of any occupation group)
- A worker died every 104 minutes in 2024
- Source: BLS Census of Fatal Occupational Injuries

### 6. Michigan DOT (May 2026)
- 3 road workers + 22 motorists killed in construction zones in 2025
- ~6,000 work-zone crashes, 1,000+ injuries in 2025
- Two MDOT workers killed on US 127 near Holt (May 27, 2026)
- Source: MDOT, Lansing State Journal

### 7. FHWA Work Zone Data (historical trend)
- BTS tracks work zone fatalities via FARS
- 2011-2020: 1,260 workers killed at road construction sites (126/year)
- 73% of worker deaths were from transportation events
- 63% of those: worker struck by vehicle
- Pickup trucks/SUVs caused 169 worker deaths, followed by automobiles (149), machinery (146), semi-trucks (141)

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing the FHWA's 850 figure with the CDC/NIOSH breakdown reveals that ~680 of the 850 work zone deaths in 2024 were motorists/passengers — not workers. The "protect the workers" framing, while important, obscures the majority of victims. This is a genuine novel finding from combining two government datasets.

## Strongest Counterargument
Work zone safety campaigns are designed to change driver behavior, which is the root cause of both worker AND motorist deaths. By focusing on "protect the workers," they create emotional urgency that benefits everyone. Reframing the problem as "motorists are dying" could actually reduce sympathy for worker protections. The 20% who are workers are disproportionately vulnerable — they're standing still while vehicles approach at highway speed.

## Limitations
- The 80/20 split is approximate (FHWA said "nearly 20%" were workers/pedestrians)
- Work zone data doesn't distinguish between crashes IN the zone vs. near it
- The 850 figure is for 2024; trend data uses older years
- Not all "pedestrian" deaths in work zones are workers — some may be stranded motorists
- The I-81 crash investigation is ongoing; final cause not determined
