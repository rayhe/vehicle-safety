# Research: NHTSA Cleared 1 Million Teslas in One Week. The Same Week, FSD Killed Someone.

## Angle
NHTSA closed two separate Tesla safety probes covering 1,071,241 vehicles in a single week (June 27 – July 2, 2026). One probe identified the root cause — Tesla's own design choice to drop radar — then closed it anyway because complaints declined. The same week, a Tesla FSD driver was charged with manslaughter for crashing into a home and killing Maria Avila while Googling "Tesla FSD too timid." The paradox: regulators clear the cars while courts prosecute the drivers.

### 2. NHTSA / Basenor — Steering control probe closed (June 27, 2026)

## Original Analysis
The complaint-decline methodology creates a structural bias toward closure:
1. OTA updates that partially address symptoms reduce complaint volume
2. Vehicle owners who experienced the defect sell or trade the car
3. Fleet aging — affected model years phase out of active use
4. Complaint fatigue — owners stop reporting when nothing happens

NHTSA found the cause (vision-only transition) and still closed the case. This is equivalent to a doctor identifying the disease, noting the symptoms subsided, and discharging the patient while saying "this doesn't mean you're not sick."
