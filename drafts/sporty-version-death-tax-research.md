# Research: The Sporty Version Death Tax

## Angle
When automakers release a "sporty" or "coupe" variant of an everyday car, the death rate spikes dramatically. This is a systematic cross-brand pattern, not a one-off.

## Kill Test
- Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES — no existing article examines this as a cross-brand pattern
- Novel angle on data? YES — cross-tabulation of base models vs their sporty derivatives
- After 144 articles? Still novel — individual vehicle stories exist but not the systemic "variant tax" analysis

## Primary Data (FARS 2014-2023)

### Sporty Variant Death Tax (deaths per 100M VMT)
| Base Model | Rate | Sporty Variant | Rate | Tax (ratio) |
|-----------|------|----------------|------|-------------|
| Hyundai Elantra | 1.50 | Hyundai Veloster | 8.54 | **5.7x** |
| Toyota Camry | 2.03 | Toyota Solara | 4.25 | **2.1x** |
| Nissan Altima | 2.88 | Nissan Maxima | 5.11 | **1.8x** |
| Dodge Charger | 0.75 | Dodge Challenger | 1.00 | **1.3x** |
| Chevrolet Malibu | 2.03 | Chevrolet Monte Carlo | 0.71 | **0.35x** (exception!) |

### Key Vehicle Stats
- Veloster: 598 deaths, 8.54 rate, Sports Car class, fleet 87,500
- Solara: 642 deaths, 4.25 rate, only 4.1% impairment (!!!)
- Maxima: 1,544 deaths, 5.11 rate, 20.9% impairment
- Challenger: 1,204 deaths... wait, that's the Camaro. Challenger: rate 1.0
- Monte Carlo: 178 deaths, 0.71 rate — SAFER than the Malibu

### The Exception Analysis
Monte Carlo (0.71) is safer than Malibu (2.03). Possible explanations:
- NASCAR heritage attracted collectors who drove carefully
- Different driver demographic (older, more male, enthusiast)
- W-body platform shared with Impala (larger than Malibu)
- Monte Carlo is actually a larger car than Malibu (built on Impala platform, not Malibu platform)

### Why Sporty Variants Kill
1. **Lower roofline** — reduced headroom compresses crumple zones, changes crash geometry
2. **Stiffer suspension** — sport tuning reduces crash energy absorption
3. **Driver behavior selection** — the person who chooses the sporty version drives differently
4. **Smaller, lighter construction** (coupes vs sedans) — less structural mass
5. **Younger demographic** — sporty variants attract younger, riskier drivers

### Impairment Context
- Solara: 4.1% impairment (one of the lowest in FARS!) — meaning 96% of Solara fatal crashes involve sober drivers
- Maxima: 20.9% (fleet average)
- Elantra: would need to check, but Veloster is 17.4%
- This suggests the Solara's death toll is almost entirely a DESIGN/physics problem
