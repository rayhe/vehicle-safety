# Research: Ford Quality Award vs. Recall Paradox

## Angle
Ford won JD Power's #1 quality ranking among mainstream brands (announced June 25, 2026) — its best quality ranking in 16 years. Eight days later, it recalled 777,000 more vehicles. Ford is airing a TV ad on July 4th celebrating the quality win. It has issued 53 recalls covering 12 million vehicles in the first half of 2026. The two facts are both true and measure completely different things.

## Primary Sources

### 1. JD Power 2026 Initial Quality Study
- Ford ranked 3rd overall, 1st among mainstream brands
- Score improved by 41 problems per 100 vehicles (14th in 2025 → 3rd in 2026)
- Porsche #1 overall (138 PP100V, -50 improvement), Genesis #2 (151), Ford #3
- IQS measures "problems per 100 vehicles in first 90 days of ownership"
- Announced June 25, 2026
- Source: JD Power press release; CarbBuzz, Detroit Free Press, USA Today coverage

### 2. NHTSA Recall Data (2026)
- 53 recalls since January 1, 2026 (as of July 2)
- 12,017,708 vehicles affected in first half of 2026
- Nearest competitor: Chrysler with 19 recalls
- 3rd consecutive year Ford has far outpaced all competitors
- Latest (June 24): 741,195 vehicles for transmission/rollaway + 36,046 Broncos for fender flare detachment
- Ford spokesperson: "More than 80% of our recalls this year are resolved through convenient, software-only updates"
- Source: NHTSA database; USA Today, Jalopnik, Fox Business coverage

### 3. NHTSA Recall Completion Rate Report
- Weighted average completion rate for recalls 2012-2022: 65.8%
- Meaning ~34.2% of recalled vehicles never get fixed
- Source: NHTSA "Improving Vehicle Safety Recall Completion Rates" report (2024/2025)
- MotorBiscuit cites average around 48% (possibly different methodology)

### 4. FARS Fatality Data (our dataset)
- Ford make-level death rate: 11.68 per 100M VMT (6th highest of 38 makes)
- 34,954 total deaths across all Ford models
- 2nd highest absolute death count after Chevrolet (35,905)
- Range within Ford: Mustang 6.02 (highest) to Bronco 0.05 (lowest) — 120x spread
- F-150 accounts for 9,194 deaths alone (26.3% of all Ford deaths)

### 5. Ford's Own Campaign
- Banner at Ford World HQ on Oakwood Blvd: "We put quality first. Now Ford is first in quality"
- 30-second TV ad airing July 4th
- CEO Jim Farley: "This is a culmination of a lot of hard work"
- Ford rehired 350 "gray beard" engineers to retrain AI quality control systems
- Warranty costs down $500M in 2025 vs 2024
- Ford spokesperson: "more than 90% of recalls are for older vehicles built between 2013 and 2020"
- Source: USA Today, Detroit Free Press, Daily Caller

## Novel Calculation
53 recalls × avg ~227K vehicles per recall = 12M vehicles recalled in 2026.
Using NHTSA's 65.8% completion rate: 12M × 34.2% = ~4.1M Ford vehicles from 2026 recalls alone may never be repaired.
This is cumulative with prior years' unrepaired recalls — Ford's 153 recalls in 2025 covered additional millions.

## Key Insight
JD Power IQS is a snapshot of production line quality at moment of purchase (90 days). Recalls are a reckoning with everything that shipped before the snapshot improved. Ford has gotten better at building cars. It hasn't gotten better at fixing the ones it already built wrong. The 4.1M estimated unrepaired vehicles from 2026 recalls alone will still be on the road when Ford airs its next quality ad.

## Counterargument (strongest)
Ford's argument is technically sound: quality and recalls measure different things. New-vehicle quality IS better. 90%+ of recalls affect older models (2013-2020). The quality improvement that earned the JD Power award may eventually reduce future recall volume. Warranty costs are already down $500M.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers 2012-2022 (latest available), doesn't capture post-improvement vehicles
- Recall completion rates are industry-wide averages, Ford-specific rates may differ
- JD Power IQS has been criticized for conflating minor infotainment gripes with safety-critical defects
- "Problems per 100 vehicles" includes things like confusing cup holder placement alongside actual mechanical issues
