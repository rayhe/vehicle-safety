# Research: Ford Mustang GTD $325K Wiper Recall

## News Hook (July 7, 2026)
Ford issued two simultaneous Mustang recalls on July 7, 2026:
1. **26V418000:** 67,842 Mustang + Mustang GTD (289 units) — windshield wipers may malfunction in cold weather, only operating at high speed; washer system may fail. Covers MY2024-2026 Mustang, MY2025-2026 GTD.
2. **26V417000:** 42,784 Mustang Mach-E (MY2021-2023) — rear differential pinion shaft may fracture, causing loss of drive power or unintended vehicle movement when parked without parking brake.

Total: 110,626 vehicles across 3 Mustang variants. Sources: NHTSA, Reuters, USA Today, NY Post, CarBuzz.

The GTD starts at ~$325,000 (MSRP). Only 289 units recalled. It's Ford's most expensive production vehicle ever, a track-focused machine that set a Nürburgring lap record. It shares its wiper motor assembly with the $30K base Mustang.

## Original Contribution
- Cross-tabulated: the Mustang generates 44.7% of all tracked sports car fatalities, more than all other tracked sports cars combined.
- Per-mile death rate comparison: Mustang at 6.02 vs Porsche 911 at 0.69 — an 8.7× multiplier, despite the 911 being the faster, harder-to-control car.
- Cost-per-death-rate calculation: Porsche 911 costs ~$115K with a 0.69 rate = ~$167K per rate unit. Mustang GTD costs $325K with a 6.02 rate (same platform) = ~$54K per rate unit. The GTD offers less safety per dollar than the base Mustang.
- The GTD and base Mustang share the same wiper assembly — the most expensive Ford ever made shares parts with the cheapest performance car Ford sells.
