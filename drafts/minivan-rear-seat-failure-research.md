# Research: Minivans Fail IIHS Rear Seat Safety — Zero Awards for Family Haulers

## Angle
The IIHS has gone two consecutive years (2025 and 2026) without awarding a single minivan any safety designation — Top Safety Pick or Top Safety Pick+. The specific failure point: rear seat passenger protection, which is exactly where children sit. The vehicles designed and marketed specifically for transporting families cannot protect the family members sitting in the back.

## Key Data Points

### IIHS 2026 Awards (March 24, 2026)
- 63 vehicles earned awards (45 TSP+, 18 TSP)
- ZERO minivans earned either award
- "No minicars, minivans or small pickups earn awards this year"
- IIHS President David Harkey: "It's disappointing that minivans continue to struggle to provide the best-available protection for passengers in the back, considering that these are supposed to be family vehicles. Based on these results, parents may want to consider some of the more affordable sedans and SUVs that earn awards."
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/iihs-pushes-improvements-in-crash-avoidance-with-2026-awards

### IIHS Moderate Overlap Test Results (Minivans)
All 4 minivans tested received Marginal or Poor overall ratings:
- **2023 Honda Odyssey: POOR** — rear dummy's head came dangerously close to front seatback
- **2023 Chrysler Pacifica: MARGINAL** — side curtain airbag FAILED TO DEPLOY; excessive belt force on rear dummy's chest
- **2023 Kia Carnival: MARGINAL** — excessive chest force on rear dummy
- **2023 Toyota Sienna: MARGINAL** — only minivan with belt pretensioners/force limiters in rear, but dummy submarined under lap belt, shoulder belt moved off shoulder toward neck

All four protected front-seat occupants well. The failure is exclusively in the back seat.
- Source: https://motorillustrated.com/minivans-fall-short-on-rear-seat-safety-in-latest-iihs-tests/122552/

### IIHS 2025 Awards (same pattern)
- Only 48 models qualified (down from 71 prior year)
- ZERO minivans earned either award
- Harkey: "The new emphasis on back seat protection appears to have winnowed minivans and pickups from the winners' ranks. That's unfortunate, considering that minivans are marketed as family haulers."
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/iihs-makes-stronger-protection-for-back-seat-passengers-a-must-for-2025

### Why Back Seat Is Falling Behind
- IIHS research: "in newer vehicles, the risk of a fatal injury is higher for belted adults in the rear seat than in the front"
- Not because rear seats got worse — front seats got dramatically better with advanced airbags and seat belts
- Most rear seats lack: force-limiting seat belts, pretensioners, rear-seat airbags
- IIHS updated moderate overlap test in 2022 to add rear-seat dummy (small female/12-year-old child)
