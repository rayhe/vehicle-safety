# Research: Brand Loyalty Is a Safety Gamble

## Angle
After 449 articles dissecting individual vehicles, a cross-brand FARS analysis reveals that "brand loyalty" is meaningless for safety. Every single manufacturer has an enormous internal safety spread between its safest and deadliest model — often 10-70x.

## Key Finding (Original Contribution)
Cross-tabulating FARS death rates across all brands reveals the internal safety spread:

| Brand | Deadliest Model (rate) | Safest Model (rate) | Spread |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|--------|
| Hyundai | Veloster (8.54) | Kona (0.12) | 71.2× |
| Ford | Mustang (6.02) | Transit (0.14) | 43.0× |
| Chevrolet | Tracker (7.83) | C/K Pickup (0.19) | 41.2× |
| Toyota | Land Cruiser (6.27) | RAV4 (0.19) | 33.0× |
| Honda | Accord (3.07) | HR-V (0.13) | 23.6× |
| Tesla | Model S (0.50) | Model Y (0.03) | 16.7× |
| Nissan | Maxima (5.11) | Rogue (0.35) | 14.6× |
| BMW | 3 Series (2.73) | X3 (0.23) | 11.9× |
| Subaru | Legacy (0.95) | Crosstrek (0.08) | 11.9× |

Every brand with 3+ models has at least a 2× spread. Most have 10×+. 

## Novel Contribution
This is a meta-analysis that nobody's run: instead of asking "which vehicle is safe?" it asks "does your choice of BRAND tell you anything?" Answer: almost nothing. A Hyundai can be 71× deadlier than another Hyundai. Brand reputation, marketing, and loyalty programs are safety-irrelevant.

## Sources
1. NHTSA FARS 2014-2023 (all data)
2. Cross-tabulation is original (rate/model within manufacturer)

## Limitations
- Some "deadliest" models are discontinued (Veloster, Tracker) — fleet age skews rates up
- Fleet size estimates introduce ±15% uncertainty for low-volume models
- FARS only captures fatal crashes, not injuries
- Commercial vehicles (Transit) have different use patterns than consumer vehicles
- Rate denominators (VMT) are estimated, not measured

## Strongest Counterargument
Brand DOES correlate with safety culture: Subaru's entire spread is 0.08-0.95 (max 12×), vs. Hyundai's 0.12-8.54 (71×). Some brands cluster tighter than others. But even Subaru's 12× spread means model choice matters far more than brand choice.

## Actionable Takeaway
Stop shopping by brand. Check FARS rates by specific model and model year. A Hyundai Kona (0.12) is statistically safer than a Toyota Land Cruiser (6.27). Brand-level marketing claims like "Toyota Safety Sense" or "Hyundai SmartSense" obscure model-level reality.
