# Research: The 2013 Hyundai Elantra Model Year Death Spike

## Angle
One single model year of the Hyundai Elantra — 2013 — produced 338 fatalities in FARS data (2014-2023). That same year, the Kia Forte (increasingly sharing Hyundai-Kia platforms) produced just 23. A 14.7x gap. By 2019, the gap had narrowed to 1.2x as the platforms converged.

## Kill Test
- **Genuinely surprising?** YES. 338 deaths from a single compact sedan model year puts it alongside Silverados and F-150s. Among compacts, only the 2008 Civic (402) and 2007 Civic (364) had deadlier single model years, and both had far higher sales.
- **Novel contribution?** Per-unit-sold death rate calculation showing the 2013 Elantra killed ~1.37 per 1,000 units sold vs the 2008 Civic at ~1.19/1K. Also: convergence timeline with Forte showing platform unification narrowing mortality gap.
- **Data-driven?** 100% FARS. Cross-referenced with IIHS crash test results, NHTSA recalls, estimated sales figures.

## Primary Sources
1. **FARS (2014-2023):** Elantra total 2,407 deaths, rate 1.50/100M VMT, 3,801 crashes, fleet 1.4M
2. **IIHS 2013 Elantra ratings:** TSP+ overall BUT "Marginal" structure in small overlap front. 22cm lower hinge pillar intrusion, 18cm upper hinge pillar intrusion. Airbag let dummy's head slide off left side.
3. **NHTSA recalls:** 200K+ Elantras recalled March 2015 for steering coupling bolt defect (Reuters)
4. **FARS toxicology:** Elantra impairment 18.6% — LOWEST of any compact sedan (Civic 20.4%, Corolla 19.2%, Focus 19.4%, Sentra 20.0%, Forte 20.1%)

### Model Year 2013 in Context
Among ALL vehicles, model years with 300+ deaths are exclusively trucks (Silverado, F-150, Ram), full-size sedans (Accord, Impala, Camry, Altima), large SUVs (Explorer, Trailblazer, Tahoe), the Cobalt, and... the 2013 Elantra. It's the ONLY Hyundai model year to reach 300+ deaths.

## Novel Calculations
1. **Per-unit-sold death rate (2013 MY):**
   - Elantra: ~247K sold in 2013, 338 deaths → 1.37 deaths/1,000 units
   - Civic: ~339K sold in 2008, 402 deaths → 1.19 deaths/1,000 units
   - Corolla: ~290K sold in 2010, 314 deaths → 1.08 deaths/1,000 units
   - The 2013 Elantra killed 15% more per unit than the deadliest Civic model year

2. **Volume-adjusted Elantra/Forte gap:**
   - 2013 sales: Elantra ~247K, Forte ~118K → 2.1x volume ratio
   - 2013 deaths: 338 vs 23 → 14.7x death ratio
   - Unexplained gap after volume adjustment: ~7x

3. **Convergence timeline:** Elantra/Forte ratio dropped from 14.7x (MY 2013) to 1.2x (MY 2019). As platforms converged, so did mortality.

## Strongest Counterargument
Sales volume explains some of the gap: Hyundai sold ~2.1x more 2013 Elantras than Kia sold 2013 Fortes. But even after volume adjustment, the Elantra is ~7x deadlier. More importantly, the 2013 Elantra's MY death count of 338 is anomalous even against the Elantra's own adjacent years (2012: 128, 2014: 132). The 338 figure is 2.6x the surrounding years, suggesting something specific to MY 2013 exposure and demographics, not just volume.

One partial explanation: the 2013 Elantra was Hyundai's best-selling car amid aggressive incentives after the MPG restatement scandal (Hyundai/Kia admitted overstating fuel economy by 1-3 MPG in November 2012, leading to buyer reimbursement). This flooded the market with price-sensitive buyers who may have been younger, less experienced drivers — a confound we can't isolate from vehicle factors.

## Limitations
- FARS death counts are cumulative 2014-2023 crashes involving that model year vehicle. A 2013 Elantra in a 2014 crash and a 2013 Elantra in a 2023 crash are both counted. Higher sales = more fleet exposure = more chances to appear in FARS.
- We don't have crash-year breakdown by model year, so we can't tell if the 2013 MY deaths cluster in specific calendar years.
- Sales figures are estimated from industry reports, not official Hyundai disclosures.
- The per-unit-sold rate assumes all sold units remained in the US fleet for the full FARS period, which is approximately true for a 2013-2023 window.
