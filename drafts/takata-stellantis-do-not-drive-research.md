# Research: Takata "Do Not Drive" + FARS Death Count

## Angle
Stellantis told 225,000 drivers to stop driving their cars because of unfixed Takata airbag inflators. FARS data shows those exact vehicle models and year ranges have already killed 4,071 people in regular crashes. The vehicles were already deadly. Now they have literal explosive devices in their steering columns.

## Self-Critique / Kill Test
- **Is this genuinely surprising after 178 articles?** YES. No existing article pairs the "do not drive" warning with FARS-specific death counts for those exact models/years. We have `unfixed-recall-epidemic.html` but that's a broader recall compliance piece, not Takata-specific with FARS model-year data.
- **Novel data combination?** YES. FARS deaths by model year for the 7 affected Stellantis vehicles (4,071 deaths across the recall-affected model years) vs. 28 confirmed US Takata deaths — the scale difference is the story.
- **Strongest counterargument:** The 4,071 FARS deaths are from normal crashes, not Takata failures. Takata has killed 28 people in the US. So the comparison is apples-to-oranges. RESPONSE: That's the point. These vehicles are already in the FARS database as frequent crash participants. The Takata defect adds a random, invisible death mechanism that can kill in crashes that would OTHERWISE BE SURVIVABLE. A fender-bender turns into a shrapnel event.
- **Actionable?** YES — check VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls, call Stellantis at 833-585-0144, repairs are free.

## Primary Sources (3+)

### 1. NHTSA FARS Database (2014-2023)
- Dodge RAM (all years): 4,407 deaths, rate 0.78/100M VMT
- Jeep Wrangler: 1,842 deaths, rate 0.84
- Dodge Dakota: 1,237 deaths, rate 2.62
- Chrysler 300: 751 deaths, rate 1.87
- Dodge Charger: 715 deaths, rate 0.75
- Dodge Challenger: 385 deaths, rate 1.00
- Dodge Durango: 356 deaths, rate 0.54
- **Total across all years: 9,693 deaths**

**Model-year-specific (Takata-affected years only):**
- Dodge RAM 2003-2010: 1,989 deaths
- Jeep Wrangler 2007-2016: 702 deaths
- Chrysler 300 2005-2015: 579 deaths
- Dodge Charger 2006-2015: 345 deaths
- Dodge Dakota 2005-2011: 252 deaths
- Dodge Durango 2004-2009: 123 deaths
- Dodge Challenger 2008-2014: 81 deaths
- **Total for Takata-affected year ranges: 4,071 deaths**

### 2. NHTSA Takata Recall Data
- 67 million Takata inflators recalled in US (100 million worldwide)
- At least 28 deaths in the US, 36+ worldwide (as of Sept 2024)
- 400+ injuries in the US
- Ammonium nitrate propellant deteriorates with heat/humidity over time
- Inflator housing ruptures, sending metal shrapnel into cabin
- Largest series of auto recalls in US history
- Takata filed bankruptcy due to the defect
- Source: NHTSA Takata recall spotlight, AP reporting (Sept 2024)

### 3. Stellantis "Do Not Drive" Warning (2026)
- ~225,000 Stellantis vehicles still unfixed
- 6.6 million recalled inflators already replaced in Chrysler/Dodge/Jeep/Ram
- Affected models: 
  - 2003-2010 Dodge Ram (Recall IDs: 15V312, 15V313, 16V352, 16V947, 18V021, 19V018)
  - 2004-2009 Dodge Durango
  - 2005-2011 Dodge Dakota
  - 2005-2008 Dodge Magnum
  - 2006-2015 Dodge Charger
  - 2007-2009 Chrysler Aspen
  - 2008-2014 Dodge Challenger
  - 2005-2015 Chrysler 300
  - 2007-2016 Jeep Wrangler
  - 2006-2009 Mitsubishi Raider
- Customer hotline: 833-585-0144
- Source: NHTSA recall database, WHEC News reporting

### 4. AP Reporting on Takata Deaths
- 28th US death confirmed September 2024 (2018 crash in Alabama, Honda Civic)
- Honda has replaced/accounted for 95% of recalled inflators
- Source: Associated Press, Sept 3, 2024
