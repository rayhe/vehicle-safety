# Research Notes: Equinox Middling Crossover Body Count

## Article #404 — The Crash Report

## Angle
The Chevrolet Equinox isn't dangerous by the numbers. Its 0.36 death rate per 100M VMT sits below the SUV median (0.54). But a direct competitor — the Mazda CX-5 — scores 0.12. Three times safer, same vehicle class. When 2.4 million Equinoxes are on the road at 3x the class leader's rate, "below average" adds up to 1,056 dead. And GM's electric replacement, the Equinox EV, has now been recalled twice for the same pedestrian alert sound defect.

## News Hook: Equinox EV Pedestrian Sound Recalls

### First Recall (Sept 2024 → filed Dec 2024)
- 23,700 units (MY 2024)
- Pedestrian alert sound system miscalibrated during production
- Did not produce sufficient sound at low speeds (<6.2 mph)
- FMVSS 141 non-compliance

### Second Recall (Dec 2025 / Jan 2026)
- 81,177 units (MY 2025-2026)
- Same defect: pedestrian alert sound system too quiet
- Different calibration from MY 2024 but same result
- Discovered by GM engineer via "Speak Up for Safety" internal program
- Total across both recalls: ~105,000 Equinox EVs

Source: NHTSA, Carscoops, IndexBox

## Key Narrative Points
1. "Below average" sounds safe. It isn't, when there are 2.4 million of you.
2. The CX-5 proves 0.12 is achievable in this class. The Equinox is 3× that.
3. MY 2018 spike: the 3rd-gen redesign brought the highest single-year death toll.
4. The EV transition: GM's electric answer to the Equinox's crash safety record can't even warn pedestrians it's rolling toward them — recalled twice for the same failure.
5. The gas Equinox kills people in crashes. The electric Equinox can't be heard approaching. Different technology, same nameplate, same company.
