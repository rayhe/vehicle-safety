# Research: The Kia Boys Body Count

## Angle
The TikTok "Kia Boys" phenomenon — in which teenagers exploited the absence of engine immobilizers in 2011-2021 Kia and 2015-2021 Hyundai models — shows up in FARS fatality data. We can cross-reference the model years that lacked immobilizers with per-model-year death counts to look for anomalous spikes.

## Kill Test
✅ **Novel contribution**: No one has mapped the Kia Boys crisis specifically to FARS model-year fatality data at a per-vehicle level. The connection has been discussed in news (NHTSA counted 14 crashes, 8 fatalities from stolen Kia/Hyundai), but our FARS data allows us to see whether the affected model years show anomalous death patterns compared to what their depreciation curves should predict.

✅ **Genuinely newsworthy**: The theft epidemic caused 1,000%+ increase in theft claims (HLDI data). NHTSA confirmed 14 crashes and 8 fatalities directly attributed to stolen vehicles, but the true body count may be higher — FARS captures all fatal crashes involving these vehicles, not just those confirmed as theft-related.

✅ **Data-driven**: 3+ primary sources — FARS model-year data, NHTSA press releases, HLDI theft claim statistics, Safety Research & Strategies petition data.

## Key Data Points

### Affected Models (no immobilizer)
- Kia: 2011-2021 Optima, Soul, Sorento, Forte, Sportage, Rio
- Hyundai: 2015-2021 models with steel keys (Elantra, Sonata, Tucson, Santa Fe, Accent)
- Only 26% of Hyundai/Kia had immobilizers as standard in MY 2015 vs 96% industry average (HLDI)

### FARS Model Year Death Patterns (anomalies)

**Kia Forte** — 2021 MY had 76 deaths, UP from 56 in 2019 and 50 in 2020. A 2021 model year should be in its safest period (newest safety tech, lowest mileage). 76 deaths is the second-highest of any Forte model year, behind only 2017 (81).

**Kia Optima** — Deaths went from 12.7 avg (2005-2010 MY) to 58.3 avg (2015-2020 MY) — a 4.6x increase, the largest of any vehicle in the FARS database.

**Kia Soul** — 2016 MY had 78 deaths (the peak), 2020 MY still at 53.

**Hyundai Elantra** — 2013 MY had 338 deaths (highest), 2020 MY still at 95. But 2017 MY also 199.

**Hyundai Sonata** — 2011 MY had 230 deaths, but newer MYs drop off normally.

### Theft Timeline
- July 2021: First TikTok videos showing how to steal Kia/Hyundai with USB cable
- 2021-2023: Epidemic spread nationwide
- HLDI: Theft claims went from ~1 per 1,000 insured vehicles to 11.2 per 1,000 by H1 2023
- NHTSA: At least 14 crashes, 8 fatalities from stolen Kia/Hyundai vehicles (as of 2023 reporting)
- NHTSA refused to issue recall, calling it a "customer satisfaction campaign"

### The Real Story
The NHTSA's official count (14 crashes, 8 deaths) is almost certainly an undercount. FARS doesn't reliably track whether a vehicle was stolen at the time of crash. The true death toll from the Kia Boys epidemic may be embedded in the overall fatality numbers — visible only as anomalous bumps in model-year death curves that should be declining.
