# Research: EU Worn-Tire Test Gap

## Angle
The EU General Safety Regulation Phase 2 took effect July 7, 2026. Among its mandates: new cars' tires must now prove wet-grip performance when worn to their legal tread depth (1.6mm). The US standard (FMVSS 139) tests tires only when brand new. ~600 Americans die per year in tire-related crashes. The vehicles with the highest FARS death rates—tall SUVs and vans most vulnerable to tire-failure rollovers—are exactly the fleet that needs worn-tire testing most.

## Primary Sources

1. **European Commission, July 7, 2026:** "Safer cars, safer roads: New rules take effect." Phase 2 mandates: advanced pedestrian/cyclist AEB, driver distraction warning, better forward vision, worn tyre tests, expanded safety glass. https://commission.europa.eu

2. **UNECE R117-04 (worn-tire regulation):** Tires must meet wet-grip, rolling-resistance, and noise criteria when machined to 1.6mm-2.0mm tread depth. Tires tested against a standard reference tire also machined to reduced tread. https://tuvsud.com — TÜV SÜD summary.

3. **NHTSA, "Safety and Savings Ride on Your Tires":** "On average, there are nearly 11,000 tire-related motor vehicle crashes each year and more than 600 people will die in those crashes." https://nhtsa.gov

4. **NHTSA tire inflation data:** 660 fatalities and 33,000 injuries per year from low-pressure-related crashes. 1 in 3 cars has significantly underinflated tires. 85% of drivers fail to properly check tire pressure.

5. **Michelin, July 9, 2024:** 50% of tires removed before reaching 3mm of residual depth. Michelin worn tires exhibit braking distances 3.4m shorter on average than a new competitor tire. https://european-rubber-journal.com

6. **NHTSA NASS/CDS 1995-2012 (NTSB presentation):** Tire-related crashes involving light vehicles, annualized ~593 fatalities. Loss of control, blowout, or flat.

7. **US FMVSS 139:** Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standard for new pneumatic tires. Tests tire performance (traction, treadwear, temperature resistance) only when new.

### The Ford Explorer / Firestone connection
The Explorer/Firestone rollover crisis (2000-2001) killed 271 people. Firestone ATX/Wilderness tires failed catastrophically when worn — tread separation at highway speed on hot pavement, causing Explorer rollovers. THIS is exactly what the EU worn-tire test is designed to catch. The US response was TREAD Act (TPMS mandate) but still no worn-tire performance testing.

## Novel Contribution
1. Connecting EU July 7 worn-tire mandate directly to FARS death rate data
2. Showing the vehicles with highest death rates are disproportionately vulnerable to tire-failure crashes (tall CG + rollover)
3. The Firestone/Explorer historical parallel: 271 deaths from worn-tire failure → TPMS mandate → but still no worn-tire GRIP testing 25 years later
4. The EU tests what the US learned kills people but still won't test

## Counterargument
- TPMS (mandated since 2008) addresses underinflation, which is the largest single tire-failure cause
- UTQGS treadwear ratings give consumers tire longevity info
- US tires sold in the US often meet UNECE requirements too (global manufacturers)
- But: TPMS doesn't measure tread depth; UTQGS doesn't measure worn-grip; global manufacturers may not apply R117-04 standards to US-market tires

## Actionable
- Check tire tread depth monthly (penny test: if you see Lincoln's head, replace)
- Replace at 3/32" (2.4mm), not the legal minimum 2/32" (1.6mm)  
- In the rain, worn tires on a 3,500-lb car need up to 87 extra feet to stop from 60 mph
- When buying tires, look for manufacturers publishing worn-tire performance data (Michelin, Continental)
- Check your vehicle at nhtsa.gov/recalls for tire-related recalls
