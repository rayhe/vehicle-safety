# Research: NHTSA's New ADAS Tests Are Certifying the Wrong Fleet

## Angle
NHTSA just launched new ADAS safety benchmarks (May 7, 2026) — Tesla Model Y was the first vehicle to pass. But FARS data shows the vehicles that are actually killing people are overwhelmingly too old to ever be tested. 92.4% of fatal crash vehicles predate 2018. Only 0.6% are from model years that would have standard ADAS. The new tests certify 0.6% of the problem.

## News Hook (May 7, 2026)
- NHTSA added 4 new ADAS tests to New Car Assessment Program: pedestrian AEB, lane keeping, blind spot warning, blind spot intervention
- Tesla Model Y (2026) was first vehicle to pass all four
- Only applies to new, in-production vehicles
- Sources: TechCrunch, Carscoops, NHTSA

## FARS Data Analysis (Original)

### The Fleet Gap
- **92.4%** of fatal crash vehicles are model years pre-2018 (before widespread AEB adoption)
- **82.7%** are pre-2015 model years
- **57.6%** are model years before 2008 (10+ years old during FARS period)
- Only **7.6%** of fatal crash vehicles are 2018+ model years
- Only **3.2%** are 2020+ model years
- Only **0.6%** are 2022+ model years (the fleet where AEB is nearly universal)
- Ratio: 143.4 deaths in pre-2018 vehicles for every 1 death in a 2022+ vehicle

### The Deadliest vs Safest
Top 10 deadliest vehicles by rate (50+ deaths):
1. Hyundai Veloster: 8.54/100M VMT — **discontinued 2021**
2. Chevy Tracker: 7.83 — **discontinued 2004**
3. Toyota Land Cruiser: 6.27 — **no recent model years in data**
4. Ford Mustang: 6.02 — active
5. Nissan Maxima: 5.11 — **discontinued 2023**
6. Chevy Cobalt: 5.10 — **discontinued 2010**
7. Chevy Impala: 5.00 — **discontinued 2020**
8. Chevy S-10: 4.83 — **discontinued 2004**
9. Toyota Solara: 4.25 — **discontinued 2008**
10. Cadillac Seville: 3.89 — **discontinued 2004**

**7 of the 10 deadliest are discontinued.** They'll never sit for the new test.

Top 5 safest:
1. Tesla Model Y: 0.03 — active, first to pass new ADAS test
2. Tesla Model 3: 0.05 — active
3. Subaru Crosstrek: 0.08 — active
4. Mazda CX-5: 0.12 — active
5. Hyundai Kona: 0.12 — active

**All 5 safest are current-production, ADAS-equipped.** They already passed before the test existed.

### Key Limitation
The model year death counts are cumulative across 2014-2023 crash years. A 2005 model had 10 years of exposure in the FARS dataset while a 2022 model had 1-2 years. This inflates older model year counts by exposure time, not just rate. The 92.4% figure reflects CUMULATIVE EXPOSURE, not instantaneous risk comparison. However, the core finding holds: the fleet currently killing people is overwhelmingly pre-ADAS, and no test regime can retroactively make those vehicles safer.
