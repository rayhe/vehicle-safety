# Research: Road Debris — The Swerve-to-Die Paradox

## Core Data (AAA Foundation for Traffic Safety, 2018-2023)
- **319,724 crashes** involving road debris (2018-2023)
- **53,000 crashes/year** average
- **5,500 injuries/year**
- **72 deaths/year** (433 total, 2018-2023)
- Source: "The Safety Impact of Road Debris: Updated Prevalences of Crashes, Injuries, and Deaths in the United States, 2018–2023" — AAA Foundation for Traffic Safety

## The Paradox
- **~50% of deaths** came from the EVASIVE MANEUVER (swerving), not from hitting the debris
- Earlier AAA study (2011-2014): 37% of all deaths from swerving to avoid
- Updated data: "about a quarter of injuries and nearly half of all deaths" from evasive maneuvers
- The debris is the lure. The swerve is what kills.

## Types of Debris
- Vehicle parts (tires, bumpers, wheels): 30% of crashes
- Unsecured cargo (furniture, BBQ grills, mattresses): ~19% of crashes  
- Natural debris (tree branches, roadkill): remainder
- Two-thirds of debris-related crashes from items falling from vehicles (improper maintenance + unsecured loads)

## FARS Context
- FARS 2024: 36,297 fatal crashes, ~39,254 fatalities
- 72 debris deaths/year ÷ ~39,000 total fatalities = ~0.18%
- Tiny fraction but 100% preventable
- Every one involves someone else's negligence (unsecured load) or a maintenance failure (detached part)

## Legal Context
- All 50 states have laws requiring load securement
- Penalties range from $10 to $2,500
- Some states allow jail time for unsecured loads causing injury/death
- Enforcement is essentially nonexistent — treated as littering

## Timeliness
- Fox News (July 19, 2026): Metal pipes impaled a sedan on California highway from unsecured cargo
- AAA updated study published late 2025
- Summer = peak moving season = peak debris season

## Original Contribution
1. The "swerve-to-die" math: hitting debris at 60mph is usually survivable (airbags, crumple zones protect you). Swerving at 60mph into a median barrier, tree, or oncoming traffic is not.
2. The legal asymmetry: the driver who drops a mattress faces a littering fine. The driver who swerves to avoid it and dies faces death. 
3. Counter-intuitive safety advice: Don't swerve for road debris. Hit it. Your car was designed to absorb impacts. The median barrier was not designed to absorb your car at full speed.
