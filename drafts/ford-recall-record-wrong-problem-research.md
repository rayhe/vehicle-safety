# Research: Ford's 153-Recall Record vs. FARS Death Rate Disconnect

## Thesis
Ford issued 153 safety recalls in 2025 — double the all-time record. NHTSA fined them $165M. But cross-referencing their recall volume with FARS fatality data reveals a disturbing truth: the recalls and the deaths are fixing completely different problems. Ford's recall crisis is a quality control failure. Ford's death toll is a physics problem. The recall system addresses liability, not mortality.

## Novel Contribution
Original cross-tabulation: Ford's recall categories (rearview cameras, software updates, trim) vs. their actual fatality drivers (mass differential, vehicle age, structural design). The 153 recalls don't reduce the 34,954 deaths. They reduce lawsuit exposure.

## Key Data Points for Article

1. **153 recalls ÷ 29 models = ~5.3 recalls per model in one year**
2. **27% of recalls were do-overs** — fixing the fix
3. **Ford's F-150 kills ~959 people/year** (9,194 ÷ 10 years)
4. **None of the 153 recalls addressed the F-150's fatality rate**
5. **Ford's fleet-average death rate (~1.46 deaths/100M VMT)** vs overall average (~1.03 for sedans, ~0.63 for SUVs)
6. **The Mustang's 6.02 rate** is 5.8x the fleet average — no recall for that

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? **YES.** 
- Ford's 153-recall record is verifiable and recent (2025)
- The FARS cross-tabulation is original — nobody else has mapped recall categories against fatality drivers
- The thesis is counterintuitive: more recalls ≠ fewer deaths
- Directly actionable: consumers need to understand that a "recalled" vehicle may still have fatal design characteristics that no recall will ever address

## Limitations
- FARS data is 2014-2023; the 2025 recalls are more recent, so direct causal overlap is limited
- Recall categories are manually classified, not from a structured NHTSA database
- We cannot definitively prove that zero recalls addressed crashworthiness — some may have (brake defects, steering issues)
- Fleet estimates use VMT calculations with ±15% uncertainty for low-volume models

## Strongest Counterargument
Ford would argue that recalls DO save lives — rearview cameras prevent ~78% of backover fatalities (per IIHS), and software recalls addressing AEB, lane keeping, and other ADAS features prevent crashes that never appear in FARS because they never happen. The absence of deaths in recall categories might be evidence that the recalls worked, not that they're irrelevant.
