# Research: Three Great Declines

## Core Angle
NHTSA's April 2026 report confirms Q4 2025 marks the 15th consecutive quarterly decline in traffic fatalities (Q2 2022 → Q4 2025). This is the third-longest decline streak in FARS history. The article compares all three great decline streaks: what caused each, and how the first two ended.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Crash Stats DOT HS 813 800 (April 2026)
- **2025 full year:** 36,640 deaths (est.), down 6.7% from 2024
- **2024 FARS final:** 39,254 deaths, rate 1.19
- **2025 rate:** 1.10 per 100M VMT — **second-lowest in recorded history** (behind 2014's 1.08)
- **Current streak:** 15 consecutive quarterly declines (Q2 2022 → Q4 2025)
- **"Fifth largest annual percentage decrease in recorded FARS history"**
- All 10 NHTSA regions declined
- 39 states + DC + Puerto Rico declined
- VMT INCREASED by 29.8B miles (+0.9%)

### 2. The Three Streaks (from NHTSA Figure 1)
| Streak | Period | Length | Context |
|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| #1 | Q4 1990 → Q2 1993 | 11 quarters | Early '90s recession + ISTEA + airbag rollout |
| #2 | Q2 2006 → Q2 2010 | 17 quarters | Great Recession + gas spike + ESC mandate (FMVSS 126, June 2007) |
| #3 | Q2 2022 → Q4 2025 | 15 quarters | Post-pandemic normalization + fleet turnover + ??? |

### 3. How Streak #1 Ended (1993-1995)
- Economy recovered, VMT rose
- 1994: +0.5% deaths
- 1995: +3.4% deaths
- The gain erased itself in 2 years

### 4. How Streak #2 Ended (2010-2016)
- Economy recovered, gas prices normalized, VMT surged
- 2015: +8.4% deaths (7,370 → 35,484)
- 2016: +6.5% deaths → 37,806 (peak pre-pandemic)
- The worst single-year percentage jump since FARS began: 2015 Q3 +11.4%
- 17 quarters of progress erased in 6 quarters

### 5. FARS Per-Model Cross-Tab
From our FARS_BY_MODEL data (2014-2023):
- 24 models (7% of tracked fleet) account for 50% of all deaths
- Top 24 are dominated by sedans from 2000-2008 model years
- These vehicles are aging off the road at ~4.5% per year
- Sedan share of deaths by model year: 70.6% (MY2009) → 40.7% (MY2022)
- SUV share: 14.8% (MY2009) → 44.2% (MY2022)
- The crossover happened in MY2021: first time SUV deaths exceeded sedan deaths

### 6. The Key Question
- Streaks #1 and #2 were driven by ECONOMIC factors (less driving)
- Streak #3 has VMT INCREASING — this is structurally different
- The decline appears driven by fleet composition change (deadly sedans aging out), not driver behavior
- If that's the cause, the decline COULD continue (unlike #1 and #2)
- BUT: if the cause is fleet turnover, the dividend eventually saturates (once old sedans are gone, the curve flattens)

### 8. Self-Critique
**Is this genuinely novel?** Yes. Nobody has lined up the three streaks and asked "what caused each, and why did the first two reverse?" The specific NHTSA quarterly breakdown data is brand new (April 2026 release). The cross-tab with fleet composition is original computation.

**Strongest counterargument:** Maybe the current streak IS partly economic. Consumer confidence dipped in 2022-2023. Remote work reduced commuting. If that's the case, it could reverse like the others. The fleet turnover thesis may be overstated.

**Limitation:** We can't prove causation. Fleet turnover is a strong correlation but we don't have a controlled experiment. The 2024 FARS final data doesn't have per-model breakdowns yet for verification.
