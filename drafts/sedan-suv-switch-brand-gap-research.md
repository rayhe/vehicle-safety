# Research: The Sedan-to-SUV Switch — Brand Gap

## Angle
"Buy an SUV for safety" is America's most popular car-buying advice. FARS data shows the safety gain of switching from sedan to SUV varies by 9x depending on the brand — from a genuine lifesaver (VW 9.0x) to statistically meaningless (Lincoln 0.9x).

## Original Contribution
Weighted VMT-normalized sedan-to-SUV death rate ratio by brand. This cross-tabulation has not been published in any existing Crash Report article.

## Novel Finding
The "switch to an SUV" advice is brand-dependent:
- VW/Mazda/BMW: 7-9x safer (dramatic, life-saving)
- Honda/Hyundai/Nissan: 4-6x safer (significant)
- Toyota/Lexus: 3.8-4.1x (moderate)
- GM brands (Chevy/Cadillac): ~2x (marginal)
- Ford: 1.6x (weak)
- Lincoln/Mitsubishi/Mercury: ~1.0x (negligible/reversed)

## Why the Variance?
Likely drivers:
1. Fleet age composition: import brands' sedans include older models (Jetta, 3 Series, Civic) with decades of fleet presence. Their SUVs (Tiguan, X3, CR-V) are newer with modern safety tech.
2. SUV design philosophy: body-on-frame legacy SUVs (Tahoe, Expedition, Navigator) carry higher death rates than unibody crossovers (CR-V, RAV4, Tiguan).
3. GM/Ford legacy: their SUV lineups include older, heavier body-on-frame designs that don't outperform their sedans by as much.

## Limitations
- Conflates model generation age with class engineering differences
- Fleet estimates carry ±15% uncertainty for low-volume models
- Does not control for driver demographics (who buys each brand's sedan vs SUV)
- Pre-2012 vehicles dominate death counts, so the ratio reflects historical, not current, engineering

## Strongest Counterargument
The gap might just measure "how old are your sedans vs your SUVs." Brands with newer SUV lineups (VW introduced Tiguan 2018, Atlas 2018) and older sedan lineups (Jetta since 1979) naturally show bigger gaps — it's a calendar effect, not an engineering achievement.
