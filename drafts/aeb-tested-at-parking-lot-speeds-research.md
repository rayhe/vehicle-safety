# Research: AEB Tested at Parking Lot Speeds

## Angle
The IIHS test that certifies your car's AEB works at 12-25 mph. Only 3% of rear-end crashes happen at those speeds. At 55 mph — where 70% of fatal rear-end crashes occur — AAA found ZERO test vehicles avoided the collision. The federal mandate to fix this (FMVSS 127, requiring 62 mph) is frozen in regulatory limbo and under industry lawsuit.

**Kill test:** Is this novel? Yes — the existing AEB articles on the site cover the lawsuit (#aeb-lawsuit-body-count), motorcycle detection gap (#motorcycle-aeb-fleet-gap), and hi-vis blindness (#hi-vis-invisible-to-aeb). None focus on the speed testing methodology gap and the AAA data showing 0% avoidance at highway speeds. This is a testing theater / certification vs reality story.

## Primary Sources

### 1. IIHS Study — David Kidd (2022-2023)
- IIHS vehicle-to-vehicle AEB test: 12 mph and 25 mph approach speeds
- 85% of 2022 model year vehicles earn "superior" rating — test no longer differentiates
- Kidd used FARS + GES databases (2016-2019) to map crash scenarios to test coverage
- **Key findings:**
  - Only 3% of police-reported rear-end crashes happen at ≤25 mph speed limits
  - Only 1% of FATAL rear-end crashes at ≤25 mph
  - 70% of fatal rear-end crashes at ≥55 mph speed limits
  - 50% of nonfatal injury crashes at 35-45 mph
  - Speed limit is valid proxy for travel speed (EDR data: average 2 mph below posted limit)
  - 40%+ of fatal rear-end crashes involve motorcycles, medium trucks, or heavy trucks as struck vehicle
- At 45 mph test, would cover 43% of police-reported and 12% of fatal rear-end crashes
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/iihs-eyes-higher-speed-test-for-automatic-emergency-braking
- Paper: https://www.iihs.org/research-areas/bibliography/ref/2250

### 2. AAA Research (October 2024)
- Tested old (2017-2018) vs new (2024) AEB systems, same make/model
- Results at 35 mph: 2024 models avoided 100% of collisions; 2017-18 avoided 51%
- At 45 mph: 3 of 4 vehicles avoided collision
- **At 55 mph: ZERO vehicles avoided collision**
- Source: https://newsroom.aaa.com/2024/10/out-old-aeb-in-new/

### 3. NHTSA FMVSS 127 (May 2024, finalized)
- Requires AEB to achieve full collision avoidance up to 62 mph (lead vehicle)
- Auto-braking up to 90 mph for imminent vehicle collisions
- Pedestrian AEB up to 45 mph (daylight and dark)
- Projected: save 360 lives/year, prevent 24,000 injuries
- Compliance deadline: September 1, 2029
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/nhtsa-fmvss-127-automatic-emergency-braking-reduce-crashes

### 4. Regulatory Freeze & Lawsuit
- Alliance for Automotive Innovation (AAI) petitioned for reconsideration (June 2024)
- NHTSA revised but largely upheld (November 2024)
- AAI filed lawsuit in D.C. Circuit (January 17, 2025)
- Trump administration regulatory freeze (January 20, 2025)
- DOT filed motion to hold appeal in abeyance (February 24, 2025) — granted
- Case remains in abeyance; September 2029 deadline still technically in effect
- Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (49 U.S.C. § 30129) REQUIRES the rule
- Source: https://www.nelsonmullins.com/insights/blogs/driving-forward-developments-in-transportation-law-and-innovation/all/the-road-ahead-for-fmvss-127-whither-the-automatic-emergency-braking-mandate

### 5. Broader AEB Effectiveness Data
- AEB reduces police-reported rear-end crashes by 50% (IIHS study)
- Forward collision warning + AEB reduces by 27-50%
- Rear-end crashes = ~1/3 of police-reported crashes, ~1/3 of injury crashes, 7% of fatal
- AEB could address up to 70% of rear-end crashes (IIHS estimate)
- AEB less effective in wet/snowy conditions, turning/lane change, and at ≥70 mph
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/front-crash-prevention-slashes-police-reported-rear-end-crashes

### 6. Traffic Death Context
- 2025 traffic fatalities: 36,640 (6.7% drop, 15th consecutive quarterly decline)
- 2024: 39,345 fatalities
- Fatality rate 2025: 1.10 per 100M VMT (second-lowest in history)
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/traffic-deaths-2025-early-estimates-2024-annual

## Original Contribution
**Cross-calculation: the actual crash pool addressed by current IIHS-certified AEB**

- ~39,000 fatal crashes in 2024
- Rear-end = 7% → ~2,730 fatal rear-end crashes
- At ≤25 mph (current IIHS test range): 1% of fatal rear-end → ~27 fatal crashes
- AEB prevents 50% of those → ~14 lives saved from the certified speed range
- At ≤45 mph (proposed new IIHS test): 12% of fatal rear-end → ~328 fatal crashes
- At 62 mph (FMVSS 127 mandate): would address substantially more
- Gap: the test certifies performance at speeds where perhaps 14 fatal crashes a year are prevented, not the 360 NHTSA projects (which assumes the 62 mph mandate)

Note: AEB does work at higher speeds than tested — just unreliably. The issue is we don't KNOW how well because the test doesn't measure it. AAA's 55 mph result (0% avoidance) suggests the real-world benefit above 45 mph is minimal with current systems.
