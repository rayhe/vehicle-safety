# Research: The Death Rate Mirage

## Angle
NSC reports Feb 2026 death rate at 1.01/100M VMT — the lowest in modern history, down from 1.32 just 3 years ago. NSC projected a 12% decline for full-year 2025. But FARS data reveals the improvement is unevenly distributed: 90 vehicle models (36% of the registered fleet) still have death rates above 1.01. These vehicles account for 69% of all FARS deaths.

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing the NSC's declining national rate with per-model FARS data to show that the "improvement" is driven entirely by the safe majority (modern crossovers/SUVs), while the dangerous minority (older sedans, sports cars, small trucks) kill at unchanged per-mile rates. The national average is a fleet-composition artifact, not a driver behavior improvement.

## Key Data Points

### NSC Preliminary Data (Feb 2026)
- Feb 2026 death rate: 1.01 per 100M VMT
- Feb 2025: 1.10, Feb 2024: 1.22, Feb 2023: 1.32
- Decline: 23% in 3 years
- Feb 2026 deaths: 2,470 (down 6% YoY, down 17% from 2024)
- NSC projects 12% decline for full-year 2025
- Source: https://injuryfacts.nsc.org/motor-vehicle/overview/preliminary-monthly-estimates/

### FARS Cross-Tab (our data, 2014-2023)
- 90 models with rate > 1.01: 36.2% of fleet, 69.2% of deaths (131,090 of 189,503)
- 189 models with rate ≤ 1.01: 63.8% of fleet, 30.8% of deaths
- 28 models with rate > 2x national (>2.26): 14M fleet, 51,047 deaths
- 4 models at 5x+ national rate: Mustang (6.02), Tracker (7.83), Veloster (8.54), Land Cruiser (6.27)
- Model years 2000-2008 account for 47.3% of all FARS deaths despite shrinking fleet share
- Peak death model year: 2005 (11,363 deaths)

### Class-level death rates
- Sedan: 0.645 deaths per crash (most lethal class)
- Sports Car: 0.682 deaths per crash
- SUV: 0.524 deaths per crash
- Van: 0.514 deaths per crash
- Pickup: 0.489 deaths per crash (most survivable)

### The Mechanism
As pre-2009 vehicles age out of the fleet, their disproportionate death contribution shrinks mechanically. This accounts for much of the decline. The safe SUV/crossover fleet (Tesla Model Y at 0.03, Subaru Crosstrek at 0.08, Mazda CX-5 at 0.12) is pulling the average down through sheer volume, not because dangerous vehicles got safer.

## Counterargument
ESC mandate (2012), AEB proliferation, and improved crash structures ARE saving lives in newer vehicles. The improvement isn't PURELY compositional — newer vehicles of the same type are genuinely safer. Also, pandemic driving behavior changes (reduced commuting, more remote work) may still be contributing to lower VMT risk patterns.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers 2014-2023; doesn't capture 2024-2026 changes
- Per-model rates use VMT estimates with ±15% uncertainty
- National rate is from NSC preliminary estimates (subject to revision)
- Cannot isolate fleet composition effect from genuine per-vehicle improvement without year-by-year fleet registration data
