# Research: Ford's Two Rollaway Recalls in Six Months

## Angle
Ford has now issued TWO separate rollaway recalls in 2026, affecting over 1 million vehicles combined. Different vehicles. Different transmission types. Same end result: your car won't stay parked. The latest — 741,195 vehicles announced June 30, 2026 — doesn't have a fix available until April 2027. That's 10 months of known-defective exposure.

## Self-Critique Gate
**Is this genuinely surprising?** YES. Two rollaway recalls for different platforms within six months is a systemic pattern, not a one-off defect. The remedy gap (10 months) for a defect that can cause vehicles to roll away is genuinely alarming. FARS data for the affected models shows they're some of America's most popular vehicles with a significant death footprint.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Recall 26S48 (June 30, 2026)
- Ford recalls 741,195 vehicles
- Affected: 2018-2021 Expedition, 2020-2021 Explorer, 2021 F-150, 2020-2021 Lincoln Aviator, 2018-2021 Lincoln Navigator
- Defect: Transmission park pawl may engage while vehicle is in motion, damaging park system → vehicle can roll away when parked
- Remedy: Software update + inspect/replace transmission components
- Remedy availability: April 2027 (10 months from announcement)
- Interim owner notification: Aug 3, 2026
- Source: NHTSA, Reuters, WSJ, USA Today

### 2. NHTSA Recall 25V863 / 25C69 (December 2025/January 2026)
- Ford recalls 272,645 vehicles
- Affected: 2022-2026 F-150 Lightning, 2024-2026 Mustang Mach-E, 2025-2026 Maverick
- Defect: Integrated park module may fail to lock into park position → vehicle can roll away
- Remedy: OTA software update or dealer fix
- Source: Reuters, KBB, NHTSA

### 4. Ford's 2026 Recall Pattern (WSJ, June 30, 2026)
- January 2026: 272,645 vehicles (rollaway — Lightning, Mach-E, Maverick)

### 5. NHTSA FMVSS 114 — Theft Protection and Rollaway Prevention
- The June 30 recall explicitly states vehicles "fail to comply with the requirements of FMVSS number 114"
- This is a federal safety standard compliance failure, not just a customer complaint

## Novel Contribution
1. **Two rollaway recalls, two platforms:** The January recall affected EVs/hybrids with electronic park modules. Today's recall affects ICE vehicles with 10-speed automatic transmissions. Different drivetrains, same failure mode → Ford has a systemic park reliability problem across its entire lineup.
2. **Remedy gap math:** 741,195 vehicles × 10 months = ~6.2 million vehicle-months of known-defective exposure. No NHTSA deadline exists for remedy availability.
3. **Cumulative 2026 "transmission doesn't do what you asked" recalls:** 272K (rollaway) + 741K (rollaway) + 1.39M (downshifting) = ~2.4M Ford vehicles with transmission behavior defects in 6 months.
4. **FARS death footprint context:** The 5 models in today's recall collectively killed 14,904 people over 2014-2023. These aren't niche models — they include the #2 best-selling vehicle in America (F-150).

## Limitations
- FARS data is all-cause fatalities, not rollaway-specific
- The recall population is limited model years (2018-2021); FARS data covers broader years
- No reports of crashes/deaths from this specific defect — yet
- Rollaway risk is primarily to bystanders and property, not occupants

## Strongest Counterargument
Ford acted voluntarily — they discovered the defect and initiated the recall without waiting for a crash. No injuries or deaths have been attributed to this defect. The 10-month remedy timeline may reflect the complexity of a software+hardware fix across multiple platforms and model years. Responsible disclosure with an interim notification is arguably better than waiting until the fix is ready.
