# Research Notes: Highlander Rear-Seat Test Failure

## Angle
The Toyota Highlander has one of the lowest fatality rates among 3-row SUVs in the FARS data. But the IIHS's updated moderate overlap test — which now includes a rear-seat dummy — gave it a MARGINAL rating because of elevated risk of head, neck, and abdominal injuries to the rear-seat dummy. For a vehicle whose entire purpose is hauling families, failing the rear-seat test is the one test it can't afford to fail.

The Subaru Ascent — which the FARS data shows has a HIGHER per-mile death rate — earned TSP+ with good marks across the board.

## Original Contribution
Novel cross-tabulation: pairing FARS model-level fatality rates with IIHS rear-seat dummy results reveals that low overall death rates can mask specific rear-seat protection deficiencies. The overall stats say "safe." The specific test that measures what happens to your kid in row 2 says "marginal."

## Key Data

### FARS Data (2014-2023)
- Toyota HIGHLANDER: 1,106 deaths / 2,434 crashes / 2.1M fleet / rate 0.42 per 100M VMT / 45.4% lethality / 16.4% impairment
- Subaru ASCENT: 426 deaths / 759 crashes / 437K fleet / rate 0.78 / 56.1% lethality
- Honda PILOT: 514 deaths / 1,135 crashes / 1.4M fleet / rate 0.29 / 45.3% lethality
- Ford EXPLORER: 3,797 deaths / 6,626 crashes / 2.0M fleet / rate 1.54 / 57.3% lethality
- Chevrolet TRAVERSE: 265 deaths / 675 crashes / 1.1M fleet / rate 0.20 / 39.3% lethality

### IIHS 2026 Results
- Toyota Highlander: NO AWARD. Marginal in updated moderate overlap front (rear-seat dummy showed elevated head, neck, abdominal injury risk). Good in small overlap, side.
- Subaru Ascent: TSP+. Good across the board including moderate overlap.
- The moderate overlap test now includes a dummy the size of a small woman or 12-year-old in the seat behind the driver.

### Key Paradox
The Highlander's FARS rate (0.42) is LOWER than the Ascent's (0.78), yet the Ascent passes the rear-seat test and the Highlander fails it. FARS aggregates all occupant positions. The IIHS test isolates rear-seat protection specifically. Different lens, different verdict.

## Limitation
FARS data does not break down fatalities by seating position at the model level, so we cannot independently verify whether rear-seat occupants die at higher rates in the Highlander. The IIHS test is a controlled lab evaluation; real-world outcomes may differ.

## Actionable Takeaway
If you're buying a 3-row SUV to carry your kids: check the IIHS moderate overlap test result specifically. NHTSA's 5-star rating doesn't test rear-seat occupant protection in frontal offset crashes. The IIHS now does. The Highlander, Palisade (pre-Nov 2025), and others have been caught by this new test.
