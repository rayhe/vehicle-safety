# Research: 36,640 — America's Safest Year on the Same Dangerous Fleet

## Angle
NHTSA released the 2025 full-year data: 36,640 traffic deaths, rate of 1.10 per 100M VMT. This is below the pre-COVID average of 1.13 for the first time on a full-year basis. Our March article predicted 37,000-39,000. We were wrong — in the right direction.

The original finding: the vehicle fleet is unchanged. The same Silverados, F-150s, Accords, and Mustangs with their FARS death rates are still on the road. No major safety mandate took effect between 2024 and 2025. The AEB requirement doesn't hit until 2029. The decline is behavioral, not engineering. This means our FARS per-vehicle data measures a floor — the hardware-imposed minimum — and the behavior component swings the national body count by thousands.

## Key Data Points
- **2025 full-year:** 36,640 deaths, 1.10/100M VMT, -6.7% from 2024 (source: NHTSA/AASHTO)
- **2024 final:** 39,254 deaths, 1.19/100M VMT (source: NHTSA)
- **Four-year decline:** 42,721 (2022) → 40,901 (2023) → 39,254 (2024) → 36,640 (2025) = 14.2% decline in 3 years
- **2025 rate of 1.10** is below the pre-COVID average of 1.13 (2013-2019)
- **2025 VMT increased** by ~29.8 billion miles (+0.9%), so MORE driving with FEWER deaths
- Fatalities decreased in **39 states + DC + Puerto Rico** in 2025
- March article predicted 37,000-39,000 → actual 36,640 beat our LOW estimate

## FARS Cross-Reference (unchanged fleet)

- Average vehicle age in US: ~12.6 years (S&P Global, 2024)

## Original Contribution
Decomposing the national decline into engineering vs. behavior. If the same fleet produced 39,254 deaths in 2024 and 36,640 in 2025, the 2,614-death difference is almost entirely behavioral. That means:
1. Speed enforcement, impaired driving crackdowns, seatbelt campaigns saved ~2,614 lives in one year
2. Per-vehicle FARS rates are measuring the ENGINEERING floor, not the actual death toll
3. Behavioral intervention has a larger marginal effect than vehicle design changes in the short term

## Strongest Counterargument
Fleet turnover IS happening — even without a mandate, newer vehicles with better ADAS (AEB, FCW, BSM) are replacing older ones. The average new car sold in 2025 has better safety tech than the average car it replaces. This is gradual, but it's real. Also, trauma medicine improvements convert some would-be fatalities into severe injuries, potentially flattering the death count.

## Limitations
- 2025 data is preliminary (NHTSA early estimate, subject to revision)
- VMT figures are estimated, not measured
- We don't have 2025 per-vehicle FARS data yet — just the national aggregate
- Fleet age composition is shifting slowly but we can't quantify the exact contribution
- State-level variation is huge — some states got much worse
