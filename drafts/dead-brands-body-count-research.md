# Research: Dead Brands Body Count

## Angle
GM killed Pontiac, Saturn, and Oldsmobile in the 2009 bankruptcy. Ford killed Mercury in 2011. These brands stopped making cars over a decade ago. But between 2014 and 2023, their vehicles killed 5,818 people — more than BMW, Subaru, Volkswagen, Mazda, Cadillac, or Lexus.

Pontiac alone killed 3,038 people after its death. That's more than Tesla, Volvo, Porsche, Jaguar, Mini, Fiat, Land Rover, and Isuzu combined (1,035 total).

## Self-Critique Gate
**Proposed:** Dead brands are still racking up body counts that exceed active luxury and specialty brands. Nobody tracks this comparison because these brands don't exist for marketing purposes anymore.
**Challenge:** Is this genuinely surprising? We already covered vehicle age in twelve-year-kill-zone and deadliest-vintage. But those were about vehicle AGE. This is about BRANDS — the corporate decision to kill a brand creates orphan vehicles with no dealer network, dropping recall completion rates and accelerating the safety deficit. The brand lens is novel.
**Verdict:** Proceed. The brand comparison framing is genuinely shareable ("a dead brand kills more people than Tesla") and the orphan vehicle recall problem adds original analytical depth.

## Primary Sources

### 1. FARS Data (2014-2023)
- Dead brand totals: Pontiac 3,038 | Mercury 1,840 | Saturn 619 | Oldsmobile 268 | Suzuki 53
- Total: 5,818 deaths from defunct brands
- Comparison: BMW 2,196 | Subaru 2,292 | VW 2,234 | Mazda 1,432 | Cadillac 1,389 | Lexus 1,256 | Tesla 278
- Top killers by model:
  - Pontiac Grand Prix: 970 deaths (rate 2.14)
  - Pontiac G6: 908 deaths (rate 1.64)
  - Mercury Grand Marquis: 1,153 deaths (rate 2.29)
  - Pontiac Grand Am: 713 deaths (rate 1.18)
  - Saturn Vue: 176 deaths
  - Saturn S Series: 170 deaths
  - Saturn Ion: 167 deaths (includes ignition switch defect vehicles)

### 2. NHTSA / Recall Data
- GM ignition switch recall (2014): Covered Cobalt, Ion, G5, HHR, Cavalier, Pontiac G5/Solstice
- Saturn Ion was a key vehicle in the ignition switch scandal — 124 deaths attributed to the defect
- Recall completion rates for discontinued vehicles are dramatically lower — no dealer network to service them
- NHTSA has acknowledged the "orphan brand" problem in recall effectiveness reports

### 3. IIHS Research
- "Retirement vehicles" study: Older vehicles with fewer safety features raise crash death risk
- IIHS fatality statistics show pre-2012 vehicles lack mandatory ESC, improved side airbags, better crash structures
- Vehicle age is a proxy for safety technology — each generation adds features that reduce fatality risk

### 4. Vehicle Fleet Age Data
- S&P Global / AAPEX: Average US vehicle age 12.8 years in 2025
- Passenger cars average 14.5 years (these dead brands are passenger cars mostly)
- These vehicles are 14-25+ years old and will keep killing for another decade as they age into the used market

## Novel Contribution
1. First composite dead-brand death toll computation from FARS
2. Brand-to-brand comparison: Pontiac killed more people post-mortem than BMW did while alive
3. Connecting the "orphan brand" recall problem to the body count — no dealers = no recall fixes = higher death rate
4. Per-death-rate analysis shows these vehicles aren't just high-volume; they kill at elevated RATES (Grand Marquis at 2.29, Grand Prix at 2.14)
