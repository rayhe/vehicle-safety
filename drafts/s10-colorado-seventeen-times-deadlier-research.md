## Key Narrative Points
1. The S-10 was GM's compact pickup from 1982-2004. Twenty-two years after discontinuation, 218,750 remain registered and killing at the highest rate in the compact pickup segment.
2. The Colorado replaced it in 2004 and has been refined through two redesigns (2004, 2012, 2023). It now has the LOWEST death rate of any compact pickup.
3. The 17.3× rate gap is the largest within-manufacturer same-segment gap in the compact pickup category.
4. The crash:death ratio tells the survivability story: in the S-10, 76% of FARS crashes end in death. In the Colorado, 49% do.
5. The IIHS structural data shows WHY: the S-10's cabin collapses nearly a foot; the Colorado's holds firm.

## Counterarguments
1. **"Old cars are less safe."** True — but the S-10 was ALREADY the worst in its class when new. And its platform twin (GMC Sonoma, rate 2.13) had EVEN WORSE crashworthiness (1.26 crash:death vs 1.32).
2. **"Survivor bias — only the worst S-10s remain."** Rate is per VMT, not per vehicle. The metric accounts for how much they're driven.
3. **"Modern safety standards didn't exist."** FMVSS standards evolved, but the Toyota Tacoma (0.80) and Nissan Frontier (1.45) of the same era had much better rates. The S-10's design was poor even by its era's standards.
