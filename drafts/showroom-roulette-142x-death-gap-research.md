# Research: Showroom Roulette — 142× Death Rate Gap Within the Same Brand

## Angle
Brand loyalty is a safety myth. Within a single manufacturer's showroom, fatality rates per 100M VMT vary by over 100×. Hyundai: 142× (Palisade 0.06 → Veloster 8.54). Ford: 120× (Bronco 0.05 → Mustang 6.02). The badge on the grille tells you nothing meaningful about whether you'll survive a crash.

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulated within-brand fatality rate spread across all major manufacturers in FARS 2014–2023. No prior analysis has ranked brands by the magnitude of their internal safety disparity. The insight: model-level engineering choices (mass, structure, ESC calibration) dominate brand-level safety reputation.

## Impairment Control (strengthens the engineering argument)
- Veloster: 17.4% impairment — LOWER than the "safe" RAV4 (18.4%)
- Tracker: 12.7% impairment — among the lowest in FARS
- Land Cruiser: 8.9% impairment — extremely sober drivers, yet 6.27 rate
- These vehicles kill at high rates despite relatively sober drivers → engineering, not behavior

## Pattern
SUVs cluster at the safe end (mass, ride height, modern crash structures). Sedans and sports cars cluster at the deadly end. But the gap within a single brand dwarfs the gap between brands.

## Counterargument (full strength)
Fleet demographics differ enormously within a brand. A Veloster buyer skews young, male, and performance-oriented. A Palisade buyer is a suburban family. Self-selection into risk isn't fully controlled by impairment rates — impairment captures substances, not speed, distraction, or risk appetite. The vehicle's death rate is partially a proxy for who drives it, not purely what it is.
