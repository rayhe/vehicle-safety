# Research: FMVSS 141 — The Quiet Car Rule Keeps Failing

## Angle
Congress recognized in 2010 that silent EVs/hybrids are dangerous to pedestrians. NHTSA took until 2016 to finalize the rule (FMVSS 141). Full compliance deadline: March 1, 2021. Now, 5+ years after the deadline, at least 3 major automakers have recalled 150,000+ vehicles because the legally required pedestrian warning sound doesn't work. The safety equipment designed to save pedestrians is itself becoming a defect vector.

## Self-Critique
- **Is this genuinely surprising?** Yes. Nobody has assembled the pattern: multiple automakers failing at the same basic requirement (make noise at low speed), years after the deadline. Each recall was reported individually; the systemic failure hasn't been connected.
- **Is this just a recall roundup?** No. The novel contribution is the timeline analysis — 11 years from legislation to full compliance, and the systems STILL fail — plus the open petition asking NHTSA to retroactively cover 9.1 million pre-2021 vehicles.
- **Would a reader share this?** Yes. "Your EV might be silently stalking pedestrians because a speaker doesn't work" is inherently shareable.

## Key Data Points

### Regulatory Timeline
- **2010**: Pedestrian Safety Enhancement Act signed (Pub. L. 111-373, Jan 2011)
- **2013**: NHTSA publishes NPRM for FMVSS 141 (Federal Register, Jan 14, 2013)
- **2016**: Final rule published (Nov 14, 2016)
- **2018**: 50% of new EVs/hybrids must comply (Sept 1, 2018)
- **2019**: Original full compliance deadline (Sept 1, 2019)
- **2021**: Actual full compliance — March 1, 2021 (2-year extension from original)
- **Total timeline: 11 years from legislation to full enforcement**

### Recall Tally (FMVSS 141 / AVAS failures)
1. **Chevrolet Equinox EV** (NHTSA 24V-925, Dec 2025): 7,606 units → expanded to **81,177 units** (2025-2026 MY). Software calibration made alert too quiet at 0–6.2 mph. Software update fix.
2. **Mercedes-Benz EQE/EQS** (NHTSA 25V-366): **94 units**. AVAS played wrong reversing sound. Software update.
3. **Ford Explorer Hybrid / Lincoln Nautilus Hybrid** (26S51, July 2026): **66,383 units** (2024-2027 MY). Digital signal processor module failure stops warning sound below 19 mph. Re-recall expanding on 25V691. Explorer hybrids are Police Interceptors.
- **TOTAL: ~155,000+ vehicles recalled for AVAS failures**

### The Petition (DP 22-005)
- Filed July 18, 2022
- Requests NHTSA apply FMVSS 141 retroactively to ALL EVs/hybrids manufactured before March 2021
- Estimated affected population: **9.1 million vehicles** driving with no pedestrian sound requirement
- Status: Open for evaluation

### UK Research (Novel Contribution tie-in)
- **Journal of Epidemiology and Community Health (2024)**: EV/hybrid pedestrian casualty rate 5.16 per 100M miles vs 2.40 for ICE vehicles — **2.15x more likely** to hit pedestrians
- In **urban areas**: EVs were **3x more dangerous** than ICE (RR 2.97)
- Rural areas: No significant difference (noise already masked by ambient)
- Later Leeds University study (2024) found post-2019 EVs (with AVAS) showed no significant difference from ICE — **suggesting the sound system works when it works**

### FARS Connection
- Pedestrian deaths in the US: ~7,768 in 2022 (CDC/MMWR), ~6,500 estimated in 2025 (GHSA H1 projection)
- The UK study found the elevated risk is specifically at low speeds in urban areas — exactly where FMVSS 141 applies
- Pre-AVAS EVs are still on the road and the petition notes 9.1M of them have NO sound requirement

### The Police Vehicle Angle
- Ford Explorer Hybrid is offered as a Police Interceptor
- 18,242 of the 66,383 recalled vehicles are Police Interceptor Explorers
- Police hybrids silently approaching pedestrians in urban enforcement scenarios
