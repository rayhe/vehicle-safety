# Research: Electronic Door Handles Are Killing People Who Survived the Crash

**Journalist:** Mia Crumplezone — Safety Engineering Editor
**Angle:** The auto industry's trend toward electronic door handles (flush exterior, button interior) has created a new category of crash fatality: people who survive the impact but die because they can't open their doors.

## Self-Critique Gate
- **Proposal:** The industry reinvented the door handle for aesthetics and aerodynamics. At least 15 people are dead because they couldn't get out of a car that replaced a mechanical latch with an electronic button.
- **Challenge:** Is this just a Tesla hit piece? No — affects 13+ brands. Is it genuinely surprising? Yes — the idea that a DOOR HANDLE could be a safety defect is counterintuitive. The death mechanism (survived crash → trapped → died of smoke/fire) is distinct from all 181 prior articles.
- **Verdict:** PROCEED. Novel angle, multiple primary sources, legislative/regulatory action, strong actionable takeaway.

## Primary Sources (3+)

### 1. Bloomberg Investigation (Dec 2025)
- **15 deaths** in Tesla crashes where electronic door handles failed
- **140 reports** of occupants trapped in Teslas with electronic handles
- Tesla and Rivian told Bloomberg they may redesign emergency releases on future vehicles
- Source: bloomberg.com/news/features/2025-12-22/tesla-door-safety-tied-to-at-least-15-auto-accident-deaths

### 2. NHTSA Defect Investigation (Jan 2026)
- Preliminary evaluation opened into **179,071 Model Year 2022 Model 3 sedans**
- Defect petition: mechanical backup release is "hidden, unlabeled, and not intuitive"
- Especially dangerous for rear-seat passengers
- First step in formal process; could lead to recall
- Source: NHTSA ODI case PE25010

### 3. Consumer Reports / SAFE Exit Act
- **35,000+ people** signed CR petition for safer electronic door handles
- Rep. Robin Kelly (D-IL) introduced **SAFE Exit Act**
- Would require intuitive manual fail-safes and emergency responder access
- CR endorses the bill
- Source: consumerreports.org/cars/car-safety/unsafe-electronic-car-door-handles-safe-exit-act

### 4. Cybertruck Piedmont Crash (Thanksgiving Eve 2024)
- 3 college students dead: Krysta Tsukahara (19), Jack Nelson (20), Soren Dixon (19)
- Crashed into tree/wall, electronic handles failed, trapped inside
- Friend pulled survivor out of front window after "10-15 hits" with tree branch
- **Cause of death: smoke inhalation and burns — NOT crash impact**
- All three survived the crash itself
- Source: electrek.co, AP News, lawsuits filed by Tsukahara and Nelson families

### 5. Wisconsin Model S Crash (Nov 2024)
- 5 dead: Jeffrey Bauer (54), Michelle Bauer (55), and 3 others
- Model S hit tree in Verona, WI (suburb of Madison)
- Electronic door systems failed after lithium-ion battery fire
- **Rear passengers would have to "lift carpeting to find a metal tab"** to escape
- A nearby homeowner told 911 she heard **screaming from within the vehicle**
- Lawsuit: Bauer et al v Tesla, Dane County, WI, No. 2025CV003601
- Source: Reuters via Northland News Radio

### 6. Model X Manual Release Procedure
- Requires removing a speaker grille and pulling a release cable, then pushing the door up
- Consumer Reports called this "a complex task that may not be apparent in a crisis"

## Brands Using Electronic Door Handles (13+)
Tesla, Cadillac, Rivian, Audi, BMW, Chevrolet, Fiat, Ford, Genesis, Lexus, Lincoln, Maserati, Volvo
- Varying degrees of complexity for manual override
- Some require pulling twice or harder; Tesla requires finding hidden levers
- China considering new standards for door handles

## Kill Test
- ✅ Genuinely newsworthy: 15+ deaths, active NHTSA investigation, pending legislation
- ✅ Novel angle: Not a FARS data piece. Design trend across 13+ brands creating new death category.
- ✅ Strong counterargument: Flush handles improve aerodynamics (range), reduce drag, enable auto-present. Most electronic handles work fine. The failures are edge cases in severe crashes.
- ✅ Actionable: Check if your car has electronic handles. Learn where manual release is NOW, before you need it.

## Strongest Counterargument
Electronic handles improve EV range (flush = less drag), enable keyless convenience, and work perfectly in 99.9%+ of scenarios. Tesla vehicles score extremely well in NHTSA and IIHS crash tests — the occupant protection in the structure itself is excellent. The deaths occurred in severe, high-speed crashes where outcomes might have been fatal regardless. The industry is trend-following, not just Tesla.
