# Research: "Park Outside" Is the Auto Industry's Newest Safety Feature

## Angle
Since 2020, automakers have issued 90+ recalls covering 11 million+ vehicles that include the instruction "park outside and away from structures." The auto industry has invented a new safety system: the customer's driveway. Rather than stop-sale or stop-drive, they tell you to exile your vehicle from your own garage. No other consumer product category works this way. Samsung recalled every Galaxy Note 7 and banned them from aircraft. The auto industry tells you to park in the yard and wait.

## Primary Sources

### 1. WSJ (Jun 12, 2026) — Meta-trend data
- Since 2020, more than 90 recalls issued with "park outside" warnings
- More than 11 million vehicles affected
- Stellantis vehicles account for roughly 20% of these
- Hyundai Motor biggest share (3.3M+ ABS recall alone)
- GM Chevy Bolt EV/EUV also in the mix

### 2. NHTSA Recall Pages (official)
**Major "park outside" recalls since 2020:**
- Hyundai/Kia ABS module: ~3.37M vehicles (NHTSA 23V-651/652) — ABS module leaks brake fluid, electrical short, fire while parked or driving
- Hyundai/Kia tow hitch harness: ~570K vehicles (Santa Cruz, Santa Fe, Carnival) — electrical short in tow hitch module
- Jeep Wrangler/Gladiator: 1,076,999 vehicles (Jun 2026) — power steering pump wiring overheats, 51 fires, 1 injury, fire even when ignition OFF
- Jeep Wrangler/Grand Cherokee 4xe: 320,065 vehicles (Nov 2025, recall 25V-741) — Samsung SDI battery separator damage, 19 fires, 1 injury. Previous recall remedy found INEFFECTIVE
- Stellantis 375K Jeep 4xe (earlier round, 2024)
- Ford Expedition/Navigator: ~39K vehicles (2021 MY) — 16 confirmed fires, mostly in rental fleet, unattended vehicles, NO KNOWN CAUSE, NO REMEDY
- Chrysler Pacifica PHEV: 16,700 vehicles — fire risk parked or off, no remedy identified at time of recall

### 3. NFPA / US Fire Administration
- 171,500 highway vehicle fires annually in the US (2014-2016 avg)
- ~345 deaths per year from vehicle fires
- Vehicle fires = 17% of all reported fires, 12% of fire deaths

### 4. Consumer comparison
- Samsung Galaxy Note 7: ~2.5M units, full stop-sale, banned from aircraft, recalled entirely
- Auto industry: 11M units, "park outside," wait months/years for fix

## Novel Contribution
1. **Scale calculation:** 11M "park outside" vehicles ÷ ~290M registered US vehicles = ~3.8% of the fleet is currently or has recently been under "park outside" orders. Roughly 1 in 26 vehicles.
2. **The "no remedy" problem:** Multiple recalls issued with NO FIX AVAILABLE. Ford Expedition: "no known cause or remedy." Chrysler Pacifica: "no remedy identified." Jeep 4xe: previous remedy found ineffective, new remedy not yet available. These aren't recalls — they're confessions.
3. **Response asymmetry:** Samsung Note 7 fires: 2.5M units, 0 deaths → complete product withdrawal. Auto industry: 11M units, 51+ fires in Jeep alone, actual injuries → "park outside." The Note 7 was banned from aircraft. Nobody has banned a fire-risk Jeep from a garage.
4. **Repeat offender pattern:** Jeep Wrangler 4xe has been recalled THREE times for fire (2024 recall, 2025 recall, 2026 recall). The 2024 remedy was found ineffective. Nine fires occurred in vehicles AFTER they received the recall fix.

## Counterargument (strongest)
Vehicles are larger, more expensive, and more essential to daily life than phones. A full stop-sale of 1M Jeeps would strand owners. The "park outside" instruction is a pragmatic middle ground that reduces the highest-consequence scenario (garage fire spreading to house) while allowing continued use. The fire rate per affected vehicle is extremely low — 51 fires out of 1.07M Jeeps is 0.005%.

## Limitations
- "90+ recalls, 11M vehicles" comes from WSJ analysis — I haven't independently verified every recall
- Fire probability per affected vehicle is very low; the headline number is dramatic but per-unit risk is small
- "Park outside" applies to the highest-risk scenario (fire spreading to structure) but doesn't eliminate fire risk entirely
- Some recalls have since been remedied; the 11M is cumulative, not current

## Actionable Takeaway
Check your VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls. If your vehicle has a "park outside" recall, follow the instruction — it exists because the manufacturer literally cannot fix the problem yet. If you've had a recall repair for a Jeep 4xe, check whether a newer recall supersedes it.
