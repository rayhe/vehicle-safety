# Research: telluride-two-belt-recalls-zero-miles

## Angle
The 2027 Kia Telluride has been recalled TWICE for seatbelt defects — 21,134 total vehicles — before most buyers took delivery. Both recalls trace to the same supplier: Samsong Mexico. Different parts, different failure modes, same factory.

## Sources

### 1. NHTSA Recall #1 (March 2026)
- 14,870 vehicles: 13,499 Tellurides (2027 MY), 1,371 K4s (2026 MY)
- Defect: Rear center seatbelt buckle won't latch properly
- Supplier: Samsong Mexico (incorrect assembly of anchor buckle)
- Stop-sale order issued
- Kia Georgia plant found issue Feb 9, 2026
- No injuries/fatalities reported
- Source: NHTSA, Carscoops (autoevolution Mar 2026)

### 2. NHTSA Recall #2 (June 2026)
- 6,264 vehicles: 4,367 Telluride Hybrid, 1,897 gas Telluride (all 2027 MY)
- Defect: Driver seatbelt ELR locks when attempting to extend webbing — belt "temporarily unavailable as occupant restraint"
- Supplier: Samsong Mexico (incorrect vehicle sensor in driver seatbelt assembly)
- Kia became aware April 2026, investigation confirmed supplier error
- Notification to owners: July 31, 2026
- Fix: Replace entire seatbelt assembly
- 1% estimated defect rate
- Source: NHTSA, Autoblog, Autoevolution

### 3. FARS Data
- Kia Telluride: 31 deaths, rate 0.04 per 100M VMT
- Among lowest death rates in entire FARS database
- Only Seltos matches at 0.04
- For context: Honda CR-V at 0.53, Toyota Highlander at 0.454

### 4. NHTSA Seatbelt Statistics
- NHTSA: seatbelts saved an estimated 14,955 lives in 2017
- ~47% of passenger vehicle occupant fatalities are unbelted
- Seatbelts reduce fatal injury risk by 45% for front seat occupants (NHTSA)

## Cross-Reference / Original Contribution
The Telluride's 0.04 death rate ranks among the 3 safest vehicles in the FARS database. But that rate was calculated from a vehicle population where seatbelts functioned. Recall #2 renders the driver's seatbelt "temporarily unavailable" — meaning the most critical passive safety device simply isn't there. A Telluride without a working driver's seatbelt isn't a 0.04-rate vehicle; it's a physics experiment.

## Counterargument
Zero injuries or deaths reported from either recall. Kia found both issues proactively through internal quality monitoring, not field incidents. The 1% defect estimate for recall #2 means ~63 vehicles actually affected. Quick identification and proactive recalls are the system working.

## Limitations
- Both recalls too new for crash outcome data in FARS
- FARS doesn't capture whether seatbelts were available vs. chosen not to use
- The 0.04 death rate includes only 31 total deaths (small sample)
- Samsong Mexico's broader safety record as a supplier is unknown from public data
