# Research: Pacifica PHEV Triple Fire Recall — Same Supplier, Three Campaigns, No Root Cause

## Angle
The Chrysler Pacifica PHEV has been recalled THREE separate times for battery fire risk (2022, 2024, 2026). Same vehicle platform, same failure mode (thermal runaway when parked), same supplier (LG Energy Solution). Each recall was supposed to fix the problem. None did. The third recall (June 2026) landed on the same day Jeep recalled 1.3 million Wranglers for a completely different fire risk — making it a two-brand fire week for Stellantis.

The LG connection is the thread: LGES cells also caused the Chevy Bolt recall (142K vehicles, $1.8B cost, $1.2B LG reimbursement). Same defect pattern — manufacturing defects in battery cells creating internal short circuits leading to thermal runaway.

**Novel contribution:** Cross-referencing Pacifica's FARS crash fatality rate (0.19/100M VMT — one of the lowest on record) with its triple fire recall history. This vehicle is among the safest in America during a crash. The danger is in your garage.

## Primary Sources

### Recall #1: NHTSA 22V077 (February 2022)
- **Vehicles:** 16,741 (2017-2018 Pacifica PHEV)
- **Defect:** Vehicle fire when parked, ignition off
- **Remedy:** Park outside, don't charge. Software update + battery inspection/replacement
- **Source:** NHTSA, CarPro.com recall listing

### Recall #2: NHTSA 24V536 + 24V538 (July 2024)
- **Vehicles:** ~19,516 US (2017-2021 Pacifica PHEV, including those already fixed under 22V077)
- **Defect:** "Folded or torn anode tab, along with a second unidentified factor" → internal short circuit → fire
- **Fires:** 7 known fires, 4 smoke inhalation reports
- **Critical detail:** Includes vehicles ALREADY repaired under recall #1
- **Source:** Consumer Reports, Autoblog

### Recall #3: NHTSA 26V362 (June 4, 2026)
- **Vehicles:** 17,277 (2020-2022 Pacifica PHEV), built Aug 5, 2020 – May 2, 2022
- **Defect:** Battery cells from LG Energy Solution "alternative assembly line" → internal cell failure → thermal runaway
- **Fires:** 4 known incidents
- **Critical detail:** Neither Chrysler nor LG has identified root cause. 1% of vehicles estimated defective.
- **Source:** CarBuzz, Carscoops, InsideEVs, KBB, EVSHIFT

### Chevy Bolt Connection (LG Energy Solution)
- **Vehicles:** ~142,000 (2017-2022 Bolt EV/EUV)
- **Cost to GM:** $1.8B+, LG reimbursed $1.2B
- **Defect:** Torn anode + folded separator in LG cells → internal short → thermal runaway
- **Settlement:** $150M class action fund (2024)

### Jeep Wrangler/Gladiator Recall (same week, same company)
- **NHTSA date:** June 9, 2026 (5 days after Pacifica recall)
- **Vehicles:** 1,076,999 US (2021-2025 Wrangler + Gladiator)
- **Defect:** Electrical connection in electric hydraulic power steering pump wiring → combustible materials overheat → fire (even parked/off)
- **Fires:** 72 known, 1 injury
- **Critical detail:** Stellantis investigated in 2023, CLOSED investigation "due to low rate of occurrence," reopened Aug 2024 after more fires
- **Source:** Reuters, NHTSA

## FARS Data
- Chrysler Pacifica: 160 deaths (2014-2023), estimated fleet 700,000, rate **0.19 per 100M VMT**
  - For context, average light vehicle is ~1.1. Pacifica is 83% below average.
- Jeep Wrangler: 1,842 deaths, rate 0.84/100M VMT
