## 6. 🔬 Research Rigor (Novel Contribution, Limitations, Counterarguments)
**Score: 8.5/10**
**Original contribution:** The 2.27× and 4× excess risk calculations are genuinely novel — nobody else in the coverage computed the inverse of ESC effectiveness to quantify what these owners lost. The $28K Mazda comparison against NHTSA's own data is original cross-referencing.
**Limitations:** Properly acknowledged — conditional failure ("may"), unknown exposure duration, fleet-wide vs. model-specific estimates.
**Counterargument:** Given at full strength (partial stability through other subsystems, mass advantage, conditional nature).
**Weakness:** The brake assist "20 to 45 percent" figure could use a more specific citation. It's directionally correct but the range is wide.

## 7. 📊 Data Presentation

IIHS data properly attributed with year and context. The NHTSA vehicle page data is an effective counter-exhibit. Could benefit from a comparison table (Grand Wagoneer vs. competitors on safety metrics) but the prose-based comparison works.
