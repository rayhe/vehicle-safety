# Research: Pedestrian Share Hits Double Digits

## Angle
The pedestrian share of all traffic fatalities hit 10.1% in 2024 — double digits for the first time since GHSA began tracking in 2015, when it was 4%. That's a 152% increase. Meanwhile, in-vehicle deaths are dropping much faster. The car got safer for the people inside it. For the people outside? Not so much.

## Key Data Points (GHSA July 14, 2026 Report — Full-Year 2025 Data)

### Headline Numbers
- 6,732 pedestrians killed by drivers in 2025 (down 7% from 2024, third straight annual decrease)
- 36,640 total roadway fatalities in 2025
- 16 consecutive quarters of fewer deaths overall
- BUT: 6,732 is still 22.5% MORE than 2015 and ~70% MORE than 2009

### The Share Explosion
- Pedestrian share of crash fatalities hit 10.1% in 2024 (first time at double digits since at least 2015)
- In 2015, pedestrian share was 4%
- That's a 152% increase in the pedestrian share
- Meaning: cars are getting better at saving occupants but NOT pedestrians

### Distraction Explosion
- In 2019: ~15% of crash fatalities attributed to distraction (both pedestrian and driver)
- By 2024: 39% of pedestrian fatalities attributed to distracted driving or walking (22% of driver fatalities)
- That's a 160% increase in 5 years for pedestrians

### Vehicle Size Factor
- GHSA: "Vehicles are getting larger and heavier, and greater force means pedestrians are less likely to survive injuries"
- Tall, blunt front ends = 43.6% increase in pedestrian fatality risk vs low/sloped front ends
- SUV percentage of pedestrian-strike fatalities still huge (though narrowed slightly in 2024)

### Geographic Patterns
- Rural pedestrian death rate: 10.4% in 2016 → 15.1% in 2024 (45% increase)
- Urban pedestrian death rate: 5.1% in 2018 → 9.2% in 2024 (80% increase)
- 28 states with declining pedestrian deaths, 17 with increases
- California: -22.4% (239 fewer, more than half of national decline)
- North Carolina: +32%

### Economic Cost
- Total economic toll of pedestrian fatalities: ~$90 billion
