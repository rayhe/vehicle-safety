# Research: Two Death Counts, 3,535 Apart

## Angle
America has two official counts of motor vehicle deaths. They come from different federal entities, use different definitions, and produce irreconcilably different numbers. For 2024: NHTSA FARS says 39,254. NCHS (CDC death certificates) says 42,789. The gap is 3,535 people — 9.0%.

Both organizations issued triumphant press releases about 2025 declines. Both claimed credit. Neither mentioned they can't agree on the body count.

## Original Contribution
Quantifying the structural gap and showing what it hides: the slow deaths (Day 31–365), the off-road deaths (parking lots, driveways, private roads), and how the number everyone cites (NHTSA) is always the lower one. The gap isn't an error — it's a policy choice about who counts.

## Key Data Points

### 2024 (final numbers)
- NHTSA FARS: 39,254 deaths (public roads, within 30 days, police-reported)
- NCHS: 42,789 deaths (all motor vehicle deaths, within 1 year, death certificates)
- Gap: 3,535 (9.0%)

### 2025 (preliminary estimates)
- NHTSA: 36,640 (-6.7% from FARS 2024)
- NSC: 37,810 (-12% from NCHS 2024 baseline)
- Gap: 1,170 (3.2%) — will likely widen when NCHS final comes in

### Decline narratives
- NHTSA: "Down 6.7%" (smaller baseline, smaller decline)
- NSC: "Down 12%" (larger baseline, larger decline)
- Both claim "historic" and "record low"

### Methodology differences
1. **Time window:** FARS = 30 days. NCHS = 1 year.
2. **Scope:** FARS = public traffic roads only. NCHS = all motor vehicle deaths (driveways, parking lots, farms, private roads).
3. **Source:** FARS = police crash reports. NCHS = death certificates (ICD-10 V01-V99).

### NSC's own accuracy
- NSC initial 2024 estimate was 44,680 vs final NCHS 42,789 — off by 4.4%, "largest percentage difference since 2002"
- NSC explicitly warns: "NSC national motor-vehicle fatality estimates are not comparable to NHTSA figures"

### Who are the 3,535?
- People who survived the crash but died on Day 31, 60, 90, or later — TBI victims, spinal cord injuries, elderly with complications, infections
- People killed in non-traffic crashes: parking lots, driveways, private roads, agricultural settings
- Children backed over in driveways (some now captured by backover rule, but many still non-traffic)

### Rate divergence
- NHTSA 2025 rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT
- NSC 2025 rate: 1.14 per 100M VMT
- Even the rate — the metric that controls for driving — disagrees by 3.6%
