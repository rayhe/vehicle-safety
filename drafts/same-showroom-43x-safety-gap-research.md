# Research Notes: same-showroom-43x-safety-gap

## Angle
Within-make safety spread: the same automaker sells vehicles with fatality rates that differ by up to 43x. Brand loyalty is meaningless for safety. The choice WITHIN a brand matters far more than the choice between brands.

## Journalist
**Clara Rollover** — Consumer Safety Advocate. "Before you sign that lease, you might want to see this."

## Key Data Points
- Ford Transit: 0.14 per 100M VMT (178 deaths, 1.05M fleet)
- Ford Mustang: 6.02 per 100M VMT (2,739 deaths, 568K fleet)
- Chevrolet Traverse: 0.20 per 100M VMT (265 deaths, 1.05M fleet)
- Chevrolet Cobalt: 5.10 per 100M VMT (1,540 deaths, 262K fleet) — also subject of GM ignition switch recalls
- Honda HR-V: 0.13 per 100M VMT (142 deaths, 875K fleet)
- Honda Accord: 3.07 per 100M VMT (7,102 deaths, 2.0M fleet)
- Toyota RAV4: 0.19 per 100M VMT (914 deaths, 3.76M fleet)
- Toyota Solara: 4.25 per 100M VMT (642 deaths, 131K fleet)

## The "Same Dollar" Problem
A 2024 Ford Escape ($29K MSRP) kills at 0.95/100M VMT. 
A 2024 Ford Mustang ($32K MSRP) kills at 6.02/100M VMT. 
For roughly the same money, you can choose 6.3x higher death odds.

## Limitations
1. FARS 2014-2023 data includes discontinued vehicles (Cobalt, Solara) still on road. Their high rates partly reflect fleet age and pre-ESC engineering.
2. Death rate per 100M VMT uses estimated VMT, not actual odometer data. Low-volume models have wider confidence intervals.
3. Vehicle choice and driver behavior are confounded. Mustang attracts different driving behavior than Transit.
4. Rate doesn't capture who dies — occupants, other road users, pedestrians.

## Strongest Counterargument
Vehicle comparison is unfair because different models attract different drivers with different risk profiles. A Mustang owner drives differently than a Transit fleet driver. The 43x gap reflects DRIVER selection, not VEHICLE safety deficiency.

Response: Even after acknowledging driver selection, IIHS studies show that vehicle choice independently predicts crash outcomes. The 2023 IIHS study on driver death rates by vehicle found 56 per million registered vehicle years for minicars vs 15 for very large SUVs — a 3.7x gap from size alone. The within-make spread of 43x far exceeds what driver selection explains. Both factors compound.

## Actionable Advice
1. When shopping within a brand: check FARS death rates, not just IIHS crash test scores
2. Newer model years of the SAME vehicle are dramatically safer (MY2005 peak → modern avg = 5-8x safer)
3. SUVs and crossovers consistently have lower death rates than sedans from the same maker
4. Fleet size matters for rate reliability — take rates for <200K fleet vehicles with wider error bars
