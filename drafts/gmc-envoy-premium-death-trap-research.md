# Research: GMC Envoy — The Premium Badge on a Death Trap

## FARS Data (Primary Source)
- **Deaths:** 988 total (2014-2023 FARS window)
- **Annual average:** 98.8
- **Fleet:** ~350,000
- **Rate:** 2.26 per 100M VMT (3.6× the SUV average of 0.63)
- **Crashes:** 1,482
- **Impairment:** 21.2% (245 of 1,153 drivers) — 16.2% alcohol, 8.4% drug

## Model Year Deaths
- 1994-2001 (1st gen / Jimmy rebadge): low numbers (6-48/year)
- 2002: 159 deaths — Motor Trend SUV of the Year
- 2003: 195 deaths
- 2004: 209 deaths (peak)
- 2005: 127 deaths (post-structural update)
- 2006-2009: rapid decline (52→24→27→6)

## Platform Context: GMT360
- Shared with: Chevy Trailblazer, Buick Rainier, Isuzu Ascender, Oldsmobile Bravada, Saab 9-7X
- Trailblazer: 2,473 deaths, rate 2.83, 700K fleet
- **Combined Trailblazer + Envoy: 3,461 deaths**
- Same bones, same crash structure, different badge and price

## IIHS Crash Test Ratings
- **1st gen (1998-2000):** POOR overall, POOR structure, POOR head/neck, POOR driver restraints
  - Dummy head hit window sill then B-pillar during crash
  - 33cm footwell intrusion, 18cm A-pillar rearward movement
- **2nd gen (2002-2004):** MARGINAL overall, ACCEPTABLE structure, POOR right leg/foot
  - 26cm brake pedal intrusion, 22cm center footwell intrusion
  - Dummy head extended out open window and hit B-pillar during rebound
- **2nd gen updated (2005-2009):** ACCEPTABLE overall after structural/airbag/seatbelt changes
- **Rear crash protection:** POOR (head restraint inadequate)
- **Side impact (IIHS):** MARGINAL

## NHTSA Ratings
- Frontal: 3/5 stars (driver), 4/5 stars (passenger)
- Side: 5/5 stars (with optional head curtain airbags)
- **Rollover resistance: 3/5 stars (20-30% rollover probability)**
- No Overall Safety Rating assigned

## NHTSA Complaints/Recalls/Investigations
- 2002 model: 574 complaints, 16 recalls, 12 investigations
- 2005 model: 624 complaints, 3 recalls, 3 investigations
- 2007 model: 277 complaints, 5 recalls, 4 investigations
- **Key recall:** Front lower control arm bracket fracture (30,476 units) — loss of vehicle control
- **Key investigation (EA12-004):** Driver Door Module (DDM) thermal events
  - 170 reports of fire/smoke/melting at power window switch
  - 14 actual fires with visible flames
  - 619 consumer complaints to GM
  - Initially only recalled in salt-belt states; NHTSA forced nationwide expansion

## Key Narrative Elements
1. **The premium illusion:** Envoy was GMC's "upscale" Trailblazer, ~$2-4K more MSRP. Same platform, same crash structure, same death rate. The premium bought leather and OnStar, not safety.
2. **Motor Trend SUV of the Year 2002:** The same year 159 people died in Envoys. By 2004, the peak: 209 dead.
3. **Head curtain airbags were OPTIONAL:** On a vehicle with 3-star rollover resistance and a body-on-frame design prone to rolling. The life-saving equipment was a checked box on the order sheet.
4. **DDM fire risk:** Your power window switch could catch fire. GM initially only recalled in 20 states + DC.
5. **IIHS dummy behavior:** In crash tests, the dummy's head hit the B-pillar. In the first gen, it also hit the window sill. This is the vehicle Motor Trend awarded.
6. **GMT360 combined: 3,461 dead.** One platform, six badges, thousands of graves.
