# Research: Joyson/Takata Successor Inflator Rupture

## Kill Test
- **Existing coverage check:** No stories about Joyson Safety Systems, non-Takata inflator rupture, or GM roof rail airbag recall. `takata-stellantis-do-not-drive.html` covers original Takata recall on Stellantis vehicles — completely different angle.
- **Novel contribution:** The corporate lineage connection (Joyson acquired Takata → now recalling inflators for rupturing) has not been the focus of automotive safety journalism. The temporal coincidence (inflators manufactured Oct 2018, 6 months after acquisition closed Apr 2018) adds an unreported layer.
- **Result:** PASS — proceed.

## Primary Sources
1. **NHTSA Recall 26V166** (March 2026): 2,819 vehicles, GM roof rail airbag inflator end cap separation/sidewall rupture, Joyson Safety Systems supplier. 2018 Silverado 1500/Sierra 1500, 2018-2019 Silverado 2500-3500/Sierra 2500-3500.
2. **NHTSA Recall 26V325** (May 2026): Expansion of 26V166, adds 2,785 vehicles. Same defect, same supplier. Total: ~5,604 vehicles.
3. **GM Safety Recall Bulletin N242474500**: Official dealer instructions. Confirms Joyson Safety Systems as supplier. Root cause: (a) microscopic crack in pressurized steel canister during manufacturing, (b) water left in canister after washing process → stress corrosion cracking over 7-8 years.
4. **Reuters, April 11, 2018**: KSS completes $1.6B acquisition of Takata assets, renames to Joyson Safety Systems. Takata's PSAN inflator liabilities NOT acquired.
5. **Wikipedia / Joyson Safety Systems corporate history**: KSS → acquired by Ningbo Joyson Electronics (2016, $920M) → acquired Takata (2018, $1.6B) → renamed Joyson Safety Systems.

## Key Facts
- **Takata crisis:** 67M+ US vehicles recalled, 22+ deaths worldwide (some counts say 27+), largest auto recall in history. Cause: ammonium nitrate propellant in inflators degraded with humidity/heat → inflator rupture → metal shrapnel propelled into cabin.
- **Joyson recall failure mode:** Inflator end cap separation OR sidewall split → compressed gas escapes → end cap or components propelled into vehicle. IDENTICAL end result to Takata.
- **Root cause (Joyson):** Manufacturing defects at Joyson Safety Systems Mexico facility. Two concurrent defects: (1) microscopic crack introduced during canister manufacturing, (2) water left in canister after washing. Combined: stress corrosion cracking weakens pressurized canister over ~7 years.
- **Trigger incident:** 2019 Silverado experienced spontaneous right-side roof rail airbag rupture WHILE PARKED AND UNOCCUPIED. No deployment command. Consumer reported Feb 2026; GM investigation started April 2026.
- **Production window:** Very narrow — October 3-27, 2018. Plants: Fort Wayne, Silao, Oshawa, possibly Flint.
- **Timeline overlap:** Takata acquisition closed April 11, 2018. These inflators manufactured October 2018 — 6 months later.

## Strongest Counterargument
The failure mechanisms are fundamentally different. Takata's problem was systemic chemical instability (ammonium nitrate propellant) affecting tens of millions of inflators across multiple manufacturers' vehicles over decades. Joyson's appears to be a narrow manufacturing window defect (3 weeks of production) affecting 5,604 vehicles. The scale difference is 12,000:1. Attributing this to the Takata lineage may be unfair guilt-by-association.

## Limitations
- Root cause "not entirely determined" per GM — the two-defect hypothesis is GM's working theory.
- Only one known rupture incident (the parked Silverado). No injuries or deaths reported.
- Unknown whether the inflators were manufactured at a former Takata facility or a legacy KSS facility.
- FARS data not applicable — this is a recall/defect story, not a fatality analysis.
