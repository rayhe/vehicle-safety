# Research: The Transatlantic Pedestrian AEB Gap

## Hook (Timely)
EU GSR2 Phase 3 takes effect July 7, 2026. As of tomorrow, EVERY existing vehicle model sold in Europe must have:
- Pedestrian + cyclist AEB (automatic emergency braking)
- Advanced Driver Distraction Warning (camera-based monitoring)
- Intelligent Speed Assist (re-enables every ignition)

The US equivalent — FMVSS No. 127 — was finalized May 9, 2024, with a Sept 2029 compliance date. But:
- Trump's Jan 20, 2025 regulatory freeze paused it
- Alliance for Automotive Innovation sued to block it (Jan 17, 2025, D.C. Circuit)
- D.C. Circuit appeal in abeyance since Feb 28, 2025
- DOT announced March 23, 2026: preparing NPRM to extend compliance by 2 years (to 2031)
- Auto industry calls it "practically impossible with available technology" and "a disastrous decision"

## The Gap
- EU: Pedestrian AEB mandatory July 7, 2026 (ALL existing models)
- US: Sept 2029 at earliest, likely 2031, possibly weaker or killed entirely
- Gap: 3-5 years minimum

## NHTSA's Own Body Count Estimate
- FMVSS 127 projected to save 360 lives/year + prevent 24,000 injuries
- 3-year delay = 1,080 lives lost + 72,000 injuries
- 5-year delay = 1,800 lives lost + 120,000 injuries
- This is NHTSA's own math, not advocacy speculation

## FARS Data Cross-Reference
- Total deaths across 337 tracked models: 191,193 (2014-2023, 10 years)
- Annual: ~19,119 deaths
- Pickups + SUVs involved in 51.7% of all fatal crashes
- In those crashes, 85,661 deaths were OUTSIDE the truck/SUV (49.3%)
- F-150 alone: 20,066 fatal crashes, 10,872 deaths outside the truck (54.2%)
- Silverado: 19,732 fatal crashes, 10,141 deaths outside (51.4%)

## IIHS Research on Vehicle Height + Pedestrians
- Vehicles with large driver-side blind zones: 70% MORE likely to strike pedestrians turning left
- Median-height pickup at 27 mph: 83% chance of moderate pedestrian injury vs. 60% for median car
- Median-height pickup at 27 mph: 62% chance of SERIOUS pedestrian injury vs. 30% for median car
- Front-end height is the key variable (not weight — any vehicle vastly outweighs a pedestrian)

## US Pedestrian Deaths (Context)
- 2023: ~7,500 pedestrian deaths (NHTSA preliminary)
- This is the highest level since the early 1980s
- Pedestrian deaths UP 77% since 2010 even as vehicle occupant deaths remained roughly flat
- The rise correlates with SUV/pickup market share increase

## Novel Contribution
Original calculation: NHTSA says FMVSS 127 saves 360 lives/year. But that's based on Sept 2029 compliance. If the DOT's proposed 2-year extension to 2031 goes through, the cumulative excess death toll during the extended gap (vs. EU's 2026 mandate) is:
- 2026-2031: 5 years × 360 lives = 1,800 lives by NHTSA's own model
- But NHTSA's model is conservative (based on 2024 fleet composition). By 2031, fleet will be even more SUV/pickup-heavy, and pedestrian deaths have been INCREASING, so the real toll is likely higher.
