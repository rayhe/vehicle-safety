# Research: Ford's Consent Order Recall Paradox

## Angle
Ford has filed 51 recalls in the first 6 months of 2026 — nearly triple the second-place manufacturer (Stellantis/Chrysler at 19). Everyone reads this as "Ford makes dangerous cars." The data says something different: Ford is under a 2024 NHTSA consent order that forces disclosure. FARS data shows Ford vehicles aren't actually more dangerous per-VMT than GM or Stellantis. The recall count measures disclosure, not danger.

## Key Data Points

### Recall Counts (2026 YTD, per USA TODAY/NHTSA June 20, 2026)
- Ford: 51 recalls
- Stellantis/Chrysler: 19 recalls
- GM: not specified but fewer than 19
- Hyundai: not specified
- Toyota: not specified
Ford outpaces #2 by 2.7x

### Why Ford Leads: The 2024 Consent Order
- Per Michael Brooks, Center for Auto Safety: Ford is "subject to the 2024 consent order they agreed to with NHTSA, which engaged an independent third party to oversee their recall issues, required safety expenditures, and cited Safety Act violations."
- "Ford's recall numbers started creeping up to significant levels right about the time the consent order was in the works" for 2024, 2025, and 2026.
- This means Ford's recalls are partly FORCED DISCLOSURE, not evidence of uniquely bad engineering.

### FARS Data: Ford vs Competitors (2014-2023)
| Parent Company | Deaths | Crashes | Survivability Ratio | 
|---|---|---|---|
| Ford Motor (Ford+Lincoln+Mercury) | 37,931 | 68,554 | 1.81 |
| GM (Chevy+GMC+Buick+Cadillac+Pontiac+Saturn+Olds) | 51,687 | 87,147 | 1.69 |
| Stellantis (Dodge+Chrysler+Jeep+Ram) | 23,245 | 46,409 | 2.00 |
| Toyota | 22,019 | 39,159 | 1.78 |

**Key finding: GM has a LOWER survivability ratio (1.69) than Ford (1.81).** This means GM vehicles are harder to survive a fatal crash in than Ford vehicles. Yet GM has far fewer recalls.

### Brand-Level Death Rates (per 100M VMT)
- Ford: 11.68
- Chevrolet: 13.75 (HIGHER than Ford)
- Honda: 12.43 (HIGHER than Ford)
- Nissan: 12.78 (HIGHER than Ford)
- Toyota: 9.47 (lower than Ford)
- Kia: 3.77 (lower, but newer fleet skews this)

### The 387,000 Re-Recall Pattern
- Ford is also re-recalling 387,000+ vehicles because previous recall repairs were done incorrectly
- Models: Focus, Fusion, Mustang, F-150, Escape, Navigator, Nautilus (2012-2020)
- Jalopnik headline: "Ford Can't Even Get Recalls Right, Has To Re-Recall Over 387,000 Vehicles"
- But: the re-recalls are ALSO a product of the consent order forcing Ford to audit previous work

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing recall frequency with FARS survivability data to show that recall count is a measure of regulatory pressure, not vehicle danger. If NHTSA applied the same consent-order-level oversight to GM (which has a WORSE survivability ratio), GM's recall count would likely dwarf Ford's.

## Counterarguments
1. Ford genuinely has more quality issues — the consent order was earned, not random
2. The survivability ratio is affected by fleet age composition, not just engineering
3. Some Ford recalls involve severe issues (ball joint failures, DO NOT DRIVE orders)
4. The consent order may be catching real problems that genuinely need fixing
