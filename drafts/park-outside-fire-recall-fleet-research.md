# Research: NHTSA's "Park Outside" Fire Recall Fleet

## Angle
NHTSA has told owners of ~4.3 million vehicles to literally park outside and away from structures because their cars might catch fire while parked. Cross-reference with FARS crash data: these same models have produced 5,126 deaths on the road. The total hazard surface — killed driving OR killed parked — is a uniquely modern safety failure.

## Kill Test
**Is this genuinely newsworthy?** YES. Novel cross-tabulation nobody has done: fire recall volume × FARS crash deaths for the same vehicles. The "park outside" advisory is a growing category — Hyundai/Kia (3.3M+), Chevy Bolt (142K), Jeep PHEV (320K), BMW, Hyundai/Genesis. No one has aggregated the full list alongside crash fatality data to show these vehicles' full risk profile.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Park Outside Recalls
- **Hyundai/Kia 2023 wave:** 3.3M vehicles. ABS module fire risk. Kia Sportage (2014-16), K900 (2016-18), Hyundai Santa Fe (2016-18), plus Kia EV6, Hyundai Ioniq 5, others.
  - Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/consumer-alert-kia-and-hyundai-park-outside
- **Hyundai/Kia/Genesis 2024 wave:** 570K vehicles. Santa Cruz (2022-23), Santa Fe (2019-23), Santa Fe Hybrid (2021-23), Santa Fe PHEV (2022-23), Kia Carnival (2022-23).
  - Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/hyundai-kia-vehicles-recalled-fire-risk
- **Chevrolet Bolt EV/EUV:** ~142,000 vehicles (all 2017-2022 MY). LG Chem battery defect. Fire while parked or charging.
  - Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/recall-all-chevy-bolt-vehicles-fire-risk
- **Jeep Wrangler 4xe (2020-2025) + Grand Cherokee 4xe (2022-2026):** 320,065 vehicles. Samsung SDI battery defect. Fire while parked or driven. 19 reports, 1 injury.
  - Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/park-outside-recall-jeep-wrangler-phev
- **Hyundai/Genesis additional:** Additional models (various MY) for engine compartment fire risk.
  - Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/es/press-releases/consumer-alert-important-hyundai-and-genesis-recalls-fire-risk

## Novel Contribution
1. **Aggregation:** First compilation of ALL "park outside" fire recalls with total vehicle count (~4.3M)
2. **Cross-tabulation:** FARS crash fatality data for the same models shows 5,126 deaths — these aren't just fire risks, they're also crash killers
3. **Hazard surface concept:** Traditional safety analysis looks at crash deaths. Adding fire-while-parked creates a new dimension: your vehicle's risk doesn't stop when you turn it off
4. **Battery supplier angle:** Samsung SDI (Jeep) and LG Chem (Bolt) — the supply chain concentration risk in EV/PHEV safety

## Limitations
- FARS data covers all model years/powertrains, not just the PHEV/EV variants under fire recall
- Fire deaths in parked vehicles are NOT captured in FARS (which tracks traffic crashes only)
- Actual fire injury/death counts from these recalls are low (1 injury for Jeep, a handful for Bolt)
- The "park outside" recall count may overstate risk — many of these vehicles will never catch fire

## Strongest Counterargument
The fire risk is extremely low per vehicle (19 reports out of 320K Jeep PHEVs = 0.006%). More people die in the FARS crash data for these models in a single year than have ever been injured by the fire defect. The "park outside" advisory is an abundance of caution, not a proportionate response to actual risk. The real danger is still driving.
