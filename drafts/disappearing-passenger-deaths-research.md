# Research: The Disappearing Passenger Death

## Angle
Passenger deaths in US traffic crashes dropped 44% from 1994 to 2024 (10,294 → 5,746), while driver deaths dropped only 13% (21,596 → 18,686). The driver:passenger death ratio shifted from 2.1:1 to 3.25:1 over 30 years. This is a novel decomposition of WHY passengers are being saved at 3x the rate of drivers.

## Primary Data (FARS Encyclopedia, verified)

### 30-Year Trend
| Year | Drivers | Passengers | Total Occ. | Pass % of Occ. | D:P Ratio |
|------|---------|------------|------------|----------------|-----------|
| 1994 | 21,596  | 10,294     | 31,998*    | 32.2%          | 2.10:1    |
| 1999 | 22,914  | 10,451     | 33,451*    | 31.2%          | 2.19:1    |
| 2004 | 23,158  | 10,042     | 33,276*    | 30.2%          | 2.31:1    |
| 2009 | 17,670  | 6,793      | 24,526*    | 27.7%          | 2.60:1    |
| 2014 | 16,470  | 5,766      | 22,307*    | 25.8%          | 2.86:1    |
| 2019 | 17,984  | 5,846      | 23,891*    | 24.5%          | 3.08:1    |
| 2024 | 18,686  | 5,746      | 24,473*    | 23.5%          | 3.25:1    |

*includes unknown

### Key Observations
- Passengers went from 32.2% of occupant deaths (1994) to 23.5% (2024) — 8.7 percentage point decline
- Biggest decade of decline: 2004-2014 (30.2% → 25.8%, -4.4 pp) — corresponds to ESC mandate, side curtain airbag proliferation, IIHS side-impact testing upgrades
- Driver deaths INCREASED 2014→2024 (16,470→18,686, +13%) while passenger deaths FLAT (5,766→5,746)
- In the last decade, drivers got MORE dangerous while passengers stayed equally safe

### Counterfactual
If the 1994 passenger:occupant ratio (32.2%) still held in 2024:
- Expected 2024 passenger deaths: 24,473 × 0.322 = 7,880
- Actual 2024 passenger deaths: 5,746
- **2,134 "missing" passenger deaths** — lives saved by either protection improvements or reduced occupancy

### Decomposition (Novel Original Contribution)
The decline has two components:
1. **Occupancy effect**: Americans drive alone more → fewer passengers in cars → fewer passenger deaths (independent of safety)
2. **Protection effect**: Side curtain airbags, stronger B-pillars, IIHS side-impact tests → passengers survive crashes they wouldn't have in 1994

Census data: Solo commuting went from ~73% (2000) to ~76% (2019) — about 4% relative decline in average vehicle occupancy. If applied linearly, this explains ~4% of the 44% passenger death decline. The remaining ~40% = REAL safety improvement.

## Actionable Insights
- If you're buying a car: check IIHS side-impact ratings specifically (not just overall rating)
- Post-2012 vehicles have dramatically better passenger protection than pre-2012
- For families: the vehicle's passenger seat safety matters more than its overall death rate
- The side-impact test upgrade in 2003 (IIHS) and FMVSS 214 update drove measurable improvement
