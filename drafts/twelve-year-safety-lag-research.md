# Research: The 12-Year Safety Lag

## Thesis
The average vehicle on American roads is 12.8 years old (S&P Global Mobility, 2025). FARS data shows the peak death bracket is 12-14 years old (18.6% of all fatalities). This is not coincidence: the fleet's center of mass and its center of death are the same age. Every safety feature introduced after a vehicle's model year — ESC, AEB, lane-keep assist — never existed for that vehicle. The safety innovation pipeline takes a decade to reach the vehicles that actually kill people.

## Key FARS Findings (2014-2023, 177,284 fatal-vehicle deaths)

### Deaths by Vehicle Age (relative to crash year midpoint ~2018)
- 0-2 years old: 15,998 deaths (9.0%)
- 3-5 years old: 20,226 (11.4%)
- 6-8 years old: 18,734 (10.6%)
- 9-11 years old: 24,665 (13.9%)
- **12-14 years old: 32,925 (18.6%)** ← PEAK
- 15-17 years old: 28,797 (16.2%)
- 18-20 years old: 19,146 (10.8%)
- 21+ years old: 16,793 (9.5%)

**65.8% of fatalities in vehicles 10+ years old. Only 9% in vehicles 0-2 years old. Ratio: 7.3x.**

### Pre- vs Post-ESC Mandate
- Pre-ESC (MY ≤2011): 134,491 deaths
- Post-ESC (MY ≥2012): 52,567 deaths
- Ratio: 2.6x

## Angle
The industry's safety innovation pipeline is a class pipeline. New-car buyers (median income ~$70K+) get AEB, lane-keep, ESC within a year of mandate. Used-car buyers (median income much lower, younger drivers, lower-income families) get the same features 10-15 years later — after a generation of preventable deaths has already occurred. The 12.8-year fleet age IS the lag. The average car IS the problem car.
