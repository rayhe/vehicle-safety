# Research: Sub-1.0 Denominator Mirage

## News Hook
- Reuters (Jul 8, 2026): NHTSA announced Q1 2026 traffic fatalities dropped 4.3% to 7,770
- Fatality rate fell to **0.99 per 100M VMT** — lowest quarterly figure since 2014
- First time the rate has dropped below 1.0 since Q2 2014
- Source: NHTSA early estimates, FHWA VMT data

## Key Numbers
- Q1 2026: 7,770 deaths (down from 8,120 estimated Q1 2025)
- Fatality rate: 0.99 per 100M VMT
- VMT Q1 2026: ~786 billion miles (0.99 * 786B / 100M = 7,770)
- **86 deaths per day** in Q1 2026
- **3.6 deaths per hour**
- **1 death every 17 minutes**

## FARS Data Cross-Reference (2014-2023)
- 191,193 total deaths in FARS dataset across 337 tracked models
- Pre-2010 model year vehicles: 122,326 deaths (64.1% of all deaths)
- Pre-2000 model year vehicles: 28,036 deaths (14.7%)
- 37 models with death rate ≥2.0/100M VMT = 73,048 deaths (38.2%)
- These high-rate models are predominantly older designs without ESC, AEB, or modern crash structures

## Seasonal Context (Why Q1 is Misleading)
- Q1 is historically lowest-fatality quarter (cold weather, fewer motorcyclists, shorter days)
- Summer months (Q2/Q3) typically see 10-25% higher fatalities
- 2025 data showed Q2 summer decline stalled (July: -1.7%, August: -2.5%)
- The sub-1.0 rate may not survive summer 2026

## Historical Rate Context
- Pre-COVID (2019): 1.11 per 100M VMT (full year)
- Post-recession low (2014): 1.08 per 100M VMT
- COVID peak (2021): 1.37 per 100M VMT
- 2024 full year: 1.19 per 100M VMT
- 2025 full year: 1.10 per 100M VMT
- Q1 2026: 0.99 per 100M VMT

## The Denominator Problem (Original Analysis)
VMT keeps growing. In 2025, Americans drove 29.8 billion MORE miles than 2024.
The rate improves because the denominator grows faster than the numerator shrinks.

If 2025's 36,640 deaths occurred at 2014's VMT level (~3.025 trillion):
- Rate would be: 36,640 / 30,250 = 1.21 per 100M VMT
- The rate only looks like "progress" because we drove 300B more miles
