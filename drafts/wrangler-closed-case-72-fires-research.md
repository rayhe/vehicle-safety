# Research: Wrangler Closed Case — 72 Fires

## Story Angle
Stellantis investigated Wrangler/Gladiator fires in 2023-early 2024, CLOSED its own investigation citing "low fire rate," then had to REOPEN in August 2024 after fires increased. After 1+ year of forensic analysis (CT scans, X-rays, material analysis, vehicle buybacks), they finally determined the defect "posed an unreasonable risk to safety" in late May 2026. Recall issued June 9, 2026.

The novel angle: the investigation lifecycle failure. How long does it take between "we know there's a problem" and "we recall the vehicles"? And what happens in the gap?

## Primary Sources

### 1. Reuters — Stellantis recall filing (June 9, 2026)
- 1.3M Jeep Wrangler (2021-2025) and Gladiator (2021-2025) recalled worldwide
- Electric hydraulic power steering pump wiring connector defect
- Loose electrical connection → melting → combustible materials overheat → fire
- **72 fires tied to the issue, 1 injury**
- Fires can occur with ignition OFF (parked vehicles)
- Recall: 1.08M US, 106K Canada, 23K Mexico, ~125K rest of world
- Remedy: inspect/replace wiring harness or EHPS pump, fix by July 2026
- **TIMELINE**: Stellantis investigated 2023-early 2024 → closed due to "low fire rate" → reopened August 2024 after incident increase → 1+ year forensic analysis → late May 2026 determined unreasonable risk
- Source: https://www.reuters.com/world/stellantis-recalling-more-than-13-million-jeep-vehicles-over-fire-concerns-2026-06-09/

### 2. NHTSA Investigation (September 2024)
- NHTSA opened independent investigation Sept 2024
- Covered ~781,000 2021-2023 Wrangler and Gladiator vehicles
- Cited hydraulic power steering pump wiring as potential fire risk
- Noted fires even with ignition off
- Source: NHTSA RECALLS database, NHTSA investigation docket

### 3. Lawsuit (November 2024)
- Filed November 7, 2024 in US District Court, Detroit
- Plaintiffs from KY, CA, OR, TN
- Alleged power steering pump electrical connector defect → spontaneous fires
- One plaintiff: 2021 Wrangler burst into flames minutes after engine started smoking
- Source: MoparInsiders reporting, February 2026 update

### 4. Additional Stellantis recalls in 2026
- **March 5, 2026**: 320,065 Jeep Wrangler 4xe (2020-2025) and Grand Cherokee 4xe (2022-2026) — battery pack fire (separator damage in cells). 19 fires. Park outside, don't recharge.
- **May 2026**: 419,000+ Stellantis vehicles — improper side airbag deployment
- **June 9, 2026**: 17,277 additional vehicles — battery pack defect, fire risk even when off
- **June 9, 2026**: 1.08M US Wrangler/Gladiator — power steering pump wiring fire
- **Total US recalls 2026**: ~1.8M+ vehicles across multiple campaigns

### 5. FARS Data — Jeep Wrangler
- 1,842 deaths (2014-2023), 184.2 per year
- 3,718 fatal crashes
- Fleet: 1,750,000
- Death rate: 0.84 per 100M VMT
- Impairment: 19.3% any (14.5% alcohol, 7.8% drug) — LOWER than US average (~21%)
- Model year deaths: 2018: 86, 2019: 31, 2020: 51, 2021: 38, 2022: 9
- Gladiator not in FARS dataset (too new, launched 2020)

## Novel Original Contribution
1. **The "closed case" timeline**: Mapping how Stellantis's own internal investigation closed the file 2+ years before the recall, while fires continued accumulating from ~initial reports to 72 known fires
2. **Forensic methodology note**: CT scans and X-rays on automotive wiring connectors is unusually intensive — this wasn't a simple "find the bad part." It took over a year of forensic engineering
3. **Cumulative recall pressure**: Stellantis has recalled more US vehicles in 2026 alone (~1.8M+) than its estimated annual US sales. The Wrangler specifically has been subject to MULTIPLE fire recalls from different root causes (battery fires in 4xe, power steering wiring fires in all models)
4. **FARS context**: While the fire investigation dragged on, Wranglers were generating 184 crash deaths per year. Fire is terrifying but rare (72 fires in 1.3M vehicles = 0.006%); crash deaths are routine and relentless

## Strongest Counterargument
72 fires out of 1.3 million vehicles is a 0.006% incidence rate. Stellantis could argue (and initially did) that the fire rate was low and didn't justify a recall. The fact that they closed and reopened their investigation could be framed as responsible engineering diligence — they needed more data before acting. The CT scans and X-rays show they took the forensic analysis seriously, not that they dragged their feet.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers 2014-2023; the recalled vehicles are 2021-2025 model years, so FARS has limited exposure data for these specific vehicles
- We cannot determine from FARS whether any Wrangler deaths in the dataset were fire-related (FARS codes fire involvement but our dataset doesn't include that field)
- Gladiator entirely absent from FARS dataset
- We don't know how many of the 72 fires occurred BEFORE vs AFTER the investigation closure
