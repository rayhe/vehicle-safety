# Research: TSP Awards Don't Require Semi-Trailer AEB Testing

## Angle
The IIHS hands out Top Safety Pick awards to vehicles it never tested against semi-trailers — the type of crash that kills 400+ people a year in underride collisions. Only TSP+ (the higher tier) requires passing the new vehicle-to-vehicle front crash prevention test, which now includes semi-trailer and motorcycle targets. TSP-only winners skip this test entirely.

## Key Data Points

### IIHS 2026 Criteria
- **TSP** requires: Good in small overlap front, side, moderate overlap front tests; Acceptable or Good in pedestrian AEB and headlights; standard AEB required. Does NOT require passing the new v2v AEB test with semi-trailer/motorcycle targets.
- **TSP+** requires all TSP criteria PLUS: Good in pedestrian AEB (not just Acceptable); Acceptable or Good in the NEW vehicle-to-vehicle front crash prevention test (includes semi-trailer + motorcycle targets at 31, 37, 43 mph).
- 2026 results (as of July): 45+ TSP+ winners, 18+ TSP-only winners. 5 vehicles tested and failed both tiers (Audi A3, Cadillac CT5, Lexus IS, Nissan Kicks, Toyota Tacoma Crew Cab).
- TSP-only vehicles include: Honda Civic hatchback, Hyundai Elantra, Toyota Prius — popular, mainstream cars that consumers trust as "safe."
- Source: IIHS.org; Consumer Reports; AutoGuide.com

### Underride Crash Deaths
- **5,936 people** died in crashes involving large trucks in 2022 (NHTSA)
- **400+** died specifically in underride collisions in 2021 (ProPublica/FRONTLINE analysis)
- **89 fatalities/year** in side underride with tractor-trailers (NHTSA estimate, accounting for 78% underreporting)
- **500 deaths + 5,000 serious injuries/year** from collisions with semis lacking side underride guards (Institute for Safer Trucking petition, citing multiple studies)
- **65% of light vehicle occupant fatalities** in rear truck crashes involved underride (NHTSA analysis)
- Only **17 states** have a field on police accident reports for underride — so crashes are massively undercounted
- Source: NHTSA Report to Congress (June 2024); ProPublica; GAO 2019

### The Gap (Original Contribution)
The TSP tier structure creates a two-tier safety illusion: consumers see "Top Safety Pick" and assume comprehensive testing, but the award doesn't require any testing against the crash type that kills 400+ people annually. The v2v semi-trailer test exists — the IIHS built it — but only TSP+ vehicles are required to pass it. A Honda Civic hatchback with a TSP award has proven it can handle a frontal offset crash. Nobody checked whether its AEB can detect an 80,000-pound tractor-trailer.

### Counterargument
AEB is a crash-avoidance technology, not a crashworthiness test. The TSP crashworthiness tests (frontal, side, headlights) measure what happens DURING a collision. AEB measures what happens BEFORE. The IIHS is phasing in the semi-trailer test gradually — requiring it only at TSP+ first is a deliberate rollout strategy to give manufacturers time to comply. If they required it at TSP immediately, most vehicles would lose their awards, potentially reducing consumer engagement with IIHS ratings.

### Limitation
We don't know which TSP-only vehicles WOULD have passed the semi-trailer test. Some may have been tested and failed; others may not have been submitted for the test at all. The IIHS doesn't publish per-vehicle v2v test results for TSP-only vehicles. We also can't attribute all 400+ underride deaths specifically to AEB failure — many involve closing speed differentials where no AEB could help. The IIHS motorcycle/semi-trailer test is at controlled speeds (31-43 mph), not highway closing speeds.
