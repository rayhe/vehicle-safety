# Research: The 100x Sedan Safety Lottery

## Slug: sedan-100x-impairment-constant
## Journalist: Dale Impactor III (Toxicology Desk Chief)
## Kicker: Existential Dread

## Core Thesis
FARS toxicology data reveals impairment rates are statistically identical across all 25 popular sedans (500K+ fleet) — mean 20.1%, standard deviation just 1.4 percentage points. Yet death rates per 100M VMT span from 0.05 (Tesla Model 3) to 5.0 (Chevrolet Impala): a 100x spread. Behavior is a constant. The car is the variable.

## Novel Cross-Tabulation
This is the first analysis to plot impairment rates against death rates within a single vehicle class for popular vehicles, demonstrating that toxicology data is essentially a flat line while fatality rates vary by two orders of magnitude.

## Impairment Statistics
- Range: 16.0% (Prius) to 22.7% (Charger)
- Mean: 20.1%
- Std Dev: 1.4 percentage points
- Coefficient of variation: 7% — almost no variance

## Death Rate Statistics
- Range: 0.05 to 5.0
- Spread: 100x
- This means that for every 1 fatal crash in a Model 3 per VMT, there are 100 in an Impala

## Strongest Counterargument
Fleet age composition. The Impala's fatal fleet skews heavily toward 2000-2012 model years (peak: 401 deaths for MY 2008). The Model 3 is all 2017+. Older cars lack modern safety features (AEB, ESC, curtain airbags, reinforced passenger cells). This isn't comparing apples to apples — it's comparing 2005 engineering to 2020 engineering.

## Rebuttal
1. ALL of these sedans are on the used car market RIGHT NOW. A buyer shopping at $8K-15K doesn't choose between eras — they choose between models at a price point.
2. Even among 2015+ model year deaths: Altima has 1,185, Camry 1,013, Corolla 1,117, Accord 831 vs Model 3 at 91. Fleet-adjusted, significant spread remains.
3. The Dodge Charger has the highest impairment (22.7%) but one of the LOWEST death rates (0.75) — its bulk protects occupants. The Honda Civic has average impairment (20.4%) but high death rate (2.25) — its size doesn't protect.

## Limitations
- FARS only captures fatal crashes (36K+/year). Non-fatal crash rates may differ.
- Death rate uses VMT estimates, not actual odometer data. ±15% uncertainty for low-volume models.
- Impairment rates reflect toxicology testing of drivers in fatal crashes, not all drivers. Selection bias: if a car is more crashworthy, only the worst crashes are fatal, potentially selecting for higher impairment in the survivors.
- Fleet estimates are rough and don't account for regional concentration.
- Tesla Model 3's extremely low rate may partially reflect newer fleet (all 2017+), higher MSRP correlating with safer infrastructure (better roads, lower-risk driving conditions).

## Actionable Takeaways
- If buying a used sedan: newer is dramatically safer (2015+ vs pre-2010)
- Within the same era, death rates still vary 5-10x by model
- The Toyota Camry and Corolla are middling, not great (rates of 2.03 and 1.85)
- Ford Fusion (1.23) and Hyundai Elantra (1.50) are better picks than Honda Civic (2.25) or Accord (3.07)
- Check your VIN at nhtsa.gov/recalls
