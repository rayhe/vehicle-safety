# Research: Ford Explorer 676K Recall Week

## News Peg (July 20-23, 2026)
Ford recalled **676,225 Explorers** this week across TWO separate campaigns:
1. **387,911** (2020-2026 Explorer + 2020-2027 Lincoln Aviator): Second-row easy-entry seats may unlatch, tip, or slide while vehicle is in motion. 14 reports of seat movement while driving. 6 of those were vehicles ALREADY recalled and fixed once (expanding Recall 25V393). No crashes/injuries reported.
2. **288,314** (2016-2019 Explorer): Roof rail covers may detach while driving. Expands previous Recall 21V316.

Additionally:
- **66,383** Explorer Hybrid + Nautilus Hybrid recalled Jul 2026 for pedestrian alert system fix that DIDN'T FIX the original defect (Recall 25SA2)
- **741,195** (2020-2021 Explorer among others) recalled Jun 2026 for transmission park pawl damage → vehicle rollaway
- 2026 Explorer has been recalled **8 times** by NHTSA per Autoblog
- Explorer reliability score: 72/100, ranked 19th of 26 midsize SUVs (Autoblog/NHTSA data)

## FARS Data
Ford Explorer:
- 3,797 deaths (2014-2023)
- 6,626 FARS crashes
- Rate: 1.54 per 100M VMT
- Crash lethality: 57.3% (Explorer occupant dies in 57.3% of fatal crashes)
- Fleet estimate: ~1,968,750
- Impairment: 19.5%

Lincoln Aviator: 189 deaths, rate 1.15

## Key Finding / Original Contribution
The Explorer's 57.3% crash lethality is the HIGHEST among all midsize SUVs in FARS — meaning when an Explorer is in a fatal crash, the Explorer's occupant dies more often than in any competitor. This is unusual for a vehicle this large. Normally, larger vehicles have lower lethality because they transfer crash energy to the other vehicle.

Possible explanations: 
1. Large fleet of aging pre-2020 Explorers with less modern safety tech
2. Higher single-vehicle crash rate (rollovers were historically a problem — Firestone tire era)
3. The Explorer's demographics may differ from competitors
