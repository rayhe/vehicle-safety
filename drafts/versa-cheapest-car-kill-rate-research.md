# Research: The Nissan Versa — America's Cheapest New Car and Its Occupant Survival Problem

## Angle
The Nissan Versa was America's cheapest new car (~$14,730 MSRP). In FARS fatal crashes, the Versa occupant dies 72.3% of the time — among the highest lethality of ANY vehicle in the database. IIHS gave the 2012-2019 Versa a POOR rating on small overlap frontal, with 42cm of lower hinge pillar intrusion. No AEB was available. Reclassified as a "minicar" because Nissan made it LIGHTER in the 2012 redesign.

The buyers aren't reckless — 18.5% impairment rate is BELOW the 20% national average. These are sober, budget-constrained commuters who die because the physics of a 2,400 lb minicar vs a 5,000 lb truck are non-negotiable.

**Kill test:** Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. The Versa specifically hasn't been covered. The budget car → occupant survival penalty is a consumer safety story with socioeconomic bite. Clara Rollover's beat.

## Primary Sources (3+)

### 1. NHTSA FARS Data (2014-2023)
- Nissan Versa: 722 deaths, 998 FARS-involved crashes, 700K fleet, rate 0.90/100M VMT
- Lethality: 72.3% (when Versa is in a fatal crash, Versa occupant dies 72.3% of the time)
- Impairment: 18.5% (below 20% national average)
- Budget subcompact comparison (all lethality):
  - Chevy Spark: 74.4%, Chevy Sonic: 75.4%, Honda Fit: 72.0%
  - Hyundai Accent: 71.7%, Kia Rio: 73.0%, Toyota Yaris: 66.9%
- Mid-range comparison:
  - Nissan Sentra: 67.1%, Honda Civic: 68.1%, Toyota Corolla: 64.1%
  - Ford Fusion: 55.1%, Chevy Traverse: 39.3%, Tesla Model 3: 43.4%

### 2. IIHS Versa Rating (2012-2019)
- Small overlap front: POOR
- 42cm lower hinge pillar intrusion
- Steering column moved 12cm up, 15cm right
- Head barely contacted airbag before sliding off
- AEB: "Not available"
- Reclassified as "minicar" after 2012 redesign (lighter)
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/ratings/vehicle/nissan/versa-4-door-sedan/2019

### 3. IIHS Driver Death Rate Study (2020)
- Minicars: 82 deaths per million registered vehicle years (highest)
- Very large SUVs: 15 deaths per million (lowest)
- 5.5x gap between smallest and largest categories
- "Smaller vehicles offer less protection and their lighter mass means they take the brunt of collisions with larger vehicles"
- 2017 Ford Fiesta: 141 deaths/million (worst single model)
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/news/detail/driver-death-rates-remain-high-among-small-cars

### 4. IIHS Vehicle Size and Weight Research
- Physics: in a head-on between two vehicles, the lighter vehicle absorbs more crash energy
- ESC + AEB help but can't overcome physics of mass disparity
- Source: https://www.iihs.org/topics/vehicle-size-and-weight

### 5. NSC Preliminary Data (Feb 2026)
- National mileage death rate: 1.01 per 100M VMT (Feb 2026)
- Declining: 1.32 (2023) → 1.22 (2024) → 1.10 (2025) → 1.01 (2026)
- National improvement driven by newer, heavier fleet — but cheapest cars lag
- Source: https://injuryfacts.nsc.org/motor-vehicle/overview/preliminary-monthly-estimates/
