# Research: Honda Odyssey Pothole Airbag Recall — 440,830 Minivans

## News Hook
Honda recalled 440,830 Odyssey minivans (2018-2022) because side and side curtain airbags may deploy after hitting potholes, speed bumps, or road debris. The SRS ECU contains incorrect deployment parameters that misinterpret G-signal inputs from road impacts as side crashes, triggering second and third row curtain airbags — directly adjacent to where children sit in car seats.

## Key Facts
- **Vehicles affected:** 440,830 Honda Odyssey (2018-2022 MY)
- **Defect:** SRS ECU incorrect deployment parameters — interprets pothole/speed bump forces as side impacts
- **Airbags affected:** Side and side curtain airbags, SECOND AND THIRD ROW
- **Injuries:** 25 reported (Jan 24, 2017 – Apr 2, 2026)
- **Warranty claims:** 130
- **Deaths:** 0 confirmed
- **Estimated affected:** Only ~0.1% of recalled population believed actually afflicted
- **Fix:** Reprogram or replace SRS ECU (free at dealers)
- **Owner notification:** Mailed beginning May 25, 2026
- **NHTSA recall number:** 26V-248 (Honda recall UNW)

## Timeline (Critical)
- **Late 2017:** Honda first receives notification of inadvertent airbag deployment in Odyssey
- **2021:** Honda's investigation identifies the potential for potholes/road surfaces to trigger deployment. Honda "determined there were no safety concerns."
- **June 3, 2022:** Fixed in production (2023 MY vehicles have corrected SRS ECU)
- **Late 2025:** NHTSA issues Preliminary Evaluation request
- **April 2, 2026:** Honda determines "a defect related to motor vehicle safety existed" — initiates recall
- **April 9-15, 2026:** Recall publicly announced

**Gap:** Honda knew since 2021. Fixed production in 2022. Didn't recall already-sold vehicles until NHTSA forced the issue 4+ years later. During those years, families continued driving with potentially defective airbags.

The Odyssey has one of the LOWEST impairment rates in the FARS database. Only 15.4% of fatal crash drivers tested positive for any substance. This means 84.6% of Odyssey fatal crashes involve sober drivers — overwhelmingly families, commuters, the exact demographic who chose this vehicle for safety.
