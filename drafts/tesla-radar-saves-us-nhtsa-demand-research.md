# Research: Tesla "Radar Saves Us" — NHTSA Demands Internal Document

## Angle
NHTSA has formally demanded Tesla hand over an internal document titled "Radar Saves Us" as part of Engineering Analysis EA26002 into FSD crashes in low-visibility conditions. This is the last step before a recall. 3.2 million vehicles covered. The document apparently shows Tesla understood radar+vision was safer than vision-only — and shipped vision-only anyway. Clara Rollover consumer safety piece: what this means for FSD owners NOW.

## Key Facts (sourced)
1. **NHTSA 24-part information demand** sent to Tesla July 2, 2026 (signed by Tanya Topka, ODI Director)
2. **"Radar Saves Us"** — internal Tesla document first submitted during preliminary investigation PE24031-01, now formally pulled into engineering analysis record
3. **EA26002** — Engineering Analysis is last investigative step before recall or case closure
4. **3.2 million Tesla vehicles** that have ever run FSD in the US are covered
5. **9 crashes** — FSD engaged in reduced visibility (sun glare, fog, airborne dust). 1 fatality, 1 injury.
6. **August 12, 2026 deadline** — Tesla must respond. $27,874/day penalties, capped ~$139M.
7. **Request No. 12** — NHTSA lists 15 specific Tesla/Musk marketing statements, asks Tesla to confirm whether each "implies a higher level of capability or automation than the system currently has"
   - "FSD Supervised can drive in whiteout conditions"
   - "Tesla FSD can operate in all conditions" (over video admitting FSD struggles in fog, rain, sunlight)
   - "$99/month robotaxi" post
   - Videos of drivers dancing, making espresso, pushing seat back
8. **Request No. 14** — 2024 change switching "FSD Degraded" alert from red to yellow, removed audible chime
9. **Radar removal timeline:** May 2021 (front radar), 2022 (ultrasonic sensors) — branded "Tesla Vision"
10. **Internal dissent:** Tesla engineers reportedly tried to talk Musk out of removing radar (Electrek reporting)
11. **Musk DM admission:** "vision with high-resolution radar would be better than pure vision" — published by Electrek

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing NHTSA's marketing claims probe (Request No. 12) with Tesla's own timeline of radar removal + FSD marketing escalation creates a regulatory paper trail nobody has mapped in one place. The key insight: NHTSA isn't just investigating crashes — they're building a case that Tesla's marketing systematically misrepresented what camera-only FSD could do in the exact conditions that produced these crashes.
