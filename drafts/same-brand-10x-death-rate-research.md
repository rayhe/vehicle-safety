# Research: Same Brand, 10× the Death Rate

## Angle
Within a single car brand, the FARS death rate gap between models is 10× or larger. This within-brand variation dwarfs the between-brand variation for similar vehicle classes. Buyers who choose by brand rather than model are making a decision that barely moves the needle on survival odds, while the model they pick within that brand matters enormously.

## Key Data Points (FARS 2014–2023, deaths per 100M VMT)

### Toyota (10.7× gap)
- Camry: 2.03 (6,328 deaths, fleet 2,712,500)
- Corolla: 1.85 (4,945 deaths, fleet 2,318,750)
- RAV4: 0.19 (914 deaths, fleet 3,762,500)
- Highlander: 0.42 (1,106 deaths, fleet 2,100,000)
- Gap: Camry (2.03) vs RAV4 (0.19) = 10.7×

### Honda (10.6× gap)
- Accord: 3.07 (7,102 deaths, fleet 2,012,500)
- Civic: 2.25 (6,553 deaths, fleet 2,537,500)
- CR-V: 0.53 (2,072 deaths, fleet 3,150,000)
- Pilot: 0.29 (514 deaths, fleet 1,400,000)
- Gap: Accord (3.07) vs Pilot (0.29) = 10.6×

### Chevrolet (13.9× gap)
- Impala: 5.00 (3,774 deaths, fleet 656,250)
- Malibu: 2.03 (3,465 deaths, fleet 1,487,500)
- Equinox: 0.36 (1,056 deaths, fleet 2,362,500)
- Traverse: 0.20 (265 deaths, fleet 1,050,000)
- Gap: Impala (5.00) vs Equinox (0.36) = 13.9×

### Cross-Brand Same-Class Comparison (sedans differ by ~1.5×)
- Toyota Camry: 2.03
- Chevy Malibu: 2.03
- Honda Accord: 3.07
- Range: 1.5×

### Cross-Brand Same-Class Comparison (compact SUVs differ by ~2.8×)
- Toyota RAV4: 0.19
- Chevy Equinox: 0.36
- Honda CR-V: 0.53
- Range: 2.8×

### Key Insight
Brand choice within same class = 1.5–2.8× variation.
Model choice within same brand = 10–14× variation.
The model matters ~5× more than the brand.

## Counterargument (at full strength)
This is largely a sedan-vs-SUV effect, not a brand-specific problem. Sedans are lower, lighter, and lose in multi-vehicle collisions with heavier trucks and SUVs. The mass mismatch is well-documented by IIHS. Any brand's sedan will have a higher death rate than its SUV lineup. This isn't a "Toyota problem" or a "Honda problem" — it's physics.

**Response:** That's exactly the point for a buyer. If you're choosing between a Camry and a RAV4, knowing they differ by 10.7× in fatal crash rates per VMT should influence the decision. Brand loyalty is comforting but statistically irrelevant when the within-brand gap is this large.

## Limitations
- FARS captures only fatal crashes (~40K/year), not the ~6.7M total crashes. A vehicle with low fatality rates could still have high injury rates.
- VMT estimates use NHTS averages per vehicle class, not actual odometer data (±15% uncertainty for low-volume models).
- Fleet age composition differs: the Accord fleet includes cars from 2000–2023, while the Pilot fleet skews newer. Older vehicles lack modern safety features.
- Driver demographics differ by model (age, gender, risk tolerance, alcohol use) — not all of the gap is structural.
- These are all-persons-killed rates (occupants + pedestrians + other-vehicle occupants), not occupant-only rates.
