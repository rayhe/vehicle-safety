# Research: Honda Salt Belt Corrosion — Safety Asterisk

## Angle
Honda's safest vehicles (Pilot at 0.29 deaths/100M VMT, Ridgeline as safest truck) are subject to a geography-specific recall: 880,514 vehicles in 23 salt-belt states for rear suspension corrosion that could cause wheel misalignment or detachment. The same car is safe in California, potentially lethal in Ohio. Your zip code creates a two-tier safety system.

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing FARS safety records with geography-specific recall data. No existing article covers geography as a safety variable within the same model.

## Primary Sources

### NHTSA Recall 26V365 / 26V367000
- **Vehicles:** 2016-2022 Honda Pilot (463,253), 2017-2023 Honda Ridgeline (110,070), 2019-2023 Honda Passport (89,674), 2014-2020 Acura MDX (217,517)
- **Total:** 880,514
- **Defect:** Improper coating specifications on rear subframe → insufficient paint adhesion → premature paint peeling near arm bracket weld area → corrosion at rear control arm/lower arm mounting points → potential fracture and failure
- **Supplier:** F&P Georgia
- **States (23 + DC):** CT, DE, IL, IN, IA, KY, ME, MD, MA, MI, MN, MO, NH, NJ, NY, OH, PA, RI, VT, VA, WV, DC, WI
- **Injuries/deaths:** Zero
- **Warranty claims:** Zero
- **Honda's estimate:** 1% of population has the defect
- **Timeline:** Honda identified paint quality difference Dec 2021 → supplier improved pre-paint treatment Aug 2022 → coating thickness increased Jan 2023 → first corrosion report Sep 5, 2025 → defect determination May 28, 2026
- **Remedy:** Dealer inspection + rear subframe reinforcement kit + repair/replace as needed (free)
- **Owner notification:** July 7, 2026

### FARS Data (from our database)
- Honda Pilot: 514 deaths, rate 0.29/100M VMT — among safest SUVs
- Honda Ridgeline: identified as safest truck in prior Crash Report analysis
- Honda Passport: newer model, lower volume
- Acura MDX: premium crossover, strong safety record

### Prior Honda Corrosion Recalls
- CR-V rear subframe corrosion recall (older)
- Ridgeline frame corrosion recall (older)
- This is Honda's THIRD corrosion recall — pattern, not anomaly

## Counterargument
Honda estimates only 1% of vehicles actually have the defect. Zero injuries, zero deaths. The recall is proactive. Most owners will get an inspection and a clean bill of health. Honda caught this before anyone got hurt.

## Limitations
- FARS data doesn't isolate state-by-state death rates for individual models (publicly available aggregates don't break this way)
- "Salt belt" isn't a formal regulatory designation — it's Honda's own determination of affected states
- No warranty claims means the defect hasn't manifested at dangerous levels yet
