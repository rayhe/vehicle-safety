# Research: Buick Century Zombie Sedan

## Core Finding
The Buick Century was discontinued in 2005. Zero new Centuries have left a factory in over 20 years. Yet between 2014 and 2023, 849 people died in FARS-recorded crashes involving Buick Centuries. That's 85 deaths per year in a car that hasn't been sold new since George W. Bush's second term.

## FARS Data
- **Deaths:** 849 (84.9/yr)
- **Crashes:** 1,106 (FARS fatal crashes)
- **Fleet:** ~306,250
- **Rate:** 2.41 per 100M VMT (above sedan weighted avg of 2.15)
- **Crash lethality:** 0.768 — 76.8% of fatal crashes involving a Century kill the Century occupant

## Toxicology
- Any impairment: 21.8% (slightly above 20.4% sedan average)
- Alcohol: 16.6%
- Drug: 9.3%
- 813 tested drivers total

## Model Year Deaths
ALL deaths come from pre-2006 model years. Peak: MY2003 (147 deaths), MY2002 (120), MY2001 (97). Death-weighted average model year: ~2001. That means the average Century in a fatal crash during 2014-2023 was approximately 17-18 years old.

## Context: The GM W-Body Graveyard
Century was built on GM's W-body platform alongside:
- Chevrolet Impala: 3,774 deaths, rate 5.0 (!!)
- Pontiac Grand Prix: 970 deaths, rate 2.14
- Buick Regal: 153 deaths, rate 1.01
- Chevrolet Monte Carlo: 178 deaths, rate 0.71
- Oldsmobile Intrigue: 20 deaths

Combined W-body platform deaths: ~5,944 deaths

## Original Contribution
Nobody has profiled a specific discontinued vehicle's continued death toll. The Century is a zombie: no longer produced, aging off roads, but still killing at a higher rate than vehicles currently on sale. The original calculation: at the Century's current mortality trajectory and fleet attrition rate, how long before the last Century-related fatality? Given ~306K fleet and ~85 deaths/year, even with fleet decline, Centuries could still be killing people through the late 2030s.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely surprising after 591 articles? YES — we've done zombie fleet stories in aggregate, but never a specific vehicle profile of a discontinued car's ongoing death toll. The Century isn't famous, isn't loved, and isn't collectible. It's the most anonymous car still killing people on American roads. That's the story.
