# Research Notes: NHTSA Four Investigators, Six Million Teslas

## Story Angle
NHTSA's autonomous vehicle investigation team went from 7 investigators to 4 after DOGE/federal workforce cuts. Those 4 people now oversee approximately 6+ million Tesla vehicles across multiple active investigations — the most complex regulatory portfolio in NHTSA's history. Meanwhile, Congress proposed cutting NHTSA's total budget by $10M+ and redirecting $78M from Bipartisan Infrastructure Law safety programs. Total annual fatalities: ~44,000.

**Kill test:** We've done investigation delay stories (617-day lag, Saturday dumps, three-investigations-39000-dead). But we've never covered NHTSA's *capacity* — the workforce numbers, the investigator-to-vehicle ratios, and the budget trajectory. This is the regulatory capacity story, not an investigation process story. Novel.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Workforce Cuts (Reuters, Feb 2025)
- NHTSA laid off 4% of staff (probationary employees)
- Pre-cut workforce: ~800 people
- Under Biden, agency grew 30%
- Post-cut: still "considerably larger" than historical
- Source: Reuters, "US auto safety agency laid off 4% of staff" (Feb 24, 2025)

### 2. Deeper Workforce Reduction (Carscoops/Reuters, Jul 2025)
- Total planned reduction: 772 → 555 employees (28% cut)
- Not all from direct layoffs — "financial incentive programs" (voluntary departures)
- Autonomous vehicle investigation team: went from 7 people → 4 people
- Source: Carscoops citing Reuters congressional data; Washington Post reporting on the 7-person AV team

### 3. NHTSA Budget Proposals (Consumer Reports coalition letter, Jul 2025)
- House Appropriations THUD subcommittee proposal:
  - Cut $10M+ from NHTSA operations and research
  - Redirect ~$78M from Bipartisan Infrastructure Law safety programs (state/local grants, crash data, behavioral research)
- Coalition letter signers: Consumer Reports, Center for Auto Safety, others
- Source: Consumer Reports Advocacy, "Consumer Reports Joins Call to Fully Fund NHTSA in FY26"

### 4. Active Tesla Investigations (NHTSA/Reuters, current)
- **EA26002** (Engineering Analysis): FSD visibility/degradation failures, ~3.2M vehicles, upgraded March 2026 — one step from mandatory recall
- **PE25012** (Preliminary Evaluation): FSD traffic violations (red lights, wrong lanes), ~2.88M vehicles, opened Oct 2025
- **Katy TX Special Crash Investigation**: Model 3 crashed into home at 73 mph, killed Maria Avila (age 76) inside her house, June 19, 2026. NTSB also opened probe — two federal agencies simultaneously
- **~50 special crash investigations** into Tesla ADAS since 2016, ~24 deaths
- **EA24001** (Tesla steering): Closed June 28, 2026 after Tesla recall of 376K vehicles
- Source: Reuters, NHTSA dockets, Memeburn/TechCrunch reporting

## Original Analysis (Novel Contribution)

### Calculation: Vehicles Under Active AV Investigation Per AV Investigator
- Active AV-related investigations: EA26002 (3.2M) + PE25012 (2.88M) + ~50 special crash cases
- Conservative total: 6.08M vehicles under active AV investigation
- AV team size: 4 people
- **Ratio: ~1.52 million vehicles per investigator**
- Before cuts (7 people): ~868,000 vehicles per investigator
- Ratio degraded 75% — same workload, 43% fewer people

### Calculation: Total Recall + Investigation Load Per NHTSA Employee
- This week alone: ~1.18M vehicles recalled (Hyundai 96K + Toyota 21K + Land Rover 251K + Honda 441K + Tesla 376K)
- Annual recall volume: typically 30-40M vehicles/year
- NHTSA staff after cuts: 555
- **Ratio: ~54,000 to 72,000 recalled vehicles per staff member per year** (vs ~39,000 to 52,000 pre-cuts)

### Key Contradiction
The same week NHTSA is closing investigations by simply accepting manufacturer recalls at face value (Tesla steering, Honda Odyssey), the agency is also being asked to deeply investigate 6M+ vehicles in the most complex ADAS cases in automotive history. With 4 people.

## Strongest Counterargument
NHTSA says it "retained positions critical to the mission" and is "still considerably larger" than pre-Biden levels. The 2023 workforce was 675, so 555 isn't historically unprecedented. Also, software OTA recalls don't require the same physical inspection infrastructure as hardware recalls. And the agency's investigation process has always been understaffed relative to fleet size — this isn't new.

## Limitations
- Exact staffing figures for the AV team come from Washington Post reporting, not official NHTSA disclosure
- "772 → 555" comes from Reuters citing congressional data; actual current number may differ
- "6 million vehicles" under investigation double-counts some vehicles that appear in multiple probes
- FARS data (2014-2023) predates many FSD deployments; Tesla's ADAS fleet has grown significantly since
- We don't know how NHTSA allocates non-AV investigators to AV-related work (the 4-person team may not be the only people working AV cases)
