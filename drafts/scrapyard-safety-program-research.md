# Research: The Scrapyard Is America's Best Safety Program

## Angle
NHTSA reported 2025 US traffic fatalities dropped to 36,640 — a 6.7% decline, with the rate hitting 1.06 per 100M VMT (lowest since 2014). Jan-June 2025 saw an 8.2% decline, the largest drop since 2008. Everyone's celebrating. But our FARS data shows this isn't about better driving or smarter policy. It's about old cars dying faster than new crashes can replace them.

## Novel Contribution
Cross-tabulation of FARS model-year death distribution with national fleet attrition data. Original finding: **62.3% of all FARS fatalities (2014-2023) involve vehicles with model year 2008 or earlier.** As these pre-ESC, pre-modern-crashworthiness vehicles get scrapped, deaths fall mechanically.

- New MY (2018-2022) deaths are 70.1% lower than old MY (2005-2009) deaths
- Sedan lethality: 0.645 (64.5% of sedan-involved fatal crashes kill the sedan occupant)
- SUV lethality: 0.524 (52.4%) — 23% safer than sedans in fatal crashes
- Pickup lethality: 0.489 (48.9%) — even lower
- Sedans still account for 46.6% of all deaths despite shrinking market share
- ESC mandate (2012) effect on model-year deaths: only -1.9% (2008-2011 vs 2013-2016)

## Key Data Points
- 2025 traffic fatalities: 36,640 (6.7% drop from 2024) — NHTSA
- Jan-Sept 2025: 27,365 fatalities (6.4% decline) — NHTSA
- Jan-June 2025: 17,140 (8.2% decline, largest since 2008) — NHTSA
- Rate: 1.06 per 100M VMT, lowest since 2014 — NHTSA
- 2024 FARS final: ~39,300 (inferred from 6.7% decline)
- Older cars (18+ years) 71% more likely to result in fatal crash — Consumer Reports / NHTSA study
- IIHS: improved vehicle designs reduce driver death rates by >1/3 in 3 years
- IIHS: 9 models achieved zero death rate in latest analysis
- IIHS: vehicles with good side-impact ratings have 70% lower driver death risk in left-side crashes

## Kill Test
- Novel? YES — cross-referencing 2025 NHTSA national trend data with FARS per-model-year death distribution. Nobody has computed the 62.3% old-vehicle death share or connected fleet attrition to the national decline.
- Newsworthy? YES — the 2025 decline is current news (NHTSA releases), and our data offers a contrarian explanation.
- Shareable? "62% of crash deaths happen in cars old enough to vote" is a hook.

## Strongest Counterargument
The decline could be driven by behavioral normalization (post-COVID commute patterns stabilizing, lower risk-taking), not fleet composition. VMT may have grown slower than GDP, reducing exposure. The NHTSA data doesn't isolate fleet age as a variable — the correlation could be confounded by economic factors (people driving newer cars in 2025 are wealthier, live in safer areas, etc.). Our 62.3% figure is from 2014-2023 FARS, not 2025 data — the actual 2025 fleet age composition may differ.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers 2014-2023 crash years; we're extrapolating to explain a 2025 trend
- "Deaths" in model-year breakdown counts deaths per vehicle model year, not crash year
- Fleet attrition rates are estimates based on average vehicle lifespan (~12 years)
- The ESC mandate figure (-1.9%) likely understates ESC impact because many vehicles adopted ESC voluntarily before the mandate
- Cannot isolate fleet age from confounding variables (driver demographics, geography, speed) in national data
