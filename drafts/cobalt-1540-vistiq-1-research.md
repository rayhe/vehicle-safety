# Research: From 1,540 Dead to 1 Saved — GM's Decade-Long Safety Reckoning

## Angle
GM's Speak Up For Safety (SUFS) program, born from the ignition switch scandal that FARS data associates with 1,540 Cobalt deaths, just caught the Cadillac Vistiq power-folding seat defect before anyone died. The cost of institutional learning, measured in bodies.

## Why This Is Novel
- Nobody has connected the FARS Cobalt death count (1,540) to the SUFS program's first major public success (Vistiq catch)
- The NHTSA official ignition switch death toll is 124. FARS says 1,540 Cobalt occupants died total. The gap between "attributed" and "actual" is 12.4x.
- Original calculation: at the Palisade defect incidence rate (1 death in ~60,000 vehicles), 14,540 Vistiqs might have produced ~0.24 fatalities statistically. SUFS potentially prevented that.
- The body count ratio: 1,540 died to create the program that saved ~1.

## Primary Sources
1. **FARS data** — Chevrolet Cobalt: 1,540 deaths, rate 5.10/100M VMT, 22.4% impairment, deaths-per-crash 0.808. Among the least survivable sedans in the dataset.
2. **NHTSA recall** — Cadillac Vistiq recall (N262555780), 14,540 vehicles, 100% defect rate. Power-folding third-row seats don't reverse when encountering obstruction.
3. **NHTSA/Congressional record** — GM ignition switch: 30M vehicles recalled, 124 deaths, 275 injuries officially attributed. SUFS created 2014 as part of reform package.
4. **Jalopnik (Jul 7, 2026)** — Confirms Vistiq engineer used SUFS program. GM found defect after "recall by different vehicle manufacturer" (Hyundai Palisade).
5. **Hyundai Palisade context** — 2-year-old Lucia Ayala crushed by power-folding seat March 7, 2026, Akron, OH. Hyundai recalled 60,000+ Palisades.

## FARS Data Points
- Cobalt: 1,540 deaths, 1,907 crashes, fleet 262,500, rate 5.10, impairment 22.4%
- Cobalt deaths-per-crash: 0.808 (5th worst among all vehicles with 200+ deaths)
- Cobalt peak model year: 2006 (328 deaths, 21.3% of total) — the exact year ignition switch failures peaked
- Cobalt was sold 2004-2010. GM knew about the switch defect by at least 2005. Recall didn't happen until 2014.
- For comparison: Toyota Camry (same class/era) — rate 2.03, deaths 6,328 but with 10x the fleet

## Key Timeline
- 2004: Cobalt launched
- 2005: GM engineers first documented ignition switch issue internally
- 2006: Peak Cobalt fatality year (328 deaths from MY 2006)
- 2010: Cobalt discontinued
- 2014: Ignition switch recall (30M vehicles). 124 deaths, 275 injuries attributed. $900M DOJ settlement. SUFS program created.
- 2014-2026: SUFS operates. GM trains every employee annually on the ignition switch scandal.
- March 7, 2026: Lucia Ayala (2) killed by Hyundai Palisade power-folding seat
- Late March 2026: GM engineer uses SUFS to report Vistiq has same defect
- July 1, 2026: GM files recall with NHTSA for all 14,540 Vistiqs
- July 7, 2026: Recall made public
