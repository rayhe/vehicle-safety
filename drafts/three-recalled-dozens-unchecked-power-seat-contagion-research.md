# Research: Power-Folding Seat Contagion — Three Recalled, Dozens Unchecked

## Angle
After one child died in a Hyundai Palisade power-folding seat (March 7, 2026), three automakers have recalled vehicles with similar defects. But there is no federal standard requiring anti-pinch protection for power-folding seats — and dozens of three-row SUVs with this feature have NOT been tested or recalled. The industry is reacting one by one, after a death, instead of proactively checking.

## Sources

### 1. Hyundai Palisade Recall (26V160000)
- **Date:** March 2026
- **Vehicles:** 68,500+ 2026 Palisade and Palisade Hybrid (Limited and Calligraphy trims)
- **Defect:** Power-folding 2nd and 3rd row seats may fail to detect contact with occupant or object during powered operation
- **Trigger:** Death of 2-year-old Lucia Ayala, Akron, OH, March 7, 2026. Pinned by third-row seatback in parking lot of Restaurant Depot. Suffocated to death.
- **Fix:** OTA software update (interim); permanent recall remedy TBD
- **Also linked:** 1 additional injury
- Source: NHTSA, NY Post, Reuters, Carscoops

### 2. Kia Telluride Hybrid Recall (26V173000)
- **Date:** March 6, 2026
- **Vehicles:** 568 2027 Kia Telluride Hybrid (SX Prestige and X-Line SX Prestige with Executive Package)
- **Defect:** Same as Palisade — "do not contain sufficient anti-pinch protection for occupants during activation of certain seat adjustment features"
- **Note:** Only higher-trim hybrids with Executive Package. Lower trims have manual folding seats (not affected)
- **Platform:** Shares Hyundai-Kia N3 platform with Palisade
- Source: NHTSA, Carscoops, CarBuzz

### 3. Cadillac Vistiq Recall (N262555780)
- **Date:** July 1, 2026 (letter to NHTSA)
- **Vehicles:** 14,540 2026-2027 Cadillac Vistiq (100% defective)
- **Defect:** Third-row seatback stops but does NOT auto-reverse when encountering obstruction
- **Discovery:** GM engineer tested after "recall by a different vehicle manufacturer" (Hyundai). Submitted findings through GM's Speak Up For Safety (SUFS) program on March 31, 2026
- **Test:** 33-40 pound box on seat → seatback stopped but didn't reverse → box couldn't be removed without manually reversing seat
- **Prior complaints:** 6 incidents (May 2025 - June 2026), no injuries
- **Fix:** Replace third-row folding seat module (not yet available). Interim: disable power-folding feature at dealership. Stop-delivery ordered June 8 for 2027 models.
- **Owner notification:** August 3, 2026
- Source: NHTSA, Jalopnik, The Drive, CarBuzz, Electrek

## Regulatory Gap
- **FMVSS 207** covers "Seating Systems" — structural strength requirements, not automated folding mechanism safety
- There is NO federal standard requiring anti-pinch protection for power-folding seats
- No FMVSS addresses automated seat movement entrapment risk
- The EU has no equivalent standard either (GSR 2019/2144 covers AEB, lane keeping, etc. — not seat mechanisms)
- This is entirely unregulated territory
