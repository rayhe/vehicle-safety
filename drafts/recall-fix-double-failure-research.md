# Research Notes: The Recall Fix Double Failure

## Angle
Two failures in the recall system exposed in the same week: most recalled vehicles never get fixed, and some that DO get "fixed" still have the defect. The recall system fails twice.

## News Hook (Week of July 7, 2026)
1. **Ram Truck Rollaway (1M+ vehicles):** NHTSA reopening investigation into whether previous recall fix was adequate. 20 complaints of continued rollaway after repair, including injuries and crashes.
2. **Kia Telluride Seat Fire (463K vehicles):** Second recall for the same defect. First fix (new bracket + switch replacement) failed — 7 localized fires and 11 seat motor melting incidents AFTER first remedy. New fix: electronic fuse to cut power if motor overheats.

## NHTSA Recall Completion Data
- **Only 61% of recalled vehicles are fixed by the fifth quarter** (15+ months after recall, 2010-2022 data)
- Lowest annual completion rate: **52%** (2015)
- Highest: **69%** (2022)
- Vehicle age is the #1 predictor — adding 5 years of age drops completion from 80% to below 60%
- Source: NHTSA Report to Congress, Recall Completion Rates (January 2025 update)

## FARS Data — Ram & Kia
- **Dodge Ram:** 4,407 deaths, rate 0.78/100M VMT, 4,200,000 fleet, 56,700M VMT
- **Ram 1500:** 714 deaths, 2,095 crashes, deaths/crash ratio 0.341 (very survivable for occupants)
- **Ram 2500:** 153 deaths, 748 crashes, deaths/crash ratio 0.205 (lowest in entire dataset — the people dying are in OTHER vehicles)
- **Kia Telluride:** 31 deaths, rate 0.04 (very low — one of safest vehicles in FARS)

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulation: vehicles with the LOWEST deaths-per-crash ratios (Ram 2500 at 0.205, Ram 1500 at 0.341) are the ones most likely to kill people in OTHER vehicles. When these trucks roll away unmanned, they become 5,000-7,000 lb uncontrolled projectiles — and the FARS data shows that when Rams are in fatal crashes, it's overwhelmingly the other party who dies. A rollaway Ram is worse than a rollaway Civic because of what the weight class data tells us about who survives these collisions.

## Key Stat
~39% of recalled vehicles are NEVER fixed. For the 61% that are — an unknown percentage have fixes that fail. We're running a system where the best case is 6-in-10 vehicles get repaired, and some of those repairs don't hold.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely surprising after 463 articles? YES — while we've covered individual recall failures (Ram rollaway, Telluride fire), we've never done the SYSTEMIC angle: the recall fix failure rate applied to the death data. The original contribution is calculating what unfixed + poorly-fixed recalls mean in terms of bodies, using the deaths-per-crash asymmetry to show that weight class makes rollaway recalls especially deadly.
