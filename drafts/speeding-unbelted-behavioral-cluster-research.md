# Research: Speeding + Unbelted — The Behavioral Cluster That Kills

## Angle
50% of speeding drivers in fatal crashes were unrestrained (vs 21% non-speeding). These aren't independent behaviors — they're a behavioral cluster. The person who won't slow down is the same person who won't buckle up. NHTSA treats them as separate enforcement campaigns. The data says they're one problem.

## Primary Sources
1. **NHTSA FARS 2024 Speeding Fact Sheet** (crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov): 50% of speeding passenger vehicle drivers in fatal crashes were unrestrained, vs 21% non-speeding. 11,288 speeding-related fatalities (29% of all 39,254). 37% of motorcycle riders speeding vs 22% car drivers.
2. **NSC Injury Facts (FARS 2024 analysis)**: Speeding-alcohol overlap: 33% of speeding drivers under 21 also alcohol-impaired (BAC 0.08+), 43%+ for ages 21-54. State variation: Vermont 54% speeding-related fatalities, Florida 11% — 5x difference.
3. **NHTSA 2024 final FARS**: 39,254 total deaths, rate 1.19/100M VMT.
4. **NHTSA Q1 2026 early estimates**: Fatality rate 0.99/100M VMT — second-lowest Q1 in history. Speeding deaths DOWN 11% to 10,035.

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulation of speeding × restraint use reveals a 2.4:1 ratio (50% vs 21%). This behavioral cluster suggests enforcement should target the overlap, not each behavior separately. "Click It or Ticket" and "Speeding Slows You Down" run as separate campaigns. The data says the target audience is the same person.

## Counterargument
Correlation ≠ causation. Speed and non-use of seatbelts might both correlate with a third factor (e.g., alcohol impairment, age, rural driving). FARS data doesn't control for these confounders. Also, the causal direction is ambiguous — does risk-tolerance cause both, or does speeding at higher speeds reduce seatbelt wearing?

## Limitations
- FARS captures only fatal crashes. The behavioral cluster might look different in injury crashes.
- State variation (VT 54% vs FL 11%) may reflect enforcement patterns, speed limits, road types, and weather, not just driver behavior.
- "Speeding-related" is officer judgment, not always instrumented measurement.
