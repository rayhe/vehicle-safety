# Research: The DUI Body Count Is Full of Camrys

## Angle
The vehicles with the most impaired-driver fatal crashes in America are not Mustangs, Corvettes, or Challengers. They're Silverados, F-150s, Accords, Camrys, and Civics. The top 5 "DUI vehicles" by raw body count are the 5 most boring, ubiquitous vehicles on American roads. Meanwhile, the Corvette — with a 26.2% impairment rate — produced only 300 impaired fatal crashes in a decade. Volume kills. Rate is a footnote.

## Kill Test
- Is this genuinely surprising? YES — the public perception of "DUI vehicles" is muscle cars and sports cars. FARS data says the impaired-driving body count belongs to commuter cars and trucks.
- Is this novel after 177 articles? YES — we've covered vehicle-specific impairment (Corvette, Charger, Park Avenue, Infiniti G), the luxury paradox, impairment-adjusted rates, and the drug testing blind spot. But we've never ranked vehicles by RAW impaired fatal crashes — the absolute body count.
- The closest existing article is "wrong-list-rate-vs-volume" which covers rate vs volume for DEATH RATES, not IMPAIRMENT. This is the impairment-specific version.

**NOT on this list:** Mustang (1,020), Corvette (300), Challenger (458), Charger (984), BMW 3 Series (435)

### The Paradox
- Corvette impairment RATE: 26.2% (highest among sports cars)
- Corvette impaired fatal drivers: 300
- F-150 impairment RATE: 18.9% (below national average)
- F-150 impaired fatal drivers: 4,011
- The F-150 produces 13x more impaired fatalities than the Corvette despite a LOWER impairment rate

### Combined Top 5
- 16,839 impaired fatal crashes from just 5 vehicle models
- That's ~1,684 per year — 4.6 impaired fatal crashes PER DAY in these 5 vehicles alone
- None of these 5 vehicles has an impairment rate above the class average

### National Average: 20% of all fatal crashes involve impairment
- Sports cars: 22.5% (highest class)
- But sports cars produce only ~3,177 impaired fatal crashes total
- Sedans: 20.4% rate, but produce ~40,327 impaired fatal crashes
- Pickups: 20.1% rate, produce ~22,375 impaired fatal crashes
