## News Hook
WHO released new global road safety data on July 20, 2026:
- Global road deaths fell 21% between 2011 and 2025
- 1.16 million deaths globally in 2025 (down from ~1.47M in 2011)
- WHO European Region: 36% decline
- Western Pacific: 15% decline
- South-East Asia: 2% decline
- **The Americas: "no change in rates"**

## Original Calculation: The SDG Gap
SDG target: 50% reduction from 2021 levels by 2030
US 2021 baseline: 42,939 deaths
US 2030 target: 21,470 deaths
Current trajectory (2023): 40,990
Gap: ~19,520 deaths/year the US needs to eliminate in 3.5 years

From FARS (annual averages):
- Top 10 vehicles by annual deaths: Silverado (959), F-150 (919), Accord (710), Civic (655), Camry (633), Corolla (495), Altima (479), RAM (441), Explorer (380), Impala (377) = 6,048/year
- Eliminating the top 10 deadliest vehicles entirely closes only 31% of the SDG gap
- The entire sedan class (89,127 deaths / 10yr = 8,913/yr) closes only 46%
- You'd need to eliminate EVERY SEDAN AND EVERY PICKUP from American roads to close the gap
