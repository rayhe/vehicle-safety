# Research: The Q2 2025 Cliff — 1,052 Fewer Deaths in Three Months

## Angle
NHTSA's 2025 annual CrashStats report (DOT HS 813 800, April 2026) reveals a striking quarterly anomaly: Q2 2025 saw 9,055 traffic deaths, a 10.4% decline from Q2 2024's 10,107. That's 1,052 fewer deaths in a single quarter — and nearly double the decline rate of any surrounding quarter. The monthly acceleration is remarkable: April -8.5%, May -11%, June -12%. Something changed in spring 2025 and the data can't tell us what.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. Most coverage of the 2025 data focuses on the annual total (36,640, -6.7%). Nobody has drilled into the quarterly pattern. The Q2 anomaly is hiding in plain sight. This is a genuine data discovery that demands investigation.

## Hypotheses for the Q2 2025 Anomaly

1. **Fleet turnover acceleration**: 2018+ model vehicles with standard AEB and ESC reaching critical mass. Average age of US vehicles is ~12.6 years. By spring 2025, even the oldest vehicles being actively driven are increasingly post-ESC-mandate (2012+) and post-voluntary-AEB-commitment (2016+).

2. **Automated enforcement expansion**: Multiple cities expanded speed cameras and red-light cameras in 2024-2025. NYC's 24/7 speed cameras (since 2022) produced measurable results.

3. **Post-COVID behavioral normalization**: The COVID driving surge (reckless behavior on empty roads) peaked in 2021 at 43,230 deaths. The "behavioral hangover" may have finally burned off by spring 2025.

4. **Seasonal anomaly / base effect**: Q2 2024 itself may have been unusually high, making Q2 2025 look better by comparison. But Q2 2024 (10,107) was already down from Q2 2023 (10,386), so it's not a base effect.

5. **Vehicle safety tech compounding**: ESC + AEB + lane departure warning + blind spot monitoring all hitting critical fleet penetration simultaneously.

## Novel Contribution
Original finding: Identifying and quantifying the Q2 2025 anomaly in the CrashStats data. The quarterly decline pattern (5.3%, 10.4%, 4.3%, 6.5%) has not been analyzed in any published coverage. The June 2025 monthly decline of 12% was the steepest of any month in the year. This is a genuine data discovery.

## Limitations
- These are NHTSA projections, not final FARS counts. Final 2025 data won't be available until ~2027.
- State-level estimates use regional inflation rates, so small-state numbers have wider confidence intervals.
- Correlation ≠ causation: we can't definitively attribute the Q2 decline to any single factor.
- VMT data is from FHWA traffic volume trends, which estimates total miles; the composition of that driving (urban vs rural, night vs day) may have shifted.

## Strongest Counterargument
The Q2 anomaly may simply be regression to the mean. Q2 is traditionally the second-deadliest quarter (after Q3), and the 2024 Q2 figure (10,107) was itself slightly elevated relative to the declining trend. A -10.4% decline from a slightly high baseline could be statistical noise rather than a structural shift. The surrounding quarters' more modest declines (4-6%) may reflect the true trend, and Q2 2025 may be an overcorrection.
