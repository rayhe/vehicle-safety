# Research: CDL License System as Safety Defect

## Angle
The CDL licensing system — supposed to be the first safety feature of every commercial vehicle — is itself broken. Nearly 1 in 4 large truck drivers involved in fatal crashes don't have a valid CDL. 202,345 CDL holders are currently in "prohibited" status. The I-95 bus crash that killed the Doncev family is the latest case study in a systemic failure.

## Primary Sources

### 1. FMCSA Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse (as of January 2, 2026)
- **328,431** total CDL/CLP holders with at least one violation
- **202,345** currently in "prohibited" status (cannot legally drive CMV)
- **159,226** have not started Return-to-Duty process AT ALL
- Clearinghouse-II rule took effect November 18, 2024 (CDLs downgraded to standard passenger license)
- Source: FreightWaves reporting on publicly available FMCSA Clearinghouse data

### 2. FARS Data on CDL Compliance in Fatal Crashes (2018-2020)
- 4,778 large truck drivers in fatal crashes
- **1,050 (22%) had NO CDL at all**
- **97 (2%) had invalid CDL** (expired, suspended, disqualified, cancelled, revoked)
- **Total: 1,147 of 4,778 (24%) had no valid CDL**
- Source: NHTSA FARS via FMCSA Large Truck and Bus Crash Facts, Table 25

### 3. FMCSA MCMIS Crash Data (2024)
- **170,745 CMV-related crashes**
- **4,822 fatalities** in 2024
- Up from 4,807 in 2023 (which was down from 5,417 in 2022)
- Source: FMCSA Motor Carrier Management Information System

## Novel Contribution (Original Calculation)
Cross-referencing FARS Table 25 (CDL compliance in fatal crashes) with Clearinghouse data:
- If 24% of truck drivers in fatal crashes don't have valid CDLs, and there are 4,822 CMV fatalities/year, that implies roughly **1,157 people die annually** in crashes where the truck driver shouldn't have been driving at all
- 202,345 prohibited drivers / ~3 million long-haul CDL holders ≈ **6.7% of the CDL workforce is currently prohibited**
- With 159,226 of those having done NOTHING about their prohibition, the question is: how many are still driving?

## Strongest Counterargument
The Clearinghouse-II rule (Nov 2024) is specifically designed to address this — it mandates CDL downgrade for prohibited drivers. And FMCSA enforcement is catching fraud (8 suspensions in Sep 2025, multiple prosecutions). The system is broken, but it's being fixed. Counter-counter: the I-95 crash happened 18 months AFTER these enforcement actions began.
