# Research: NHTSA Vestigial Vehicle Safety Regulations RFC

## Angle
NHTSA published an RFC (Docket No. NHTSA-2026-0133, Federal Register Jan 23, 2026) asking the public to identify Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) to remove. The agency compared its own safety regulations to "vestigial organs" — like the appendix or wisdom teeth — that "no longer serve a functional role and, in some cases, can cause harm."

The paradox: these same standards prevented 860,000+ deaths between 1968-2019. The agency that published those life-saving numbers is now running a process to gut them, under a 10:1 repeal-to-new-rule mandate from Executive Order 14192.

## Key Data Points

### FMVSS Effectiveness (NHTSA's own research, published 2024)
- **860,000+ deaths prevented** (1968-2019)
- **49 million nonfatal injuries prevented**
- **65 million vehicles** saved from damage
- **$17.3 trillion** in societal benefits (2019 dollars) vs ~$1 trillion in costs → **17:1 return on investment**
- In 2019 alone: ~40,000 deaths prevented, 1.9M injuries prevented, 3.8M vehicle damage events prevented
- Key technologies: seat belts, airbags, ESC, improved brakes

### The Deregulatory Framework
- **Executive Order 14192** ("Unleashing Prosperity through Deregulation") — mandates **10 regulations repealed for every 1 new one**
- **EO 14219** — "Department of Government Efficiency" deregulatory agenda
- **April 3, 2025** — DOT issued initial RFI (Request for Information)
- **May 30, 2025** — NHTSA initiated 16 deregulatory actions (15 NPRMs + 1 withdrawal), including removing requirements from FMVSS No. 207 (Seating Systems) and FMVSS No. 210 (Seat Belt Assembly Anchorages)
- **January 23, 2026** — RFC on "Vestigial Vehicle Safety Regulations" published (Docket NHTSA-2026-0133)
- Contact: Shashi Kuppa, Office of Crashworthiness (shashi.kuppa@dot.gov, 202-366-1810)
- Administrator who signed: Jonathan Morrison

### The RFC Language
The RFC explicitly asks commenters to identify standards that:
1. Require specific hardware/design elements superseded by more effective modern technology
2. Contain testing procedures incompatible with or unnecessarily restrictive for innovative vehicle designs (e.g., vehicles without traditional manual controls)
3. Impose compliance burdens that do not result in a measurable increase in real-world safety
4. Act as a barrier to entry for new safety technologies by failing to be technology-neutral

### Consumer Reports Response
- CR noted that EO 14192's 10:1 ratio creates pressure to target rules with high compliance costs **regardless of safety value**
- Warned against conflating "updating a standard to better reflect technology" (pro-safety) with "removing a standard to reduce compliance costs" (deregulatory)
- Cited FMVSS No. 302 (flammability) as genuinely outdated — uses a 1971 open-flame test; fewer than 10% of vehicle fires start from open flames
- Peer-reviewed May 2024 study found harmful flame retardants in interiors of all 101 vehicles tested across 22 brands — manufacturers add toxic chemicals to pass an obsolete test
- CR also supported updating tire test procedures (FMVSS 109, 119, 139) — plunger-based tire strength test designed for bias tires, not radials

### Context
- NHTSA funding expires September 30, 2026 — Congress must reauthorize
- NHTSA already cut staff (DOGE-driven, reported Feb 2026, AP/Washington Post)
- ~40,000 annual US traffic deaths
- AEB mandate finalized April 2024 (FMVSS 127) would save 360 lives/year — but under 10:1 ratio, adding it means removing 10 other standards

## Self-Critique Gate
**Proposed angle:** The safety agency that proved its own regulations saved 860,000 lives is now required to identify 10 of those regulations to kill for every new one it adds.

**Challenge:** Is this genuinely surprising? Yes — the 10:1 ratio math is the novel contribution. Nobody is doing the arithmetic: the AEB rule alone triggers a need to remove 10 other standards. The "vestigial organ" metaphor from the agency itself is darkly absurd. And the FMVSS 302 example shows how "vestigial" can mean both "genuinely outdated" AND "still saving lives" depending on which standard you're looking at.

**Verdict:** PROCEED. This is a policy investigation with real data, a genuine paradox, and actionable information for readers.
