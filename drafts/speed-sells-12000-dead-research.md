# Research: Speed Sells — The $5 Billion Ad Machine Behind 12,000 Deaths

## Angle
The auto industry spends roughly $5 billion per year on advertising that glorifies speed, while speed-related crashes kill 12,000+ Americans annually. The same companies that compete for IIHS TOP SAFETY PICK awards simultaneously run ads encouraging the single most dangerous driving behavior. The US has essentially zero regulation on this, unlike the EU and other countries. NHTSA's entire anti-speeding campaign budget is $9.6M — the industry outspends the government's counter-campaign by ~540:1.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy after 250+ articles? YES. This is not a FARS data dump — it's an institutional paradox. The novel contribution is the spend-ratio calculation: $5.2B glorifying speed vs. $9.6M fighting it. No one has run this number. The story connects automotive advertising to FARS outcomes in a way that hasn't been done on this site.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: IIHS Content Analysis of Car Advertising (2024)
- Analyzed TV car ads from 2018-2022
- 43% of vehicle ads emphasized speed, performance, or traction
- Performance as PRIMARY theme rose from 14% (2018) to 19% (2022)
- Only 3% of ads emphasized safety
- Follow-up to original 1998 IIHS study that found 50% of ads had performance themes (17% primary)
- 2017 Consumer Reports analysis: 40% performance themes
- The trend has been stable for 25+ years
- Published in IIHS research, cited by Autoblog, Streetsblog, Repairer Driven News, Automotive World
- IIHS refs: https://www.iihs.org/topics/bibliography/ref/1270 (1998 study)

### Source 2: NHTSA Speed-Related Fatality Data (FARS)
- Speed-related crashes = "almost one-third" of all traffic fatalities (NHTSA)
- 2021: 14-year high for speed-related fatalities (NHTSA speeding page)
- 11,288 speed-related deaths in 2024 (per Repairer Driven News citing IIHS/NHTSA)
- Over 300,000 people injured annually in speed-related crashes (IIHS VP)
- 2024 total traffic fatalities: 39,345 (NHTSA estimate)
- 2025 total: 36,640 (NHTSA estimate, 6.7% drop)
- Speed share has been persistently ~29-30% of all fatalities for decades
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/research-data/fatality-analysis-reporting-system-fars

### Source 3: US Automotive Advertising Spend Data
- US automotive advertising market: ~$12B in 2024 (Statista)
- Dealer advertising: $8.9B in 2023 (NADA)
- Top advertisers: Toyota ($661.3M), Chevrolet ($603M), Ford ($314.6M)
- Digital = 74.7% of total ad spend
- National TV auto spending: ~$2B/year (MediaPost)

### Source 4: NHTSA Anti-Speeding Campaign Budget
- NHTSA speeding campaign: $9.6M in media ads (per NHTSA campaign announcement)
- Target demographic: drivers 18-44
- This is the ENTIRE federal counter-campaign against speeding

### Source 5: US Regulatory Landscape (from IIHS VP commentary, Automotive World)
- FTC "truth-in-advertising" laws: no mention of unsafe driving promotion
- FCC "public interest" requirement: no mention of unsafe driving
- ViacomCBS: prohibits "risky behavior portrayed positively" (undefined)
- ABC: "safe and lawful driving practices should be depicted" — mentions seatbelts, NOT speed
- NBCUniversal: "portray compliance with standard safety precautions" — mentions seatbelts, NOT speed
- EU: has stricter advertising regulations on depicting dangerous driving
- US is essentially self-regulated with no enforcement on speed glorification
- Federal law prohibits tobacco ads on electronic media but not speed-glorifying car ads
- Voluntary restrictions exist on alcohol ads but not on speed promotion

### Source 6: 2024 FARS Final Data Release (May 2026)
- NHTSA released final 2024 FARS data alongside 2025 estimates
- 2025: 36,640 total fatalities, 6.7% drop from 2024
- Fatality rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT — second-lowest in US history
- Speed still ~29% = ~10,600-11,000 speed deaths in 2025

## Novel Contribution: The Speed-Ad-to-Death Ratio

### Calculation
- US automotive national ad spend: ~$12B/year
- 43% features speed/performance themes (IIHS 2018-2022 content analysis)
- Speed-themed ad spend: ~$5.2B/year
- Speed-related fatalities: ~12,000/year (using 2022-2024 average)
- **Cost per speed death in ad spend glorifying speed: ~$430,000**
- **NHTSA anti-speeding budget: $9.6M**
- **Ratio: industry glorifies speed at ~540x the rate government fights it**

Alternative framing:
- For every $1 NHTSA spends telling you to slow down, the auto industry spends $540 telling you to go fast
- $5.2B would fund 541 NHTSA anti-speeding campaigns
- $5.2B / 300,000 injuries = $17,333 per speed-related injury in ad glorification

### Important caveats for this calculation
- Not all "speed-themed" ads directly cause crashes — correlation ≠ causation
- IIHS content analysis captures themes, not consumer behavioral response
- The 43% figure includes traction/handling, not just raw speed
- $12B includes all auto ads, not just those the IIHS analyzed (TV-focused)
- The IIHS studied TV ads specifically; digital ads may differ
- Industry would argue ads show "controlled environments" with disclaimers

## Strongest Counterargument
Automakers would argue: (1) ads show professional drivers in controlled environments with clear disclaimers; (2) consumers understand fantasy vs. reality; (3) performance advertising drives purchase interest, not driving behavior; (4) automakers invest billions in actual safety tech (AEB, ESC, airbags) that saves lives. The rebuttal: media effects research consistently shows advertising influences behavior (documented for tobacco, alcohol, food), disclaimers don't neutralize aspirational messaging, and the 25-year trend of speed-themed advertising coincides with speed remaining stubbornly at ~30% of all fatalities despite massive safety technology improvements.
