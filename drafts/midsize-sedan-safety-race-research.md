# Research: The Midsize Sedan Safety Race

## Angle
Head-to-head FARS comparison of America's top-selling midsize sedans. The Honda Accord — the gold standard — has the highest death rate in its class. Korean entries beat Japanese rivals on every metric. But the data hides a confounding factor: fleet age.

## Novel Contribution
Cross-tabulating death rate, crash lethality, impairment rate, AND fleet age composition across 7 midsize sedans reveals that FARS per-VMT death rates heavily penalize brands with longer US histories. The Accord's 3.07 rate vs the Sonata's 1.56 is partly a function of Honda selling Accords in America since 1976 — 66.4% of Accord deaths come from pre-2010 model years that lack ESC, side curtain airbags, and modern crash structures.

## Fleet Age Composition (deaths from MY2000+)
- **Honda Accord:** 66.4% of deaths from pre-2010 model years. Peak: MY2005 (444 deaths).
- **Toyota Camry:** 55.7% pre-2010. Peak: MY2007 (426 deaths).
- **Hyundai Sonata:** Only 34.7% pre-2010. Peak: MY2011 (230 deaths).

## Impairment Control
All 7 sedans cluster tightly between 19.2-22.0% impairment. This is critical: driver behavior is NOT the confounding variable. Something else explains the 5.3x death rate gap between the Optima and the Accord.

## Lethality (deaths per crash)
- Sonata: 0.541 (lowest — crash most survivable)
- Fusion: 0.551
- Optima: 0.570
- Camry: 0.593
- Altima: 0.628
- Malibu: 0.634
- Accord: 0.644 (highest — crash least survivable)

## Strongest Counterargument
This comparison is apples-to-oranges across fleet age. A 2024 Accord is as safe as a 2024 Sonata in IIHS/NHTSA tests. The Accord's high rate reflects decades of older models still on the road. The Sonata/Optima only became competitive in the US in the 2010s, so their fleet is newer.

## Limitations
- FARS only captures fatal crashes, not injuries or property damage
- Fleet estimates use VMT extrapolations with ±15% uncertainty for lower-volume models
- Rate calculation doesn't age-adjust within models
- Lethality ratio doesn't account for crash type (single vs multi-vehicle)

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. Everyone "knows" the Accord and Camry are safe. Nobody has done a per-VMT death rate comparison of the full midsize field showing the Accord dead last. The fleet age insight is genuinely novel — it reveals how FARS data systematically penalizes established brands.
