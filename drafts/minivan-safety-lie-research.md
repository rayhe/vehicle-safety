# Research: The Minivan Safety Lie

## Angle
Minivans are the vehicle Americans buy specifically to protect their families. But within the category, there's a staggering death rate gap — the Dodge Grand Caravan kills at 2.7x the rate of the Toyota Sienna and 7x the rate of its own replacement, the Chrysler Pacifica. Combined with IIHS's finding that no minivan earns adequate rear-seat safety ratings, the "family car" category has a design problem nobody talks about.

## Kill Test
- **Genuinely newsworthy?** YES. No one publishes minivan-to-minivan FARS death rate comparisons. Parents assume all minivans are safe.
- **Novel angle on data?** YES. Cross-tabulation of death rates, lethality ratios, impairment rates, and model year trends within the van class only. Never done on the site.

## Primary Data (FARS 2014–2023)

### Death Rates (per 100M VMT)
| Vehicle | Deaths | Rate | Crashes | Lethality |
|---------|--------|------|---------|-----------|
| Dodge Grand Caravan | 1,782 | 1.33 | 3,085 | 57.8% |
| Chrysler Town & Country | 1,303 | 1.26 | 2,298 | 56.7% |
| Honda Odyssey | 864 | 0.93 | 2,028 | 42.6% |
| Toyota Sienna | 430 | 0.49 | 905 | 47.5% |
| Chrysler Pacifica | 160 | 0.19 | 327 | 48.9% |

### Key Calculations
- Grand Caravan rate (1.33) / Sienna rate (0.49) = 2.71x gap
- Grand Caravan rate (1.33) / Pacifica rate (0.19) = 7.0x gap
- Grand Caravan + Town & Country (same Chrysler platform) = 3,085 combined deaths
- Grand Caravan lethality (57.8%) = highest among minivans; when it crashes, someone dies 58% of the time
- Honda Odyssey lethality (42.6%) = lowest; it crashes more but kills less per crash
- Impairment: Grand Caravan 15.3%, Odyssey 15.4% — nearly identical, lowest in class. These aren't drunk drivers.

### Model Year Peak Deaths
- Grand Caravan peaked at 2005 MY: 192 deaths (the 4th-gen)
- Town & Country peaked at 2005 MY: 201 deaths (same platform)
- Model year 2005 Chrysler minivans: 393 deaths combined for a single model year

### Comparison to Crossover SUVs (same price bracket)
- Toyota RAV4: rate 0.19 — same as Pacifica
- Honda Pilot: rate 0.29
- Toyota Highlander: rate 0.42
- The Grand Caravan at 1.33 has a HIGHER death rate than the Toyota Highlander (0.42), Honda Pilot (0.29), and RAV4 (0.19). Families who chose the minivan "for safety" over a crossover SUV often chose a more dangerous vehicle.

## Strongest Counterargument
The Grand Caravan's high death rate partially reflects fleet age — many 2005–2010 model years still on the road during the 2014–2023 FARS window, lacking modern safety tech (AEB, lane departure, side curtain airbags in all rows). The Pacifica's low rate benefits from being newer with more advanced safety features. This isn't a perfectly controlled comparison.

## Limitations
1. FARS captures only fatal crashes. Minivan injury crashes (survivable) aren't in this data.
2. VMT estimates use household travel survey data, not actual odometer readings. ±15% uncertainty on low-volume models.
3. Grand Caravan was the *cheap* minivan — base price significantly lower than Sienna/Odyssey. Owner demographics (income, driving patterns, maintenance habits) may confound the vehicle design signal.
4. The Pacifica benefits from newer model years with better crash structures; the comparison includes vehicles from different eras.
5. "Deaths" includes all fatalities in crashes involving the vehicle — other vehicle occupants, pedestrians — not just minivan occupant deaths.
