# Research: NHTSA's 617-Day Investigation Lag

## Angle
NHTSA's Office of Defects Investigation (ODI) misses its own deadlines on 94% of safety probes. The average preliminary investigation takes 617 days — 5.1x the 120-day target. Congress is now proposing to cut NHTSA's budget by $10M+ while the agency already can't keep up. Novel calculation: the "cost of delay" in human lives, using known investigation timelines and confirmed body counts.

## Primary Sources

### 1. DOT OIG Audit (2023)
- **Finding:** NHTSA missed deadlines on 33 of 35 probes sampled (94%)
- **Preliminary investigations:** 617 days average vs 120-day goal (5.1x over)
- **Engineering analyses:** 1,001 additional days average vs 365-day goal (2.7x over)
- **Documentation:** 22 of 24 investigations missing key documentation
- **All 8 investigations sampled in 2021:** ODI didn't follow procedures for evaluating risk
- **No integrated computer system** for managing investigations
- Source: DOT Inspector General audit; AP reporting via TheTrucker.com

### 2. NHTSA Recall Statistics (2025)
- 997 vehicle and equipment recalls in 2025
- 88% "uninfluenced" — manufacturer-initiated without NHTSA involvement
- 12% "influenced" — followed NHTSA investigation
- Even influenced recalls were NOT ordered by the agency
- Source: Sidley Austin legal analysis of NHTSA recall order data

### 3. FY26 Budget Cut (2026)
- House THUD appropriations bill would cut NHTSA operations/research by $10M+
- Would redirect $78M from Bipartisan Infrastructure Law safety programs
- Consumer Reports coalition urging Congress to reject cuts
- ODI budget previously ~$31.3M (Obama-era data; current figure likely higher but being cut)
- Source: Consumer Reports advocacy, House Appropriations

### 5. FARS Context
- 36,640 traffic deaths in 2025 (6-year low, 15-quarter decline)

- NHTSA overseeing ~280 million registered vehicles

## Novel Calculations

### Investigation Lag Surplus
- Target total investigation time: 120 + 365 = 485 days (1.33 years)
- Actual average total: 617 + 1,001 = 1,618 days (4.43 years)
- **Excess per investigation: 1,133 days (3.1 years)**
- 94% of probes exceed targets

### Vehicles Per Investigator
- ~280 million registered vehicles in the US
- ODI staffing: ~60 investigators (various estimates)
- **That's ~4.7 million vehicles per investigator**

### Cost of Delay (Conservative)
- GM ignition switch: 124 deaths over ~7 year lag → ~18 deaths/year during delay
- If recall had been issued within 120-day target: ~6 deaths instead of 124
- **Delay cost: ~118 preventable deaths in one case alone**

### Budget Per Vehicle
- If ODI budget is ~$35M and oversees 280M vehicles:
- **~$0.125 per vehicle per year** for defect investigation
- The price of a gumball to find lethal defects in your car
