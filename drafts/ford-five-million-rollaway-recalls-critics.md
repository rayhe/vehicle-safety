## 3. ⚖️ Ethics Reviewer — Moral Reasoning, Positions
**Score: 9.0/10**

The article takes a clear editorial position (Ford has a systemic park/rollaway problem) supported by the recall record. It does not moralize or preach. The Farley/quality-improvement line is sharp but fair — it lets Ford's own PR claims collide with the recall record.

The limitations paragraph is genuinely honest: "A rollaway recall isn't the same as a rollaway fatality" and "FARS doesn't isolate rollaway crashes as a cause code, so we can't quantify exactly how many" — this is good intellectual honesty. The article doesn't claim the 13,000+ deaths are ALL from rollaways; it correctly frames them as the total FARS deaths for the recalled model fleet.

The disclaimer appropriately notes some vehicles may appear in multiple campaigns.

## 6. 🔬 Research Rigor — Novel Contribution, Limitations, Counterarguments
**Score: 9.0/10**

**Novel contribution:** Yes. The cumulative recall total across campaigns is original synthesis. Each individual recall was news; nobody tallied the running total across mechanisms. The observation that three different park mechanisms (bushing, software module, transmission defect) across three different drivetrains (ICE, hybrid, BEV) all produce the same failure mode is a genuinely novel pattern.

**Limitations:** Explicitly stated. FARS can't isolate rollaway-specific crashes. The 13,000+ figure is total deaths for recalled models, not rollaway deaths. Some vehicles may be double-counted across campaigns. These are honest, specific limitations, not boilerplate hedging.

**Strongest counterargument:** Implied but could be stronger. The article notes "a rollaway recall isn't the same as a rollaway fatality" but doesn't explicitly state the strongest countercase: that recalls demonstrate a manufacturer being responsive and that Ford's proactive OTA fix approach is actually best practice. This is a minor gap.

**Verifiability:** All NHTSA recall numbers are traceable. FARS data is cited with proper date range. Reuters is named for the news hook.
