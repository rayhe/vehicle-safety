# Research: Volvo Moose Dummy / No US Animal Crash Test

## Angle
Volvo has been crash-testing for large animal impacts since the 1980s with a 794-pound moose dummy. In July 2026, a USA Today investigation confirmed that Ford does NOT test for animal impacts, GM declined to comment, and Stellantis gave a non-answer. NHTSA confirmed it has NO safety standards for large animal crashes. This is despite 2.1 million animal-vehicle collisions per year in the US — killing ~440 people, injuring 59,000, and causing $10B+ in damage annually.

## Kill Test
- **Is this genuinely surprising after 500+ articles?** YES. Zero existing animal/wildlife/deer crash stories on the site. Complete gap.
- **Is there a novel finding?** Yes: NHTSA admits 204 fatal animal crashes in 2024 out of 36,297 total (0.5% of fatal crashes). But total animal-vehicle collisions are 2.1M/year — roughly 30% of all ~6.7M crashes. The disconnect between fatal crash share (tiny) and total crash share (enormous) explains why NHTSA doesn't test for it. Their mandate is fatality prevention, not total crash reduction.
- **Primary sources:** 3+ (NHTSA statement, NPS estimate, State Farm/IIHS data, USA Today investigation, Volvo engineers on record, UW Current Biology study)

## Sources
1. **NHTSA statement** (via USA Today, Jul 17 2026): "NHTSA does not have any applicable safety standards for large animal crashes." 204 of 36,297 fatal crashes in 2024 listed collision with a live animal.
2. **National Park Service estimate**: 2M collisions/year, ~440 deaths, 59,000 injuries, $10B+ losses.
3. **State Farm / IIHS data** (MoneyGeek, May 2026): 1.7M insurance claims Jul 2024-Jun 2025, down 5.6%. 1-in-139 odds of hitting animal. 1.1M deer claims (65% of all). November highest month.
4. **University of Washington study** (Current Biology, Nov 2022): Permanent DST would prevent 36,550 deer deaths, 33 human deaths, 2,054 injuries, $1.19B costs annually. Collisions spike 16% when DST ends.
5. **Ford Motor Co.** (via USA Today): Ford spokesman Dan Barbossa confirmed "the automaker does not do crash testing for large animal impacts."
6. **GM** (via USA Today): Spokesman Kevin Kelly declined to comment.
7. **Stellantis** (via USA Today): Non-answer about "real-world vehicle safety" without confirming or denying testing.
8. **Volvo engineers** (Thomas Broberg, Lotta Jakobsson — via USA Today): Moose dummy is 360 kg, 116 rubber discs + steel, 3rd generation. Testing since 1980s. Large animal detection standard across lineup since 2017.
9. **Michigan Traffic Crash Facts**: 58,324 deer crashes in Michigan alone in 2024, 1,816 injuries, 14 deaths.
10. **Pew Charitable Trusts**: 1-2M crashes/year, 200 deaths, 26,000 injuries, $8B+ costs. Animal crashes = ~20% of all reported collisions in rural states.

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulation: NHTSA's federal crash test suite covers frontal impacts, side impacts, rollover resistance, and pedestrian protection. ZERO animal impact tests. Yet animal strikes are the #1 single-vehicle collision type by total count (2.1M/year), outpacing every other crash category by volume. NHTSA's response — "we don't have safety standards for this" — means the test suite was designed around what kills you, not what hits you. Volvo fills the gap because Sweden has 6,000 moose strikes/year and the government made it their problem. America has 350× more deer strikes and made it nobody's problem.
