# Research: Unfixed Vehicle Recall Epidemic

## Kill Test
- **Newsworthy?** YES — 58.1M vehicles with open recalls (1 in 5), 16% increase in 2 years, most people don't know
- **Novel angle?** The completion rate collapse by vehicle age (83% new → 15% for 10+ years) combined with the fact that used car dealers have NO legal obligation to disclose open recalls. The system is designed to fail.
- **Data-driven?** Multiple primary sources (CARFAX, NHTSA, CMT)

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: CARFAX 2025 Unfixed Recall Report (Jan 22, 2025)
- 58.1 million vehicles on U.S. roads have at least one open recall (1 in 5)
- 16% increase in two years
- 14 million have TWO or more open recalls
- 5.7 million still carry defective Takata airbags (dropped 750K in past year)
- State breakdown:
  - California: 6.2 million vehicles with open recalls; Los Angeles alone: 3 million+
  - Texas: 1.6 million with multiple open recalls
  - Florida: 901,000 with multiple open recalls
- All recall repairs are FREE to vehicle owners
- URL: carfax.com/press/unfixed-recalls-2025

### Source 2: NHTSA 2022 Recalls Annual Report
- Average annual vehicle recall count: 36 million (2013-2023), 110% increase over previous decade
- Average recall completion rate: 64%
- 2022 completion rate: 58%
- 2021 completion rate: 52% (lowest since 2010)
- More vehicles recalled each year than new vehicles sold
- URL: nhtsa.gov/sites/nhtsa.gov/files/2023-03/2022-Recalls-Annual-Report_030223-tag.pdf

### Source 3: Alliance for Automotive Innovation / CMT Research
- Completion rate by vehicle age:
  - New vehicles: 83%
  - 5-10 years old: 44%
  - 10+ years old: 15%
- 70% of drivers decide based on perceived risk (2018 survey)
- 26% of drivers don't believe recalls are serious
- 36% don't bring in vehicles because they fear it takes too long
- Car dealers NOT required by federal law to repair or inform buyers about recalls on used vehicles
- URL: cmtelematics.com/blog/automotive-blog/the-challenges-of-increasing-vehicle-recall-completions/

### Source 4: Takata Airbag Recall
- 67 million Takata airbags total affected (largest recall in history)
- At least 28 deaths, 400+ injuries from shrapnel-shooting airbag inflators
- 5.7 million vehicles still carry these airbags
- 2018 case: Arizona man died after purchasing used Honda Civic 3 months prior; Honda had sent 12 notices and made 20 calls to previous owner
- URL: nhtsa.gov/equipment/takata-recall-spotlight

### Source 5: NHTSA Recall Completion Rate Congressional Report (2024)
- Required under FAST Act Section 24104
- Risk-based process for monitoring recall performance
- URL: rosap.ntl.bts.gov/view/dot/79374

## Angle
The paradox: recall repairs are free. Completely free. Zero cost. And yet 58.1 million vehicles — one in five — remain unrepaired. The completion rate drops to 15% for cars over 10 years old. Meanwhile, used car dealers have no legal obligation to tell you your car has an open recall. The system effectively guarantees that the people driving the oldest, most dangerous vehicles will never know they're at risk.
