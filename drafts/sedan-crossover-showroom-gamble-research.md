# Research: The Showroom Gamble — Sedan vs Crossover Death Rates From the Same Brand

## Angle
When you walk into a dealership, the choice between the sedan and the crossover from the same manufacturer may be the single most consequential safety decision you make. FARS data reveals staggering death rate gaps between sedan-crossover pairs that share a brand, a price bracket, and often a platform — but not a survival rate.

### Class-Level Context
- Sedans: 32.5% of fleet, **46.6% of deaths** (death rate 2.55x SUV rate)
- SUVs: 39.6% of fleet, **24.3% of deaths**
- Average sedan-crossover premium across 13 pairs: **~535%**

## Primary Sources
1. NHTSA FARS database (2014-2023) — all death rate calculations
2. IIHS fatality statistics — vehicle size/weight as safety factor
3. IIHS vehicle size and weight research — physics of crash protection
4. NHTS travel survey — VMT estimates underlying rate calculations

## Strongest Counterargument
Fleet age confound. The Camry has been sold since 1983; the RAV4 became a mass-market vehicle more recently. Older model years in the sedan fleet lack modern safety features (ESC, AEB, side curtain airbags), inflating the sedan's aggregate death rate. A 2024 Camry is dramatically safer than a 2004 Camry, but both are in the same FARS bucket. The crossover fleets are younger on average.

**However:** Even controlling for era, IIHS data consistently shows that larger, heavier vehicles protect occupants better in crashes. The physics advantage of a crossover's higher ride height, greater mass, and better crash geometry is real and independent of fleet age. The Ford Fusion vs Escape comparison (+29%) shows the narrowest gap — and these two share a platform (C2/CD4), suggesting the structural difference is the minimum floor.

## Limitations
- Rate calculations use estimated VMT, not actual odometer readings (±15% uncertainty for some models)
- FARS captures only fatal crashes — nonfatal injury rates could tell a different story
- Driver demographics differ between sedan and crossover buyers (age, income, driving behavior)
- Some "pairs" aren't true platform twins (e.g., Accord/CR-V were on different platforms for part of this period)

## Methodology
- Death rate = FARS deaths (2014-2023) / estimated VMT × 100M
- Estimated VMT = fleet size × average annual miles (from NHTS)
- Sedan premium = (sedan rate - crossover rate) / crossover rate × 100%
