# Research: Virginia ISA vs. NHTSA Speed Campaign

## Angle
Virginia's ISA (Intelligent Speed Assistance) law took effect in July 2026 — judges can now order GPS-based speed limiters on reckless drivers' cars. Meanwhile, NHTSA's approach to the same problem is a month-long ad campaign ("Speeding Catches Up with You") and $665M in enforcement grants. One state is treating speeding like DUI (device-based intervention). The federal government is treating it like a marketing problem. 10,035 people died from speeding in 2025, and NHTSA is celebrating because that's "only" as many as died before COVID made it worse.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Speeding Data (2025 estimates, released with Q1 2026 data)
- 2024: 11,288 speeding-related deaths (29% of all 39,254 traffic fatalities)
- 2025 estimate: 10,035 speeding-related deaths (11% decline)
- Return to pre-pandemic levels (~2019: 9,478 speed-related deaths per NHTSA archives)
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/traffic-crash-deaths-early-estimates-jan-march-2026

### 2. NHTSA 2025 Full Year Data
- 36,640 total traffic fatalities in 2025 (6.7% decline from 2024's 39,254)
- Fatality rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT (second-lowest in recorded history)
- VMT increased 29.8 billion miles (0.9%)
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/2025-traffic-death-estimates-2024-fars

### 3. NHTSA Q1 2026 Early Estimates
- Fatality rate dropped to 0.99 per 100M VMT in Q1 2026
- 7,770 deaths, down 4.3% from Q1 2025
- "Speeding Catches Up with You" campaign July 6-31, 2026
- $665 million in traffic safety grants to state highway safety offices

### 4. Virginia ISA Law (effective July 2026)
- Va. Code §§ 46.2-393, 46.2-506, 46.2-507, 46.2-865 (2026)
- Judges may order ISA devices for reckless driving offenses involving speeding/street racing
- Virginia DMV requires ISA for drivers accumulating too many traffic points (alternative to license suspension)
- Second state after Washington DC
- Source: NCSL, nolo.com, AAMVA Move Magazine

### 5. EU ISA / GPS Speed Governors
- ISA mandatory on all new EU cars since July 2024 (advisory/warning systems)
- July 16, 2026: EU reportedly considering GPS-based speed governors that would PREVENT exceeding the speed limit (no override)
- New York City fleet pilot: 64% reduction in speeding instances with ISA
- Source: Autoblog (July 16, 2026), AAMVA Move Magazine

### 6. US Federal ISA Status
- NHTSA has NOT mandated ISA for new vehicles
- ISA listed as "Not required" in US for 2026 (vs. mandatory in EU)
- NHTSA truck speed limiter proposal returned without approval
- No pending federal ISA rulemaking

### 7. State ISA Adoption
- Washington DC: First jurisdiction, ISA for suspended drivers (effective Sept 2025)
- Virginia: Second, judges can order ISA (effective July 2026)
- Washington State: Third, effective January 2029
- Georgia: Passed ISA law for reckless driving offenders
- New York and Maryland: Pilot programs for repeat speeders
- Strong bipartisan support in all jurisdictions

## Strongest Counterargument
Speed campaigns work through general deterrence — they don't just catch speeders, they make ALL drivers think twice. You can't put ISA on every car, and voluntary technology adoption takes decades (average vehicle age: 13+ years). Virginia's law only catches convicted speeders; campaigns reach everyone. Also, the 11% decline IS meaningful — 1,253 fewer deaths is 1,253 families that didn't lose someone.

## Limitations
- NHTSA 2025 speeding data is preliminary (early estimate)
- Virginia's ISA law just took effect — no outcome data yet
- EU ISA effectiveness data is limited (NYC fleet study is municipal, not consumer)
- $665M in grants covers ALL traffic safety (not just speed), so the per-death math is approximate
- Speed-related crash attribution is inconsistent across jurisdictions
