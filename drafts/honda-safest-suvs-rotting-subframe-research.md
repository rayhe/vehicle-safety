# Research: Honda's Safest SUVs Are Rotting From Underneath

## Angle
Honda recalled 880,514 of its safest family vehicles — the Pilot, Ridgeline, Passport, and Acura MDX — because their rear subframes were manufactured with improper paint coating. In salt-belt states, the exposed welds corrode, thin, fracture, and eventually fail, potentially causing wheels to become misaligned or "inadequately retained."

The original contribution: cross-tabulating this recall population with FARS fatality rates reveals a paradox that no safety publication has drawn. These 4 models are among the safest family vehicles in America by crash outcome. Their subframes were just painted wrong at the factory.

## Recall Details (NHTSA 26V367000)
- Total: 880,514 vehicles
- Honda Pilot 2016-2022: 463,253
- Acura MDX 2014-2020: 217,517
- Honda Ridgeline 2017-2023: 110,070
- Honda Passport 2019-2023: 89,674
- 23 salt-belt states only
- Root cause: "improper coating specifications may result in insufficient paint adhesion and premature paint peeling near the arm bracket weld area"
- Honda aware since Dec 2021; no recall until Jun 2026 (4.5 years)
- Honda estimates 1% have the defect (~8,800 vehicles)
- No injuries/deaths/warranty claims
- Remedy: inspect rear subframe, install reinforcement kit, repair/replace free

## IIHS/NHTSA Safety Ratings
- Honda Pilot: NHTSA 5-star overall, IIHS Good on most crash tests (Marginal on updated side)
- Acura MDX: IIHS Top Safety Pick+ (multiple years)
- All 4 models: among the highest-rated family vehicles in both IIHS and NHTSA testing

## Novel Finding
Crash tests measure what happens when a new car hits a barrier at 40 mph. They cannot measure what happens when the structure holding the rear wheels in place has been dissolving in road salt for 8 winters. IIHS tests the car at the factory. Ohio tests the car at year 8.

The gap: safety ratings tell you the ceiling. They don't tell you the floor — what happens when materials degrade, coatings fail, and the structure that earned those star ratings is no longer the structure you're driving.
