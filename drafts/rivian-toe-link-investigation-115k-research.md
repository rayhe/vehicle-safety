# Research: Rivian Toe Link Investigation — 115K Vehicles

## Angle
Rivian recalled 19,641 vehicles in January 2026 for improperly assembled rear toe links — a defect caused by their OWN service technicians using a flawed procedure. Five months later, NHTSA expanded its investigation to 114,922 vehicles — nearly the entire R1 fleet. The question has shifted from "did a tech mess up?" to "is there a design problem?" And Rivian's official response is that the joints are "operating as intended" — on the same component they recalled five months ago.

## Kill Test
✅ NOVEL: The 5.8x expansion (20K recalled → 115K investigated) is the data point nobody is doing the math on. The investigation population is essentially Rivian's entire cumulative R1 production. This isn't a service error anymore — it's potentially a design defect.
✅ ACTIONABLE: R1 owners can check symptoms, document them, file NHTSA complaints.
✅ SURPRISING: The company's "operating as intended" defense contradicts its own recall from January.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: Reuters (May 28, 2026)
- NHTSA opened preliminary probe into 114,922 Rivian R1S and R1T vehicles
- Two vehicle owner questionnaires (VOQs) reported left rear toe link separating while driving
- Vehicles swerved across multiple lanes of traffic
- One incident resulted in collision with adjacent vehicle and roadside barrier
- Rivian statement: "internal data indicates R1 toe link joints are operating as intended"
- Rivian claims the two VOQs "do not implicate the joint itself"
- NHTSA will assess sensitivity of rear toe link joint to foreseeable road/service conditions
- NHTSA will evaluate Rivian's current toe link repair procedure
- URL: https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/us-auto-safety-regulator-opens-probe-into-nearly-115000-rivian-vehicles-2026-05-28/

### Source 2: Rivian Recall Page (rivian.com/support/article/recall-information)
- 22 recalls listed since May 2022
- January 2026 recall (26V-003/FSAM-1794): 19,641 R1S/R1T vehicles
- Cause: toe link joints reassembled during vehicle SERVICE with procedure before March 10, 2025
- "vehicle motion may eventually cause toe link joint separation"
- "Separation of the toe link while the vehicle is being driven increases the risk of a crash without prior warning"
- One single-vehicle accident with alleged minor injuries
- Remedy: replace rear toe link bolts using updated service procedure

### Source 3: HotCars (May 28, 2026)

- R1T/R1S weighs 7,000+ lbs — makes toe link failure at highway speed significantly more dangerous
- Rear can "unexpectedly shift or step out during emergency maneuvers"
- URL: https://www.hotcars.com/rivian-trucks-and-suvs-investigated-over-sudden-loss-of-control/

## The Novel Calculation
- January recall: 19,641 vehicles (service-induced defect)
- May investigation: 114,922 vehicles
- Expansion factor: 5.85x
- If 114,922 ÷ ~165,000 cumulative R1 deliveries = ~70% of every R1 ever built is under investigation
- 22 recalls in 49 months of production = one recall every 2.2 months
- Rivian has issued more recalls than months of production for some model years

## Counterarguments
- Only 2 VOQs reported — very small complaint sample
- Rivian says their "internal investigation" doesn't implicate the joint
- Preliminary evaluation ≠ recall — many PEs close without action
- Some recalls are minor (labels, software fixed same-day)
- EV startups naturally have more growing pains than established OEMs
