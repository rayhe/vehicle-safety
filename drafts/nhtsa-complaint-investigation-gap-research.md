# Research: The NHTSA Complaint-to-Investigation Gap

## Angle
NHTSA's Office of Defects Investigation received 73,099 Vehicle Owner Questionnaires (safety complaints) in FY25 while opening only 33 investigations. That's 2,215 complaints per investigation. Meanwhile, the agency processed 968 recalls covering 30.8 million vehicles — roughly 10.6% of America's registered fleet. The consumer complaint pipeline is a funnel that swallows 200 reports per day and produces an investigation once every 11 days.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. The NHTSA quarterly metrics are publicly available but nobody has done the ratio math. The Hyundai FCA recall (421K vehicles, 376 complaints, 4 crashes) demonstrates the problem: it took 376 complaints over 18 months before action. This is original analysis of public data.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NHTSA Office of Defects Investigation Quarterly Metrics (FY25)
- Source: nhtsa.gov — ODI Quarterly Metrics page
- FY25 Vehicle Owner Questionnaires by quarter:
  - Q1: 16,055
  - Q2: 18,427
  - Q3: 18,421
  - Q4: 20,196
  - **TOTAL: 73,099**
- Newly Initiated Safety Recalls by quarter:
  - Q1: 236 vehicle + 29 equipment + 6 tire = 271 total, population 9,461,260
  - Q2: 197 vehicle + 18 equipment + 7 tire = 222 total, population 3,841,327
  - Q3: 240 vehicle + 19 equipment + 6 tire = 265 total, population 8,068,300
  - Q4: 208 vehicle + 21 equipment + 5 tire = 234 total, population 9,412,504
  - **TOTAL: 992 recalls, ~30,783,391 vehicles**
- Investigations opened: Q1: 10, Q2: 8, Q3: 5, Q4: 10 → **TOTAL: 33**

### 2. NHTSA 2025 Traffic Fatality Report (April 1, 2026)
- 36,640 traffic fatalities in 2025 (6.7% decrease from 39,254 in 2024)
- Rate: 1.10 fatalities per 100M VMT (second-lowest in recorded history)
- VMT increased 0.9% (29.8 billion miles)
- Fatalities decreased in 39 states, DC, and Puerto Rico

### 3. GAO Report on Recall Completion Rates (GAO-24-106222, 2024)
- NHTSA reported 69% of passenger vehicles recalled in 2018 had been repaired (as of 2021)
- Average completion rate ~70% for passenger vehicles
- Airbag recall completion: 51-70% depending on year
- Larger recalls underperform smaller recalls
- Annual completion rate (volume-weighted): ~67%

### 4. Hyundai FCA Recall (May 2026) — Case Study
- 421,078 vehicles recalled (2025-2026 Santa Cruz, Tucson, Tucson Hybrid, Tucson PHEV)
- Between Oct 28, 2024 and April 27, 2026: 376 complaints received
- 4 crashes, 4 injuries reported
- Root cause: front camera software causes FCA system to activate prematurely
- 376 complaints over ~18 months before recall issued

### 5. FARS Data (2014-2023) — Cross-reference
- Hyundai Tucson: 735 deaths, rate 0.34 per 100M VMT (from FARS_BY_MODEL)
- The recalled Tucson models (2025-2026) aren't yet in FARS data, but the platform's safety record is strong for crashworthiness
- Broader point: FARS tracks deaths, not near-misses or injury crashes. Complaints capture the wider funnel.

## Novel Calculations

### Complaint-to-Investigation Ratio
- 73,099 complaints ÷ 33 investigations = **2,215 complaints per investigation**
- 73,099 ÷ 365 = **200.3 complaints per day** (~8.3 per hour)
- 33 investigations ÷ 365 = **one investigation every 11.1 days**
- 73,099 ÷ 252 working days = **290 complaints per working day**

### Recall Coverage
- ~30.8M vehicles recalled in FY25
- US registered vehicles: ~290M (2024 estimate from BTS/FHWA)
- 30.8M / 290M = **10.6% of registered fleet**

### Unfixed Recall Estimate
- At ~67-70% average completion rate (GAO data)
- ~30% of 30.8M = **~9.2M vehicles from FY25 alone remain unfixed**
- This is a single year; cumulative unfixed recalls from prior years add millions more

### Complaints Per Death
- 73,099 complaints in the year, 36,640 deaths in 2025
- Ratio: ~2.0 complaints per traffic death
- But complaints ≠ fatal events; most complaints are about defects that COULD cause harm

## Counterarguments (must address at full strength)
1. **Many complaints are frivolous or duplicates.** Not all 73,099 are legitimate safety defects. Some are performance complaints, warranty issues, or duplicates. NHTSA triages before investigation. The funnel is designed to filter.
2. **33 investigations ≠ 33 outcomes.** Some investigations lead to voluntary recalls without formal investigation. The 968 recalls include manufacturer-initiated ones. The system works partly because manufacturers self-police.
3. **ODI's resources are finite.** With ~100 engineers and investigators, 33 investigations in a year is arguably reasonable. Each investigation is resource-intensive: technical analysis, data collection, manufacturer negotiations.

## Limitations
- FY25 quarters span Oct 2024-Sept 2025; 2025 fatality data is calendar year. Slight temporal mismatch.
- Recall population counts may include vehicles already scrapped or exported
- Complaint volume alone doesn't measure severity; one complaint about a steering column failure outweighs 100 about infotainment glitches
- The ratio of complaints to investigations is a crude metric that doesn't capture the triage process
