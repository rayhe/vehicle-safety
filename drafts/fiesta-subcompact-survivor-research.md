# Research: The Ford Fiesta — Subcompact Survivor

## Angle
The Ford Fiesta has the lowest crash lethality (deaths per crash) of ANY subcompact in the FARS dataset — and its 0.473 lethality is actually LOWER than the average SUV (0.516). A tiny car that out-survives an entire vehicle class.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? **YES.** The core finding — that a subcompact can have better crash survivability than the average SUV — directly challenges the "bigger is safer" assumption that drives millions of purchase decisions annually. This is a novel cross-tabulation not covered in any of our 168 existing stories.

## Primary Sources

### 1. FARS Data (2014-2023)
- Ford Fiesta: 513 deaths, 1,084 crashes, lethality 0.473
- Avg subcompact lethality: ~0.72 (weighted across Spark, Sonic, Versa, Fit, etc.)
- Avg SUV lethality: 0.516
- Avg sedan lethality: 0.652
- The Fiesta is 34% less lethal per crash than the average subcompact
- The Fiesta is 8% less lethal per crash than the average SUV

## Novel Contribution
First FARS analysis showing a specific subcompact has lower crash lethality than the average SUV. The "bigger is safer" heuristic fails when engineering quality varies. Weight protects, but structure protects more.

## Strongest Counterargument
Lethality (deaths per crash) doesn't fully capture risk. The Fiesta's RATE (1.02 per 100M VMT) is higher than many SUVs (RAV4 at 0.19, CR-V at 0.53). You crash more often in a Fiesta AND face more danger from the other vehicle's mass in every crash. The Fiesta's low lethality might partly reflect crash TYPE (more urban, lower-speed) rather than structural superiority.

## Limitations
- FARS only records fatal crashes, so crash TYPE distribution (urban vs highway, intersection vs head-on) isn't controlled
- Fiesta fleet skews urban/commuter which might bias toward lower-speed crashes
- "Deaths per crash" counts all fatalities (occupant + other party) — need to investigate whether Fiesta's low lethality means fewer occupant deaths or fewer multi-fatality incidents
- Fleet size estimate (437,500) introduces ±15% uncertainty
