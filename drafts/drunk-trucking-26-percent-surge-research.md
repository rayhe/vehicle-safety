# Research: Drunk Trucking 26% Surge (2024 FARS)

## Primary Source: NHTSA 2024 FARS Annual Report (DOT HS 813 791, April 2026)
- URL: https://crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov/Api/Public/ViewPublication/813791
- Title: "Overview of Motor Vehicle Traffic Crashes in 2024"

## Key Finding (Table 6, p.8)
Alcohol-impaired drivers involved in fatal crashes, by vehicle type, 2023→2024:
- Passenger cars: 4,462 → 4,239 (-223, -5.0%)
- Light trucks: 4,928 → 4,839 (-89, -1.8%)
  - SUVs: 2,641 → 2,624 (-17, -0.6%)
  - Pickups: 2,048 → 1,994 (-54, -2.6%)
  - Vans: 238 → 221 (-17, -7.1%)
- Motorcycles: 1,656 → 1,606 (-50, -3.0%)
- **LARGE TRUCKS: 178 → 224 (+46, +26%)**
- Total: 11,724 → 11,367 (-357, -3.0%)

## Additional 2024 FARS Context
- Total traffic fatalities: 39,254 (down 4.3% from 41,025 in 2023)
- People killed in large-truck crashes: 5,340 (down 2.5%)
  - Of those, only 920 were truck occupants (down 4.4%)
  - 3,753 were occupants of OTHER vehicles (down 2.3%)
  - 667 were nonoccupants (down 0.9%)
- Large-truck occupants injured in single-vehicle crashes: +12% increase
- Overall alcohol-impaired fatalities: 11,904 (30% of all traffic deaths)
- Alcohol-impaired fatality rate: 0.36 per 100M VMT (down 5.3%)

## The Anomaly
Every vehicle type saw FEWER alcohol-impaired drivers in fatal crashes in 2024. 
Large trucks went up 26%.

CDL holders have a legal BAC limit of 0.04 g/dL (half the 0.08 standard for other drivers).
Commercial Motor Vehicle (CMV) drivers are subject to mandatory pre-employment, random, post-accident, and reasonable suspicion drug/alcohol testing under 49 CFR Part 382.

So: the driver population with the strictest legal threshold, the most testing, the most at stake (CDL revocation), is the ONLY population where drunk driving in fatal crashes got worse.

## Novel Original Contribution
Cross-tabulation: drunk truck drivers went up 26% in FARS while total truck-involved fatalities went DOWN 2.5%. The drunk trucking problem is getting worse on a per-crash basis. If 224 out of approximately how many total truck drivers in fatal crashes were drunk, what's the rate?

From Table 6: total drivers column shows percentages. Large trucks: 178 (3%) in 2023, 224 (4%) in 2024.
So ~5,933 total truck drivers in 2023 fatal crashes, ~5,600 in 2024 fatal crashes.
Rate went from 3% to 4% — a one-third increase in the rate, not just absolute numbers.
