# Research: 70% of Children in Fatal Crashes Weren't Properly Restrained

## Angle
We solved child car seat safety decades ago. Car seats reduce infant fatality risk by 71%. And yet: 70% of children under 13 involved in fatal crashes from 2011-2021 had suboptimal restraint use. Three kids die every day. The technology exists. The data is clear. Nobody's using it right.

## Kill Test
- **Is this genuinely newsworthy?** Yes — a peer-reviewed FARS-based study (50,000 children) finding 70% misuse rate is shocking
- **Novel angle?** Yes — specific breakdown of HOW restraints fail (36% premature transition, 20% unrestrained, 15% front seat)
- **Data-backed?** Uses FARS database (same as our site), published in Traffic Injury Prevention
- **Actionable?** Extremely — specific guidance on car seat stages, when to transition, state law lookup

3. **IIHS / NHTSA Child Restraint Effectiveness Data**
   - Child restraints reduce fatality risk by 71% (infants) and 54% (toddlers age 1-4)
   - CRS reduce mortality risk by 28% vs seatbelts alone (Durbin et al., 2006)
   - https://www.iihs.org/topics/child-safety

4. **AAP Child Passenger Safety Guidelines**
   - Rear-facing until at least age 2 (or until max height/weight)
   - Forward-facing with harness until 40-65 lbs
   - Booster until 4'9" (typically age 8-12)
   - Rear seat until age 13

## Key Statistics

- **70%** of children <13 in fatal crashes had suboptimal restraint (2011-2021)
- **36%** prematurely transitioned to less protective seat
- **20%** completely unrestrained
- **15%** riding in front seat (should be rear)
- **9%** in front seat AND unrestrained
- **3 child deaths per day** from motor vehicle crashes
- **429 child injuries per day** from motor vehicle crashes
- **75 county-level hotspots** identified
- **50,000 children** analyzed in FARS data
- Ages 4-12 most at risk
- Under-resourced communities disproportionately affected
- Stricter state CRS laws significantly reduce suboptimal practices

## Story Breakdown

**The headline stat:** 70% wrong. Not 70% slightly suboptimal — 70% not properly restrained in crashes that killed someone.

**The human cost:** 3 deaths/day = ~1,095 child deaths/year from motor vehicle crashes. If 70% had suboptimal restraints, and proper restraints reduce fatality by 28-71%, the math suggests hundreds of children die each year specifically because of restraint misuse.

**The paradox:** Car seats are the most studied, most proven, most available child safety technology. They've been mandatory since the 1980s. Every hospital requires one before discharge. And still — 70%.

**The premium kill:** 36% were prematurely graduated. This means parents BOUGHT the next seat — they're engaged, they're trying — but they moved their kid up too early. A 3-year-old in a forward-facing seat instead of rear-facing. A 5-year-old in a booster instead of a harnessed seat. Good intentions, wrong timing.

**The geographic angle:** 75 county hotspots. States with weaker CRS laws have worse outcomes. Policy works.

**The class angle:** Under-resourced communities hit hardest. The Child Opportunity Index correlates with restraint misuse.

## Actionable Takeaways
1. Check NHTSA.gov/equipment/car-seats-and-booster-seats for stage guidelines
2. Rear-facing until AT LEAST age 2 (many experts say until max height/weight of seat)
3. Don't rush the transition — a kid who "looks big" for their seat is safer than one who graduated early
4. Kids under 13 belong in the rear seat, no exceptions
5. Free car seat inspections: NHTSA.gov/equipment/car-seats-and-booster-seats/get-car-seat-checked
6. Check your state's CRS law — some are stricter than others
