# Research Notes: March 2026 — First Monthly Rate Below Pre-COVID

## Angle
For one month, America's roads were statistically safer than the pre-COVID baseline. March 2026 fatality rate: 1.07/100M VMT vs the 2014-2019 average of ~1.13. This is the first time a monthly rate has dropped below the pre-COVID floor. But it's one month, it's seasonal, and the geographic divergence is extreme.

**Clara Rollover** — Consumer Safety Advocate, "Trend Watch" kicker

## Primary Sources

- Deaths: 3,030 (estimated)
- Down 6% from March 2025, down 9% from March 2024
- Miles driven: up 1.6% from March 2025, up 2.7% from 2024
- Mileage death rate: 1.07/100M VMT

### NHTSA 2024 Final FARS
- 39,254 deaths in 2024 (final count)
- Rate: 1.19/100M VMT
- First time below 40,000 since 2020
- 11 consecutive quarters of decline

### Pre-COVID Baseline
- 2014-2019 average rate: ~1.13/100M VMT (from NHTSA historical data)
- 2019 rate: ~1.11 (the last "normal" year)
- COVID spike: 1.33 in 2021 (peak)
- Recovery trajectory: 1.33 → 1.24 → 1.19 → now 1.07 (monthly)

## Novel Calculations

### Annualized projection (with caveats)
- If 1.07 rate held for full year at ~3.36 trillion VMT: ~35,952 deaths
- That would be the lowest since before 2014
- BUT: March is historically one of the SAFEST months (less driving, shorter days, fewer road trips)
- Summer months (June-August) historically 15-25% deadlier than March
- More realistic: 2026 full-year rate likely 1.10-1.15, deaths 37,000-39,000

### The "COVID debt" calculation
- At pre-COVID rate of 1.13, excess deaths from 2020-2024:
  - 2020: ~1.34 rate on ~2.83T VMT = ~37,922 (expected ~31,979) = ~5,943 excess
  - 2021: ~1.33 rate on ~3.21T VMT = ~42,693 (expected ~36,273) = ~6,420 excess
  - 2022: ~1.24 rate on ~3.27T VMT = ~40,548 (expected ~36,951) = ~3,597 excess
  - 2023: ~1.24 rate on ~3.30T VMT = ~40,920 (expected ~37,290) = ~3,630 excess
  - 2024: ~1.19 rate on ~3.30T VMT = ~39,270 (expected ~37,290) = ~1,980 excess
  - Total excess: ~21,570 deaths attributable to post-COVID driving behavior change
  - March 2026 at 1.07 suggests we MAY have finally paid the "COVID debt"

### Fleet turnover mechanism (tie to FARS data)
- Our FARS data shows vehicles like the Chevy Cobalt (5.10 rate), S-10 (4.83), Ford Ranger (2.91) are dying off
- Average vehicle age in US: 12.6 years (2024 IHS Markit)
- Vehicles manufactured 2018+ have mandatory AEB (NHTSA Final Rule 2022, voluntary since 2017)
- ESC mandate (2012) now covers most of the fleet
- The improving rate reflects ~28 years of safety mandates finally reaching fleet saturation

## Geographic Pattern Analysis
The divergence is extreme:
- Large coastal states with newer fleet composition show massive improvement (NY -72%)
- Rural states with older fleets and higher speed limits show increases (Mississippi +33%)
- DC's +300% is noise (base of 3 → 12 in a small geography)
- The pattern suggests fleet age, not driver behavior, is the mechanism

## Counterarguments (strongest stated at full strength)
1. **One month proves nothing.** Monthly data is noisy. March is low-season. The summer could erase this.
2. **Seasonal effects dominate.** March has shorter daylight, less recreational driving, fewer motorcycles. A March-to-March comparison partially controls for this, but year-to-year noise is still large.
3. **State-level data is too sparse.** North Dakota's +38% is 5 extra deaths. Small states swing wildly.
4. **The rate doesn't capture severity changes.** More injuries, fewer deaths could mean better trauma care, not better driving.

## Limitations
- Monthly NSC data is preliminary — can be revised 5-10%
- VMT estimates from FHWA are also preliminary
- Rate-based comparison assumes VMT mix is similar (it may not be — more urban miles = fewer deaths per mile)
- FARS 2014-2023 dataset doesn't break out monthly patterns
- We cannot directly attribute the rate improvement to specific vehicle retirements without monthly FARS detail
