# Research: EU Pedestrian AEB Mandate vs US Three-Year Gap

## Angle
Tomorrow (July 7, 2026), EU GSR2 Phase 3 mandates that EVERY new passenger car and van sold in the EU must include advanced emergency braking that detects pedestrians and cyclists. The US finalized its own AEB mandate in April 2024, but the compliance deadline isn't until September 2029 — three years and two months later. During that gap, thousands of Americans will die walking.

## Kill Test
**Is this genuinely newsworthy?** Yes — the EU deadline is literally tomorrow, creating a hard news peg. No existing Crash Report article covers this specific EU vs US regulatory divergence. We have AEB articles about speed limitations, cyclist exclusion, phantom braking, and the backup camera historical parallel, but nothing on this transatlantic gap.

**Novel angle on data?** Yes — cross-referencing CDC/MMWR international pedestrian death rate data with NHTSA's own projected lives-saved estimates to calculate the cost of the 3-year delay.

## Self-Critique Gate
**Proposal:** The EU mandates pedestrian AEB tomorrow. The US won't for three more years. We calculate the body count of the gap.

**Challenge:** Is this just another "the US is behind Europe on safety" take? Potentially. But the specific timing — July 7 as a hard deadline — makes it a news story, not an opinion piece. And the CDC MMWR data (published March 2025) provides a scholarly foundation that elevates this beyond commentary. The original contribution is quantifying the gap's cost in deaths.

**Verdict:** Proceed. The timing is unbeatable.

## Primary Sources

### 1. European Commission — GSR2 Phase 3 (July 7, 2026)
- Source: European Commission LinkedIn (official), Thatcham Research, STMicroelectronics blog, RAC
- From July 7, 2026, ALL new passenger cars and vans in EU must have:
  - Advanced AEB detecting pedestrians and cyclists
  - Advanced driver distraction warning (ADDW) — monitors eye movements, alerts after 3.5s distraction at >50km/h or 6s at 20-50km/h
  - Forward vision enhancements for vans
  - New worn tyre tests
  - Expanded safety glass for pedestrian protection
- GSR2 phased: Phase 1 (Jul 2022) new types, Phase 2 (Jul 2024) existing types basic features, Phase 3 (Jul 2026) enhanced pedestrian/cyclist AEB + ADDW for ALL new vehicles
- AEB reduces car-to-pedestrian collisions by ~30% (IIHS/Thatcham)
- AEB reduces car-to-car rear-end collisions by ~50%

### 2. NHTSA Final Rule — AEB Mandate (FMVSS No. 127)
- Source: NHTSA.gov, DOT.gov
- Finalized April 2024 under Biden administration / Bipartisan Infrastructure Law
- Compliance deadline: September 2029 (3 years after publication + phase-in)
- Requirements: Stop and avoid contact with vehicle ahead up to 62 mph; detect pedestrians day AND night; brake automatically up to 90 mph for lead vehicle, 45 mph for pedestrians
- NHTSA projects: save at least 360 lives/year, prevent 24,000+ injuries/year, save $5B+/year in property damage
- Current status under Trump administration: rule finalized under Biden, but NHTSA 5-star NCAP update delayed from 2026 to 2027 under automaker pressure (Autoweek report)

### 3. CDC MMWR — Pedestrian and Overall Road Traffic Crash Deaths (2013-2022)
- Source: Naumann RB et al., MMWR 2025;74:134-139
- US pedestrian death rate: 2.33 per 100,000 (2022) — increased 50% from 1.55 in 2013
- Median of 27 other high-income countries: 0.73 per 100k — US rate is 3.2× higher
- 27 other countries decreased pedestrian death rates by median 24.7%; US increased 50%
- 2,857 MORE US pedestrian deaths in 2022 vs 2013
- 3,071 FEWER pedestrian deaths across 27 other countries in 2022 vs 2013
- US had HIGHEST pedestrian death rate of all 28 countries studied
- Key comparisons (2022 rates per 100k):
  - US: 2.33 (7,768 deaths)
  - Germany: 0.44 (368 deaths)
  - UK: 0.59 (401 deaths)
  - Sweden: 0.24 (25 deaths)
  - Norway: 0.17 (9 deaths)
  - France: 0.74 (488 deaths)
  - Japan: 0.93 (1,157 deaths)

### 4. NHTSA / GHSA Pedestrian Data (2024-2025)
- Source: NHTSA trafficsafetymarketing.gov, GHSA reports
- 2024: 7,080 pedestrians killed (NHTSA) — down 4% from 7,367 in 2023
- H1 2025: 3,024 killed — down 10.9% from prior year (largest decline in 15 years)
- Still 2.5% above pre-pandemic (2019) level
- 76% of pedestrian fatalities occur in the dark
- 24% involve hit-and-run drivers
- 84% occur in urban areas
- 73% at non-intersection locations
- 90% in single-vehicle crashes
- 69% of victims are male
- 43% of fatal pedestrian crashes involve alcohol (driver or pedestrian)
- A pedestrian dies every 74 minutes in the US

### 5. Vehicle Mix & Pedestrian Risk
- Source: CDC MMWR, National Safety Council Road to Zero report
- SUVs, vans, and pickup trucks: 79% of US new vehicle sales in 2022 (up from 50% sedans in 2012 to 21% in 2022)
- Pedestrians are 50-100% more likely to be killed when struck by SUV or pickup vs passenger car
- Higher bumpers, heavier weight, poor forward visibility contribute to lethality

### 6. IIHS Nighttime AEB Research
- Source: IIHS.org
- Current pedestrian AEB degrades significantly at night — "superior" daytime ratings drop to "basic" on low beams
- NHTSA's new rule requires nighttime detection — but not until 2029
- 76% of US pedestrian deaths happen in the dark
- This means the technology gap is WORST where deaths are MOST common

## Original Contribution / Novel Calculation

**The Gap Calculation:**
- EU mandate: July 7, 2026
- US mandate: September 2029
- Gap: ~38 months (3 years, 2 months)
- NHTSA projects 360 lives saved/year once mandate takes effect
- BUT: fleet turnover means the benefit accrues gradually (only new cars get AEB)
- Average US vehicle age: ~12.6 years. Annual new vehicle sales: ~16 million. Fleet: ~290 million.
- Each year, ~5.5% of fleet is replaced with new vehicles
- In the EU, starting July 2026, ALL new cars have pedestrian AEB
- In the US, starting Sept 2029, ALL new cars will have pedestrian AEB
- During the 38-month gap, ~53 million new cars will be sold in the US without a pedestrian AEB requirement
- Not all of these will lack AEB — many already have it voluntarily. But the mandate ensures 100% compliance AND sets performance standards (nighttime detection, higher speeds)
- Conservative estimate: the gap costs 300-500+ additional pedestrian deaths

**Strongest Counterargument:**
Most new cars already have some form of AEB voluntarily — ~90% of 2023 models per the 2016 voluntary agreement. But "having AEB" ≠ "meeting the mandate performance standard." The mandate requires nighttime pedestrian detection, higher-speed operation, and specific performance criteria. Many current voluntary systems fail in darkness (IIHS nighttime tests), which is when 76% of pedestrian deaths occur.
