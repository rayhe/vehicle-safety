# Research: The Kia Forte Failed Two IIHS Crash Tests. FARS Says It's the Safest Compact Sedan in America.

## Story Angle
The Kia Forte earned Poor ratings in IIHS's updated moderate overlap front and updated side crash tests. Yet FARS data shows it has the lowest death rate (0.40 per 100M VMT) of any compact sedan in America — safer per mile than many SUVs. Why does the lab say one thing and the road say another?

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulating IIHS lab ratings versus FARS real-world death rates reveals that lab tests are LEADING indicators of future vulnerability, not measurements of current real-world performance. The Forte's low death rate reflects its 2010-era design performing well against real crash scenarios. Its Poor scores on 2022-era updated tests (which evaluate rear passenger protection in specific updated crash modes) predict emerging risks as crash dynamics evolve — but those risks haven't materialized in FARS data yet because:
1. The updated test modes represent scenarios that are relatively uncommon in the overall crash landscape
2. The Forte's fleet is young — all 2010+, no ancient pre-safety models diluting the rate
3. The biggest factor in compact sedan death rates is fleet age, not lab scores

## Strongest Counterargument
The Forte's low death rate may be substantially a fleet age artifact. The Civic FARS data includes 1990s and 2000s models without ESC, modern airbags, or updated crash structures. If you compared only 2014+ model year Civics to 2014+ Fortes, the gap might narrow dramatically. The Forte doesn't necessarily have better engineering — it just doesn't carry dead weight from a pre-safety era.

## Limitations
- FARS only captures fatal crashes, not injury crashes. The Forte could have high injury rates despite low fatality rates.
- Fleet VMT estimates introduce ±15% uncertainty for lower-volume models.
- The Forte was introduced in 2010; FARS data (2014–2023) captures its entire production run. The Civic has been sold since 1973 — comparing their rates conflates car engineering with fleet composition.
- The Forte got POOR in IIHS updated tests introduced in 2022-2023. These tests are too new to have affected FARS outcomes yet.

## Actionable Takeaway
If you're shopping compact sedans on a budget: the newest-design-generation vehicle is almost always the safest, regardless of brand reputation. The Forte (and its successor K4) benefit from having no ancient models on the road. Check FARS data, not just lab scores, when evaluating real-world risk. And never sit in the rear seat of a Forte in a frontal crash if you can avoid it.
