# Research: Record Low, Fragile Miracle

## News Hook
NHTSA announced April 1, 2026: 2025 had record-low traffic deaths at **36,640** (down 6.7% from 2024's 39,254). Fatality rate: **1.10 per 100M VMT** (second-lowest ever, behind 2014's 1.08). VMT actually increased 0.9%. All 10 NHTSA regions projected to have decreases.

## FARS Data Analysis (2014-2023)

### The 2003-2005 Retirement Effect
Model years 2003-2005 have the highest aggregate death counts in FARS:
- MY 2003: 10,714 deaths
- MY 2004: 11,221 deaths
- MY 2005: 11,363 deaths
Total: **33,298 deaths** across the reporting period for just these 3 model years

17 vehicles have their deadliest model year in MY 2004 alone (the most of any single MY).
These vehicles are now 21-23 years old in 2026. They're scrapping out of the fleet at accelerating rates.

### The Replacement Problem: MY 2019-2021
30 vehicles have their peak death rate in MY 2019-2021:
- MY 2019: 13 vehicles peak here (tied with MY 2004 as the most-concentrated peak year)
- MY 2020: 7 vehicles
- MY 2021: 10 vehicles

Top examples (deaths per year of exposure):
- Nissan Altima MY 2015: 42.7/yr
- Nissan Sentra MY 2019: 34.6/yr (vs 20.1/yr for MY 2014 = 72% increase)
- Ram 1500 MY 2019: 31.2/yr
- Kia Forte MY 2021: 25.3/yr (vs 2.3/yr for MY 2013 = 11x)
- Equinox MY 2020: 22.8/yr
- Nissan Rogue MY 2019: 21.4/yr (vs 2.4/yr for MY 2010 = 9x)

### What's Actually Driving the Decline (FARS inference)
1. **Fleet attrition** — deadliest vintage (2003-2005) is aging out
2. **ESC mandate** (2012) — all vehicles post-2012 have electronic stability control
3. **Backup cameras** (2018) — mandatory since MY 2018
4. **AEB adoption** — voluntary industry commitment, >90% of new vehicles by 2023

### What's NOT declining
- Motorcycle deaths: UP 1% in H1 2024 while everything else dropped 1-12%
- Truck-involved injuries: UP 8.2% in 2024 (even as truck deaths DOWN 2.5%)
- 65+ fatalities: UP 1% in H1 2024

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? **Yes.** The record-low announcement is 12 days old. Nobody has cross-referenced it with model-year FARS death concentration data. The thesis — that the improvement is structurally driven by fleet attrition rather than driver behavior change — is a genuinely novel contribution that provides a framework for predicting whether the improvement will continue or stall.

## Angle
The record low is real and worth celebrating. But the FARS data suggests it's a one-time dividend from scrapping the deadliest generation of vehicles, not a behavioral revolution. When the 2003-2005 fleet is fully retired, the improvement curve may flatten unless modern vehicles prove meaningfully safer.
