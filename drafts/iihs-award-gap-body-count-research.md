# Research: IIHS 2026 Awards vs. FARS Body Count

## Angle
The IIHS 2026 Top Safety Pick awards went to 63 vehicles. Zero minivans. Zero small pickups. Only 2 large pickups (Tesla Cybertruck, Toyota Tundra crew cab) out of hundreds sold. Meanwhile, FARS data shows pickups and vans are responsible for 42,199 deaths (2014-2023). The categories Americans trust most for family hauling are the ones IIHS can't award.

## Key finding (NOVEL)
The failure reason is **back-seat safety** — the moderate overlap front test now requires good rear-passenger protection. Pickups and minivans fail this test. These are the vehicles most likely to carry passengers in the back seat (kids in minivans, crew cab passengers in pickups). The segment that needs rear-seat safety most is the segment that delivers it least.

Cross-referencing IIHS 2026 results with FARS 2014-2023 data creates a "Safety Award Gap" metric: vehicles that can't earn awards have death rates 3-5x higher than vehicles that do.

## Kill test: Is this genuinely newsworthy?
YES. This is a timely IIHS data release (2026 award season) cross-tabulated with FARS fatality data. The original contribution is quantifying the combined body count of non-awardable vehicle categories and computing the death-rate gap between TSP+ winners and non-winners. No other outlet has done this cross-tab.

## IIHS President Quote
"It's disappointing that minivans continue to struggle to provide the best-available protection for passengers in the back, considering that these are supposed to be family vehicles."

## Strongest counterargument
The FARS data spans 2014-2023 and includes all model years, while IIHS tests 2026 models. Older pickups (pre-ESC, pre-modern crashworthiness) dominate FARS numbers. Current-generation F-150s and Silverados may be substantially safer than their FARS-average rate suggests. The death rate gap between award winners and non-winners may partly reflect fleet age composition rather than current-model engineering.

## Limitations
- FARS data covers all model years 2014-2023; can't isolate current-generation models
- IIHS awards test new models only; the gap may narrow for model-year-matched comparisons
- Van deaths include commercial vans (E-350, Transit, Express) which aren't family minivans
- Pickup death rates include single-vehicle crashes and impaired driving, which aren't design failures
