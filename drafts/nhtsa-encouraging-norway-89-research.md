# Research: NHTSA Called 39,345 Deaths 'Encouraging.' Norway Lost 89 People.

## Journalist

## Angle
NHTSA just released 2024 full-year data: 39,345 deaths, the first time below 40,000 since 2020. Administrator Jonathan Morrison and Chief Counsel Peter Simshauser called the decline "encouraging." Norway lost 89 people. Sweden lost 213. The entire EU-27 (450M people) lost 19,940. America, with 340M people, lost twice as many as a continent with 110M more people.

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulation of NHTSA's 2024 US data with EU/OECD 2024 data to compute the exact excess body count — the number of Americans who die every year specifically because the US has chosen not to adopt what every peer nation already does.

## Calculations

### US rate per million (2024)
- 39,345 deaths / 340M population ≈ 116 per million

### Excess body count (vs EU average of 45/million)
- If US had EU rate: 340M × 45/1M = 15,300 expected deaths
- Excess: 39,345 - 15,300 = **24,045 excess deaths/year**

### Excess body count (vs Norway, 16/million)
- If US had Norway's rate: 340M × 16/1M = 5,440
- Excess: 39,345 - 5,440 = **33,905 excess deaths/year**

### Excess body count (vs Sweden, 20/million)
- If US had Sweden's rate: 340M × 20/1M = 6,800
- Excess: 39,345 - 6,800 = **32,545 excess deaths/year**

### The divergence
- US rate went UP 22.5% (2013-2022)
- Peer median went DOWN 19.4%
- Combined divergence: ~42 percentage points in opposite directions over one decade

### 9/11 comparison  
- 24,045 excess deaths/year = 65.9 per day = one 9/11 every 45 days (vs EU average)
- 33,905 excess deaths/year = 92.9 per day = one 9/11 every 32 days (vs Norway)

## Strongest Counterargument
The US is fundamentally different from European nations: vastly larger, more rural, higher VMT per capita (~13,500 miles/yr vs ~7,500 in EU), higher speed limits, and built around cars instead of public transit. Apples-to-apples comparison requires normalizing for VMT, not population. On a per-VMT basis, the gap narrows somewhat. But even on a per-VMT basis, the US rate (1.20 per 100M VMT) remains higher than peer nations, and the TREND is the killer: the US went up while peers went down.

## Limitations
- EU data uses 30-day mortality definition; US FARS uses slightly different methodology (but similar)
- VMT data not available for all EU countries, preventing perfect per-VMT comparisons
- Country size, rural fraction, and driving patterns differ significantly
- 2024 US data is preliminary estimate; final FARS data won't be available for 12-18 months
- Norway (5.5M population) and similar small countries have small-number volatility

## Actionable Takeaway
The gap is not destiny. It's a policy choice. NHTSA's own P2SS initiative focuses on enforcement; peer nations invest in road design, vehicle regulation, and speed management. The US has the knowledge, wealth, and engineering capacity to match EU rates. It chooses not to.
