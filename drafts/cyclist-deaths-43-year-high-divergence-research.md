# Research: Cyclist Deaths Hit 43-Year High While Overall Traffic Deaths Drop

## Angle
While America celebrates declining traffic fatalities (down 4.3% in 2023, down 3.8% in 2024 estimates), cyclist deaths just hit 1,166 — the highest since at least 1980. The safety improvement trend is one-directional: it protects people INSIDE vehicles while externalizing lethality onto unprotected road users. The divergence of these trend lines is the story.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: NHTSA FARS 2023 Final Data — Bicyclists and Other Cyclists
- DOT HS 813 739 (crashstats.nhtsa.dot.gov/Api/Public/ViewPublication/813739)
- 1,166 pedalcyclist fatalities in 2023 (2.9% of all traffic fatalities)
- 4% increase from 1,117 in 2022
- Highest count in 43+ years (since at least 1980)
- 49,989 cyclists injured, 8% increase from 46,195 in 2022
- Male death rate 7× higher than female
- 34% of fatal crashes involved alcohol (cyclist or driver)
- 22% of deceased cyclists had BAC > 0
- 81% of fatalities in urban areas
- 62.7% of cyclist fatalities at non-intersections

### Source 2: NHTSA Early Estimates for 2024
- 39,345 total traffic deaths in 2024 (down 3.8% from 2023)
- First time below 40,000 since 2020
- Fatality rate: 1.20 per 100M VMT (lowest since 2019)
- 11th consecutive quarterly decrease

### Source 3: IIHS Cyclist Death Trend Data (2013-2023)
- 2013: 747 cyclist deaths
- 2014: 723
- 2015: 828
- 2016: 848
- 2017: 800
- 2018: 868
- 2019: 856
- 2020: 942
- 2021: 971
- 2022: 1,096
- 2023: 1,155 (IIHS count) / 1,166 (NHTSA count — slight methodology difference)
- 10-year increase: 56% (747 → 1,166)
- Helmet wearing: Flat at ~62% unhelmeted across all years

### Source 4: Pedestrian & Bicycle Information Center
- Non-motorist fatalities (ped + cyclist + other) increased 50.9% from 2014-2023
- Non-motorists now 21.6% of total traffic fatalities, up from 17.8% in 2014
- 76.3% of pedestrian fatalities in dark conditions vs. 49.7% of cyclist fatalities

### Source 5: FARS VEHICLE data (our dataset)
- Aggressor vehicles (pickups, large SUVs) involved in majority of fatal crashes where OTHER road users die
- Ram 2500: 79.5% of fatal crashes kill others, not the Ram occupant
- F-150: 54.2% aggressor ratio (10,872 of 20,066 fatal crashes killed someone else)
- Fleet composition shifting toward heavier vehicles — average transaction price and weight both increasing

## State Data (2023 FARS)
- Florida: 234 cyclist deaths (most of any state, 2× second-place)
- California: 145
- Texas: 106
- These 3 states = 42% of national total
- 4 states (AK, ME, ND, SD) had ZERO cyclist deaths

## Original Contribution
Cross-tabulation: the DIVERGENCE between vehicle occupant death rates (declining) and cyclist death rates (increasing at record pace). This isn't just "cyclists are dying more" — it's that the safety engineering that's reducing occupant deaths may be actively worsening outcomes for cyclists. Heavier vehicles, higher hoods, less visibility. The same SUV that gets a 5-star NHTSA rating is more lethal to a cyclist than the sedan it replaced.

## Kill Test
Is this genuinely newsworthy? YES. The 43-year record is news. The divergence from overall trends is a novel finding. The connection to fleet composition is an original contribution this site is uniquely positioned to make.

## Strongest Counterargument
More cycling is happening (e-bikes, commuter infrastructure, COVID-era shift to cycling), so the raw count going up could partly reflect exposure increase. Per-trip or per-mile cyclist death rates might tell a different story. We acknowledge this limitation.
