# Research: The Opioid-Crash Correlation

## Thesis
America's opioid overdose deaths dropped 26.2% from 2023 to 2024 — the largest recorded decline. Traffic deaths dropped 6.7% in 2025 (to 36,640). Nobody has connected these two declining curves. FARS toxicology data reveals ~8.5% of fatal-crash drivers test drug-positive. If drug-impaired driving fell proportionally to overdose deaths, the opioid decline alone could account for roughly one-third of the national traffic death reduction.

## Kill Test
- **Is this genuinely newsworthy?** YES — two simultaneous public health milestones that share a common variable (drug impairment) haven't been explicitly linked in data coverage.
- **Novel angle?** YES — cross-tabulating FARS toxicology rates with CDC overdose decline to estimate the traffic death contribution is a calculation nobody has published.
- **After 189 articles, is this fresh?** YES — we have cannabis-impairment-blind-spot, poly-impairment-cocktail-effect, fars-drug-testing-blind-spot (testing gaps) but NOT an article connecting the macro overdose trend to the macro crash trend.

## Primary Sources (3+)

### 1. NHTSA 2025 Traffic Fatality Data (April 2026 release)
- 2025: 36,640 traffic fatalities (preliminary), down 6.7% from 2024
- 2024: 39,254 deaths (final), rate 1.19 per 100M VMT
- 2025 rate: 1.10 per 100M VMT — second-lowest in recorded history
- VMT increased 0.9% (29.8B more miles), so decline is real, not just less driving
- Fatalities decreased in 39 states + DC + Puerto Rico
- Source: https://www.nhtsa.gov/press-releases/traffic-deaths-2025-early-estimates-2024-annual

### 2. CDC NCHS Drug Overdose Data (January 2026 release)
- Drug overdose deaths dropped 26.2% from 2023 to 2024 (31.3 → 23.1 per 100k)
- LARGEST DECLINE EVER RECORDED, surpassing 2018 record
- 2024 total overdose deaths: 79,384
- Synthetic opioid deaths (fentanyl): dropped 35.6% (22.2 → 14.3 per 100k)
- Cocaine-involved: dropped 26.7%
- Methamphetamine-involved: dropped 19.8%
- Source: https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/products/databriefs/db549.htm
- Source: https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/pressroom/releases/20260129.html

### 3. FARS Toxicology Data (our dataset, 2014-2023)
- Average drug-positive rate across all fatal-crash drivers: ~8.5%
- Average alcohol-positive rate: ~15%
- Average any-impairment rate: ~20%
- Total drivers in dataset with toxicology: ~470,000+
- Key finding: drug-positive rate is consistent across vehicle classes (8.1-9.5%)

### 4. GHSA Pedestrian Fatality Data (March 2026)
- Pedestrian deaths fell 11% in H1 2025 vs H1 2024
- 3,024 pedestrian deaths in H1 2025, down 371
- Largest drop since GHSA began reporting 15 years ago
- Still above pre-pandemic 2019 levels
- Source: https://www.ghsa.org/resource-hub/pedestrian-traffic-fatalities-2025-preliminary

### 5. AASHTO Journal / NHTSA reporting
- $665M in traffic safety grants announced alongside data
- Source: https://aashtojournal.transportation.org/nhtsa-traffic-deaths-declined-significantly-in-2025/

## Novel Calculation
- FARS avg drug-positive rate: ~8.5% of fatal-crash drivers
- Applied to 2024's 39,254 deaths: ~3,337 drug-involved fatal crash deaths (estimate)
- CDC says overdose deaths dropped 26.2% from 2023-2024
- If drug-impaired driving dropped proportionally: 3,337 × 0.262 = ~874 fewer drug-involved traffic deaths
- Actual total traffic decline (2024→2025): 2,614 fewer deaths
- **Estimated drug-decline contribution: ~33% of the total traffic death reduction**

## Caveats (must include)
1. Correlation ≠ causation — many factors drive traffic death trends
2. The overdose decline is 2023→2024; the traffic decline is 2024→2025 (lag)
3. FARS drug testing is incomplete — fars-drug-testing-blind-spot article documented this
4. Not all drug-impaired crashes involve opioids; cannabis and meth are significant
5. Other factors: seatbelt enforcement, AEB fleet penetration, speed cameras
6. The 26% overdose decline doesn't mean 26% fewer impaired drivers — some users are switching substances

## Strongest Counterargument
The traffic death decline may be entirely attributable to vehicle safety technology (ESC, AEB, lane departure) and infrastructure improvements, not behavioral changes. The NHTSA credited "sustained roadway safety efforts, including a commitment to the Safe System Approach." If this is purely a technology story, the drug correlation is coincidental.
