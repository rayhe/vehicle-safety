# Research: NHTSA EA26002 — When Marketing Becomes a Safety Defect

## Angle
NHTSA's Engineering Analysis EA26002 isn't just investigating whether Tesla FSD works in fog. It's investigating whether Tesla's *marketing* of FSD constitutes a safety defect. The 24-part information demand sent July 2, 2026, includes Request No. 12 listing 15 specific Tesla/Musk marketing claims and asking Tesla to confirm whether each "implies a higher level of capability or automation than the system currently has." This is NHTSA building a case that the gap between marketing and capability IS the defect.

## Key Facts

### Investigation Timeline
- PE24031: Preliminary Evaluation opened (date TBD, pre-2026)
- EA26002: Engineering Analysis opened March 18, 2026 (last step before recall)
- July 2, 2026: 24-part formal information demand sent to Tesla
- August 12, 2026: Tesla's response deadline
- Investigator: Dylan Copestick. Reviewer: Sharon Yukevich. Approver: Tanya Topka (ODI Director)
- Population: 3,203,754 vehicles (2016-2026 Model S, X; 2017-2026 Model 3; 2020-2026 Model Y; 2023-2026 Cybertruck)

### Crash Data
- 9 crashes total where FSD engaged in reduced visibility
- 1 fatality (pedestrian, November 28, 2023)
- 1 injury
- Conditions: sun glare, fog, airborne dust — every single one involved cameras being blinded

### The "Radar Saves Us" Document
- Internal Tesla document submitted privately during PE24031
- NHTSA now pulling it formally into EA26002 record (Request No. 4)
- Document appears to contain Tesla's own analysis showing radar catches scenarios cameras miss
- Name has never appeared publicly before this letter

### Marketing Claims Under Scrutiny (Request No. 12)
15 specific claims, including:
- "FSD Supervised can drive in whiteout conditions"
- "Tesla FSD can operate in all conditions" (Musk, over video admitting FSD struggles in fog/rain/glare)
- "$99/month robotaxi" post
- Videos of drivers dancing, making espresso, pushing seat back

### The Alert Downgrade (Request No. 14)
- In 2024, Tesla changed "FSD Degraded" alert from RED → YELLOW
- Removed the audible chime
- This means when FSD literally can't see, the warning got softer
- NHTSA wants every study and justification for this change

### Technical Details
- "Direct Photon-Count Capability" — camera improvement touted Q1 2025 earnings
- "Vision Failsafe Ranker" — internal system
- "Regressions" as performance metric
- Tesla went camera-only May 2021 (removed front radar, branded "Tesla Vision")
- Ultrasonic sensors removed 2022
- Tesla's own post-incident analysis: updated degradation detection system "may have affected 3 of 9 incidents"

### Penalties
- Civil penalties: up to $27,874 per day per violation
- Maximum cap: ~$139 million for related series

### Congressional Pressure
- Senators Markey & Blumenthal letter to NHTSA (June 16, 2026)
- Asked 5 specific questions about NHTSA's validation of Tesla's FSD safety claims
- Questioned Tesla's 5-second disengagement window vs SGO's 30-second reporting window

### FARS Context (Tesla vehicles)
- Model S: 0.50 death rate, 100 deaths in 134 fatal crashes
- Model 3: 0.05 death rate, 92 deaths in 212 fatal crashes
- Model Y: 0.03 death rate, 57 deaths in 112 fatal crashes
- Model X: 0.15 death rate, 29 deaths in 45 fatal crashes
- Total: 278 deaths, 503 fatal crashes across ~3.66M fleet
- Model 3 and Y have among the LOWEST death rates in the entire FARS database
- But that makes the marketing gap MORE dangerous — a genuinely safe car being marketed as self-driving
