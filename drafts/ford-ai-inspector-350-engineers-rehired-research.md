# Research: Ford Replaced Its Safety Inspectors With AI. Then It Had to Hire 350 of Them Back.

## Angle
Ford's CEO declared AI could replace "literally half" of white-collar workers. The company installed 900 AI-powered cameras for quality inspection and leaned heavily on automated systems. Recalls spiked to all-time industry records. They then quietly rehired 350 veteran "gray beard" engineers to retrain the AI and fix the mess. This is a vehicle safety story about what happens when you automate safety-critical inspection without human expertise.

### 2. JD Power 2026 Initial Quality Study

- Up from #15 in 2023

- 7 of top 10 Ford models ranked top 3 in their segments
- F-150, Mustang, Super Duty each won their categories for 2nd straight year

### 3. NHTSA recall data
- Ford: 153 recalls in 2025 (industry record)
- 51 recalls in H1 2026 (next closest: Chrysler with 19)
- More recalls than all other automakers combined, April 2025-April 2026
- 12 million vehicles recalled in 2026 alone (vs 2.2M sold in 2025)
- Subject to 2024 NHTSA consent order with independent third-party oversight
- Center for Auto Safety: consent order is why Ford leads on recalls
- Ford: >90% of recalls are for vehicles built 2013-2020
- Ford: >80% of 2026 recalls resolved through software updates

### 4. FARS Data (2014-2023) — Our Analysis
- Ford total: 34,954 deaths across 29 models = 18.3% of all FARS fatalities
- Ford + Lincoln: 36,091 deaths = 18.9% of all fatalities
- The 5 models in the latest recall (F-150, Explorer, Expedition, Navigator, Aviator):
  - F-150: 9,194 deaths
  - Explorer: 3,797 deaths
  - Expedition: 1,515 deaths
  - Navigator: 209 deaths
  - Aviator: 189 deaths
  - Combined: 14,904 deaths, ~7.8% of all FARS fatalities
- Ford's fleet scale means quality control isn't abstract — it's life and death

### 5. Financial data

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing Ford's recall-era timeline with FARS death data to show the body count stakes of quality control. Ford vehicles account for 18.3% of all US traffic fatalities. The five models under the latest recall alone account for 14,904 deaths in our dataset. When the company responsible for nearly 1 in 5 US road deaths decided to hand quality control to AI that wasn't ready, the consequences extend far beyond warranty costs.

## Counterargument (at full strength)
Ford's own defense is compelling: recalls are a lagging indicator. Vehicles being recalled now were designed years ago, before the quality overhaul. The JD Power #1 ranking measures NEW vehicle quality (problems in first 90 days), not the legacy fleet. And the consent order may actually be INFLATING Ford's recall count — they're under extra scrutiny and proactively reporting issues other manufacturers might ignore. The 90% legacy vehicle figure supports this. Additionally, recalls themselves are a safety mechanism — a company that recalls aggressively may be safer than one that hides defects.

## Limitations
- FARS data (2014-2023) predates the AI quality control adoption. We cannot directly attribute specific deaths to AI QC failures
- The "AI-only era" is imprecise — Ford didn't flip a switch; it was gradual automation
- JD Power IQS measures "initial quality" (90 days), not long-term reliability or safety
- Ford's fleet share inflates its share of total deaths — per-VMT rates are more meaningful for some models
- We don't know exactly which defects the AI missed that humans would have caught
