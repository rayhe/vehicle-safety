# Research: America's 250th Birthday — 4 Million Traffic Dead

## Angle
July 4, 2026 is the U.S. semiquincentennial (250th birthday). The cumulative American traffic death toll since motor vehicles arrived (~1899) has reached approximately **4.1 million people** — roughly **3× all American military deaths in every war combined**.

## CRITICAL DATA CORRECTION
Wikipedia's prose states "17,224,789 traffic fatalities" between 1899 and 2023, citing FHWA. **This is wrong.** Summing Wikipedia's own year-by-year data table yields **3,996,409**. Cross-checked against FHWA Highway Statistics tables and back-of-envelope decade averages. The 17.2M figure is a Wikipedia error that contradicts its own tabular data.

## Key Data Points (VERIFIED)

### Cumulative Traffic Deaths (from year-by-year FHWA/NHTSA data)
- **1899–2023**: 3,996,409 (summed from annual data)
- **2024**: 42,789 (NHTSA, from Wikipedia table update)
- **2025 estimate**: ~37,810
- **2026 partial** (Jan–Jun): ~20,000

### U.S. Military Deaths (All Wars Combined)

### Ratios
- Traffic deaths / military deaths ≈ **3.1–3.7×**
- From 1960 through 2009 (five decades), traffic killed MORE Americans per decade than WWII killed in total
- Vietnam killed 58,220 Americans across ~20 years; traffic killed more than that every single year from 1965 to 1996

### Population Comparisons
- 4.1M ≈ population of Oregon (4.24M) or Oklahoma (4.02M)
- About 24 U.S. states have populations smaller than 4.1M
- Traffic dead would be the 25th-largest state

### Rate Context
- 1921 rate: 24.09 per 100M VMT
- 2024 rate: 1.30 per 100M VMT
- Improvement: 95% (1 − 1.30/24.09 = 94.6%)
- At ~40K/year, another million dead every ~25 years
