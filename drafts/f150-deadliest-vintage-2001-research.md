# Research Notes: The 2001 Ford F-150 — Deadliest Vintage in FARS

## Core Finding
The model year 2001 Ford F-150 has killed 672 people in the FARS database — the highest body count of any single model-year combination in the entire dataset. No other vintage comes close. The #2 slot is the 2004 Chevrolet Silverado at 663.

## Key Data Points

### F-150 Overall
- Deaths: 9,194 | Rate: 1.04/100M VMT | Fleet: 6.56M | Annual: 919
- Crashes: 20,066 | Crashes per death: 2.18
- Impairment: 18.9% any (below fleet avg 20%), alcohol 14.4%, drug 8%

### Comparisons
- vs Silverado: 9,591 deaths at rate 1.25 (F-150 is 17% safer per mile but nearly tied on body count)
- vs Dodge Ram: 4,407 deaths at rate 0.78 (Ram is 25% safer per mile)
- vs Sierra: 3,337 deaths at rate 1.01 (nearly identical rate)
- vs Tundra: 1,223 deaths at rate 0.94 (smaller fleet, similar rate)

### Top 5 deadliest model-years in all of FARS
1. Ford F-150 MY2001: 672
2. Chevrolet Silverado MY2004: 663
3. Chevrolet Silverado MY2007: 604
4. Chevrolet Silverado MY2003: 597
5. Ford F-150 MY2004: 586

All five are full-size pickups. The deadliest sedan model-year doesn't appear until #18 (Honda Accord MY2005: 444).

### News Hook
July 2026: Ford recalled ~1M vehicles in 2 weeks (Bronco fenders, Mustang wipers, Mach-E differential). None were F-150s. The F-150 has more FARS deaths than any other Ford product by 2.4x (Explorer at 3,797 is #2).

### Why MY2001 Specifically?
- 10th gen F-150 (MY1997-2003) was the generation before the 11th gen's major safety improvements
- MY2001 was peak production volume in this era
- These trucks are now 25 years old — many are cheap used trucks driven hard
- NO electronic stability control (ESC mandate came in 2012)
- No side-curtain airbags standard
- Ladder-frame design without modern crash energy management

### What this means for buyers
- MY2001-2004 F-150s are still common on used market ($3-8K range)
- Pre-ESC, pre-side-airbag vintage
- The volume effect: at 6.56M fleet, even a "normal" rate produces ~919 deaths/year
- 919 deaths/year = one F-150 occupant death every 9.5 hours
