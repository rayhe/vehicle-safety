# Research: IIHS First-Ever Commercial Vehicle Safety Evaluation

## Angle
IIHS released its first-ever safety evaluations for commercial vehicles on June 9, 2026 — after 30 years of rating passenger cars. Only 4 of 9 heavy-duty pickups and cargo vans met all driver protection criteria. Half are missing features standard in passenger cars since the mid-2000s. FARS data quantifies the body count from this regulatory blind spot.

## Primary Sources

### Source 1: IIHS Commercial Vehicle Evaluation (June 9, 2026)
- First-ever IIHS safety evaluation of commercial vehicles
- 9 vehicles evaluated: 3 heavy-duty pickups + 6 cargo vans
- Criteria: standard front and side airbags, advanced seat belt technology, effective seat belt reminders
- **PASSED all 5 criteria:**
  - Chevrolet Silverado 3500HD (pickup)
  - Ford F-350 SuperCrew (pickup)
  - Chevrolet BrightDrop 400 (cargo van)
  - Ram ProMaster 2500 (cargo van)
- **FAILED one or more criteria:**
  - Ram 3500 — missing effective seat belt reminder
  - Chevrolet Express 2500 — missing force limiters AND seat belt reminders
  - Ford Transit T-250 — missing effective seat belt reminders
  - Mercedes Sprinter 2500 — missing effective seat belt reminders
  - Rivian Delivery 500 — missing effective seat belt reminders
- IIHS plans to assess crash avoidance (AEB, headlights) in coming months
- Plans to expand to Classes 4-6 cab chassis in 2027, eventually tractor-trailers

### Source 2: IIHS strategic context
- "Many government safety standards that apply to passenger vehicles don't apply to commercial vehicles"
- "Most people who die in truck crashes are other road users"
- 16% of all roadway fatalities (6,535 deaths in 2023) involve heavy/medium trucks and light vans
- IIHS VP David Kidd: "Cargo vans and big pickups are only the beginning"
- Three strategic pillars: commercial vehicle safety, reducing risky behavior, extending safety to everyone

### Source 3: NHTSA FARS Data (2014-2023) — our dataset
**Van class rates (deaths per 100M VMT):**
- Ford E-350: 2.51 (776 deaths) — HIGHEST in class, 38x the Transit
- Chevrolet Express: 0.92 (475 deaths) — IIHS found it missing force limiters
- Ford Transit: 0.14 (178 deaths) — IIHS found it missing seat belt reminders
- GMC Savana: 0.54 (166 deaths) — wasn't even in the IIHS evaluation

**Van class average rate: 0.065**

**Commercial van impairment (FARS_TOXICOLOGY):**
- Ford Transit: 13.6% impairment — LOWEST for any commercial van
- Chevrolet Express: 19% impairment
- Ford E-350: 15.7% impairment
- These are LOWER than passenger car averages (~20%), suggesting these are mostly sober, working drivers

**Key insight:** Commercial vehicle drivers have LOWER impairment rates than passenger car drivers. They're dying sober, on the clock. The vehicles are killing them, not the drivers' behavior.

### Source 4: Regulatory gap data
- Force limiters became standard in passenger cars in early 2000s (FMVSS 208 upgrade)
- Advanced seat belts (pretensioners + force limiters) standard since ~2005 in most passenger vehicles
- No FMVSS equivalent requires these in commercial vehicles over 10,000 lbs GVWR
- AEB not required for commercial vehicles (passenger vehicle mandate finalized 2024, effective by ~2029)
